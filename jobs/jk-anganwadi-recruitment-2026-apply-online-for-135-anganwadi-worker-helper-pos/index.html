Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must fulfil the residence and domicile conditions notified for the Sangini and Sahayika posts before applying for the CDPO Poshan URI Anganwadi Recruitment 2026. Applicants must be women candidates only. The candidate must be a domicile of the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir. The candidate must be a resident of the Electoral Ward where the Anganwadi Centre (AWC) is situated. Name in the voter list of the ward is treated as proof of residence. If the candidate's name appears in the voter list along with her parents, she must provide a certificate of being unmarried issued by the concerned Tehsildar. In case of any residence dispute, a residence proof certified by the concerned SDM/ACR will be considered. Educational Qualification

The minimum and maximum educational qualification differs for Sangini (Anganwadi Worker) and Sahayika (Anganwadi Helper) posts as per the notification. Minimum qualification: 10+2 pass. Maximum qualification: Graduation. Candidates with qualification higher than graduation shall not be considered. If no 10+2 pass candidate is available in the ward, a 10+2 pass candidate from the adjoining ward may be considered with prior approval of the Mission Director, Mission Poshan. Selection shall be purely on merit basis using marks obtained in 10+2; no other criteria shall be considered. Minimum qualification: Matriculation (Class 10th). If no matriculate candidate is available in the panch ward, candidates with qualification not less than 8th standard shall be considered. Maximum qualification for the Helper post is Class 10th as per the applicable HR policy.

Honorarium / Pay

Both Sangini and Sahayika posts under POSHAN Project URI are engaged purely on a honorarium basis. The notification does not specify the exact honorarium amount; candidates may confirm the current honorarium rate applicable to AWW/AWH posts from the CDPO Poshan URI office or the HR policy No. 222-JK(SWD) of 2022 dated 30-11-2022.

JK Anganwadi Recruitment 2026 : Selection Process & Exam Pattern

Selection for both Sangini and Sahayika posts follows a merit-based process as envisaged in Government Order No. 103 JK(SWD) of 2023. Selection is made purely on the basis of marks obtained in the qualifying examination — 10+2 for Sangini and Matriculation (or 8th standard, where applicable) for Sahayika. Candidates belonging to the family of a retiring AWW/AWH receive an additional two percent weightage in merit. In case of a tie in merit, the candidate with higher age is preferred. All applicants must submit a self-attested affidavit, duly attested by a 1st Class Magistrate, certifying that submitted documents are correct and free from mismatch, duplication, or fallacious information.
