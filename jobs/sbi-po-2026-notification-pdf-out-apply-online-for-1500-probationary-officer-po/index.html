SBI PO Eligibility Criteria 2026

Eligibility for the SBI PO post is built on two pillars: an age limit checked as on a fixed cut-off date, and an academic qualification checked as on a separate, later cut-off date. Candidates must satisfy both conditions independently to be considered eligible for this recruitment.

SBI PO Age Limit 2026: 21 to 30 Years

Candidates applying for SBI PO Recruitment 2026 must be not below 21 years and not above 30 years as on 01 April 2026 , meaning they must have been born between 02 April 1996 and 01 April 2005 (both dates inclusive). Age relaxation is available to specific categories, but the notification clarifies that cumulative age relaxation across multiple categories is not permitted. SBI PO Educational Qualification 2026

Candidates need a Graduation degree in any discipline from a recognised university, or an equivalent qualification recognised by the Central Government, as on 30 September 2026 . Final-year or final-semester students can also apply provisionally, but they must produce proof of having passed the graduation examination on or before 30 September 2026 if shortlisted for interview. Candidates holding professional qualifications such as Medical, Engineering, Chartered Accountancy, or Cost Accountancy, as well as those with an Integrated Dual Degree (IDD) passed by the cut-off date, are also eligible.

Number of Attempts Allowed in SBI PO Exam

SBI has fixed a maximum number of attempts permissible for this exam based on category, and candidates who have already exhausted their permissible chances are not eligible to apply again. Appearing in the Main Examination counts as one attempt, while appearing only in the Preliminary Examination does not count as an attempt.

SBI PO Application Fee 2026

The application fee for SBI PO Recruitment 2026 is Rs 750 for Unreserved (UR), EWS, and OBC candidates , while SC, ST, and PwBD candidates are exempted from paying any fee . This fee is non-refundable once paid and cannot be carried forward or held in reserve for any other examination, so candidates should confirm their category and details before making the payment. CIBIL Score Requirement for SBI PO Candidates

A distinctive eligibility condition for this recruitment relates to credit history, since SBI PO is a banking role with financial responsibility. Candidates who have defaulted on repayment under any loan, NBFC, or financial institution arrangement, including credit card dues , and whose accounts reflect adverse credit history in CIBIL or another recognised credit agency, must regularise or repay all outstanding dues on or before the date of joining the Bank . If the adverse status has not been updated in CIBIL records by the joining date, candidates must either get the status updated or produce a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the lender confirming there are no outstanding dues; failure to do so will render the candidate ineligible and the offer of appointment will be withdrawn. Candidates are advised to check their CIBIL score before applying.

SBI PO Selection Process 2026: Prelims, Mains & Interview

Selection for SBI PO Recruitment 2026 follows a three-phase process : Phase-I Preliminary Examination, Phase-II Main Examination, and Phase-III Psychometric Test, Group Exercise & Interview. Candidates must clear each phase to be considered for the next, and only the marks from Phase-II and Phase-III are carried forward to prepare the final merit list. The Preliminary Examination is an online objective test carrying 100 marks , to be completed in 1 hour , with separate timing for each of its three sections. There is no sectional cut-off in the Preliminary Exam, but a category-wise merit list is prepared based on the aggregate score, and candidates numbering approximately 10 times the vacancies in each category are shortlisted for the Main Exam. Joining, Bond and Probation Period

Selected candidates must complete a basic banking knowledge online course before joining and will be designated as Probationary Officers , placed on probation for two years with continuous performance assessment. At the time of joining, candidates must execute a bond of Rs 2.00 Lacs , committing to serve the Bank for a minimum period of three years ; this bond is invoked if the candidate resigns before completing three years of service. Officers who clear the probation assessment standards are confirmed in JMGS-I, and SBI's promotion policy offers a path for high-performing officers to reach senior and top management grades, including opportunities for international postings. Documents and Identity Proof Required at Examination

Candidates must carry specific documents at each stage of the selection process, and missing or mismatched documents can lead to disqualification.
