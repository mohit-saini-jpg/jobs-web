Eligibility Criteria: Age Limit and Qualification

Eligibility for every post is assessed as on 01 June 2026 , the cut-off date fixed by the bank, and depends on a candidate's age, educational qualification, and post-qualification work experience in banks, financial institutions, or NBFCs. Key Roles and Responsibilities

A few common themes run across most of these positions. The Vice President and Deputy Vice President-level Relationship Head and Senior Relationship Manager roles centre on growing and managing the bank's existing corporate relationships in their specialised segment - real estate, MNCs, credit-lite clients, or conglomerates - sourcing new-to-bank corporate clients, cross-selling fee-based products such as forex and cash management services, and building account plans with opportunity-income analysis while keeping risk exposure within the bank's Exposure Risk Framework. The Transaction Banking roles (Head-Product Sales, Senior Product Sales Manager, and Product Sales Manager) focus on selling trade, forex, and cash management products, managing transaction banking client relationships, and driving fee income and CASA balances. Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Application Fee Details

Bank of Baroda has set a two-tier, non-refundable application fee for this recruitment. Unreserved (UR), EWS, and OBC candidates must pay Rs 850 (inclusive of GST), plus applicable payment gateway charges, while SC, ST, PWD, Ex-servicemen/DESM, and Women candidates pay Rs 175 (inclusive of GST), plus payment gateway charges. The fee can be paid online using a Debit Card, Credit Card, Internet Banking, or UPI. It is payable irrespective of whether an online test is eventually conducted and irrespective of whether the candidate is shortlisted for an interview, and once paid, it will not be refunded under any circumstances. Contract Tenure, Remuneration and Credit Score Requirement

These are fixed-term contractual roles, and the tenure depends on the grade. For AVP I and AVP II , the initial contract runs for 5 years from the date of joining and can be renewed at the bank's discretion, subject to satisfactory performance, for one additional period of up to 5 years. For VP and DVP , the initial contract is for 3 years and can be extended in increments of 1 year each, provided the total engagement does not exceed 5 years. In every case, the engagement automatically ends at contract expiry unless renewed, and it cannot continue beyond the candidate reaching 60 years of age. Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Selection Process & Exam Pattern

Selection proceeds through Application Scrutiny, followed by an Online Test, Psychometric Test, or any other test the bank considers suitable, and then a Personal Interview along with compensation negotiation for those selected. The Psychometric Test and/or Interview assess a candidate's personality, communication, clarity, problem-solving ability, efficiency, and willingness to work anywhere in the country. To qualify in the Personal Interview, a candidate must score at least 60% for the Unreserved category and 55% for Reserved category candidates, though the bank reserves the right to revise this cut-off. If multiple candidates tie at the cut-off mark, they are ranked in descending order of age, meaning the older candidate ranks higher. Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for Bank of Baroda C&IC Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date for submission of the application and payment of fee is 06 July 2026; online registration started on 16 June 2026.
Q2How many vacancies are there in this recruitment?
A
There are 65 vacancies across 12 positions - VP, DVP, AVP I, and AVP II grades - in the Corporate and Institutional Credit Department.
Q3What is the application fee?
A
The fee is Rs 850 (inclusive of GST) plus payment gateway charges for UR/EWS/OBC candidates, and Rs 175 (inclusive of GST) plus payment gateway charges for SC/ST/PWD/ESM-DESM/Women candidates. The fee is non-refundable.
Q4Is there a minimum CIBIL score requirement?
A
Yes. Candidates must have a CIBIL score of 680 or above at the time of joining; a default on loan or credit card repayment, or adverse credit reports, will make a candidate ineligible.
Q5What is the age limit for these posts?
A
Age limits vary by grade as on the 01 June 2026 cut-off date: 36–52 years for VP posts, 32–45 years for DVP posts, 30–40 years for AVP II posts, and 28–38 years for AVP I posts, with relaxation available for eligible categories where reservation roster points exist.
Q6What is the selection process?
A
Selection includes Application Scrutiny, an Online Test/Psychometric Test or other suitable test, a Personal Interview, and compensation negotiation. The minimum qualifying mark for the Personal Interview is 60% for UR and 55% for Reserved category candidates.
Q7How can I apply for this recruitment?
A
Applications must be submitted online only, through www.bankofbaroda.bank.in under the Career section's Current Opportunities page. No other mode of application is accepted.
Q8What is the tenure of these contractual posts?
A
AVP I and AVP II posts have an initial 5-year contract, extendable by up to 5 more years. VP and DVP posts have an initial 3-year contract, extendable by 1 year at a time up to a total of 5 years. The engagement ends at age 60 or the end of the contract term, whichever is earlier.

No. Out of 65 vacancies, only 8 are reserved for OBC candidates (across the AVP II–SPSM, AVP II–Relationship Manager, and AVP I–PSM posts), and the remaining 57 are unreserved. No vacancies are reserved for SC, ST, or EWS.
