PGCET, DCET 2026 Answer Key Out

The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) has published the PGCET and DCET 2026 provisional answer keys online, enabling candidates to download the keys, verify their responses and submit objections within the specified deadline through the official KEA website cetonline.karnataka.gov.in. This release is an important step before result declaration, as the final scores and ranks will be calculated on the basis of the final answer keys issued after resolving all valid challenges.

Karnataka Post Graduate Common Entrance Test (PGCET) and Diploma Common Entrance Test (DCET) are state-level entrance examinations conducted by KEA for admission to postgraduate programmes and lateral entry engineering seats in Karnataka. With the answer keys out, aspirants can get an early estimate of their scores and understand their chances of securing admission through the upcoming counselling rounds.

PGCET 2026 Answer Key: Release Details and Key Highlights

The Karnataka PGCET 2026 answer key has been released in provisional form, allowing candidates to check the official correct options for every question in their respective paper. KEA issues separate answer keys for various PGCET papers such as MBA, MCA and M.Tech/ME/M.Arch, ensuring that candidates can verify the exact question booklet they received in the exam.

The provisional PGCET answer key is an important transparency measure, giving candidates a chance to identify suspected errors and submit objections if they find any discrepancy. After the objection window closes, KEA reviews all challenges with supporting documents, makes necessary corrections and publishes the final PGCET 2026 answer key, which then forms the basis for score computation and ranking.

Karnataka PGCET 2026 exam overview

Karnataka PGCET is a pen-and-paper based entrance exam conducted for admissions to postgraduate programmes in management, computer applications and engineering/architecture in colleges across Karnataka. The exam is divided into different papers and sections according to the course, with each paper carrying its own pattern and marking scheme notified by KEA.

Type of PGCET 2026 answer key (provisional and final)

There are two main stages of the PGCET answer key-provisional and final. The provisional answer key is the first version released for public scrutiny, while the final answer key is issued after evaluating objections and is considered binding for result preparation.

Important dates for PGCET 2026 answer key and result

KEA publishes the schedule for answer key release, objection window, final answer key and result announcement through its official PGCET notifications. Candidates must regularly visit cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea to stay updated on these dates and avoid missing any important deadline related to objections or result checking.

DCET 2026 Answer Key: Release Details and Key Highlights

The Karnataka DCET 2026 answer key has also been released in provisional mode for all sets of the Diploma Common Entrance Test. Through this answer key, diploma candidates can tally the options they marked in the exam with the official correct answers and roughly estimate their DCET score even before the results are announced.

Just like PGCET, DCET 2026 answer key is first provided as a provisional key, and KEA accepts objections for a limited period. After examining all valid challenges with documentary proof, KEA publishes the final DCET 2026 answer key, which is then used to calculate marks and prepare the DCET rank list.

Karnataka DCET 2026 exam overview

Karnataka DCET is conducted for diploma holders and eligible B.Sc graduates seeking lateral entry into the second year of Bachelor of Engineering (BE) and Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) courses in engineering colleges across the state. The test is typically held offline, and KEA provides multiple versions of the question paper, each supported by a corresponding answer key.

DCET 2026 provisional and final answer key

In the provisional DCET answer key, KEA lists the correct option for every question in each set, allowing candidates to quickly cross-check their answers. Once objections are resolved, KEA publishes the final DCET 2026 answer key; this version is not open to further challenge and directly influences the scoring process.

Important dates for DCET 2026 answer key and result

The detailed date-wise schedule for DCET answer key release, objections and results is indicated through official KEA DCET notifications and calendar. Candidates must keep a close watch on the DCET section of the KEA website to know the last date for raising objections and the expected result declaration timeline.

How to Download PGCET 2026 Answer Key from cetonline.karnataka.gov.in

Step-by-step process to login and download

Candidates can follow the steps below to download the Karnataka PGCET 2026 answer key from the official KEA portal:

  • Open your browser and go to the KEA website at cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea.
  • On the homepage, locate the “PGCET 2026” link or a notice titled “PGCET 2026 Provisional Answer Key” or “Answer Key / Objections”.
  • Click on the relevant answer key/objection link; this may redirect you to the KEA Answer Key/Objection portal.
  • Log in, if required, using your PGCET application number, candidate name, date of birth and other details as specified in the instructions.
  • Choose the correct course (MBA, MCA, M.Tech/ME/M.Arch) and select your question paper version.
  • View the PGCET 2026 answer key on your screen and download the PDF version for offline reference.
  • Save or print the answer key so you can carefully verify each response and calculate your estimated PGCET score.

Details mentioned in the Karnataka PGCET answer key 2026

The PGCET answer key generally includes the question number, four options and the correct option for every question in the paper. The document may also indicate the question paper code or version, helping candidates ensure they are checking the right set.

How to Download DCET 2026 Answer Key from KEA Portal

Step-by-step process to access DCET answer key

To access the Karnataka DCET 2026 answer key, candidates can use the steps below:

  • Visit the KEA website at cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea.
  • Go to the “DCET 2026”, “Diploma CET” or similar section under the admissions menu.
  • Look for a link named “DCET 2026 Provisional Answer Key” or “Diploma CET Answer Key”.
  • Click on the link; KEA may host the answer key as a PDF or via the answer key/objection portal.
  • Select your question paper set or version and open the related DCET 2026 answer key.
  • Download and save the answer key in PDF format for detailed verification of your responses.

How to match your responses with DCET answer key

While matching answers, candidates should use their OMR sheet or memory of responses and compare each question with the official key. For every question, they need to note whether their marked answer matches the official correct option, count total correct attempts and use the marking scheme to estimate their DCET score.

How to Calculate Your PGCET, DCET 2026 Score Using Answer Key

Marking scheme for Karnataka PGCET 2026

In recent years, Karnataka PGCET has followed a pattern where each correct answer carries one mark, and there is typically no negative marking for incorrect responses. Candidates should confirm the exact marking scheme from the current year’s PGCET information brochure but can generally estimate scores quickly by counting correct answers.

Marking scheme for Karnataka DCET 2026

For DCET, KEA generally allots a fixed number of marks for each correct answer and usually does not apply negative marking in many past cycles. However, candidates must check the DCET 2026 brochure or official notification for the precise marking pattern applicable this year.

Example of calculating expected score

Suppose a PGCET candidate attempts 120 questions and finds 90 answers correct and 30 incorrect based on the answer key; with 1 mark per correct response and no negative marking, the estimated score would be 90 marks. Similarly, a DCET candidate can compute their tentative score by multiplying the number of correct responses by the marks per question as specified by KEA.

How to Raise Objections to PGCET, DCET 2026 Answer Key

KEA answer key objection link and login details

KEA provides an online objection facility where candidates can challenge any question or answer they believe is incorrect in the provisional key. The link to this objection portal is usually provided along with the answer key notice on the KEA website and requires candidates to log in using their exam credentials, such as application number and date of birth.

Documents required to challenge answer key

While submitting objections, candidates typically need to provide:

  • Exam name (PGCET or DCET), subject and question number
  • Question paper version code or set number
  • The answer they think is correct, with a clear explanation
  • Supporting reference such as a textbook, syllabus document or authoritative source.

Incomplete challenges or objections without proper supporting material may be rejected by KEA during the scrutiny process.

Rules and guidelines for PGCET, DCET answer key objections

Objections to PGCET and DCET answer keys can be submitted only within the officially specified dates; any complaint raised after the deadline is generally not entertained. Once KEA completes the evaluation of all valid objections, the final answer keys are uploaded, and no further changes or objections are permitted to those keys.

PGCET, DCET 2026 Eligibility and Next Steps After Answer Key

Basic eligibility for Karnataka PGCET 2026 (MBA, MCA, M.Tech)

The basic eligibility criteria for PGCET differ for MBA, MCA and M.Tech/ME/M.Arch, but in general, candidates must hold a relevant bachelor’s degree from a recognized university with the minimum aggregate marks prescribed by KEA. For example, MBA applicants typically need a bachelor’s degree in any discipline, while M.Tech/ME/M.Arch aspirants must possess a BE/B.Tech or equivalent degree in a related branch as per course requirements.

Basic eligibility for Karnataka DCET 2026 (lateral entry)

Karnataka DCET is aimed at diploma holders and eligible B.Sc graduates who wish to join BE/B.Tech programmes through lateral entry into the second year. Candidates must have a diploma in engineering or an equivalent qualification from a recognized board, with a minimum aggregate percentage specified by KEA in the official information brochure.

What happens after final answer key and result (rank, counselling)

After publishing the final PGCET and DCET answer keys, KEA releases the results and rank lists for all candidates who appeared for the exams. Based on these ranks, category reservations and available seat matrix, candidates are invited to participate in KEA’s online counselling process, where they can register, fill options, lock choices and receive seat allotments for their preferred courses and colleges.
