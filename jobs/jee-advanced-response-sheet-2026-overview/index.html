How to Download JEE Advanced Response Sheet 2026

Step-by-Step Process to View Responses

Candidates can follow these simple steps to download their JEE Advanced 2026 response sheet from the official portal.

  • Visit the official JEE (Advanced) website: jeeadv.ac.in.
  • On the homepage, click on the link for “JEE Advanced 2026 Response Sheet” or “Copy of Candidate Responses.”
  • You will be redirected to the candidate portal (for example, cportal.jeeadv.ac.in) where login is required.
  • Enter your JEE Advanced 2026 registration number, date of birth and other required details as mentioned on the screen.
  • Click on the “Submit” or “Login” button to access your account.
  • Under the candidate dashboard, locate the “View Responses” or “Response Sheet” section.
  • Click to open your response sheet for Paper 1 and Paper 2 and verify the recorded answers.
  • Download the response sheet in PDF format and save it for future reference and score calculation.

Details Required to Login

To download the JEE Advanced response sheet 2026, candidates should keep the following login details ready as per the official portal requirements.

  • JEE Advanced 2026 registration number
  • Date of birth (in the format specified on the portal)
  • Additional fields such as registered mobile number or password, if asked on the candidate portal

Entering accurate login credentials is essential, as incorrect entries may prevent access to the response sheet or show no data.

How to Use JEE Advanced Response Sheet 2026 to Estimate Score

The primary purpose of the JEE Advanced response sheet is to let candidates verify their marked responses and estimate their probable score before the results are out. Once the provisional answer key is released, candidates can compare each response with the official correct answer to calculate their marks.

Matching Responses with Provisional Answer Key

After downloading the response sheet, candidates should wait for the provisional answer key, which is scheduled to be published on May 25, 2026. When both documents are available, you can:

  • Place the response sheet and provisional answer key side by side.
  • For each question, check whether your recorded response matches the correct option in the answer key.
  • Note questions left unattempted and those where you might have chosen an incorrect option.

This process helps you understand your performance in each section and identify topics where you did well or need improvement.

Calculating Probable Marks

JEE Advanced uses its own marking scheme, including full, partial and negative marks depending on the question type. Using the response sheet and answer key:

  • Add marks for each correct response as per the marking scheme for that particular question.
  • Subtract marks for questions with negative marking if your response does not match the correct answer.
  • Sum the scores for all questions across Paper 1 and Paper 2 to get an approximate total.

This estimated score is only indicative and the final score will be based on the official evaluation and final answer key.

JEE Advanced 2026 Exam - Basic Details (For Reference)

JEE Advanced is conducted for admission to undergraduate programmes in engineering, sciences and architecture at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). For 2026, IIT Roorkee is the organising institute and the exam consists of two compulsory papers conducted on the same day.

Each paper typically includes questions from Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics, with a mix of single-correct, multiple-correct and numerical answer types. Candidates must appear in both Paper 1 and Paper 2 to be considered for ranking, and the total score is calculated based on performance in both papers combined.

Important Instructions for JEE Advanced 2026 Candidates

Candidates should follow certain important instructions while checking their JEE Advanced 2026 response sheet and using it for evaluation.

  • Ensure you download response sheets for both Paper 1 and Paper 2 and verify that your name and roll number are correctly mentioned.
  • Always download the response sheet from the official website only and avoid sharing your login details with others.
  • Save the PDF on a secure device and consider taking a printout for easy comparison with the provisional answer key.
  • Use the response sheet only to estimate your score and not as final proof of marks, since the official result may consider other evaluation factors.
  • If you find discrepancies related to question options or answers, wait for the provisional answer key and use the official objection mechanism within the given window.

Following these instructions will help you make the best use of your response sheet without relying on unverified information from external sources.
