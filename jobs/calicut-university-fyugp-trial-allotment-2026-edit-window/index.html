Calicut University FYUGP Trial Allotment 2026 Edit Window : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 18/06/2026

The University of Calicut released the Calicut University FYUGP trial allotment 2026 on June 17 at the official admission portal, admission.uoc.ac.in. This trial allotment is for candidates who registered for the Four Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUGP) under the UGCAP 2026 admission process. Candidates can log in with their application credentials to view their probable college and programme allotment, and those who wish to revise their choices can use the editing window open from June 17 to June 19. The Calicut University FYUGP first allotment 2026, which is the first binding allotment, will be published on June 22.

This article covers everything candidates need to know about the Calicut University FYUGP trial allotment 2026, including the editing schedule, the complete UGCAP admission timeline, how to check the allotment online, and the steps to follow after receiving an allotment.

Calicut University FYUGP Trial Allotment 2026 Edit Window

Candidates who are not satisfied with their FYUGP trial allotment can revise their college and course preferences during the official editing window, which is open from June 17 to June 19, 2026. No fee is charged for editing preferences at this stage. Once the edit window closes, no further changes can be made, and the Calicut University FYUGP first allotment 2026 will be generated based on whichever preference order — edited or unedited — is in place when the window shuts.

How to Check Calicut University FYUGP Trial Allotment 2026?

This section gives the exact step-by-step process to view and download the FYUGP trial allotment result online.

Candidates can check the Calicut University FYUGP trial allotment 2026 by following these steps:

  • Visit the official admission portal at admission.uoc.ac.in.
  • Click on the FYUGP link under the "Admission" section on the homepage.
  • This redirects to the admission page for the Four Year Undergraduate Programme.
  • Click on the "Rank List" or "Trial Allotment" tab to access the allotment list.
  • Log in using the application number and required credentials.
  • The trial allotment result will be displayed on screen, showing the probable college and programme.
  • Download the PDF copy of the allotment for future reference.

Calicut University FYUGP Seat Allotment Process 2026

This section outlines what candidates must do once a seat is confirmed in a later, binding allotment round.

Once a candidate receives a confirmed seat allotment (starting from the first allotment), the general process to secure admission involves:

  • Downloading the provisional admission letter from the portal.
  • Reporting to the allotted college for document verification within the specified deadline.
  • Paying the prescribed admission fee to confirm the seat.
  • Appearing for any required counselling or verification at the college to finalise the admission.

Other Calicut University Admission Programmes Running Alongside FYUGP

This section briefly notes the other admission processes the university is conducting in the same period, since several candidates track multiple programmes together.

Along with FYUGP, the University of Calicut is simultaneously conducting admissions for several other programmes under the Common Admission Process (CAP) framework:

  • PGCAP (Postgraduate): The PGCAP first allotment 2026 was also published on June 17, with fee payment required between June 17 and 20.
  • B.Ed CAP: The B.Ed trial allotment 2026 is scheduled for June 19.
  • Afzal-Ul-Ulama programmes: These follow a separate registration and allotment timeline, with registration closing around June 15.
  • Integrated PG Programmes (IPCAP): These run on their own parallel schedule of trial, first, and second allotments.

Candidates applying to more than one programme should track each portal section separately, as allotment dates differ across courses.

What to Do After Calicut University FYUGP Trial Allotment 2026?

This section explains the practical next steps candidates should take immediately after viewing their trial allotment.

After checking the FYUGP trial allotment, candidates should compare their allotted college and programme against their original preference list. If satisfied, no action is required, and the same preference order will carry forward to the first allotment. If not satisfied, candidates should use the June 17 to 19 editing window to rearrange or modify their college and course choices. No fee is needed for this step. Once the first allotment is published on June 22, candidates who receive a seat will need to complete document verification and mandatory fee payment within the given deadline to confirm admission.
