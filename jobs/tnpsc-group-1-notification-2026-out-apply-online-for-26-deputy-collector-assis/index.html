Exam Pattern

The TNPSC Group 1 selection process consists of three stages: Preliminary Examination, Main Examination, and Interview. Understanding the exam pattern helps in strategic preparation. | The Preliminary Examination is purely a screening test. Marks scored in the Prelims are not counted for final merit ranking. | Type: Objective (OMR-based) Language: Tamil and English | Candidates who qualify Prelims (up to 20 times the number of vacancies) are shortlisted for the Main Examination, held at Chennai only. | Paper I (Tamil) is qualifying in nature - Papers II, III, IV are evaluated only if Paper I is cleared Appearance in all papers is compulsory - absence in any paper disqualifies the candidate The Interview carries 100 marks with no minimum qualifying marks Differently abled candidates can claim exemption from Paper I (Tamil Eligibility Test) | Candidates are shortlisted for the Interview based on Main Examination scores - 2 times the vacancies (if vacancies ≥ 5 in a reservation group) or 3 times (if vacancies ≤ 4). The interview assesses personality, aptitude, general knowledge, and suitability for civil service. | The final merit list is prepared based on combined marks of Main Exam (Papers II + III + IV) and Interview (total 850 marks). Prelims marks are excluded. In case of a tie, the candidate senior in age is ranked higher; if age is also the same, the one with higher Main Exam marks ranks higher; if still tied, the earlier application submission date determines the rank.

FAQs

Q1What is the last date to apply for TNPSC Group 1 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for TNPSC Group 1 2026 is July 29, 2026 at 11:59 PM. The Application Correction Window is open from August 2 to August 4, 2026.
Q2How many vacancies are there in TNPSC Group 1 2026?
A
There are a total of 26 vacancies in TNPSC Group 1 2026 across five posts: Deputy Collector (12), District Registrar (8), Deputy Registrar of Co-operative Societies (3), Assistant Commissioner (Commercial Taxes) (2), and Assistant Commissioner of Labour (1).
Q3What is the age limit for TNPSC Group 1 2026?
A
The minimum age is 21 years. The maximum age for general (Others) category candidates is 34 years (35 for BL degree holders for post 1003). SC/ST/MBC/BC candidates get a 5-year relaxation, i.e., up to 39 years (40 for BL degree holders). Age is calculated as on July 1, 2026.
Q4What is the educational qualification required for TNPSC Group 1 2026?
A
Candidates must possess a degree from any recognized University in India (incorporated by an Act of Parliament/State Legislature or declared a deemed university under the UGC Act, 1956). Specific degree preferences apply for the Assistant Commissioner (Commercial Taxes) post.
Q5What is the exam date for TNPSC Group 1 Preliminary Exam 2026?
A
The TNPSC Group 1 Preliminary Examination 2026 is scheduled on September 6, 2026, from 9:30 AM to 12:30 PM.
Q6What is the salary for TNPSC Group 1 posts?
A
All TNPSC Group 1 posts are at Pay Level 22 of the Tamil Nadu Government Pay Matrix, making it one of the highest-paying state civil service positions in Tamil Nadu.
Q7What is the application fee for TNPSC Group 1 2026?
A
The Preliminary Examination application fee is Rs. 100/-. The Main Examination fee (for shortlisted candidates) is Rs. 200/-. SC/ST candidates, Persons with Benchmark Disability, and Destitute Widows are fully exempt. BC/BCM/MBC/DC candidates get three free chances and Ex-Servicemen get two free chances.
Q8How many stages are there in TNPSC Group 1 selection process?
A
The TNPSC Group 1 selection process has three main stages: Preliminary Examination (objective screening test), Main Examination (four descriptive papers), and Interview (100 marks). Additionally, onscreen certificate verification, physical certificate verification, medical examination, and counselling are also part of the process.
Q9Where is the TNPSC Group 1 Main Examination held?
A
The TNPSC Group 1 Main Examination 2026 will be held at Chennai only (Centre Code: 0101). Candidates from all districts must appear at Chennai for the Main Exam.
Q10Is Tamil knowledge mandatory for TNPSC Group 1?
A
Yes, candidates must possess adequate knowledge of Tamil as on the date of notification. This is satisfied if the candidate has passed SSLC/HSC/degree with Tamil as a subject, studied in Tamil medium, or passed the Second Class Language Test (Full Test) in Tamil conducted by TNPSC. Paper I of the Main Exam is a Tamil Eligibility Test (though differently abled candidates can claim exemption).
