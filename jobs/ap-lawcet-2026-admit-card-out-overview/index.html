AP LAWCET 2026 Admit Card Out - Overview

Andhra Pradesh Law Common Entrance Test (AP LAWCET) 2026 is conducted for admission into 3‑year LL.B and 5‑year integrated LL.B programmes offered by law colleges across Andhra Pradesh. The admit card (hall ticket) is a mandatory document that candidates must carry to the test centre, as it confirms their candidature and provides details such as exam date, timing and venue.

With the AP LAWCET 2026 admit card now available online, candidates should immediately download and verify it to avoid last‑minute issues. This article covers key highlights, download steps, details mentioned on the hall ticket, exam‑day instructions, official links and quick FAQs to fully satisfy user intent.

AP LAWCET 2026 Exam Date, Time and Centres

AP LAWCET 2026 exam schedule

AP LAWCET 2026 will be conducted on 4 May 2026 in online mode. The exam is held in separate sessions for different courses, typically with one session for 3‑year LL.B and another for 5‑year integrated LL.B on the same day.

Candidates should carefully check on their hall ticket:

  • Exact exam date (it will show 4 May 2026).
  • Session timing (morning or afternoon).
  • Reporting time, which is usually at least 60 minutes before the exam start.

Exam centres

AP LAWCET is conducted at multiple test centres across Andhra Pradesh. The hall ticket displays:

  • Centre name.
  • Centre code.
  • Full address (including city and pin code).

You can advise students to locate their centre on a map a day before the exam and plan travel time, especially if the centre is in another town or on the outskirts of the city.

Courses and Basic Eligibility (Quick Snapshot)

Although this article focuses on the admit card, a short eligibility reminder helps new users.

Courses covered

  • 3‑year LL.B - after graduation in any discipline from a recognised university.
  • 5‑year LL.B - after 10+2 or equivalent from a recognised board.

Basic eligibility overview

  • For 3‑year LL.B: Candidates should hold a Bachelor’s degree with minimum qualifying marks as per category.
  • For 5‑year LL.B: Candidates should have passed 10+2 with prescribed minimum marks.
  • Category‑wise relaxation, age conditions and other details are given in the official AP LAWCET information brochure.

Step‑by‑step hall ticket download guide

  • Visit the official website Go to cets.apsche.ap.gov.in in your browser and click on the LAWCET section from the list of CETs.
  • Open the hall ticket page On the AP LAWCET homepage, click on the link labelled “Download Hall Ticket” or directly open the LAWCET_GetPrintHallTicket URL.
  • Enter login details On the hall ticket page, you will typically be asked to enter: Registration number / application number Qualifying examination hall ticket number Date of birth (as per application form)
  • Registration number / application number
  • Qualifying examination hall ticket number
  • Date of birth (as per application form)
  • Submit the details Click on “Submit” / “Get Hall Ticket” after entering all details correctly.
  • View and verify your admit card Your AP LAWCET 2026 admit card will appear on the screen. Check your: Name and personal details Course (3‑year or 5‑year LL.B) Exam date and time Exam centre address Photograph and signature (if printed)
  • Name and personal details
  • Course (3‑year or 5‑year LL.B)
  • Exam date and time
  • Exam centre address
  • Photograph and signature (if printed)
  • Download and print Download the hall ticket as a PDF and take a clear printout on A4 paper. Keep at least two copies so you have a backup.

If there is any technical error (page not loading or details not accepted), candidates should retry after some time or use a different browser. Persistent issues should be reported through the official help contacts.

Details Mentioned on AP LAWCET 2026 Hall Ticket

Candidates should cross‑check every detail printed on the admit card. Typically, it includes:

  • Candidate’s name.
  • Father’s / mother’s name.
  • Photograph and signature.
  • Registration number / application number and roll number.
  • Course type (3‑year LL.B / 5‑year LL.B).
  • AP LAWCET 2026 exam date and session.
  • Exam timing and reporting time.
  • Exam centre code, name and full address.
  • Important exam‑day instructions and guidelines.

If any major discrepancy is found (for example, wrong course, wrong category, or significant spelling mistakes in the name), candidates should contact the exam authority for rectification before the exam.

Important Exam‑Day Instructions for AP LAWCET 2026

A focused instruction section helps you capture “People also ask” intent around what to carry, timings, etc.

  • Carry mandatory documents Bring a printed AP LAWCET 2026 hall ticket and a valid government photo ID (Aadhaar card, PAN card, voter ID, passport, driving licence, etc.). Without these, entry will likely be denied.
  • Reach the centre on time Reach at least 60 minutes before the exam start time to complete frisking, ID verification and seating. Latecomers may not be allowed into the exam hall.
  • Follow the dress and gadget rules Do not carry mobile phones, smart watches, calculators, earphones, Bluetooth devices, notes or any electronic gadgets inside the examination centre.
  • Adhere to CBT guidelines AP LAWCET is conducted in computer‑based mode, so candidates should follow the instructions displayed on their screen and those given by invigilators.
  • Check seat and system details Sit only on the allotted system and verify that the question paper displayed corresponds to your course (3‑year or 5‑year).

Encourage candidates to read the complete set of instructions printed on the admit card before the exam day.
