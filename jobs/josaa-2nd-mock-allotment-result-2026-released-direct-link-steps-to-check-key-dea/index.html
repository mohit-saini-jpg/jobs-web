What Is the JoSAA 2nd Mock Allotment 2026?

The mock allotment is a practice simulation, not a confirmed seat. It shows candidates where they are likely to land based on their current rank and choices, giving them a chance to reconsider preferences before locking them in.

The mock allotment serves as a practice round, giving candidates an estimate of the seats they may receive based on their rank and selected preferences before the actual allotment process begins. This allows students to review their likely outcomes and make necessary changes to their choice list including adding, removing, or rearranging preferences before the final seat allocation rounds.

There are two mock rounds in JoSAA 2026 counselling. After the round 1 mock seat allotment was released, the authority uploaded the round 2 results. Both are only for reference, and thereafter the actual seat allocation results will be released.

How to Check JoSAA 2nd Mock Allotment Result 2026

Checking your mock allotment result is straightforward. Here is a step-by-step walkthrough for candidates.

Students need to go to the official website to check the JoSAA mock seat allotment result. Go to the official website, josaa.nic.in. Select the link 'View Mock Seat Allotment Result' under the 'Candidate Activity' section. Provide the JEE Main 2026 application number, password, and security pin and log in. The JoSAA 2026 mock seat allotment results will be displayed on a new tab.

Who Is Eligible to View the 2nd Mock Allotment Result?

Not all registered candidates will see a result in the second mock round. Eligibility depends on when choices were submitted.

Candidates who completed their registration and saved their choices by June 9, 5 PM can check the JoSAA mock seat allotment round 2 result on the official website, josaa.nic.in. Those who registered and filled choices after that deadline will only be reflected in the final allotment rounds, not in this mock.

What to Do After Checking Your 2nd Mock Allotment Result

The mock result is a decision-making tool, not a final outcome. Here is what candidates should do immediately.

After seeing their mock allotment, candidates should check whether they received their top choice. If the result shows a lower-ranked preference, this is the opportunity to re-evaluate and reorder the list add institutes, remove less-preferred options, or rearrange priorities before choice locking begins.

With the release of Mock Round 2, candidates should carefully review and finalise their college and branch preferences on the official JoSAA portal. Choice locking is currently active and began on June 10, 2026, at 1:00 PM IST. The deadline for registration and choice filling is June 11, 2026, at 5:00 PM IST, after which the system will automatically lock the last saved choices of candidates who have not manually completed the locking process.

What Happens If You Don't Lock Your Choices?

For candidates who fail to manually lock their choices before the deadline, JoSAA will automatically lock the submitted preferences after 5:00 PM on June 11, 2026. The automatically locked choices will be treated as final and will be used for subsequent seat allocation rounds.

This means even candidates who do not actively lock their list will still have their last saved preferences considered but it is always safer to manually lock so there are no last-minute technical surprises.

JoSAA 2026 Counselling Process - Overview

JoSAA is the centralised counselling and seat allocation platform for engineering admissions to India's top national institutions. It is a centralised counselling and seat allocation platform in India established by the Ministry of Education.

JoSAA consists of six rounds. Each round consists of the following steps: seat allotment, seat confirmation by candidates, online reporting at the allotted institute, and payment of fees. After all the rounds, any vacant seats remaining will be allotted via the CSAB counselling 2026.

Institutions Covered Under JoSAA 2026

JoSAA 2026 handles seat allocation for the following categories of institutions:

  • IITs - Indian Institutes of Technology (JEE Advanced qualifiers)
  • NITs - National Institutes of Technology
  • IIITs - Indian Institutes of Information Technology
  • GFTIs - Government Funded Technical Institutes
