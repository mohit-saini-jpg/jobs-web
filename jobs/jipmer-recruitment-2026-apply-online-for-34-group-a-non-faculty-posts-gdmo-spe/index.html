Qualification / Eligibility

Details
  1. Each post in the JIPMER 2026 recruitment carries specific educational qualification and experience requirements. Candidates must fulfil all conditions as on the last date of online registration (1 July 2026)
  2. Candidates must hold a recognised MBBS degree registered under the Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, along with the relevant postgraduate qualification and post-PG experience as per the specialty:
  3. Anaesthesiology: M.D. in Anaesthesiology + 3 years post-PG experience in a recognised institution. Cardiology: D.M. in Cardiology. 1 year post-DM experience required only for 2-year or 5-year DM courses; no experience required for 3-year or 6-year DM. Medicine: M.D. in Medicine + 3 years post-PG experience in a recognised institution. Nephrology: D.M. in Nephrology. 1 year post-DM experience required for 2-year or 5-year DM; no experience for 3-year or 6-year DM. Obstetrics & Gynaecology: M.D./M.S. in Obstetrics & Gynaecology + 3 years post-PG experience. Orthopaedics: M.S. in Orthopaedics + 3 years post-PG experience. Radio-Diagnosis: M.D. in Radio-Diagnosis + 3 years post-PG experience
  4. Candidates must hold a medical qualification included in Schedule I or II or Part II of the Third Schedule to the Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, along with 3 years of post-internship experience after completing the 1-year compulsory internship
  5. M.Phil. in Clinical Psychology or equivalent from a recognised institute/university. Minimum 2 years of experience in Child and Adolescent Mental Health in a recognised institute or hospital. Valid registration with the Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) - Central Rehabilitation Register (CRR) is mandatory

Important Instructions

Candidates must read these critical points before and after submitting their online application to avoid rejection or disqualification at any stage:
The application once submitted is final. No changes will be entertained under any circumstances. If an error is found, a fresh application with a new fee must be submitted before the closing date; the earlier application stands automatically cancelled. Candidates recruited for a particular JIPMER campus cannot claim transfer to another campus. However, the competent authority reserves the right to transfer candidates if needed. For the post of GDMO, candidates must indicate their order of campus preference (Puducherry, Karaikal, Yanam) at the time of application. This preference is final and cannot be changed at any stage. All updates regarding CBT, interview, and results will be published only on the JIPMER website. Individual letters will not be sent to shortlisted candidates. Candidates must appear for Certificate Verification/Interview at their own cost. No TA/DA will be paid. Mobile phones, calculators, digital watches, and all electronic gadgets are strictly prohibited at the examination centre. Legal jurisdiction for any disputes arising from the recruitment process will be the Union Territory of Puducherry.

FAQs

Q1What is JIPMER Recruitment 2026 notification number and date?
A
The JIPMER Group A Non-Faculty Recruitment 2026 notification number is JPADMN-1REC031/1/2026, dated 12 June 2026.
Q2What are the posts available in JIPMER Recruitment 2026?
A
Three categories of posts are available: Specialist Grade II (Junior Scale) in 7 specialties (Anaesthesiology, Cardiology, Medicine, Nephrology, Obstetrics & Gynaecology, Orthopaedics, Radio-Diagnosis) at Yanam; General Duty Medical Officer (GDMO) at Puducherry, Karaikal, and Yanam; and Child Psychologist at Puducherry. Total vacancies are 34.
Q3What is the last date to apply for JIPMER Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date for online registration is 1 July 2026 at 4:30 PM at jipmer.edu.in.
Q4What is the JIPMER 2026 exam date?
A
The Computer Based Test (CBT) is scheduled on 15 July 2026 (Wednesday). Hall tickets will be available for download from 9 July 2026 (tentative).
Q5What is the salary for GDMO in JIPMER 2026?
A
The pay for General Duty Medical Officer is ₹56,100/- in Level-10 of the 7th CPC Pay Matrix, plus Non-Practising Allowance (NPA).
Q6Is there negative marking in the JIPMER CBT 2026?
A
Yes, for GDMO and Child Psychologist posts, 1 mark is deducted for each incorrect answer, while each correct answer carries +4 marks. However, there is no negative marking for the Specialist Grade II (Junior Scale) posts.
Q7What is the application fee for JIPMER Recruitment 2026?
A
UR and OBC candidates must pay ₹1,500 + transaction charges; SC/ST candidates must pay ₹1,200 + transaction charges. PwBD candidates are exempted from the application fee.
Q8What documents are required to upload during the JIPMER 2026 online application?
A
Required uploads include a passport-size photograph (with name placard), signature, community certificate (if applicable), PwBD certificate (if applicable), experience certificate, NOC from employer (if applicable), educational qualification certificates, and valid Medical Council Registration Certificate.
