Mulugu Anganwadi Vacancy 2026 Breakdown

The Mulugu AWT AWH Recruitment 2026 covers vacancies spread across 115 Anganwadi Centres (AWCs) under the Venkatapuram and Tadvai ICDS projects. Vacancies are notified for Anganwadi Teacher and/or Anganwadi Helper posts at each centre, depending on local requirement. Each Anganwadi Centre listed in the notification specifies whether the AWT post, the AWH post, or both are vacant, along with the reserved category (SC, ST, or General) applicable to that specific post. Most vacancies in the Venkatapuram and Tadvai projects are reserved for Scheduled Tribe (ST) candidates, reflecting the tribal demographic composition of these mandals, with some centres reserved for Scheduled Caste (SC) candidates.

Mulugu Anganwadi Recruitment 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Candidates asking about Mulugu Anganwadi Bharti 2026 eligibility should know that only women applicants are eligible, and qualification requirements differ slightly for AWT and AWH posts. Local residency within the concerned Anganwadi Centre area is also a mandatory condition. For the Anganwadi Teacher (AWT) post, candidates must have passed Intermediate or 12th class as the mandatory minimum qualification. For the Anganwadi Helper (AWH) post, candidates need Intermediate qualification; however, in tribal (Girijan) areas where Intermediate-qualified candidates are not available, candidates who have passed SSC/10th class will also be considered eligible. Candidates wondering about the Mulugu Anganwadi age limit can note that applicants must be between 18 and 35 years of age as on 1 July 2026. No separate age relaxation categories are mentioned in the notification beyond this standard age

Mulugu Anganwadi Recruitment 2026 Selection Process

Candidates preparing for the Mulugu Anganwadi Teacher and Helper Recruitment 2026 should know that selection is based entirely on a merit-cum-interview system, with no written examination involved. Marks are awarded based on Intermediate qualification, special category status, and a personal interview. The final merit list is prepared by the District Selection Committee, Mulugu, based on the combined score of Intermediate marks, applicable special category marks, and interview performance. The District Collector, who heads the District Selection Committee, holds full authority to make necessary modifications to the recruitment process or reject applications submitted through CDPO/DWO or public offices outside the official online portal.
