Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. How many vacancies are there in BPSSC Havildar Instructor Recruitment 2026 and what is the post name?

Bihar Police Subordinate Services Commission has released 122 vacancies for the post of Adhinayak Anudeshak (Havildar Instructor), Level‑4 in the Bihar Home Guard Service Cadre under the Home Department (Special Branch), Government of Bihar.

Q2. What are the online application dates for Bihar BPSSC Havildar Instructor Recruitment 2026?

The online application window will be open from 01 May 2026 to 01 June 2026, and candidates must submit their forms and examination fee through the official BPSSC website bpssc.bihar.gov.in within this period; no offline applications are accepted.

Q3. How are the 122 Havildar Instructor vacancies distributed category‑wise and what is the women’s reservation?

The 122 posts are distributed among UR, SC, ST, EBC, BC, EWS and BC‑Women categories, with 35% horizontal reservation for women within each category, applicable only to women who are original residents of Bihar. In addition, 2% (2 posts) are reserved horizontally for grandchildren/great‑grandchildren of State freedom fighters receiving pension from the Central Government.

Q4. What is the educational qualification required for BPSSC Havildar Instructor 2026?

Candidates must have passed Intermediate (10+2) or an equivalent examination from a recognised board on or before the cut‑off date 01 August 2025. The written examination standard will be of Matric/10th level, as per Bihar School Examination Board syllabus, even though the minimum qualification is 12th pass.

Q5. What is the examination fee for BPSSC Havildar Instructor 2026 and how to pay it?

All candidates (male, female and third gender, all categories) must pay a non‑refundable examination fee of Rs. 100/-, which is payable online only through Net Banking, Debit/Credit Card or UPI on the BPSSC application portal; any chargeback or unsuccessful fee payment will lead to rejection of the application and may invite legal action.

Q6. How many candidates will be called for PET and what are the key PET benchmarks?

Candidates up to 5 times the number of vacancies in each category (based on written‑exam marks) will be called for PET. In the PET, male candidates must complete 1.6 km running within a maximum of 6 minutes (less than 5 minutes for 50 marks), while female candidates must run 1 km within a maximum of 5 minutes (less than 4 minutes for 50 marks), with graded marks for intermediate timings. Shot Put and High Jump also carry graded marks with three attempts per event, and failure to meet minimum distance/height or time leads to disqualification.
