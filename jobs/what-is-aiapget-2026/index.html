What is AIAPGET 2026? : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 11/07/2026

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has released the official notification for the All India Ayush Post Graduate Entrance Test (AIAPGET) 2026 on 10 July 2026. AIAPGET is the common entrance examination for admission to MD/MS postgraduate courses in Ayurveda, Unani, Siddha, and Homoeopathy for the Academic Session 2026-27. Online applications are open from 10 July to 30 July 2026 at the official website exams.nta.nic.in/aiapget.

What is AIAPGET 2026?

AIAPGET is conducted by NTA on behalf of the Ministry of Ayush, the National Commission for Indian System of Medicine (NCISM), and the National Commission for Homoeopathy (NCH), with the approval of the Ministry of Education. NTA has been conducting this examination since 2019. AIAPGET serves as the common entrance test for admission to All India Quota (AIQ) and State Quota seats in MD/MS programmes offered by all ASU&H (Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani & Homoeopathy) Colleges, Institutions, Universities, and Deemed Universities across India.

How to Apply for AIAPGET 2026

  • Visit the official website: https://exams.nta.nic.in/aiapget/
  • Carefully read the Information Bulletin before starting the application.
  • Register and fill in the online Application Form with accurate personal, educational, and category details.
  • Upload the prescribed photograph and signature as per specifications.
  • Complete the mandatory live photograph capture step during submission.
  • Complete Aadhaar-based authentication if opted, ensuring Aadhaar details match UIDAI records.
  • Select preferred examination cities carefully, as changes may not be permitted after the correction window closes.
  • Pay the examination fee through Credit/Debit Card, Net Banking, or UPI.
  • Download and save the Confirmation Page and proof of fee payment.

Note: Only one Application Form will be accepted from a candidate. Submission of multiple Application Forms may lead to rejection of candidature.

AIAPGET 2026 New Security & Verification Measures

To strengthen the security, transparency, and integrity of the examination process, NTA has introduced the following initiatives for AIAPGET 2026:

  • Aadhaar-based Authentication: Candidates are encouraged to authenticate themselves through Aadhaar while submitting the Application Form and should ensure their Aadhaar photograph and demographic details are updated as per UIDAI records. NTA reserves the right to verify identity and credentials at any stage of the admission process.
  • Live Photograph Capture: In addition to uploading a passport-size photograph, candidates must capture a live photograph during the application process. This live photograph will also appear on the Admit Card and Score Card. Candidates should ensure proper lighting, a plain light-coloured background, and clear visibility of the face, including ears, without a mask. Where webcam support is unavailable, the system allows capture through a mobile device via QR code.
  • UDID-based Verification for PwD/PwBD Candidates: Candidates claiming reservation under the PwD/PwBD category and holding a Unique Disability ID (UDID) Card must upload it for verification through the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities portal. Candidates without a UDID Card must upload a valid Disability Certificate issued by the Competent Authority, which may also be verified from the issuing authority.

AIAPGET 2026 Exam Cities

NTA will conduct AIAPGET 2026 in approximately 122 cities across India, covering all states and union territories. Major exam cities include New Delhi (Delhi NCR), Mumbai/Navi Mumbai/Thane/Kalyan, Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Jaipur, Lucknow, Bhopal, Patna, Guwahati, and Puducherry, among others. The complete city-wise list with city codes is published as Annexure-1 of the official notice. Candidates should select their preferred cities carefully during application, as changes may not be permitted after the correction window.
