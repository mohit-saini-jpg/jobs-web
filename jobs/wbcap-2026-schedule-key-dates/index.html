West Bengal Centralised Admission Portal (WBCAP) has been launched by the West Bengal Department of Higher Education for UG admission in government and government‑aided colleges. Under this system, admission is done purely on merit based on Class 12 (HS or equivalent) marks, and the entire process - from application to seat allotment - is online at wbcap.in.

With WBCAP, students can apply for various B.A., B.Sc. and B.Com programmes and other UG courses offered in different colleges using a single registration and application form. The system follows the National Curriculum and Credit Framework (NCCF) and covers newly introduced 4‑year undergraduate programmes in the state.

Courses and Colleges Under WBCAP 2026

Under WBCAP College Admission 2026, students can take admission to various UG courses in Arts, Science, Commerce and other disciplines in government and aided institutions. Popular programmes include B.A., B.Sc., B.Com (both Honours and general), along with other subject combinations offered by different colleges.

Many well‑known government and aided colleges in West Bengal participate in the centralised admission system, including top institutions in Kolkata, district towns and other regions. For example, colleges such as Asutosh College, Bethune College, Scottish Church College, Maulana Azad College, Vidyasagar College, and many more government degree colleges are part of the UG admission ecosystem in the state.

WBCAP 2026 Eligibility Criteria (Course‑wise)

Admission through WBCAP 2026 is strictly based on merit from the Higher Secondary (10+2) or equivalent examination. Students must meet minimum percentage and subject‑specific conditions as notified by the Department of Higher Education and individual colleges.

Below is a general overview of eligibility criteria, but students must always check specific college‑wise rules before applying.

B.A. (Honours) and B.A.

CourseEligibility (General Guidelines)
B.A. (Honours)Minimum around 50% aggregate in last qualifying exam, with merit calculated from one language and three best elective subjects. For Honours in a subject, the student should have studied and passed that subject in Class 12.
B.A. (General)Minimum around 50% aggregate in Higher Secondary or equivalent exam (subject to college‑specific relaxation and rules).

B.Com (Honours) and B.Com

CourseEligibility (General Guidelines)
B.Com (Honours)Must have studied and passed Mathematics or Business Mathematics at 10+2 level. Selection is generally based on marks in one language and three best subjects, with minimum aggregates around 45-50% depending on category and college.
B.Com (General)Usually requires around 50% aggregate marks in Higher Secondary, with merit based on one language and three best subjects, including Commerce‑related papers.

B.Sc. (Honours) and B.Sc.

CourseEligibility (General Guidelines)
B.Sc. (Honours)Must have studied and passed relevant science subjects such as Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry or Biology in Class 12. Minimum aggregate is generally around 50%, with higher marks (for example around 60%) in the Honours subject required by many colleges.
B.Sc. (General)Around 50% aggregate in Higher Secondary with Science stream subjects, merit again calculated from one language plus three best subjects.

Reservation, relaxation in marks and specific subject combinations may vary across institutions, so candidates should verify details in official college notices or on the WBCAP portal before locking choices.

Documents Required for WBCAP College Admission 2026

Students need to upload and later produce several essential documents during WBCAP 2026 application and counselling.

List of important documents for WBCAP 2026

  • Class 10 marksheet and certificate.
  • Class 12 (HS or equivalent) marksheet and certificate.
  • School leaving certificate.
  • Recent passport‑size photograph.
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC/PwD), if applicable.
  • Migration certificate, if applicable.
  • Copy or print of WBCAP/WB UG merit list or allotment letter.
  • Identity proof (as required by college, e.g., Aadhaar, voter ID).

All original documents must be produced during physical verification at the allotted college, otherwise admission may be cancelled.

How to Apply Online for WBCAP College Admission 2026?

The entire WBCAP 2026 admission process is online, and applications are accepted only through the official WBCAP portal. Students should keep all academic details and scanned documents ready before starting the form.

Step‑by‑step WBCAP 2026 online apply process

  • Visit the official website: Go to the official WBCAP portal at wbcap.in when the registration window opens.
  • Register as a new candidate: Click on the registration link and enter personal details like name, mobile number, email ID and basic academic information to create a login.
  • Fill the WBCAP application form: Log in with your credentials and complete the detailed form by providing Class 10 and Class 12 marks, subjects, board, passing year and other required information.
  • Upload documents: Upload scanned copies of photograph and necessary documents such as marksheets, certificates and category proof in the specified format and size.
  • Choose colleges and courses: Select your preferred colleges and courses (Honours/General) as per availability under WBCAP 2026 and arrange them in order of preference.
  • Submit the application: Carefully re‑check all entries and submit the WBCAP 2026 online form before the last date to avoid rejection due to mistakes.
  • Save printout: Download and keep a printout or PDF copy of the submitted application for future reference during counselling and admission.

Students must ensure that information entered in the online form matches the documents; discrepancies can lead to cancellation of candidature at the verification stage.
