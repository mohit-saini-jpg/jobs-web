What Is RIE CEE 2026? A Quick Overview

Before diving into admit card specifics, it helps to understand what this examination is and who it is meant for.

The Regional Institute of Education Common Entrance Examination (RIE CEE) is a national-level entrance test conducted by NCERT for admission to teacher education programmes across five prestigious Regional Institutes of Education (RIEs) in India. It is the single gateway for candidates seeking seats in BSc BEd, BA BEd, BEd, BEd-MEd, MSc Ed, and MEd programmes.

How to Download RIE CEE 2026 Admit Card - Step-by-Step Guide

Downloading the RIE CEE 2026 hall ticket is a straightforward process. Follow these six steps carefully to access your admit card without any issues.

  • Visit the official website - Go to cee.ncert.gov.in or use the direct login link provided by NCERT.
  • Click on the Admit Card tab - Look for the 'Admit Card' or 'Login' link on the homepage.
  • Enter your login credentials - Key in your Application Number / User ID and Password.
  • Complete OTP / security verification - Enter the security code sent to your registered mobile number when prompted.
  • View and verify your hall ticket - Your RIE CEE 2026 Admit Card will appear on screen. Check all details thoroughly.
  • Download and print - Save a PDF copy and take at least two printouts on A4 paper. Keep them safe until the admission process is fully complete.

Tip: Download your admit card well before the exam day. Heavy portal traffic closer to July 3 could cause delays.

The admit card carries all the essential information a candidate needs on exam day. After downloading, cross-check each of the following entries against your original application data.

  • Candidate's Full Name
  • Roll Number / Application Number
  • Photograph (passport-size)
  • Candidate's Signature
  • Date of Birth
  • Category (General / OBC / SC / ST / EWS / PwD)
  • Programme Applied For (Group A / B / C)
  • Exam Date and Reporting Time

If any detail appears incorrect or does not match your application, contact the NCERT CEE helpdesk immediately at 022-61306271 (Monday to Friday, 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM) before the exam date. Corrections cannot be made after the examination begins.

Programme Groups - Who Should Apply for What?

RIE CEE 2026 offers admissions across three groups, each covering different programmes and targeting different eligibility levels.

Group A - Integrated Undergraduate-Postgraduate Programmes

For candidates who have cleared Class 12 and want to enter teaching directly. Programmes include B.Sc B.Ed and B.A B.Ed (4-year integrated). Minimum eligibility: 50% in Class 12 for General/OBC/EWS candidates; 45% for SC/ST/PwD.

Group B - Postgraduate Integrated Programme

For graduates entering teaching at the postgraduate level. Programmes include M.Sc Ed (5-year integrated). Eligibility is based on bachelor's degree marks in relevant science or humanities subjects.

Group C - Professional Degree Programmes

For experienced candidates seeking advanced qualifications. Programmes include B.Ed, B.Ed-MEd (3-year integrated), and M.Ed. Requires a bachelor's or master's degree with a minimum of 50% marks (55% in some cases).

RIE CEE 2026 Composite Merit Score - How Is Your Rank Determined?

A candidate's final admission rank is not based solely on their CEE performance. NCERT uses a weighted composite score:

  • 60% weightage → RIE CEE examination score (out of 160 marks)
  • 40% weightage → Qualifying examination marks (Class 12 or graduation, depending on the group)

This means your qualifying examination marks matter significantly. A strong score in both components improves your chances of securing admission at a preferred institute.

RIE CEE 2026 Result and Counselling - What's Next After the Exam?

After appearing for the exam on July 3, candidates naturally want to know when results and counselling will be announced.

Based on the 2025 cycle, results are expected to be declared group-wise on cee.ncert.gov.in in August-September 2026. Group A results typically come first (around 5-6 weeks after the exam), followed by Groups B and C. NCERT will publish exact dates on the official portal after the examination concludes.

After results, qualifying candidates will be called for RIE CEE Counselling 2026, during which seats are allotted based on composite merit scores, category, and programme preferences. Counselling usually takes place in multiple rounds. Candidates must carry all original documents - admit card, mark sheets, ID proof, category certificates (if applicable), and photographs - to the counselling centre.

Exam Day Reporting Tips - Don't Arrive Unprepared

The final days before any major examination can be stressful. Here are some practical tips to ensure your exam day goes smoothly.

  • Check your exam centre address on the admit card a day in advance and plan your route.
  • Arrive at least 30-45 minutes before the reporting time mentioned on your hall ticket. Entry to the exam hall typically closes 15 minutes after the exam begins.
  • Paste your passport-size photograph on the admit card before leaving home - the invigilator may refuse entry if this is missing.
  • Carry your photo ID in original - photocopies or digital scans are not accepted.
  • Do not bring prohibited items - if a security scanner detects electronic devices or accessories, you may be denied entry.
  • Dress in light, comfortable clothing and avoid accessories that could trigger security checks.
