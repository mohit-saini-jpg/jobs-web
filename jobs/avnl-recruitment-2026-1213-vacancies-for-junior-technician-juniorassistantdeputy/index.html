AVNL Recruitment 2026: Exam Pattern

The written exam is a single Computer Based Test (CBT), objective multiple-choice type, for a maximum of 100 marks, with no negative marking. Part A questions are up to 10th Standard level for Junior Technician posts, and Graduate level for all other posts. Total time allowed for the written exam is 2 hours. Written exam weightage is 100% for Junior Technician posts and 85% for Junior Manager, Assistant Manager, and Deputy Manager posts. There is no written exam (CBT) for Senior Manager, Deputy General Manager, Additional General Manager, and Consultant posts.

AVNL Recruitment 2026: Selection Process

Junior Technician posts: CBT, followed by a qualifying Trade Test (Practical) at the respective AVNL unit. Merit is decided solely on Written Test marks; the Trade Test syllabus is per Annexure-A of the official notification. Junior Manager, Assistant Manager, Deputy Manager posts: CBT, followed by an Interview (50 marks, 15% weightage) assessing technical knowledge, prior work experience, initiative, and decision-making. Merit is decided on combined Written Exam and Interview performance. Senior Manager, Deputy General Manager, Additional General Manager, Consultant posts: No written CBT. Candidates are assessed through Presentation, Experience, Expertise, Knowledge, and Interview, conducted at AVNL CO, Avadi. Candidates qualifying the CBT will be called for Interview (other than Junior Technicians) or Trade Test (Junior Technicians) in a post:candidate ratio of 1:3 in order of merit wi

AVNL Recruitment 2026: Test Centres

The CBT will be held at: Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Jabalpur, Nagpur, Pune, Mumbai, New Delhi, Kolkata, and Kanpur. Candidates must indicate their preferred test centre in the online application form. AVNL reserves the right to modify, add, or cancel test centres depending on the number of eligible applicants; affected candidates will be assigned a new centre.
