NMDC Steel Limited Recruitment 2026: Eligibility Criteria

The minimum percentage of marks in the qualifying graduate/post-graduate degree/diploma courses shall be 60% aggregate for General candidates against Un-Reserved (UR), EWS, and OBC category. For candidates belonging to SC/ST/PwD category, the minimum percentage of marks in the respective Degree/PG Degree/PG Diploma shall be 50%. For Departmental candidates belonging to SC/ST/PwD category, the minimum percentage of marks shall be 45%. For Departmental candidates belonging to Un-Reserved category and OBC, the minimum percentage of marks shall be 55%. Candidates studying in the final year are also eligible to apply, but must pass the qualifying examination before GD & Interview and produce the final pass certificate at the time of GD & Interview; otherwise, their candidature will not be considered.

NMDC Steel Limited Recruitment 2026: Emoluments

Executive Trainees will undergo training for 6 months initially and will be offered a Basic Pay of Rs. 50,000/- plus Dearness Allowance (IDA) as per Company rules. On successful completion of training, Executive Trainees will be designated as Assistant Managers in the scale pay of Rs. 60,000-1,80,000/-, with initial Basic Pay fixed at Rs. 60,000/- per month. On successful completion of training, they will be entitled to Basic Pay, Dearness Allowance, perquisites under the Cafeteria Approach, PF, Gratuity, PRP, and other allowances and benefits as applicable under NSL rules. Being direct recruitment, the Company will not bear any liability on account of salary/leave salary/pension contribution of previous employment, if any.

NMDC Steel Limited Recruitment 2026: Selection Process

Candidates will be required to appear for a Computer Based Test (CBT) consisting of Multiple-Choice Questions at centres decided by the Management. Based on the CBT score and requirement, candidates will be shortlisted discipline-wise, category-wise for Group Discussion (GD) & Interview. The CBT will be conducted in Hindi & English and will consist of objective-type multiple-choice questions. Minimum qualifying marks in CBT to be considered for GD & Interview: 40 marks for SC/ST/PwBD, 45 marks for OBC-NCL, and 50 marks for UR/EWS category. Candidates will be called for GD & Interview based on CBT marks, discipline-wise and category-wise, in the ratio of 1:3. Where two or more candidates in the same discipline and category secure equal marks in CBT and the 1:3 ratio is exceeded, all candidates with equal marks will be called for GD & Interview irrespective of the ratio. Before joining, candidates must undergo a Pre-Employment Medical Examination by NSL's recognized hospital(s); the decision of NSL Management will be final and binding, and no relaxation in health standards is allowed.
