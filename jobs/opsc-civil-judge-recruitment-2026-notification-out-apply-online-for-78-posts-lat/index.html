The latest Odisha Public Service Commission (OPSC) Recruitment 2026 has been announced for Civil Judge (Junior Division) posts! Age Limit

DetailsCandidates must be not below 23 years and not above 42 years of age as on the 1st day of the month of April, 2025 (i.e., born not earlier than 2nd April 1983 and not later than 1st April 2002). Age limits prescribed cannot be relaxed beyond the provisions stated. | Date of birth as entered in the High School Certificate or equivalent certificate issued by the concerned Board/Council will only be accepted. A candidate coming under more than one category of age relaxation is eligible for only one benefit of upper age relaxation, whichever is most beneficial.

Important Instructions

  • Only candidates possessing the requisite qualifications, within the prescribed age limit and fulfilling all eligibility conditions strictly by the closing date will be considered eligible. No hard copy/offline applications will be accepted – online submission only. Examination fee is non-refundable in general; it will be refunded only to those who appear in the Preliminary Written Examination. Applications without payment of examination fees (except SC/ST/PwD candidates) will be outrightly rejected. SC/ST/SEBC reservation and relaxation are admissible only to SC/ST/SEBC of Odisha by birth. OBC certificates will not be accepted in lieu of SEBC certificates. SEBC candidates must submit Non-Creamy Layer Caste Certificate not more than 3 years old as on the date of submission. Community (caste status) once mentioned in the online application form shall not be changed subsequently under any circumstances. PwBD candidates must upload Permanent Disability Certificate only; temporary disability certificates will not be accepted. Government servants must obtain NOC from their Head of Office well in advance and produce it at document verification stage. Women candidates must submit caste certificates by birth (showing "daughter of…"); caste certificates by virtue of marriage (showing "wife of…") are not acceptable. Any complaint on the conduct of examination must be sent to the Grievance Wing by email ([email protected]) within 7 days of completion of the examination. Candidates submitting forged/manipulated documents will be debarred from appearing in OPSC examinations for 3 years. Mobile phones and electronic gadgets are banned inside examination premises. Candidates are advised to visit https://opsc.gov.in regularly for updates. For technical support during online registration, candidates may contact OPSC Technical Support at Telephone No. 0671-2304707 (10:30 AM to 1:30 PM & 2:00 PM to 5:30 PM on working days). All decisions of the Commission regarding eligibility, conduct of examinations, selection, and other matters are final and binding.
