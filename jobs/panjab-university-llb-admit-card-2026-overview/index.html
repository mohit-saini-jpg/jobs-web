Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026 - Overview : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 22/04/2026

Panjab University, Chandigarh has released the Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026 for the 5‑year integrated PU LLB entrance examination. Candidates who registered for the PU LLB 5‑year law entrance test can now download their admit card from the official law admission portals using their login credentials.

As per the schedule, the PU BA LLB 2026 entrance exam will be conducted on 26 April 2026 in offline mode at designated centres in Chandigarh.

PU LLB Admit Card 2026 Download Link

The university has activated the “Download Admit Card” link for PU LLB 5‑Year Integrated Course 2026 on the UGLAW portal. Candidates must log in using their registered credentials to download the Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026 and take a printout to carry on exam day.

The admit card is a mandatory document for entry into the exam hall and must be preserved until the completion of the admission and counselling process.

How to Download Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026

Candidates can follow the steps given below to download their PU LLB 2026 hall ticket:

  • Visit the official UGLAW portal for 5‑year LLB admissions: puchd.ac.in.
  • Click on the “Download your Admit Card” link for PU‑B.A./B.Com. LL.B. (Hons.) 5 Years Integrated Course – 2026.
  • Log in using your registered login ID/application number and password/date of birth as required.
  • After successful login, your Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026 will appear on the screen.
  • Check all details like your name, photograph, exam date, timing and test centre address carefully.
  • Download the admit card PDF and take one or more printouts to carry to the examination centre.

In case of any discrepancy in the hall ticket, candidates should immediately contact the Panjab University examination/UGLAW admission branch for rectification before the exam.

PU LLB 2026 Exam Date and Timing

The PU LLB 2026 entrance examination will be conducted on 26 April 2026. As per the official schedule, the test will be held in a single shift from 10:00 AM to 11:30 AM. The exam will be conducted in offline mode at designated test centres in Chandigarh, and candidates must reach well before the reporting time mentioned on the admit card.

Details Mentioned on Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026

The PU LLB 5‑Year admit card carries crucial information about the candidate and the exam. Candidates should verify the following details:

  • Candidate’s name and parents’ name
  • Roll number, application number and date of birth
  • Category and gender
  • Photograph and signature of the candidate
  • Exam name and course (LLB 5‑year)
  • Exam date: 26 April 2026
  • Exam timing: 10:00 AM to 11:30 AM
  • Test centre name and full address in Chandigarh
  • Important exam‑day instructions to be followed inside the exam hall

Any errors in these particulars should be reported immediately to Panjab University authorities.

Documents Required with Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026

On the PU LLB 2026 exam day, candidates must bring:

  • A printed copy of the Panjab University LLB Admit Card 2026 (preferably in good quality print)
  • One original photo identity proof such as: Aadhaar Card Voter ID Driving Licence Passport Any other government‑issued ID
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Voter ID
  • Driving Licence
  • Passport
  • Any other government‑issued ID
  • Additional passport‑size photographs if instructed by the university/admit card

Candidates without a valid hall ticket and acceptable ID proof will not be allowed to appear in the entrance test.

Exam‑Day Instructions for PU LLB Entrance 2026

Panjab University has issued important exam‑day guidelines which will also be mentioned on the admit card:

  • Reach the allotted exam centre well before the reporting time to complete verification and seating formalities.
  • Carry only essential items: PU LLB admit card, valid ID proof and permitted stationery (blue/black ball‑point pens, etc.).
  • Do not carry mobile phones, smart watches, calculators, Bluetooth devices or any electronic gadgets inside the exam hall.
  • Follow instructions given by invigilators and maintain discipline throughout the test.
  • Keep the admit card safe even after the exam, as it may be required during counselling/admission.
