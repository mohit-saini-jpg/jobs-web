WBBSE Madhyamik 2027 - Quick Overview : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 14/07/2026

The West Bengal Board of Secondary Education (WBBSE) has released the routine for the Madhyamik Pariksha (Class 10 board exam) 2027. According to the official schedule, the Madhyamik 2027 exams will begin on 15 February 2027 and end on 25 February 2027 for the main theory subjects. This article covers the complete subject-wise date sheet, exam timings, eligibility details, and what is still pending from WBBSE.

There is no exam on 17 February 2027 or on the weekend of 20–21 February 2027, based on the released routine.

Exam Timing and Reading Time

  • All Madhyamik 2027 theory papers will be held in a single morning shift, from 10:45 AM to 2:00 PM.
  • The first 15 minutes (10:45 AM to 11:00 AM) are reserved only for reading the question paper. Students cannot start writing during this time.
  • Only one subject will be examined per day.

Dates Not Yet Announced by WBBSE

Some parts of the Madhyamik 2027 schedule have not been released yet. WBBSE is expected to announce these separately:

  • Physical Education, Social Service, and Work Education exam dates: Not yet announced.
  • Practical exam dates for Music (Vocal/Instrumental), Computer Application, and Vocational subjects: Not yet announced. The venue, date, and time will be notified later for students who have opted for these subjects.

Students should keep checking the official WBBSE website for these updates rather than relying on unofficial estimates.

How to Download the WBBSE Madhyamik Routine 2027 PDF

  • Go to the official WBBSE website: wbbse.wb.gov.in.
  • Look for the notice or link titled "Madhyamik Pariksha Routine 2027."
  • Click on the link to open the routine PDF.
  • Check the complete subject-wise schedule and exam timings.
  • Download and save the PDF, or take a printout for reference.

Important Instructions for Madhyamik 2027 Candidates

  • Reach the exam centre well before the reporting time to complete verification formalities.
  • Carry the admit card issued by your school; it is mandatory for entry into the exam hall.
  • Use the first 15 minutes strictly for reading the question paper, not for writing answers.
  • Check for updates on Physical Education, Social Service, Work Education, and practical exam dates closer to the exam period.
