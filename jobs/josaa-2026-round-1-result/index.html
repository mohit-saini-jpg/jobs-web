How to Check the JoSAA 2026 Round 1 Seat Allotment Result

This section walks you through the exact steps to log in and download your allotment letter from the official JoSAA portal. Note that no separate notification will be sent - it is every candidate's responsibility to log in and check their allotment status.

Step-by-step process:

  • Visit the official JoSAA website: josaa.nic.in
  • Click on the "View Seat Allotment" or "Candidate Login" link on the homepage
  • Enter your JEE Main 2026 Application Number and Password
  • Enter the Security PIN/CAPTCHA as displayed
  • Click "Login" to access your allotment dashboard
  • Check your allotted institute and course
  • Download the "Initial Seat Allotment Intimation Slip" - this document contains full details of your allotted seat and the steps you must follow next

Pro Tip: The josaa.nic.in server experiences extremely heavy traffic on result day. If the page does not load immediately, wait 5-10 minutes and try again rather than repeatedly refreshing.

Understanding the JoSAA Seat Allotment Process

JoSAA functions as a large-scale matching engine that assigns seats based on three primary factors. Knowing how the algorithm works helps candidates understand their allotment and make smarter decisions.

The allotment algorithm considers:

  • Your JEE Main 2026 or JEE Advanced 2026 rank
  • Your category (General, OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST, PwD)
  • The order of choices you filled and locked during counselling

Important distinction: Seats in IITs and IISc are filled based on JEE Advanced ranks, while seats in NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs (Other Technical Institutes) are filled based on JEE Main ranks.

This year, JoSAA 2026 added 4,470 new seats compared to 2025, bringing the total to 67,323. The 3,104 female supernumerary seats are specifically created to increase female enrollment in engineering programmes and are over and above the regular seat matrix.

What to Do After Checking Your Round 1 Allotment

Once you have checked your allotment, you must complete a mandatory four-step process within the reporting deadline. Failing to complete all four steps will result in immediate cancellation of your allotted seat, and you will be barred from future JoSAA rounds.

Step 1: Choose Your Seat Preference Option (Freeze / Float / Slide)

After receiving your allotment, you must declare your willingness regarding the seat. There are three options:

OptionWhat It MeansWho Should Choose It
FreezeAccept the allotted seat and exit all future roundsCandidates fully satisfied with their current allotment
FloatAccept the seat but remain open to upgrades at a better institute or course in future roundsCandidates who want their current seat secured but hope for a better college
SlideAccept the seat but seek a better branch within the same institute in future roundsCandidates happy with the institute but want a different/preferred programme

Key rules to remember:

  • Freeze, Float, and Slide options are only available after paying the seat acceptance fee
  • If you were offered your first-choice programme, only the Freeze option is available
  • Float and Slide options are not available in the last counselling round
  • Rejecting a higher-preference upgrade offered after choosing Float or Slide means you forfeit all seats and exit JoSAA entirely

Step 2: Upload Required Documents

After selecting your willingness option, you must upload scanned copies of the following documents on the JoSAA candidate portal:

  • Class 10 Certificate and Mark Sheet (for date of birth verification)
  • Class 12 Certificate and Mark Sheet
  • JEE Main 2026 Admit Card / Rank Card
  • Category Certificate - OBC-NCL, SC, ST, or EWS (if applicable) OBC-NCL certificate must be for the current financial year and explicitly mention Non-Creamy Layer status
  • OBC-NCL certificate must be for the current financial year and explicitly mention Non-Creamy Layer status
  • PwD Certificate from a competent medical authority (if applicable)
  • Passport-size photographs

Documents are verified by online verification officers. If they raise a query under your login, you must respond within the set time limit. Unanswered queries will disqualify you from further participation.

Step 3: Pay the Seat Acceptance Fee

The seat acceptance fee must be paid online through the JoSAA portal. The fee structure is as follows:

CategorySeat Acceptance Fee
General / OBC-NCL / EWS₹35,000
SC / ST / PwD₹15,000

Note: The fee includes ₹5,000 towards JoSAA processing charges. The remaining amount (₹30,000 for General and ₹10,000 for reserved categories) is adjusted against the admission fee of the allotted institute.

If you experience a banking failure during payment before the deadline, JoSAA 2026 provides an extension window until June 29, 2026 at 1:00 PM to resolve payment issues - provided an initial transaction log trace exists.

Step 4: Report to the Allotted Institute

As a final step, candidates must physically report to their allotted institute for document verification and formal admission as per the orientation and class commencement schedule announced by the respective college.

What If You Don't Receive a Seat in Round 1?

Not getting an allotment in Round 1 is common and does not mean you are out of the running. This section explains what happens and what you should do next.

There are several reasons why Round 1 may not show an allotment:

  • Your rank is lower than the closing rank for all the choices you filled
  • The specific branch/institute quota you applied under was already filled
  • A mismatch in category eligibility

What to do: Stay registered and continue participating in subsequent rounds (Rounds 2 through 5). As candidates with allotments in earlier rounds Freeze their seats and exit the process, more seats become available in later rounds - sometimes at better institutes and programmes.

After all five JoSAA rounds conclude, any remaining vacant NIT, IIIT, and GFTI seats are filled through CSAB (Central Seat Allocation Board) Special Rounds. Note that IIT seats are not part of CSAB counselling.

JoSAA 2026 Cutoffs - Opening and Closing Ranks

The JoSAA 2026 cutoffs (opening and closing ranks) for all institutes and programmes are officially released alongside each round's seat allotment result. These ranks indicate the highest and lowest JEE ranks at which candidates received a seat in a given institute-programme combination.

The 2026 cutoffs are expected to be slightly more relaxed than 2025 in some branches, owing to the addition of 4,470 new seats this year. Key factors that influence cutoff trends include:

  • Total number of registered candidates
  • Category-wise rank distribution (AI, Home State, Other State quotas)
  • Number of new seats added
  • Candidate preferences and Freeze decisions from previous rounds

Official 2026 cutoffs for all five rounds will be updated on josaa.nic.in as each round's result is announced.

Institutes Participating in JoSAA 2026

JoSAA 2026 manages admissions to 114 premier engineering institutes across India. The participating institutions fall into four categories:

  • IITs (Indian Institutes of Technology) - including IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kanpur, IIT Kharagpur, and others
  • NITs (National Institutes of Technology) - including NIT Trichy, NIT Warangal, NIT Surathkal, and others
  • IIITs (Indian Institutes of Information Technology) - including IIIT Hyderabad, IIIT Allahabad, and others
  • GFTIs (Government-Funded Technical Institutes) - including IIEST Shibpur, NIT Puducherry, and others
