Eligibility – Qualification and Age Limit

According to the Personnel Department notification dated 23 May 2022 and 13 October 2022, the educational qualification required to appear in the CET (Senior Secondary Level) is Senior Secondary ( Class 12th ) or its equivalent . Candidates appearing in their final year of Senior Secondary may also apply, but must acquire the qualification as per the relevant departmental service rules. The specific educational qualification, experience, and other eligibility conditions for each included post will be governed by the applicable service rules of that department. These will be detailed in the department-wise main recruitment notification. Candidates must be at least 18 years old and not more than 40 years old as on 1 January 2027 to be eligible for Rajasthan CET Senior Secondary Level 2026. Application Fee (OTR One Time Registration)

The fee for Rajasthan CET 2026 is collected as a One Time Registration (OTR) charge through the SSO Portal. Candidates who have already paid OTR fees in a previous cycle do not need to pay again. Payments can be made through state-authorised e-Mitra kiosks, Jan Suvidha Kendras (CSC), net banking, ATM-cum-debit cards, or credit cards. Candidates from other states belonging to SC/ST/OBC/MBC/EWS categories are treated as General category and must pay ₹600. If an applicant remains absent in two or more exams in a financial year (1 April–31 March) across RPSC, RSSB, or any other state recruitment agency, their OTR facility will be blocked; reactivation requires a penalty fee of ₹750 (first instance) or ₹1,500 (second instance).

Minimum Qualifying Marks

Candidates who wish to be eligible for departmental selection must score a minimum of 40% marks in the CET. SC/ST candidates are eligible for a relaxation of up to 5 percentage points , i.e., a minimum of 35%. Divyang candidates are entitled to a 5% relaxation in the minimum qualifying marks as per Rajasthan Divyangjan Rights (Amendment) Rules 2021, Rule 6(B). Scoring above 40% in the CET does not guarantee a job; it only makes a candidate eligible to appear in the separate departmental recruitment exam (written test and/or interview) for specific posts.

Rajasthan RSSB CET Senior Secondary Level Notification : Selection Process & Exam Pattern

The Rajasthan CET Senior Secondary Level 2026 is only a qualifying/eligibility examination. After the CET, the actual recruitment for each post is conducted through a separate departmental process. Candidates should understand that clearing CET is the first step in a multi-stage recruitment cycle. Stage 1 – CET (Common Eligibility Test) An OMR-based offline exam of 120 questions carrying 240 marks, to be held 23–25 October 2026. Duration is 2 hours 30 minutes. Each wrong answer carries a negative mark of 1/3 of the marks allotted to the question. Each question has five options (A, B, C, D, E) ; if a candidate does not attempt a question, they must darken option E, otherwise 1/3 marks will be deducted for non-darkening in more than 10% of questions. Reservation Policy

Rajasthan CET 2026 follows the state reservation norms. Horizontal (cross-category) reservation of 30% applies for women across all categories. Within the women's quota, 8% is reserved for widows and 2% for divorced women (marriage-separated women). If eligible widow candidates are not available, the seats are filled by divorced women of the same category, and vice versa. Reservation for Scheduled Tribes and Scheduled Castes: backlog vacancies are carried forward for up to three subsequent recruitment years if eligible candidates are unavailable in a given year. Outstanding Athletes receive 2% horizontal reservation of total vacancies per notification dated 21.11.2019. Candidates from other states belonging to SC/ST/OBC/MBC/EWS categories are treated as General category for all reservation purposes.
