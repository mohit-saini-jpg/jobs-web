What Are Opening Rank and Closing Rank in JoSAA?

Understanding the difference between Opening Rank and Closing Rank is essential before interpreting the cutoff tables. These two figures together define the realistic admission zone for any institute-branch combination in a given counselling round.

  • Opening Rank is the rank of the highest-ranked candidate (i.e., the best AIR) who received a seat offer in a particular institute, branch, category, and quota in that round.
  • Closing Rank is the rank of the last candidate to receive a seat offer in that same combination — in other words, the cutoff beyond which no seat was allotted.

Anything between these two numbers is your realistic admission zone for that round. A lower opening rank means higher competition; if your rank falls between the opening and closing ranks, you have a strong chance of admission in that round.

Factors Affecting JoSAA 2026 Opening and Closing Ranks

Several key factors determine how competitive the cutoffs are for a given institute and branch. Candidates should keep these in mind while analysing Round 1 OR-CR data and planning their choices for upcoming rounds.

Number of Qualified Candidates - A higher number of JEE Advanced/Main qualifiers increases competition and can tighten cutoffs. Seat Availability - If an institute increases its seat intake in a particular branch, closing ranks may relax. Exam Difficulty Level - The tougher the exam, the lower the absolute score required to achieve a given AIR, which can shift cutoff trends. Candidate Preferences - High demand for branches like CSE, AI, and Data Science keeps their cutoffs consistently tight across all IITs and IIITs. Reservation Categories - Opening and closing ranks vary significantly across OBC-NCL, SC, ST, EWS, and PwD categories.

How to Check JoSAA 2026 Round 1 Opening and Closing Ranks

Candidates can access the official OR-CR data by following these steps:

  • Visit the official JoSAA portal at josaa.nic.in
  • Click on the link for "Opening and Closing Ranks 2026 - Round 1"
  • Select the institute type (IIT / NIT / IIIT / GFTI)
  • Choose the relevant institute, programme, category, gender, and quota
  • Download the PDF or view the data in tabular form on screen
  • Cross-check your rank against the closing rank to assess your admission chances

What Happens After Round 1 Seat Allotment?

Once Round 1 results are out and the OR-CR data is published, candidates must take action within the reporting window. Missing any deadline can result in forfeiture of the allotted seat.

Candidates who receive a seat in Round 1 have three options: Freeze (accept the seat and exit further rounds), Float (retain the seat and participate in higher-preference rounds), or Slide (participate in rounds for a better branch within the same institute). Candidates who do not receive a seat remain in the pool for subsequent rounds. The authority releases separate opening and closing rank PDFs for every counselling round, so candidates should track cutoff trends after each round.
