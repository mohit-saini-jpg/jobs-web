Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility for AIIMS Bhubaneswar Senior Resident (Non-Academic) Recruitment 2026 is primarily governed by the department-wise essential qualifications listed in the vacancy table above (Annexure-I), along with the following common conditions applicable to all departments. Candidates must pass their qualifying examination by 31 August 2026 , and the result must be declared to this effect on or before this date. The tenure of the qualifying degree must also be completing latest by 31 August 2026. Only candidates who have been/are declared successful in their qualifying degree exam/viva (DNB/MD/MS/DM/MCh, as applicable) and will be completing their tenure for the same on or before 31 August 2026 will be eligible to be considered. The upper age limit for applying to these posts is 45 years on the last date of application (Authority: Ministry of Health and Family Welfare Letter U.12016/10/2019-ME dated 2 January 2020). The cut-off date to determine the maximum age limit, essential qualification, and experience will be the last date of submission of the application form.

Pay Scale

Selected Senior Residents (Non-Academic) will be paid Rs 67,700 as per 7th CPC, Level-11 of the Pay Matrix, plus Non-Practising Allowance (NPA) and other usual allowances as admissible under the rules.

Reservation of Posts

Reservation for OBC/SC/ST/EWS candidates is as per Central Government rules, with 4% reservation for Persons with Bench-mark Disabilities (PwBD), including Orthopedically Handicapped (OPH) candidates, on a horizontal basis. PwBD Criteria: The disability certificate must be signed by a duly constituted and authorized Medical Board of a State or Central Government Hospital/Institution, and countersigned by the Medical Superintendent/CMO/Head of the Hospital/Institution. The candidate will be allowed to join only after medical fitness certification by the duly constituted Medical Board, and must possess a valid document certifying their physical disability in conformity with the judgment of the Supreme Court of India. Selection Procedure

A candidate can apply for more than one post, up to a maximum of 3 departments , out of the departments listed in Annexure-I, but must apply separately for each post/department and deposit the application fee separately for each. No TA/DA will be paid to attend the interview, and any fee paid cannot be claimed back later. A written examination will only be conducted when the number of applicants is more than three times the number of posts advertised for that department. There will be no written test for posts where fewer applications are received than the number of vacancies. The Executive Director, AIIMS Bhubaneswar, has the discretion to decide for which posts a written examination will be conducted, and this decision is final. Q3What is the age limit for AIIMS Bhubaneswar Senior Resident (Non-Academic) posts?
A
