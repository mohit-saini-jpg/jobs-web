Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is the last date to apply for Central Bank of India AGM Specialist Recruitment 2026?

The last date to submit the online application for Central Bank of India AGM Specialist Recruitment 2026 is 07 June 2026, and fee payment can also be done up to this date.

Q2. How many vacancies are released for CBI AGM Specialist 2026?

The bank has notified a total of 15 vacancies for Assistant General Manager (Scale V) in Specialist Category across Risk Management, Finance & Accounts and Credit streams.

Q3. What is the age limit for Central Bank of India AGM Specialist posts?

The minimum age is 33 years and the maximum age is 45 years as on 31 March 2026, with relaxations for reserved categories as per Government of India rules.

Q4. What is the selection process for CBI AGM Specialist Recruitment 2026?

The selection process consists of an online examination for 100 marks followed by an interview for 100 marks, and the final merit is prepared with 70 percent weightage to the online test and 30 percent to the interview.

Q5. Is there any negative marking in the Central Bank of India AGM Specialist online exam?

No, there is no penalty for wrong answers in the online examination for AGM Specialist posts, so candidates can attempt all questions without fear of negative marking.

Q6. What is the application fee for Central Bank of India AGM Specialist 2026?

SC, ST, PwBD and women candidates need to pay Rs. 175 as intimation charges, while all other candidates including General, OBC and EWS must pay Rs. 850 as application fee plus intimation charges.

Q7. What qualifications are required for AGM – Finance & Accounts in Central Bank of India?

For AGM – Finance & Accounts, candidates must be full‑time graduates who have passed the final ICAI examination and are members of ICAI, with at least 10 years’ experience in a scheduled commercial bank and 7 years at head or administrative office in core finance functions.

Q8. Is work experience mandatory for Central Bank of India AGM Specialist posts?

Yes, all AGM Specialist posts require a minimum of 10 years’ officer‑level experience in banking or specified domains, along with stipulated years in Risk, Finance or Credit in large scheduled commercial banks with more than 2000 branches.
