Delhi Cantonment Board Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

Delhi Cantonment Board (DCB) has released Recruitment Advertisement No. 01/2026 to fill 9 vacancies across the posts of Assistant Engineer (Civil), Assistant Engineer (Mechanical), Junior Engineer (Civil), and Junior Engineer (Electrical). Online applications open on 15 July 2026 and close on 14 August 2026, with selection based purely on a Computer Based Test (CBT) - there is no interview or skill test. The notification was issued on 17 June 2026 under reference number DCB/12/VI/AE&JE Rec./2026/FMS-30388.

Delhi Cantonment Board Recruitment 2026 Vacancy Breakdown

Candidates checking how many seats are available in their category will find that the 9 total vacancies are split unevenly across the four posts, with Junior Engineer (Civil) carrying the highest number of openings. Reservation has been applied according to standard government norms, covering UR, OBC, SC, ST, and EWS categories. One vacancy under Junior Engineer (Civil) is also earmarked for Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD) under Category (b) - Deaf and Hard of Hearing. PwBD sub-categories as defined in the notification are: Category (a) Blindness and Low Vision, Category (b) Deaf and Hard of Hearing, Category (c) Locomotor Disability (including cerebral palsy, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims, and muscular dystrophy), and Category (d) & (e) Autism, intellectual disability, specific learning disability, mental illness, and multiple disabilities.

Application Fee for DCB Recruitment 2026

The application fee structure for this recruitment is straightforward, with a flat charge for general applicants and a wide list of exemptions. The fee for each post is Rs. 850/- per candidate, payable only through online mode within the application form itself, and once paid, it is non-refundable under any circumstances. Note that Ex-Servicemen who have already secured regular re-employment under DCB by availing earlier reservation benefits are not eligible for this fee concession.

Selection Process for Assistant Engineer & Junior Engineer Posts

Selection for all four posts - Assistant Engineer (Civil), Assistant Engineer (Mechanical), Junior Engineer (Civil), and Junior Engineer (Electrical) - follows a single-stage process built entirely around a written test. There is no interview and no skill test, which means the final merit list is generated purely from CBT scores, followed by document verification and a medical fitness check for shortlisted candidates.
