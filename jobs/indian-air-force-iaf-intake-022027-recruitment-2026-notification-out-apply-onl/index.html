IAF Agniveervayu Intake 02/2027 - Eligibility Criteria

Agniveervayu eligibility is divided across age, marital status, and educational qualification requirements specific to Science and Non-Science streams. Candidates born between 01 July 2005 and 01 January 2010 (both dates inclusive) are eligible to apply. If a candidate clears all stages of selection, the upper age limit on the date of enrolment must not exceed 22 years. Only unmarried male and female candidates are eligible. Candidates must undertake not to marry during the four-year engagement period; female candidates must additionally undertake not to become pregnant during this period, as pregnancy or marriage during service can lead to dismissal. Candidates eligible under Science Subjects may also opt to appear for both Science and Other-than-Science papers in a single sitting while registering. IAF Recruitment 2026 - Physical & Medical Standards

Both posts require candidates to meet height, chest, hearing, dental, and visual standards, with separate visual acuity criteria for each trade. Chest (Male): Minimum 77 cms with at least 5 cms expansion. Chest (Female - Agniveervayu only): Minimum 5 cms expansion. Hearing: Able to hear forced whisper from 6 metres, each ear separately. Dental: Healthy gums and minimum 14 dental points; orthodontic appliances and dental implants are disqualifying. LASIK/PRK history: Not accepted for either post. Tattoos: Permanent tattoos are restricted to the inner forearm and back of the hand only; offensive tattoos are disqualifying regardless of location. Candidates who clear the online test must pass a 1.6 km run followed by push-ups, sit-ups, and squats within a defined time limit.

IAF Recruitment 2026 - Stipend & Benefits

Pay structures differ significantly between the two postings, reflecting their different service models - Agniveervayu under the Agnipath Scheme versus a regular 20-year Airmen cadre. On completion of four years, Agniveervayu receive a one-time Seva Nidhi package of approximately ₹10.04 lakh (excluding interest), along with a non-contributory life insurance cover of ₹48 lakh during the engagement period. They are not entitled to gratuity, pension, or Provident Fund contributions, and do not receive Ex-Servicemen (ESM) status after discharge. Up to 25% of each batch may be offered the opportunity to apply for the regular Airmen cadre based on performance. Training Stipend: ₹14,600 per month during training. Post-Training Salary: Starting gross emoluments of minimum ₹26,900 per month (including Military Service Pay), plus applicable Dearness Allowance. Other Allowances: Transport Allowance

IAF Selection Process 2026: Exam Pattern

Both posts follow a three-phase selection process - an online written test, physical/adaptability tests, and a final medical examination. Marking Scheme (both posts): +1 mark for each correct answer, 0 for unattempted questions, and -0.25 for each wrong answer. Marks may be normalised to adjust for variation in difficulty across exam sessions, and candidates must qualify in each subject/paper individually. Shortlisted candidates undergo the Physical Fitness Test (detailed above) followed by two written Adaptability Tests assessing suitability for military life and ability to adjust to Air Force working conditions and environments. Candidates who clear Adaptability Test-II undergo a detailed medical examination by the Air Force Medical Team, including blood haemogram, urine tests, biochemistry profile (blood sugar, cholesterol, kidney & liver function), chest X-ray, and ECG.
