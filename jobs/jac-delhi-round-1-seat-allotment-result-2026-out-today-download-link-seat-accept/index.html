JAC Delhi Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026 - Key Highlights

Before diving into the details, here is a quick overview of the most important facts every candidate must know about the JAC Delhi Round 1 allotment.

How to Check JAC Delhi Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026

Candidates can access their seat allotment result directly through the official JAC Delhi counselling portal. The download link will be activated after 5:00 PM on June 15. Follow these steps to check your result:

  • Visit the official website at jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in
  • Click on the "Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026" link on the homepage
  • Enter your JEE Main 2026 Application Number and Password
  • Your allotment status will appear on the screen
  • If a seat has been allotted, click "Download" to save your Provisional Seat Allotment Letter
  • Print at least two copies for use during fee payment and document verification

JAC Delhi Round 1 Seat Acceptance Process - Step-by-Step Guide

After the result is out, allotted candidates must complete the seat acceptance process within the deadline. Missing this window will result in immediate cancellation of the allotted seat and exclusion from further JAC Delhi counselling rounds.

This section explains every step of the post-allotment procedure in the correct order.

Step 1 - Download Your Provisional Allotment Letter

Log in to the candidate dashboard and download the Provisional Seat Allotment Letter immediately. This document contains your allotted institute, branch, and reporting schedule, and is required at every subsequent stage of admission.

Step 2 - Choose Freeze or Float

Every allotted candidate must declare their seat status preference:

  • Freeze - Choose this option if you are completely satisfied with your allotted institute and branch. Selecting Freeze confirms your seat and removes you from consideration for higher-preference upgrades in subsequent rounds.
  • Float - Choose this option if you want to retain your current seat as a safety while remaining eligible for a better branch or college in Round 2 onward. If an upgrade becomes available based on your locked choices, it will be automatically assigned to you.

Candidates who receive a lower-preference allotment are strongly advised not to withdraw from JAC Delhi counselling. Selecting Float is the smarter strategy, as significant cutoff movement and upgradation opportunities typically open up in later rounds.

Step 3 - Pay the Seat Acceptance Fee

After selecting Freeze or Float, pay the seat acceptance fee of ₹95,000 through the online payment gateway on the portal. Before making the payment, verify that your net banking daily transfer limit or credit/debit card single-transaction limit is set above this amount to avoid declined transactions.

Failure to pay the fee within the deadline leads to automatic cancellation of the allotted seat.

Step 4 - Report for Physical Document Verification

After fee payment, candidates must physically report to their allotted institution starting June 17, 2026, in rank-wise order. Carry the following documents in original along with self-attested photocopies:

  • Class 10 mark sheet and certificate
  • Class 12 mark sheet and certificate
  • JEE Main 2026 Scorecard
  • Provisional Seat Allotment Letter
  • Seat Acceptance Fee Payment Receipt
  • Category certificate (if applicable OBC-NCL, SC, ST, EWS)
  • Delhi Domicile / Region certificate (if applicable)
  • Three recent passport-size photographs

Candidates who skip physical reporting will have their seat allocation forfeited, regardless of online payment completion.

What If No Seat Is Allotted in Round 1?

Candidates whose allotment status displays "No Seat Allotted in Round 1" do not need to take any action at this stage. They are automatically carried forward and included in the Round 2 allotment computation without any additional fee payment or change in their locked choices. JAC Delhi will process the next round based on seat vacancies created by withdrawals and migration of Round 1 candidates to other counselling systems like JoSAA.

JAC Delhi 2026 Seat Distribution - Delhi vs Outside Delhi Quota

JAC Delhi operates a dual-quota system that significantly impacts admission chances based on candidates' regional eligibility.

Delhi Region candidates enjoy a substantial advantage in seat availability and have historically secured admissions at significantly more relaxed closing ranks across all institutes and branches. Due to the limited 15% share, Outside Delhi candidates face intense competition, particularly for high-demand branches like CSE, IT, and Computer Engineering.
