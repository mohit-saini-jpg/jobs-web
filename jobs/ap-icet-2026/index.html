AP ICET 2026

Andhra Pradesh Integrated Common Entrance Test (AP ICET‑2026) is a state‑level entrance test conducted by Andhra University on behalf of the Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE) for admission to first‑year MBA and MCA programmes in colleges across Andhra Pradesh. The exam is held in online Computer‑Based Test (CBT) mode and measures a candidate’s aptitude in logical reasoning, quantitative skills and English communication.

AP ICET Syllabus 2026 - Section‑wise Topics

The AP ICET 2026 syllabus is officially defined in the AP ICET‑2026 Instruction Booklet and covers all topics from which questions will be set. Candidates should treat this syllabus as the final reference for planning their preparation and selecting study material.

AP ICET Analytical Ability Syllabus 2026

The Analytical Ability section carries 75 questions in total, divided into 20 questions on Data Sufficiency and 55 questions on Problem Solving. This section tests your logical thinking, pattern recognition and ability to interpret numerical and logical information quickly.

Key topics under Analytical Ability include:

  • Data Sufficiency: Questions followed by two statements where you must decide whether statement (i), statement (ii), both together or none are sufficient to answer the question.
  • Sequences and Series: Number and alphabet analogies, identification of missing terms, finding the odd one out, and completion of patterns.
  • Data Analysis: Interpretation of data presented in tables, bar diagrams, pie charts, line graphs, Venn diagrams and short passages.
  • Coding-Decoding: Questions based on coding and decoding of words and letter groups according to specified rules and alphabet patterns.
  • Date, Time and Arrangement Problems: Calendar and clock questions, blood relations, arrival and departure schedules, seating arrangements, and interpretation of symbols and notations.

AP ICET Communication Ability Syllabus 2026

The Communication Ability section consists of 70 questions and checks your command over English language usage in both everyday and business contexts. It focuses on vocabulary, functional grammar, business terminology and reading comprehension.

Important areas are:

  • Vocabulary: Commonly used words in day‑to‑day communication, including synonyms, antonyms and word usage in context.
  • Functional Grammar: Correct application of basic grammar rules in sentences used in daily life and workplace communication.
  • Business and Computer Terminology: Essential terms and expressions related to business and computing, as well as formats and language used in letters, reports, memoranda, agenda and minutes.
  • Reading Comprehension: Four passages followed by questions to test understanding of main ideas, supporting details and logical inferences.

AP ICET Mathematical Ability Syllabus 2026

The Mathematical Ability section has 55 questions and is divided into Arithmetical Ability, Algebraical and Geometrical Ability, and Statistical Ability. Most topics are of Class 10-12 level with some additional concepts from higher mathematics relevant for management and computer applications.

Key topics include:

  • Arithmetical Ability: Laws of indices, ratio and proportion, simple and compound interest, surds, numbers and divisibility, LCM and GCD, rational numbers and ordering, percentages, profit and loss, partnership, pipes and cisterns, time and distance, time and work, areas and volumes, mensuration and modular arithmetic.
  • Algebraical & Geometrical Ability: Statements and truth tables, implication, converse and inverse, tautologies, sets, relations and functions, equation of a straight line in various forms, trigonometric ratios and standard angles, trigonometric identities, height and distance problems, polynomials, remainder theorem, linear equations and expressions, progressions, binomial theorem, matrices, basic ideas of limits and derivatives, plane geometry (lines, triangles, quadrilaterals, circles) and coordinate geometry (distance between points).
  • Statistical Ability: Frequency distributions, measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), standard deviation, correlation and simple probability questions.

AP ICET 2026 Eligibility Criteria (Brief Overview)

Before investing time in preparation, candidates should confirm that they meet the AP ICET 2026 eligibility conditions specified in the notification. Fulfilling these criteria is mandatory for admission even if you qualify in the test.

Important eligibility points include:

  • Nationality and local status: Candidates must be Indian nationals and satisfy local/non‑local requirements under the Andhra Pradesh Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admission) Order, 1974.
  • Educational qualifications: MBA: A 3/4‑year bachelor’s degree from a recognised university or equivalent qualification. MCA: A 3/4‑year bachelor’s degree with Mathematics as a subject at Intermediate or Degree level.
  • MBA: A 3/4‑year bachelor’s degree from a recognised university or equivalent qualification.
  • MCA: A 3/4‑year bachelor’s degree with Mathematics as a subject at Intermediate or Degree level.
  • Minimum marks: At least 50% aggregate in the qualifying degree for general category candidates and 45% for SC/ST/BC candidates for admission through AP ICET.
  • Final‑year students: Candidates appearing for the final year of their qualifying degree are also eligible to write AP ICET 2026, subject to submission of the provisional certificate at the time of admission.
  • Distance education: Degrees obtained in distance mode must be recognised by the Joint Committee of UGC, AICTE and DEC/DEB.

AP ICET 2026 Preparation Tips Based on Syllabus

A good preparation plan for AP ICET 2026 should be tightly aligned with the official syllabus and exam pattern. Focusing on frequently asked topics and practising a variety of questions will significantly improve your score.

For Analytical Ability, regularly practise data interpretation sets, coding-decoding questions, sequences, and seating or arrangement problems while tracking your speed and accuracy. For Mathematical Ability, revise fundamental concepts from school‑level mathematics first, then move to advanced topics like progressions, trigonometry, coordinate geometry, and statistics with plenty of practice problems.

In Communication Ability, build a habit of reading English newspapers, magazines and online articles to enhance vocabulary and comprehension. Combine this with targeted grammar practice and exposure to business‑related passages to handle all question types comfortably in the exam. Solving previous year AP ICET question papers and full‑length mock tests will give you a clear idea of the difficulty level and help refine your time management strategy.
