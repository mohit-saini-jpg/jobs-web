CFA Aruvankadu Selection Process 2026

Selection is made purely on the basis of merit, with separate merit lists prepared for the Non-ITI and Ex-ITI categories. Non-ITI merit list: Based on the overall average percentage of marks obtained in Madhyamik/Matriculation (Class X or equivalent) - either aggregate of all subjects or the best 5 subjects, as per the concerned Board's criteria. Ex-ITI merit list: Based on the simple average of percentage of marks in both Matriculation and ITI (minimum 50% aggregate required in each). Where a candidate's Class X result is declared in CGPA/GPA, marks must be entered after conversion as per the concerned Board's own criteria (not the CGPA grade itself). If the Board has not issued a clear conversion formula, or if the candidate does not know it, a standard multiplication factor of 9.5 is applied uniformly. Reservation for SC/ST/OBC and PH/PWD Candidates

Reservation applies as per the relevant schedule under the Apprentices Act, 1961, the Apprenticeship Rules, 1992, the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, and existing Government of India rules. Vacancies for PH/PWD candidates are not separate but are included within the total notified vacancies. If the prescribed number of SC or ST candidates is not available, unfilled seats are transferred as per the standard inter-category rules described in the notification (SC/ST interchange, and OBC seats going to non-OBC candidates if unfilled).

Duration of Training

Non-ITI and ITI candidates: As specified under the Trade Apprentices Act, 1961, and Apprenticeship Rules, 1992, and amendments thereof. Ex-ITI candidates: As per Schedule I of the Apprenticeship Rules, 1992, and amendments thereof, for the respective trade.

Medical Examination and Physical Fitness

Physical fitness standards follow the Apprentices Act, 1961, and Apprenticeship Rules, 1992. After successful document verification, provisionally selected candidates undergo a medical examination at Cordite Factory premises. The date, time, and venue will be communicated separately by the factory. Only candidates who pass the medical examination proceed to Trade Apprentice training. Medical fitness is certified by the Cordite Factory Hospital.

Selected candidates must execute a Contract of Apprenticeship (by the guardian, if the candidate is a minor).
