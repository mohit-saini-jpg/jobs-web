KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026 Out

The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) has released the KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026 for candidates applying under the Agriculturist quota. The admit card download link has been activated on the official KEA website for all eligible candidates whose documents for agri quota have been accepted.

As per KEA’s latest update, the KCET agriculture practical exam 2026 will be conducted on 10 May 2026 at 17 designated practical test centres across Karnataka, and the hall ticket was released on 8 May 2026 (some notices mention 7 May depending on the portal update). The practical exam will start at 9:00 AM, and candidates must report to the centre by 8:00 AM for verification and instructions.

How to Download KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026

Eligible Agriculturist quota candidates can download their practical exam hall ticket by following these steps:

  • Visit the KEA official website: https://cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea and go to the UGCET‑2026 page.
  • Click on the “KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Admit Card 2026 / Hall Ticket” link available under the UGCET‑2026 or Agriculture quota section.
  • You will be redirected to the agri quota login page (Common Online Application Agricultural Quota portal).
  • Enter your Application Number and Password (or date of birth, as required), then enter the captcha/security code.
  • Click on “Login / Submit”; your KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026 will be displayed on the screen.
  • Check all details carefully (name, application number, exam date, centre, reporting time), then download and print 2-3 copies of the hall ticket on A4‑size paper.

Only candidates whose names appear in the agri quota eligibility list / who have valid documents uploaded can download the hall ticket; others will not see the admit card link.

Details Mentioned on KCET Agriculture Practical Hall Ticket 2026

The KCET agriculture practical admit card contains important details that candidates must verify:

  • Candidate’s name and photograph
  • KCET 2026 application number / registration number
  • Exam name: KCET Agriculture Practical Exam 2026 (Agriculturist Quota)
  • Practical exam date: 10 May 2026
  • Reporting time (8:00 AM) and test start time (9:00 AM)
  • Practical exam centre name and full address (one of the 17 designated centres)
  • Instructions regarding documents to carry and exam‑day rules

If there is any discrepancy in the name, application number or centre details, candidates should immediately contact KEA via email keauthority‑[email protected] or helpline 080‑2360 4600.

Documents Required with KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026

On the KCET 2026 Agriculture practical exam day, candidates must carry:

  • A printed copy of the KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026 (clear A4‑size printout; colour or good‑quality black and white).
  • One original valid photo ID proof, such as: Aadhaar Card PAN Card Passport Driving Licence Voter ID School/college ID, if allowed as per KEA’s instructions.
  • Aadhaar Card
  • PAN Card
  • Passport
  • Driving Licence
  • Voter ID
  • School/college ID, if allowed as per KEA’s instructions.
  • Original agriculture quota supporting documents, if specifically mentioned in the admit card/instructions (e.g., RTC / Pahani, agriculture land documents, relevant certificates already uploaded).

Candidates who do not bring the hall ticket and valid ID may not be allowed to appear for the practical test.

Exam‑Day Instructions for KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026

To ensure smooth conduct of the KCET 2026 Agriculture Practical Exam, candidates should:

  • Reach the practical exam centre well before the reporting time of 8:00 AM mentioned on the hall ticket so that document verification, batch allocation and briefing can be completed; latecomers after the reporting/entry time may not be allowed to take the test.
  • Carry only essential items: the KCET Agriculture Practical Exam Hall Ticket 2026, a valid photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Driving Licence, Voter ID or school/college ID as permitted), and any agriculture quota documents/ originals specified in the instructions; follow KEA’s guidelines on allowed materials and dress code for the practical.
  • Not carry mobile phones, smart watches, Bluetooth devices, notes, books or any electronic gadgets inside the practical exam premises; such items are strictly prohibited and may lead to disqualification from the Agriculturist quota test.
  • Follow all instructions printed on the agriculture practical hall ticket and those given by the exam officials at the centre; any unfair means, disturbance or non‑compliance with the rules can result in cancellation of the practical exam and loss of eligibility for the Agriculturist quota.
