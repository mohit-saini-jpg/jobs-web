Mahatma Gandhi University, Nalgonda, on behalf of the Telangana Council of Higher Education (TGCHE), has released the TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026 for the Telangana Integrated Common Entrance Test. Registered candidates seeking admission to MBA and MCA programmes in Telangana can now download their hall ticket from the official ICET portal icet.tgche.ac.in.

According to the official schedule, TG ICET 2026 will be conducted on 13 and 14 May 2026 in computer‑based mode across various exam centres in Telangana. The admit card is a mandatory document that must be produced along with a valid photo ID at the examination centre, failing which candidates will not be allowed to appear for the test.

TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026 Download Link

The TG ICET admit card download link is active on the official exam website icet.tgche.ac.in. Candidates must log in using their registration number, date of birth and qualifying exam hall ticket number to access and download their hall ticket. It is advised to download the TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026 as early as possible to avoid last‑minute server issues and to cross‑check all details well before the exam.

How to Download TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026

Candidates can follow the official step‑by‑step process to download the TG ICET 2026 hall ticket:

  • Visit the TG ICET 2026 official website: Click Here
  • On the homepage, click on the “Download Hall Ticket” link under the Application or Hall Ticket section.
  • You will be redirected to the TG ICET hall ticket login page (TGICET_GetHallTicket).
  • Enter your Registration Number, Date of Birth (in DD/MM/YYYY format) and Qualifying Examination Hall Ticket Number in the respective fields.
  • Click on the “Download Hall Ticket” / “Get Hall Ticket” button.
  • The TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026 will appear on the screen; verify all details and then download and print at least two A4‑size copies to carry on exam day.

In case of any technical error or if the hall ticket is not displayed, candidates should immediately contact the TG ICET helpdesk using the contact details given on the official website.

Details Mentioned on TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026

The TG ICET 2026 hall ticket contains important candidate and exam information which must be checked carefully:

  • Candidate’s name, photograph and signature
  • Registration number and hall ticket number
  • Exam date and session (13 or 14 May 2026; forenoon/afternoon)
  • Reporting time and exam timing
  • Test centre name, code and full address
  • Course applied for (MBA/MCA)
  • Gender, category and other profile details
  • Important exam‑day instructions and guidelines

If there is any discrepancy in the details (name, photograph, exam centre, exam date, etc.), candidates must contact the exam authorities for clarification or correction before the exam day.

Documents Required with TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026

On the day of the TG ICET 2026 examination, candidates must carry the following documents to the exam centre:

  • A printed copy of the TG ICET Hall Ticket 2026 (clear A4‑size printout, preferably in colour or good‑quality B/W).
  • One original valid photo ID proof, such as: Aadhaar Card Voter ID PAN Card Passport Driving Licence
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Voter ID
  • PAN Card
  • Passport
  • Driving Licence
  • Any additional documents mentioned in the instructions, if applicable (for example, PwD certificate for candidates claiming reservation).

Without the hall ticket and valid ID proof, candidates will not be allowed to enter the examination hall under any circumstances.

Exam‑Day Instructions for TG ICET 2026

To ensure smooth conduct of the TG ICET exam, candidates must follow these key exam‑day guidelines:

  • Reach the allotted exam centre well before the reporting time, keeping buffer for travel and security checks; late entry after gate‑closing time will not be allowed.
  • Carry only essential items: TG ICET hall ticket 2026, valid photo ID proof and any other documents explicitly permitted in the instructions.
  • Do not carry mobile phones, smart watches, calculators, Bluetooth devices, earphones or any electronic gadgets or study material into the exam hall; these are strictly prohibited.
  • Follow all instructions given by the invigilators and those printed on the hall ticket and displayed at the test centre; maintain discipline and avoid any unfair practices.
  • Keep the TG ICET admit card safely even after the exam, as it may be required during result, counselling and document verification processes.
