How to Apply

  • 1Obtain the application form in the Prescribed Standard Form from the detailed notice available at District Home Guards Commandant offices (Shillong, Jowai, Tura), District Training Centres (Nongpoh, Nongstoin, Williamnagar, Baghmara, Khliehriat, Mawkyrwat, Ampati, Resubelpara), Civil Defence Offices, Deputy Commissioner of all Districts, and the departmental website www.meghomeguards.gov.in.
  • 2Fill in the application form carefully with accurate details.
  • 3Paste one passport-size photograph on the Standard Form and keep one additional copy ready.
  • 4Attach self-attested copies of all required certificates listed above.
  • 5Also attach the Deployment Period Certificate in the prescribed format, obtained from the District Commandant/Officers Commanding District Training Centre/Deputy Controller of Civil Defence.
  • 6Submit the completed application along with all attachments to the respective District Home Guards Office / Officers Commanding District Training Centre / Civil Defence Office from where you are currently deployed with Requisitioning Agencies.
  • 7Government servants must also route their application through the appropriate administrative authority.
  • 8Ensure the application reaches the office by 6 July 2026 - applications received after this date will not be considered.
  • 9The Department shall not be responsible for any damage or injury to volunteers during the enrolment process.
  • 10Volunteers must come prepared to stay for 2–3 days at their own expense. No TA/DA will be admissible.
  • 11Valid ID cards must be carried at all times for identification purposes, and original documents must be presented to the Board during the Personal Interview.
  • 12Canvassing, directly or indirectly, will lead to disqualification. Any volunteer found offering a bribe to anyone involved in the enrolment process or in a position of authority shall be disqualified and/or prosecuted.
  • 13The Department reserves the right to cancel the enrolment process wholly or partially without assigning any reasons.
  • 14State Reservation Policy/Roster will not be applicable as the deployment is purely voluntary.

FAQs

Q1What is the last date to apply for Meghalaya BWHG Part-Time Naik and Lance Naik Enrollment 2026?
A
The last date for receipt of applications at the concerned offices is 6 July 2026. Applications received by hand or by post after this date will not be considered under any circumstances.
Q2How many posts are available in Meghalaya BWHG Enrollment 2026?
A
A total of 108 posts are available — 54 posts of Part-Time Naik and 54 posts of Part-Time Lance Naik (Volunteers) in the Meghalaya Border Wing Home Guards Battalion.
Q3Who is eligible to apply for Meghalaya BWHG Part-Time Naik Enrollment 2026?
A
Eligibility is limited to Home Guards Volunteers who are currently deployed/engaged with various Requisitioning Agencies for a total of 3 years (1095 days), are Indian citizens, and are domiciled in the state of Meghalaya. The upper age limit is 50 years as on 15 April 2026.
Q4What is the age limit for Meghalaya BWHG Enrollment 2026?
A
The upper age limit for applicants is 50 years, reckoned as on 15 April 2026. No minimum age is specified in the notification.
Q5What is the pay or salary for Part-Time Naik and Lance Naik in Meghalaya BWHG?
A
Selected volunteers will be paid monthly Honoraria under the 'Wages', Highly Skilled Category at the rate prescribed by the Department of Labour, Employment & Skill Development, Govt. of Meghalaya from time to time, including the Risk Allowance as prescribed by the State Government. A specific figure is not stated in the notification.
Q6What is the selection process for Meghalaya BWHG Enrollment 2026?
A
The selection process consists of document scrutiny followed by a Personal Interview, which will be conducted at Tura and Shillong centres. No separate intimation will be sent; candidates must check the selected list and interview dates on the Departmental Notice Boards and the official website www.meghomeguards.gov.in.
Q7Where will selected volunteers be posted?
A
Selected volunteers will have to serve in remote border areas of Khasi, Jaintia & Garo Hills bordering Bangladesh.
Q8How long is the deployment period for Meghalaya BWHG Part-Time Naik 2026?
A
The initial deployment period is 1 (one) year. It may be extended based on the performance of the volunteers in the tasks assigned to them. The deployment is purely temporary and voluntary — no volunteer can claim regularisation or permanent retention.
