TN 10th, 11th, 12th Supplementary Exam 2026 - Quick Overview

Students who did not clear one or more subjects in the March-April 2026 Tamil Nadu board exams appeared for the supplementary (arrear) exams in June-July 2026. Once evaluation is complete, DGE Tamil Nadu will publish the result on its official websites, tnresults.nic.in and dge.tn.gov.in.

  • Exam-conducting body: Directorate of Government Examinations (DGE), Tamil Nadu
  • Classes covered: 10th (SSLC), 11th (HSE First Year), 12th (HSE Second Year)
  • Result mode: Online, via registration number/hall ticket number and date of birth
  • Official websites: tnresults.nic.in, dge.tn.gov.in

How to Check TN Supplementary Result 2026 Online

  • Visit the official website: tnresults.nic.in or dge.tn.gov.in.
  • Click on the link for "TN 10th/11th/12th Supplementary Result 2026" once it is activated.
  • Enter your registration/hall ticket number and date of birth (DD/MM/YYYY format).
  • Click Submit.
  • Your result will appear on screen with subject-wise marks and overall status.
  • Download and save a copy for your records; collect the original marksheet from your school later.

Students can also check results through the DigiLocker app once DGE uploads digital marksheets, and through the official TN result mobile apps where available.

What the Supplementary Result Will Show

  • Student name and school name
  • Registration number and date of birth
  • Subject-wise marks for the supplementary subject(s)
  • Updated pass/fail status
  • Overall qualifying status for the academic year

Passing Marks for TN Supplementary Exam

ClassPassing Criteria
10th (SSLC)Minimum 35 out of 100 in each subject (practical subjects: 20/75 theory + 15/25 practical)
11th & 12th (HSE)Minimum 35% marks in each subject and in aggregate

If a Student Does Not Clear the Supplementary Exam

Students who still do not clear the minimum required subjects generally have these options, subject to DGE's rules for that year:

  • Re-totalling: Apply through your school for a recheck of totals, for a fee per subject.
  • Scanned copy of answer sheet: Apply for a photocopy of the evaluated script to assess whether revaluation is worth pursuing.
  • Repeat the year: Students who fail in more than the permitted number of subjects (typically more than two) generally need to repeat the academic year and reappear in the next board exam cycle.

Exact fees and deadlines for these options are announced by DGE separately after the supplementary result and are not fabricated here students should confirm current fees and last dates on dge.tn.gov.in.

Important Instructions for Students

  • Keep your registration number, hall ticket number, and date of birth ready before the result is announced, since the official website usually sees heavy traffic on result day.
  • Cross-check every detail on the result (name, subjects, marks) and report discrepancies to your school immediately.
  • The online result is provisional; collect the original marksheet from your school once it is issued.
  • Do not rely on unofficial WhatsApp/Telegram links claiming early access to results use only tnresults.nic.in or dge.tn.gov.in.
