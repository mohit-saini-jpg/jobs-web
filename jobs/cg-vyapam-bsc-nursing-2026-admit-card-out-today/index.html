How to Download CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card

  • Visit the official website vyapamcg.cgstate.gov.in.
  • Click on the “Admit Card” or “B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card” link on the homepage.
  • You will be redirected to the login page.
  • Enter your Application Number / Registration ID and Password / Date of Birth.
  • Fill the captcha code and click on Login.
  • Your CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card will appear on the screen.
  • Download and print 2-3 clear A4-size copies of the admit card.

If the website is slow due to heavy traffic, candidates are advised to try again after some time.

Details Mentioned on CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card

The CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 hall ticket carries important information which must be checked carefully:

  • Candidate’s Full Name
  • Application Number / Roll Number
  • Candidate’s Photograph and Signature
  • Exam Name: B.Sc Nursing Entrance Exam 2026
  • Exam Date, Time & Shift
  • Exam Centre Name, Code and Full Address
  • Reporting Time and Gate Closing Time
  • Category and Gender details
  • Important instructions for the exam day

In case of any discrepancy in your name, photo, or exam centre details, contact the CG Vyapam helpline immediately.

Documents Required with CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card

On the day of the examination, candidates must carry:

  • A printed copy of the CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026 Admit Card (clear A4 size printout).
  • One original valid Photo ID Proof (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Passport, Driving Licence, or School/College ID).

Entry to the examination centre will not be allowed without the admit card and valid photo ID.

Exam-Day Instructions for CG Vyapam B.Sc Nursing 2026

  • Reach the exam centre well before the reporting time mentioned on the admit card.
  • Carry only permitted items: Admit card, original photo ID, and transparent water bottle.
  • Do not carry mobile phones, smart watches, calculators, notes, or any electronic devices.
  • Follow all instructions given by invigilators and on the admit card.
  • Maintain discipline and avoid any unfair means.
  • Keep your admit card safe for future reference during counselling and admission.
