DVET ITI Admission 2026

DVET ITI Admission 2026 for the August 2026 session is open. Online applications have started from 21 May 2026 for admission in Government and Private ITIs in Maharashtra under Craftsman Training Scheme. The Directorate of Vocational Education and Training (DVET), Maharashtra has announced centralized online admission for August 2026 session in all Government and Private Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs). Admissions are conducted through Centralized Online Admission Process. Candidates can apply online and get documents verified at nearby ITI centers.

DVET ITI Eligibility Criteria 2026

Candidates who have passed 8th, 10th or 12th standard from a recognized board are eligible for various Engineering and Non-Engineering trades. Minimum age should be 14 years as on the date of admission. Both Maharashtra domiciled and other state candidates can apply. Reserved category candidates must provide valid certificates.

How to Apply for DVET ITI Admission 2026

  • Visit the official website: https://admission.dvet.gov.in/
  • Register as New Candidate or Login if already registered.
  • Fill personal, address, category, qualification and other details.
  • Pay the application fee online.
  • Get documents verified at nearest Government or Private ITI for Application Confirmation.
  • Fill Option Form for trade and institute preference.

Documents Required for DVET ITI Admission 2026

  • Aadhaar Card
  • 8th / 10th Marksheet and Leaving Certificate
  • Domicile Certificate (for Maharashtra candidates)
  • Caste Certificate (for reserved category)
  • Income Certificate (if applicable)
  • Bank Account Details
  • Passport Size Photo and Signature

List of Trades Offered in DVET ITI 2026

DVET offers 100+ Engineering and Non-Engineering trades including Electrician, Fitter, Welder, Computer Operator & Programming Assistant (COPA), Mechanic Motor Vehicle, Draughtsman, etc. Full list is available on the official admission portal.

Selection Process

Admission is based on merit prepared from academic qualification marks. Candidates have to fill preference for trades and institutes. Seat allotment is done through multiple rounds of counselling.
