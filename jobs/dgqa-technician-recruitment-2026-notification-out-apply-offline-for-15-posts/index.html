DGQA Technician Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

The Directorate General of Quality Assurance (DGQA), Department of Defence Production, Ministry of Defence has invited applications for Direct Recruitment of 15 Technician (Semi-Skilled), Group 'C', Non-Ministerial, Industrial posts under Advertisement No. CQA(HV)/2026/Tech(SS). The posts are spread across three establishments in Tamil Nadu and Telangana, covering the trades of Fitter Auto, Electrician Mate, and Labourer. Applications must be submitted offline by Speed Post.

Disqualification

A person shall not be eligible for appointment if they have entered into or contracted a marriage with a person having a living spouse, or if they, having a living spouse, have entered into or contracted a marriage with any other person - unless the Government is satisfied that such marriage is permissible under the personal law applicable and grants an exemption. Candidates who are convicted or currently under trial for association with criminal offences are also disqualified.

Medical Fitness

Candidates must be in good mental and physical health, free from any physical deformity that may interfere with efficient discharge of duties. Provisionally shortlisted candidates must undergo a medical examination at a Government hospital before an appointment offer is issued; candidates found unfit will not be offered appointment. Candidates diagnosed with night blindness or colour blindness will be declared medically unfit and will not be considered for selection or appointment at any stage, irrespective of their performance in the recruitment process. The decision of the designated Medical Board/Medical Officer on visual defects is final and binding.

DGQA Technician Recruitment 2026 : Selection Process & Exam Pattern

Selection consists of a Written Test followed by a Trade Test for shortlisted candidates. The written test is objective, multiple-choice type, with no negative marking for wrong answers. It is conducted in a single session - either forenoon or afternoon. Question paper syllabus is based on the Tenth Class/Matriculation level of a Central or State Board. Parts (i), (ii), and (iv) will be set in both English and Hindi. Part (iii) will be in English only. Candidates shortlisted based on written test marks and category of reservation will be called for a Trade Test worth 100 marks. The minimum qualifying mark in the Trade Test is 40. After both the written test and trade test are completed, a merit list will be prepared based on trade and establishment.
