RRC Central Railway Sports Quota Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

Railway Recruitment Cell, Central Railway (RRC/CR) has invited online applications from eligible sportspersons who are citizens of India for filling 59 posts under the Open Advertisement Sports Quota for the year 2026-27. This includes 21 posts of Group 'C' (Level 5/4 and Level 3/2) and 38 Level-1 posts (erstwhile Group 'D'). The posts are open to all candidates regardless of community, with no reservation for SC/ST/OBC/EWS. Only candidates who qualify in the Trials will be considered for further selection.

Central Railway Sports Quota Vacancy Details

Vacancies are distributed across three pay levels based on sports achievement standards. Candidates who meet higher eligibility norms (Level 5/4 or 3/2) may also apply for Level-1 posts. If Level 5/4 vacancies in a discipline remain unfilled due to unavailability of suitable candidates, they will be filled from Level 3/2 of the same discipline. Sportspersons meeting Level 5/4 norms but applying only for Level 3/2 will be considered solely at Level 3/2 and must submit an undertaking not to claim a higher pay level later based on pre-joining achievements.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must be minimum 18 years and maximum 25 years as on 01/01/2027. Only candidates born between 01/01/2002 and 01/01/2009 (both dates inclusive) are eligible. Candidates born on or before 31/12/2001 or on or after 02/01/2009 are not eligible. No age relaxation is available for any community. Candidates possessing higher qualifications must produce the graduation/post-graduation certificate at the time of Document Verification and Trial. Candidates appointed as Clerk-cum-Typist must acquire typing proficiency (30 wpm English or 25 wpm Hindi) within four years of appointment. Selection Procedure

All eligible candidates are called for Document Verification (DV) and Trial. Only candidates securing 25 or more out of 40 marks in game skill, physical fitness, and coach's observation during trial are assessed further; candidates declared UNFIT by the Trial Committee are not considered by the Recruitment Committee. The offer of appointment is purely merit-based. If two sportspersons score equal marks, preference is given to the younger candidate.
