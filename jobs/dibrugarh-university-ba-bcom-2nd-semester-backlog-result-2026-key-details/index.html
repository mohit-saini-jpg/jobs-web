What Does "B.A./B.Com 2nd Semester (Backlog) Examination, 2025" Mean?

This section explains why the result is labelled "2025" even though the exam was actually conducted in 2026, and who this result applies to.

Dibrugarh University names its CDOE examinations after the academic session under which a batch of students is registered, not necessarily the calendar year the exam paper is written. So the "2025" examination for B.A./B.Com 2nd Semester was scheduled to be held in 2026, specifically in February-March 2026, and the term "Backlog" indicates that this attempt was meant for students who could not clear one or more papers in their regular 2nd Semester attempt, or who had a pending paper to clear from an earlier cycle. This result, therefore, is most relevant to distance and online education students of B.A. and B.Com programmes under CDOE, Dibrugarh University who appeared for backlog papers in this cycle.

What Is a Backlog Examination?

This section clarifies the meaning of "backlog" for students who may be checking this term for the first time.

A backlog examination is a supplementary attempt given to students who were unable to clear one or more papers in their original semester examination, or who could not appear for certain papers earlier. It allows students to clear pending papers without losing an academic year, so they can proceed to the next semester or complete their degree on time. Backlog results are usually declared after the regular semester results of the same batch.
