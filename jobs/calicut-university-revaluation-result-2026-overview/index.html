How to Check Calicut University Revaluation Result 2026 Online

Follow these steps to check and download your Calicut University revaluation result:

  • Visit the official result portal at results.uoc.ac.in.
  • Look for the result link that matches your exact course, semester, examination session, and admission batch, for example, "Revaluation Result of Fourth Semester M.Sc. Mathematics CBCSS Examination 9/2024 (2020 Admission)."
  • Click on the relevant result link.
  • Enter your university registration number in the field provided.
  • Submit the form to view your revaluation result.
  • Download or take a screenshot/printout of the result page for future reference.

Details Required to Check the Result

  • University Registration Number
  • Course/Programme name
  • Semester and admission batch (for selecting the correct result link)

What the Revaluation Result Shows

Typically, a Calicut University revaluation result page displays the candidate's register number, subject-wise marks after revaluation, and the updated result status. Exact fields and formatting may vary by programme. Since Calicut University has not published a sample result layout for these specific notifications, candidates should refer to the actual result page for precise details.

Important Instructions for Candidates

  • Cross-check that you are opening the result link corresponding to your correct semester, examination session (9/2024 or 9/2023), and admission year, as similarly named results for other batches may also be listed on the portal.
  • Keep your registration number handy before visiting the portal to avoid repeated searches.
  • Save or print the result page immediately after it is displayed, since online result pages may be replaced by newer notifications over time.
  • For discrepancies in marks, revaluation status, or any technical issue while accessing the result, contact the Calicut University Examination Section (Pareeksha Bhavan) directly rather than relying on third-party websites.
  • Official mark lists/grade cards, where applicable, are usually issued separately by the university; the online result is generally provisional and for immediate reference only.
