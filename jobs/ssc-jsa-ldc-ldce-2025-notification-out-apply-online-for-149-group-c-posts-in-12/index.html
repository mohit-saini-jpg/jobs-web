Exam Pattern

The examination carries 260 marks in total - 200 for Part-I (the exams) and 60 for Part-II (service record). Note that Paper-I is descriptive (written) and Paper-II is the objective Computer Based Test (CBT). | *Scribe-eligible candidates get 2 hours for Paper-I and 2 hours 40 minutes for Paper-II, plus 20 minutes compensatory time per hour. | Negative marking of 0.25 marks per wrong answer applies to Paper-II (CBT) only. There is no negative marking in the descriptive Paper-I. Candidates must attempt the exam in the language (English/Hindi) chosen in the application form. | 60 marks are reserved for evaluation of the APARs (Annual Performance Appraisal Reports). These marks are added to determine the final rank. Once evaluated, no request to change APAR grading is entertained.

Syllabus

Paper-I (Short Essay): One essay of 400–500 words on any of the specified subjects. Paper-II (a) Language: Simple composition and applied grammar (in the chosen English/Hindi medium). Paper-II (b) General Knowledge: Current events, matters of everyday observation and experience, and general information about India.

Important Instructions

The exam is conducted in Delhi-NCR only; there is no centre abroad. A candidate posted abroad must appear in India at their own expense. The Admission Certificate is issued online via sscnr.nic.in (3–4 days before the exam); it is never sent by post. Carry your original Departmental Identity Card along with a hard copy of the Admission Certificate. Candidates not Aadhaar-authenticated during OTR must report early with two passport-size photographs and an original photo ID showing date of birth. No Travelling Allowance is paid by SSC. Mobile phones, Bluetooth/wireless devices, smartwatches, and electronic gadgets are strictly prohibited. SSC conducts post-exam log, biometric, and CCTV analysis, and malpractice attracts debarment of 1 to 7 years depending on the offence.

FAQs

Q1Can a general candidate or private-sector employee apply for this exam?
A
No. This is a Limited Departmental Competitive Examination, open only to existing regular Group 'C' / MTS employees of the 12 participating Central Government cadres, with at least 3 years of service. It is not open to the general public or freshers.
Q2How many vacancies are there in SSC JSA / LDC LDCE 2025?
A
A total of 149 vacancies across 12 departments - the largest shares being Central Passport Organisation (50), Armed Forces HQ Clerical Service (31), O/o Registrar General of India (17), and O/o Development Commissioner-MSME (13).
Q3What are the application dates?
A
The online application is open from 05th June 2026 to 26th June 2026 (up to 11:00 PM). The forwarded hard copy must reach SSC-NR by 03rd July 2026.
Q4Is there an application fee?
A
No application fee is prescribed for this departmental examination. (A ₹50-per-question charge applies only to optional answer-key challenges.)
Q5Where will the exam be held and when?
A
The Computer Based Examination will be conducted in Delhi-NCR only, tentatively in July–August 2026.
Q6What is the exam pattern?
A
Part-I has Paper-I (descriptive Short Essay, 100 marks) and Paper-II (objective CBT - Language 50 + General Knowledge 50 = 100 marks, with 0.25 negative marking). Part-II is APAR evaluation worth 60 marks. Final merit is out of 260 marks.
Q7Do I need to send a physical copy of my application?
A
Yes. Apart from the online form, your Head of Department/Office must forward the printed application with service documents to SSC (Northern Region). Without proper-channel forwarding, the application is rejected.
