What Is JAC Delhi Round 1 Cutoff 2026?

The JAC Delhi cutoff is one of the most important benchmarks for engineering aspirants targeting Delhi's premier government institutions. It helps candidates assess their chances before the next counselling round begins.

The JAC Delhi cutoff is expressed as opening and closing ranks the rank of the first student admitted and the rank of the last student admitted to a particular branch in a given round. If your JEE Main rank falls within this range, you have a strong chance of securing admission in that specific course and institution. The opening and closing ranks are released institute-wise after each round on the official website of JAC Delhi.

How to Download JAC Delhi Round 1 Cutoff 2026?

Knowing exactly how to access the official cutoff PDF saves time and helps you make quick decisions during the counselling process.

Follow these simple steps to download the cutoff:

  • Visit the official JAC Delhi website: jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in
  • On the homepage, navigate to eServices > OR-CR (Opening and Closing Ranks)
  • Click on the link for the Round 1 cutoff
  • Select your preferred institute from the list
  • Download the branch-wise and category-wise cutoff PDF

After each round of the JAC Delhi counselling process, the cutoff PDF can be downloaded here.

JAC Delhi Counselling Structure 2026

Understanding how many rounds are scheduled helps you plan your freeze/float/slide strategy better.

This year, there are 4 regular rounds, 1 upgradation round, and 1 online spot round (if needed) in JAC Delhi 2026. This gives candidates multiple opportunities to secure their preferred branch and institute, especially if they don't get their top choice in Round 1.

DTU Round 1 Cutoff 2026: What to Expect

Delhi Technological University remains one of the most sought-after engineering colleges in the JAC Delhi counselling process, with consistently competitive cutoffs across branches.

Competitive branches like DTU Computer Science and NSUT typically close early in the initial rounds. To secure Computer Science Engineering (CSE) at DTU or NSUT through JAC Delhi 2026, Outside Delhi candidates need a JEE Main rank under 4,000 (99.6+ percentile), while Delhi region candidates are safe up to a rank of 12,000 (98.8+ percentile).

Historically, branches like Mathematics & Computing, Information Technology, and Electronics & Communication Engineering are also highly competitive at DTU. Candidates with ranks beyond the CSE closing rank may still find good options in emerging branches like Software Engineering, Environmental Engineering, or Biotechnology.

Usually, the cutoff for DTU stays around the 90 percentile mark even for reserved categories. Therefore, getting into B.Tech courses at DTU is difficult with a JEE Main percentile score of 90 or below.

Factors That Determine the JAC Delhi Cutoff

Understanding the variables that drive cutoff movements helps candidates make better decisions about their college choice preferences.

The JAC Delhi cutoff depends on: the total number of candidates who sign up for the counselling process; how difficult the JEE Main paper was for that specific year; the total number of spots available within the participating colleges; the specific number of choices submitted for a particular degree or branch; and the specific quota or category (General, SC, ST, BC) used for the application.

Additionally, whether a candidate belongs to the Delhi Home State quota or the Outside Delhi quota significantly impacts the closing rank they need to target. Delhi region candidates generally have higher closing ranks (numerically), meaning more students can qualify under this quota.

JAC Delhi 2026: What to Do After Round 1 Seat Allotment

Whether you received your preferred seat or not, there are clear next steps every candidate must follow after Round 1 allotment.

After JAC Delhi seat allotment, candidates must decide whether to Freeze or Float their allotted seat. Understanding rank movement trends and upgrade possibilities can help you make an informed choice before the next round.

Here is a quick guide:

  • Freeze: Accept the current allotment and withdraw from further rounds. Choose this if you are satisfied with your college and branch.
  • Float: Retain your current seat but remain in the pool for upgrades in subsequent rounds. Choose this if you want a better branch or college.
  • Slide: Opt for a better branch within the same college while keeping options open for upgrades elsewhere.

Candidates who do not pay the seat acceptance fee within the stipulated time will forfeit their Round 1 allotment. Always check the official schedule for payment deadlines.
