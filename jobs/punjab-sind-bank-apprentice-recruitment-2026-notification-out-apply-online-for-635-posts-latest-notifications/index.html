Physical Eligibility

DetailsThe competition level for PSB Apprentice Recruitment 2026 is expected to be moderate to high. With only 635 seats spread across 29 States/UTs, the vacancy count is significantly lower than comparable bank apprenticeship drives such as SBI. Selection is purely merit-based on 10+2 marks without any written examination, which means candidates with high HSC scores - especially from states like Uttar Pradesh (100 seats), Punjab (90 seats), Bihar (40 seats), Maharashtra (30 seats), and Haryana (50 seats) - will face intense competition within their district and category. | Since there is no written exam, the effective competition depends entirely on the candidate's 10+2 percentage and age as a tie-breaker. Attrition at later stages will occur through local language proof verification, document verification, and medical fitness, which will reduce the final eligible pool. Candidates who do not have proof of studying the local language at 8th/10th/12th level are at a disadvantage as they must demonstrate local language proficiency separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is Punjab & Sind Bank Apprentice Recruitment 2026?

Punjab & Sind Bank Apprentice Recruitment 2026 is a fresh notification for 635 Apprentice posts under the Apprentices Act, 1961. The drive is open to Regular Graduates aged 20–28 years (with relaxations) who wish to undergo 12‑month on‑the‑job training in Bank branches across 29 States/UTs.

Q2. How many vacancies are there in PSB Apprentice 2026?

The Bank has announced 635 training seats for Apprentices, spread across 29 States/UTs including Uttar Pradesh (100), Punjab (90), Bihar (40), Haryana (50), and Maharashtra (30). The vacancies are distributed category‑wise (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/UR) with horizontal reservation for PwBD.

Q3. What is the eligibility for Punjab & Sind Bank Apprentice 2026?

Eligible candidates must: Be Indian citizen or fall under specified foreign‑national categories. Hold a Regular Graduation degree from a recognized university by 06‑06‑2026. Be 20–28 years as on 01‑05‑2026 (with relaxations for SC/ST/OBC/PwBD/widows/1984‑riot‑affected). Have 100% completed profile on both NAPS and NATS portals. Not be PG‑qualified, no ≥1 year work experience after graduation, and not previously/currently undergoing apprenticeship under the Apprentices Act.

Q4. What is the age limit and relaxation for PSB Apprentice exam 2026?

The age band is 20–28 years as on 01‑05‑2026 for General/EWS candidates. Relaxation is given as: SC/ST: +5 years OBC (Non‑Creamy Layer): +3 years PwBD: +10 years Widows/divorced/separated women: up to 35 (UR/EWS), 38 (OBC), 40 (SC/ST).

Q5. What is the selection process for PSB Apprentice 2026?

There is no written exam; selection is purely merit‑based on HSC/10+2 marks: State‑wise, District‑wise, Category‑wise merit list on 10+2 marks. Document verification (originals) for shortlisted candidates. Medical examination to confirm fitness. In case of a tie, older age is the tie‑breaker within category, state, and district.

Q6. What is the stipend and training duration?

Selected Apprentices will get a monthly stipend of ₹12,300 for a 12‑month training period (10:00 AM to 5:00 PM), with no daily/conveyance or travel allowance. Apprentices are not treated as Bank employees and have no right to permanent absorption.

Q7. What is the application fee for PSB Apprentice Recruitment?

SC/ST/PwBD: ₹100 + taxes + payment‑gateway charges. General/EWS/OBC: ₹200 + taxes + payment‑gateway charges. Fees are non‑refundable and must be paid online.

Q8. What documents are required for PSB Apprentice 2026?

Mandatory documents include: Xth/XIIth/Graduation mark sheets and certificates Caste certificate (SC/ST/OBC‑NCL/EWS) PwBD disability certificate, age‑relaxation proof, Aadhaar, and valid ID proof (PAN, Passport, DL, etc.; Ration Card or Learner’s DL not accepted). Candidates must also upload photograph, signature, thumb impression, and handwritten declaration while applying.
