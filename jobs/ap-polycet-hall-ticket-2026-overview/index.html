AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 Overview

SBTET, Andhra Pradesh has issued the AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 on 18 April 2026, and the hall ticket download link is now active on the official website polycetap.ap.gov.in. Candidates who have successfully registered for the Andhra Pradesh Polytechnic Common Entrance Test can download their AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 using their Class 10 hall ticket number or mobile number and date of birth.

The AP POLYCET 2026 exam will be held on 25 April 2026 for admission to diploma (polytechnic) courses in engineering and non‑engineering branches across various government and private polytechnic colleges in Andhra Pradesh.

How to Download AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026

Once the link is activated, candidates can download the AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 by following these steps.

  • Visit the official AP POLYCET website: polycetap.nic.in or polycetap.ap.gov.in.
  • On the homepage, click on the “Print Hall Ticket” or “Download Hall Ticket” link.
  • Select the appropriate option and enter the required login details, such as: SSC (Class 10) hall ticket number Date of birth OR registered mobile number and year of passing / appearing
  • SSC (Class 10) hall ticket number
  • Date of birth
  • OR registered mobile number and year of passing / appearing
  • Enter the security / captcha code displayed on the screen.
  • Click on “View & Print Hall Ticket” or “Submit”.
  • Your AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 will appear on the screen as a PDF.
  • Download the file and take 2–3 clear printouts (black-and-white or colour) for exam day and future use.

Login Credentials Required

To access the AP POLYCET admit card 2026, candidates may need any of the following details, as specified on the official website and major education portals.

  • SSC (Class 10) hall ticket number
  • Date of birth (as per SSC records)
  • Registered mobile number used during application
  • Year of passing / appearing for Class 10 / qualifying exam
  • Application / registration details where applicable

Ensure that the information you enter matches the data used during registration to avoid login issues.

Details Printed on AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026

Candidates must carefully verify all data printed on the AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 immediately after download.

Typical details available on the admit card include:

  • Candidate’s name and photograph
  • Father’s / Mother’s name
  • Roll number / hall ticket number
  • Date of birth and category (if applicable)
  • Exam date: 25 April 2026
  • Exam time: 11:00 AM to 1:00 PM
  • Exam centre name and full address
  • Important instructions for candidates

If any error is noticed in the hall ticket details (name, photograph, signature, exam centre, etc.), candidates should immediately contact the designated helpline centres or authorities before the exam date.

AP POLYCET 2026 Exam Day Guidelines

Candidates must follow all exam-day instructions mentioned on the AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026 and in the official guidelines.

Documents to Carry

On the day of the exam, candidates should carry:

  • A printed copy of the AP POLYCET Hall Ticket 2026
  • One valid photo ID proof, such as: Aadhaar card School ID card Passport Voter ID PAN card
  • Aadhaar card
  • School ID card
  • Passport
  • Voter ID
  • PAN card

Entry may not be permitted without the hall ticket and valid ID.

Items Allowed Inside the Exam Hall

As per the instructions shared for AP POLYCET 2026, candidates are generally allowed to carry:

  • Two 2B pencils
  • Eraser
  • Transparent water bottle

Check your admit card and official website for any updated list of allowed items.

Items Not Allowed

The following are usually prohibited inside the exam hall:

  • Mobile phones and smart devices
  • Calculators
  • Written or printed study material
  • Bags and electronic gadgets

Violation of these rules may lead to disqualification, so candidates should follow all instructions strictly.

AP POLYCET 2026 Exam Pattern

AP POLYCET 2026 will be conducted in offline mode using OMR sheets with multiple-choice questions.

Key features of the exam pattern are:

  • Mode of exam: Offline (pen-and-paper OMR)
  • Question type: Objective, multiple-choice questions (MCQs)
  • Total questions: 120 MCQs
  • Subjects covered:
  • Mathematics
  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Duration: 2 hours (11:00 AM to 1:00 PM)
  • Negative marking: No negative marking for wrong answers.

The subject-wise distribution of questions is generally provided in the detailed exam notification and information brochure.
