How to Check Your Anna University Result 2026 Online

Accessing your Anna University semester result takes less than two minutes if you have your credentials handy. Here is the exact process to follow, step by step.

  • Open your browser and go to the official examination portal: coe.annauniv.edu
  • On the homepage, click on the "Results" section.
  • Select the appropriate result link - UG Semester Result or PG Semester Result - based on your course.
  • Enter your Registration Number in the required field.
  • Enter your Date of Birth as your login credential.
  • Click the "Submit" button to proceed.
  • Your result will appear on screen with subject-wise marks and status.
  • Download the result as a PDF and save a printed copy for your records.

Tip: If the page does not load due to server load, try using a different browser or access the portal during off-peak hours such as early morning.

How to Calculate GPA and CGPA for Anna University

Understanding how your academic performance is quantified helps you plan better for future semesters, internships, and higher studies applications. Here is how Anna University computes both GPA and CGPA.

GPA (Grade Point Average) - Semester Performance

GPA measures your academic performance within a single semester. The formula is:

GPA = Σ (Credit × Grade Point) ÷ Σ Credits

In simple terms, multiply each subject's credits by the grade points earned, sum all these values, and divide by the total number of credits in that semester.

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) - Overall Performance

CGPA reflects your cumulative academic performance across all completed semesters. The formula is:

CGPA = Σ (Semester Credits × Semester GPA) ÷ Total Credits Earned

Converting CGPA to Percentage

Anna University uses a straightforward conversion formula widely accepted for job applications, higher studies admissions, and visa processes:

Percentage = CGPA × 10

Example: A CGPA of 8.4 equals 84%.

Revaluation and Photocopy Process After Anna University Results 2026

Students who feel their marks do not reflect their actual performance have the right to apply for a photocopy of their answer script and, if necessary, request revaluation. The process for the 2026 result cycle follows a structured two-step approach.

Key Details

  • Applications submitted through: Respective study centers or college examination cells (not directly by students)
  • Photocopy Fee: ₹300 per answer script
  • Revaluation Fee: ₹400 per answer script
  • Revaluation Deadline: Applications must be submitted within approximately 5 days of the photocopy portal opening

The Correct Process

  • Apply for a photocopy of the answer script through your college examination cell.
  • Have a subject expert at your college review the photocopy for any discrepancy.
  • Only if a discrepancy is confirmed, proceed to apply for revaluation.

Important: Direct revaluation applications are not accepted. The photocopy-first approach is mandatory. Missing the deadline means losing the right to challenge your marks for that examination cycle.

What to Do After Checking Your Anna University Result 2026

Knowing what steps to take immediately after viewing your result can save you time and prevent missed deadlines. Here is a quick action plan based on your result outcome.

If you have passed all subjects:

  • Download and save your provisional marksheet as a PDF
  • Track your college notice board for the original marksheet distribution date
  • If applying for higher studies or internships, note your CGPA and percentage equivalent

If you have one or more RA (arrear) subjects:

  • Note the subject codes and names carefully
  • Register for the supplementary or arrear examination in the upcoming cycle
  • Monitor official university notifications for arrear exam schedules and hall ticket releases

If your result shows Withheld (W) or Incomplete (I):

  • Contact your college examination cell immediately with all relevant documents
  • Resolve pending dues, attendance shortfalls, or CIA submission issues at the earliest
