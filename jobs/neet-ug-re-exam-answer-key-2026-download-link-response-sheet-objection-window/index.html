Over 20 lakh medical aspirants can now download their response sheet, match it against the provisional answer key, calculate their probable score, and raise objections if needed before the deadline.

What Details Are Mentioned on the NEET OMR Response Sheet

The OMR response sheet allows candidates to verify exactly what they marked on exam day before deciding whether to raise an objection. It typically displays the following details:

  • Candidate's Application Number
  • Roll Number
  • Exam Date
  • Question IDs
  • Option IDs
  • Response Marked by Candidate
  • Recorded Answer (as per NTA system)

Candidates are advised to compare this response sheet carefully against the provisional answer key before submitting any objection.

How to Challenge the NEET UG Re-Exam Answer Key 2026

Candidates who believe a particular answer in the provisional key is incorrect can raise an objection through the official portal by following these steps:

  • Visit neet.nta.nic.in
  • Log in using your Application Number and Password
  • Click on "Challenge Answer Key"
  • Select the Question ID you want to dispute
  • Choose the answer option you believe is correct
  • Upload supporting documentary evidence (such as NCERT textbook references) as PDF
  • Pay the prescribed challenge fee of ₹200 per question
  • Submit the challenge and save the confirmation receipt

According to NTA's official statement, the ₹200 fee per question is non-refundable in general, but will be refunded specifically if the objection is later accepted.

NEET UG Re-Exam Result 2026: Expected Date

The NEET UG Re-Exam Result 2026 is expected to be declared in July 2026, after NTA completes the review of all submitted objections and releases the final answer key. The final answer key cannot be challenged further, and the result will be based entirely on it. Once declared, the scorecard will include details such as the candidate's marks obtained, percentile score, All India Rank (AIR), category-wise rank, qualifying status, and cut-off marks.

Background - Why Was the NEET UG Re-Exam Conducted?

The NEET UG 2026 exam was originally scheduled for 3rd May 2026 but was cancelled. The re-examination was subsequently conducted on 21st June 2026 in offline (pen-and-paper) mode across 5,440 centres in India and 14 centres abroad, covering more than 20 lakh candidates, including arrangements for around 10,000 differently-abled candidates.
