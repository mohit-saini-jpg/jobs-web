Eligibility Criteria for NPSC Combined Technical Services Exam 2026

Every candidate applying for the NPSC CTSE 2026 must meet both the age limit and the post-specific educational qualification as on 1 January 2026. The qualifications must have been obtained from a recognised university or institution at the time of applying - degrees in progress are not accepted. Candidates claiming tribal or indigenous inhabitant reservations must hold the appropriate certificates signed by an authority not below the rank of Additional Deputy Commissioner.

Selection Process - NPSC Combined Technical Services Examination 2026

The NPSC CTSE selection process consists of two stages: a written competitive examination and an interview/personality test. Candidates are assessed first on the written exam, and only those who qualify are called for the interview stage. The exact exam pattern and syllabus for CTSE-2026 can be downloaded from the Commission's website at https://npsc.nagaland.gov.in . Candidates sit for a written exam that includes both a general paper and an elective subject paper specific to the post they have applied for. Each item number has a designated elective subject - for example, Item 1 (Regimental Medical Officer) requires Medical Science as the elective, Item 4 (Veterinary Assistant Surgeon) requires Veterinary Science, and Item 6(a) (SDO Civil Engineering) requires Civil Engineering (Degree). NPSC CTSE Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for NPSC Combined Technical Services Examination 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application form for NPSC CTSE 2026 is 17 July 2026 at 12:00 noon. The OTR (One Time Registration), which is a mandatory prerequisite, closes earlier on 15 July 2026 at 12:00 noon.
Q2How many vacancies are notified in NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
The NPSC CTSE 2026 notification covers 49 item numbers containing multiple posts across fresh vacancies (2026) and backlog vacancies from 2016, 2017, 2018, 2021, 2023, 2024, and 2025 under various departments of the Government of Nagaland.
Q3What is the age limit for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
The age limit for NPSC CTSE 2026 is a minimum of 21 years and a maximum of 32 years, calculated as on 01 January 2026. SC/ST candidates get a relaxation of 5 years, making their upper limit 37 years. Serving government employees also get age relaxation equal to their years of service, up to a maximum of 5 years.
Q4What is the application fee for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
The examination fee is ₹300 plus a convenience fee of ₹25, along with applicable payment gateway charges. The OTR (One Time Registration) is free of cost.
Q5What is the selection process for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
The selection process consists of two stages: a written competitive examination followed by an interview/personality test. Candidates who qualify the written exam are called for the interview. NPSC's decisions on all selection matters are final.
Q6Is OTR mandatory for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
Yes, OTR (One Time Registration) is mandatory for all candidates appearing in CTSE-2026. OTR is free of charge and must be completed between 27 June 2026 and 15 July 2026 at 12:00 noon. Without completing OTR, candidates cannot submit the examination application form.
Q7What is the official website for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
The official website is https://npsc.nagaland.gov.in. Both OTR and the online application form are available on this portal. For queries, candidates can call the NPSC helpdesk at 9402988506 during office hours (10:00 to 15:00).
Q8What documents are needed for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
Required documents include the Matriculation/HSLC Admit Card for age proof, Indigenous Inhabitant/ST/BT certificates (signed by an authority not below Additional Deputy Commissioner rank), educational mark sheets and pass certificates, and an NOC from the Head of Department for government employees. PwBD candidates also need a certificate from a competent medical authority.
Q9What pay level do NPSC CTSE 2026 selected candidates get?
A
Selected candidates are placed on the Government of Nagaland Pay Matrix. Class-I Gazetted posts such as Regimental Medical Officer, Veterinary Assistant Surgeon, and SDO posts are at Pay Matrix Level 13. Junior Engineer posts are generally at Level 11, and Class-III non-gazetted posts such as Foreman, Pharmacist, and Sectional Overseer are at Level 8.
Q10Can candidates from outside Nagaland apply for NPSC CTSE 2026?
A
Candidates from outside Nagaland may apply for unreserved posts, but 80% of the vacancies are reserved for Indigenous Inhabitants of Nagaland belonging to specified groups (any Naga Tribe, Kuki, Kachari, Garo, or Mikir) who are indigenous inhabitants of the State.
