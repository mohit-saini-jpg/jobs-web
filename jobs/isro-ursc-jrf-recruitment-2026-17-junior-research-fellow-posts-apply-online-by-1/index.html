Qualification / Eligibility

DetailsEligibility for ISRO URSC JRF Recruitment 2026 is two-part: a qualifying postgraduate degree with the required minimum marks, and a valid national-level qualifying test score. Both conditions must be satisfied simultaneously as on the last date, i.e., 11 July 2026. Only passed candidates are eligible - students awaiting final results should not apply. | For positions JRF-E1 to JRF-E12, JRF-M1, JRF-M2, JRF-M3: M.E./M.Tech./M.Sc.(Engg.) or equivalent postgraduate degree in First Class with an aggregate minimum of 60% or CGPA/CPI of 6.5 on a 10-point scale. | For position JRF-E13 (M.Sc. route): M.Sc. in First Class with an aggregate minimum of 65% average of all semesters or CGPA/CPI of 6.84 on a 10-point scale. | For position JRF-E13 (M.Tech. route): M.Tech. or equivalent in First Class with an aggregate minimum of 60% or CGPA/CPI of 6.5 on a 10-point scale. | All degrees must be duly recognised by AICTE/UGC or any other organisation authorised by the Government of India. Essential qualifications must be completed within the stipulated duration of the course as prescribed by the university. Candidates who pursued professional courses through Open and Distance Learning (ODL) are not eligible to apply for JRF positions. | Where a university mentions both CGPA and percentage in its degree certificate or consolidated mark sheet, at least one criterion must meet DOS/ISRO eligibility norms. Conversion of CGPA into percentage of marks is not permitted to determine eligibility regardless of any conversion formula prescribed by the university. Rounding of percentage/CGPA is not allowed - values must be entered to the nearest 2 decimal places (e.g., 8.235 entered as 8.23; 8.236 as 8.24). | Candidates must have qualified one of the following national-level examinations. This is a mandatory requirement - possession of the degree alone does not make a candidate eligible to apply. | CSIR-UGC National Eligibility Test including NET-Lectureship (Assistant Professorship) GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering) conducted by MoE JAM (Joint Admission Test) conducted by MoE JEST (Joint Entrance Screening Test) National level examinations conducted by Central Government Departments and their agencies/institutions such as DST, DBT, DAE, DOS, DRDO, MoE, ICAR, ICMR, IIT, IISc, IISER, NISER, etc. | The maximum age for all JRF positions is 28 years as on 11 July 2026. Candidates wondering about age relaxation can note that SC/ST candidates get 5 years of relaxation and OBC candidates get 3 years. Additional relaxations as per Government of India orders apply to Ex-Servicemen, Persons with Benchmark Disabilities, Widows, Divorced Women, and Women judicially separated from their husbands and not remarried.

Salary & Pay Scale

DetailsSelected JRFs at ISRO URSC receive a monthly fellowship stipend of ₹37,000 per month. After completion of two years as JRF, at the sole discretion of ISRO/DOS/URSC, the fellow may be awarded Senior Research Fellow (SRF) status with a stipend of ₹42,000 per month. HRA at prescribed rates is paid if hostel accommodation is not provided at the place of posting. Medical benefits for self are admissible as per extant rules. No Dearness Allowance (DA) is paid. The fellowship amount is treated as basic pay for this purpose. | JRFs are eligible only for casual leave. All female JRFs are entitled to maternity leave as per Government of India instructions. JRFs are also eligible for subsidised canteen facilities. Selected candidates are not entitled to any other benefits or concessions admissible to regular employees. The initial engagement is for two years, extendable based on assessment of work and project requirements. The maximum combined period of engagement as JRF and SRF together shall not exceed five years. JRFs with aptitude for research will be encouraged to register for a Ph.D. with an external university.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Q1. What is the last date to apply for ISRO URSC JRF Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date for receipt of online applications is 11 July 2026. This date is also the cut-off for determining age and qualification eligibility for all purposes.
Q2 Q2. How many JRF posts are available in ISRO URSC Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 16 Junior Research Fellow positions have been notified under Advertisement No. URSC:02:2026 across Electronics, Computer Science, Optics, Mechanical, and allied disciplines. The number is provisional and may vary based on actual requirements.
Q3 Q3. What is the age limit for ISRO URSC JRF 2026?
A
The maximum age is 28 years as on 11 July 2026. SC/ST candidates get 5 years relaxation and OBC candidates get 3 years. Ex-Servicemen, PwBD, and certain women categories receive relaxation as per Government of India orders.
Q4 Q4. Is GATE or NET mandatory to apply for ISRO URSC JRF?
A
Yes, it is mandatory. Candidates must have qualified CSIR–UGC NET (including Lectureship), GATE, JAM, JEST, or a national-level examination conducted by Central Government departments or agencies such as DST, DBT, DAE, DOS, DRDO, MoE, IIT, IISc, IISER, NISER, etc. Possession of the postgraduate degree alone does not make a candidate eligible.
Q5 Q5. What is the minimum percentage required for ISRO JRF 2026?
A
For most positions, a minimum of 60% aggregate or CGPA/CPI of 6.5 on a 10-point scale in M.E./M.Tech./M.Sc.(Engg.) is required. For JRF-E13 via the M.Sc. route, the minimum is 65% aggregate or CGPA/CPI of 6.84.
Q6 Q6. What is the fellowship stipend for ISRO URSC JRF?
A
JRFs receive ₹37,000 per month. This may be enhanced to ₹42,000 per month upon promotion to Senior Research Fellow at the sole discretion of ISRO/DOS/URSC after two years. HRA is also paid if hostel accommodation is not provided. No DA is paid.
Q7 Q7. What is the selection process for ISRO URSC JRF Recruitment 2026?
A
Selection involves two stages: an initial screening based on academic performance and application parameters, followed by an Interview conducted in Bengaluru. Call letters for the interview are sent by e-mail only. The result is published on the ISRO website.
Q8 Q8. Can candidates awaiting final results apply for ISRO JRF 2026?
A
No. Only passed candidates are eligible to apply. Students waiting for their final results are not eligible and should not apply.
Q9 Q9. Where should I apply for ISRO URSC JRF 2026?
A
Applications must be submitted online only at www.isro.gov.in under the "CAREERS" section. No other mode of application is accepted. For clarifications, write to [email protected].
Q10 Q10. Does the JRF position lead to a permanent job at ISRO?
A
No. The JRF position does not confer any claim or right for regular appointment in URSC or in any ISRO/Department of Space centre. The maximum combined engagement as JRF and SRF together shall not exceed five years.
