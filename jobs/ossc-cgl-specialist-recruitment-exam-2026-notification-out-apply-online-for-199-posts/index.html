Selection Process

  • Mode of Examination Total Marks No. of Questions Duration Type Negative Marking

  • CBRE/OMR mode 150 marks 150 questions 2 hours 30 minutes (150 minutes) Objective type with MCQs 0.25 mark per wrong answer

  • CBRE/OMR mode 200 marks 100 questions 2 hours 30 minutes (150 minutes) Objective type with MCQs 0.5 mark per wrong answer

  • The competitive examination shall comprise three stages. The Commission at its discretion will conduct the examination either through OMR or through CBRE (Computer Based Recruitment Examination) mode. A Normalisation Formula will be applied if the examination is conducted in multiple batches using different question paper sets.

  • Stage I - Preliminary Examination (One Paper)

  • Indicative Syllabus: Arithmetic (10th Standard)

  • Data Interpretation (Chart

  • Graph

  • Table

  • Data Sufficiency)

  • Logical Reasoning and Analytical Ability

  • General Mental Ability

  • Current Events of National and International Importance

  • Computer/Internet Awareness.

  • Candidates numbering a minimum of 5 (five) times of the vacancies post-wise and category-wise shall be shortlisted for the Main Written Examination based on marks secured in the Preliminary Examination.

  • Stage II(a) - Main Written Examination (Technical Paper)

  • The Preliminary and Main Examination shall be conducted in both Odia and English except for Paper-I of the Main exam (language paper). The subject of the technical paper

  • once chosen

  • is final and cannot be changed later. Post-wise detailed syllabus for the Technical Paper is given in Annexure-D of the advertisement. Candidates applying for the post of Senior Economic Investigator must choose one subject from 13 subjects listed in the syllabus in the Online Application Form.

  • Stage II(b) - Computer Skill Test

  • All posts except Inspector of Legal Metrology (FS&CW Department)

  • Laboratory Assistant (FS&CW Department)

  • Junior Laboratory Assistant (Directorate of Factories & Boilers)

  • and Junior Laboratory Assistant (State Food Testing Laboratory under Commissioner of Food Safety) are required to qualify the Computer Skill Test. Qualifying marks in Computer Skill Test will be 50% of the total marks. Syllabus in Annexure-C of the advertisement.

  • Stage III - Certificate Verification

  • Candidates numbering 1.5 times of the vacancies advertised (post-wise and category-wise) shall be shortlisted for Certificate Verification based on marks in the Technical Paper(s) of the Main Written Examination and having qualified in Computer Skill Test/Skill Test wherever applicable. Candidates who fail to appear for certificate/document verification will not be considered for final selection. Candidates will be required to produce original academic certificates

  • mark sheets

  • caste certificate

  • special category certificate

  • NOC (if Govt. servant)

  • and other documents as per the admission letter along with self-attested photocopies.

  • Merit list tie-break criteria (in order): (i) Marks in Preliminary Examination

  • (ii) Date of birth (older candidate placed higher)

  • (iii) Alphabetical order.

Detailed step-by-step procedure is available on the website. Candidates are advised to apply well before the closing date to avoid the last-hour rush.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026 is the Combined Graduate Level Recruitment Examination by Odisha Staff Selection Commission under Advertisement No. IIE-46/2025/5919/OSSC to fill 199 Group-B and Group-C specialist posts across various Government Departments of Odisha.

Q2. How many vacancies are available in OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

A total of 199 vacancies are available across 16 posts including Food Safety Officer (84), Librarian in DIETs/BIETs (30), Assistant Embankment Inspector (23), Laboratory Assistant (20), Senior Economic Investigator (16), and other specialist positions, with 52 seats reserved for women.

Q3. What is the last date to apply for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

The last date for online application submission is 11 July 2026, with applications opening on 09 June 2026 and an application correction window from 29 June to 14 July 2026.

Q4. What is the eligibility for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

Eligibility varies by post: candidates must have a Bachelor's or Master's degree in relevant disciplines (Food Technology, Economics, Science, Commerce, Library Science, Fine Arts, Computer Science, etc.) from AICTE/UGC-recognized institutions and must be able to read, write, and speak Odia fluently.

Q5. What is the age limit for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

Candidates must be between 21 to 42 years as on 01 January 2025, with 5 years relaxation for SEBC/SC/ST/Women and 10 years relaxation for PwD candidates, plus additional relaxation for Ex-Servicemen.

Q6. What is the selection process for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

The selection process includes Stage-I Preliminary Examination (150 marks, 150 questions), Stage-II Main Written Examination Technical Paper (200 marks, 100 questions) plus Computer Skill Test (qualifying), and Stage-III Certificate Verification, with negative marking at all stages.

Q7. Is there any application fee for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 Recruitment 2026?

Yes, examination fee is ₹500 for General/OBC/EWS candidates (refundable to those who appear in Preliminary Examination) payable through Odisha Treasury portal, while SC/ST/PwD candidates are exempted from fee payment.

Q8. What is the salary for OSSC CGLRE Specialist-2025 posts?

Group-B posts (Food Safety Officer, Senior Economic Investigator, Librarian, etc.) offer Pay Matrix Level-9, Cell-1 of ORSP 2017, while Group-C posts offer Level-4 to Level-8, Cell-1 depending on the position.
