JNU Non-Teaching Recruitment 2026 – Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility for the JNU Bharti 2026 varies significantly by post - from 10th pass for Multi Tasking Staff to postgraduate degrees with years of experience for Group A roles. Candidates must fulfil the prescribed qualification and experience as on the closing date of application. The maximum age limit for most posts is 40 years , with 45 years for Executive Engineer posts. Candidates wondering about age relaxation for the JNU Non-Teaching Posts 2026 can note that the standard upper age limit is relaxable for reserved categories as per Government of India norms. There is no upper age limit for regular employees of JNU itself, provided they have completed at least three years of regular service as on the closing date of application. A relaxation of 5% in minimum qualifying marks is also available to SC, ST, OBC, and PwD candidates wherever a percentage has been prescribed.

JNU Non-Teaching Recruitment 2026 – Selection Process

The selection process for JNU Non-Teaching Staff Recruitment 2026 is multi-stage and varies by post group. All candidates will appear in a Computer Based Test (CBT), and shortlisted candidates proceed to subsequent stages. There is no interview for Group B and Group C posts as per MoE guidelines. Paper I is an MCQ-type written test of 2 hours carrying 300 marks across 150 questions. It covers General Awareness (30 questions, 60 marks), Reasoning Ability (35 questions, 70 marks), Mathematical Ability (35 questions, 70 marks), Test of Language - English or Hindi (30 questions, 60 marks), and Computer Awareness (20 questions, 40 marks). There is negative marking of 1/4th of the marks per wrong answer. Visually Handicapped and Cerebral Palsy candidates receive 15 minutes extra per hour. Q4What is the application fee for JNU Non-Teaching posts 2026?

A
For Group A posts, the selection involves Paper I (General Test), Paper II (Domain-specific), and an Interview/Personality Test. The final merit is drawn with 70% weightage for the written exam and 30% for the interview.

Candidates must hold a Bachelor's Degree from any recognized university or institute, along with proficiency in typing at 35 wpm in English or 30 wpm in Hindi, and basic computer operation skills.
