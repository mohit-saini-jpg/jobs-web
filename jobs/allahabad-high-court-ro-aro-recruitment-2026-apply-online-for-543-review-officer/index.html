Allahabad High Court RO ARO Recruitment 2026 : Syllabus

The examination consists of Three Stages. Stage-I is a screening test; Stage-II and Stage-III marks are used for final merit. All three stages must be individually qualified. The detailed syllabus for each stage is as under: | Stage-I — Preliminary Examination Syllabus (General Studies, 200 Marks, 200 MCQ, 3 Hours) | No Negative Marking. No Minimum Qualifying Marks. Question paper available in English and Hindi both; in case of any disparity, the English version shall be treated as final. | Stage-II — Mains Examination Syllabus (Subjective Type, 150 Marks, 2 Hours) | Stage-III — Computer Knowledge Test Syllabus (CBT Mode, 50 Marks, 20 Minutes) | Minimum Qualifying Marks: 25 out of 50. No Negative Marking for wrong answers; deductions apply for typing errors only (1 mistake = 0.1 marks deducted). Stage-II and Stage-III are conducted simultaneously in a single shift with a gap of 20 minutes on the same day at the same venue. | Allahabad High Court RO ARO Syllabus and Exam Pattern 2026

Allahabad High Court RO ARO Recruitment 2026 : Important Instructions

Candidates must appear in all three stages of the examination; it is mandatory to qualify each stage individually. No exemption from any stage shall be granted to any candidate including those belonging to the Physically Handicapped category. Final Merit List shall be prepared exclusively on the basis of aggregate marks in Stage-II and Stage-III. Candidates selected for RO post shall not be considered for ARO or CA; candidates selected for ARO shall not be considered for CA. Biometric information (Photograph, Thumb Impression and IRIS) of all candidates shall be captured at examination centres. Candidates must cooperate with security personnel for identity and security checks. The Provisional Answer Key for Stage-I shall be displayed on the official websites for 2-3 days, and candidates may challenge any answer key by paying Rs. 500/- per question challenged (refundable if challenge is accepted). No Score Card will be dispatched by post; candidates must download their Score Cards from the official websites after declaration of final result, as they will be available online for a limited period only. Mobile phones, Bluetooth devices, pagers, calculators, electronic watches with calculator facility or any other electronic gadget are strictly prohibited inside the examination premises. Possession of barred items will be treated as Unfair Means (UFM). Merely appearing and passing in the examination does not confer any right to the candidate for selection or appointment. Selection is subject to eligibility, rank in merit list, medical fitness and verification of original documents. Information uploaded on the official website shall not be provided under the RTI Act during the course of the recruitment process; factual information shall be provided only after declaration of the final result. All candidates already employed in Central/State Government or Public Undertakings must apply subject to production of NOC from their Cadre Controlling Authority as and when asked for. All disputes pertaining to the conduct of this examination shall fall within the jurisdiction of Allahabad only. The High Court reserves the right to alter the number of vacancies, modify the examination process or modify/alter the minimum cut-off marks without assigning any reason. Examination records will be retained for up to 90 days from the date of declaration of the final result. Canvassing in any form shall disqualify the candidature of the applicant. Candidates are bound by a Non-Disclosure Agreement upon registering. Sharing or reproducing any question paper content in part or whole through any medium is a punishable offence leading to cancellation of candidature.
