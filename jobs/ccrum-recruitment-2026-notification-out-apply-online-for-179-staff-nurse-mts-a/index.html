CCRUM Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

Application Fee - CCRUM Recruitment 2026

The CCRUM 2026 application fee structure is divided into a non-refundable Processing Fee (applicable to all candidates) and an Examination Fee (waived for exempted categories). SC/ST, PwBD, EWS, Women candidates, and Ex-Servicemen are exempt from the Examination Fee but must still pay the Processing Fee. Payment is accepted only through the online payment gateway via Debit Card, Credit Card, Internet Banking, or UPI. Cash, Demand Draft, and Postal Orders are not accepted. The fee is inclusive of GST; bank charges will be levied additionally depending on the payment mode.

Selection Process - CCRUM Ayush Vacancy 2026

The CCRUM 2026 selection process differs by post group. Group A candidates face both a written test and an interview, while Group B and C candidates are selected primarily through the Computer Based Test. Group A Posts (Research Officers): Online CBT - 70 marks (subject knowledge + research methodology + general awareness) Interview - 30 marks Shortlisting ratio: 1:5 for 1 vacancy, 1:4 for 2 vacancies, 1:3 for 3 or more vacancies Minimum qualifying marks: 25% for SC/ST/PwBD; 30% for all others Group B & C Posts: Online CBT - 100 marks Skill Test (mandatory and qualifying) for LDC, Stenographer Grade-III, Staff Car Driver, and wherever applicable Minimum qualifying marks: 25% for SC/ST/PwBD; 30% for all others Negative Marking: There is a 0.25 mark deduction per wrong answer in the CBT for all posts except MTS, where there is no negative marking. Exam Syllabus at a Glance

The CCRUM CBT syllabus is post-specific. Every paper follows a common structure: subject knowledge (70 marks) + research methodology/relevant technical knowledge (20 marks) + general awareness/IT & computing (10 marks) for Group B and C posts. Group A post papers total 70 marks (subject: 40 + research methodology: 20 + general awareness/IT: 10), followed by an interview of 30 marks. MTS and Staff Car Driver follow SSC's MTS/Matric-level examination syllabus.

Modification Window

After the application window closes, CCRUM provides a Modification Window from 3 August 2026 (10:00 AM) to 5 August 2026 (23:59 hrs) . A modification fee of ₹300 per attempt per post is charged. Note that account creation details from Step I cannot be changed through this window. If a modification makes a candidate ineligible for the post, the change will not be permitted.

Salary / Pay Scale

The CCRUM Recruitment 2026 offers 7th CPC pay structure with NPA (Non-Practising Allowance) for Group A medical posts as per Government of India rules. Selected candidates are placed on probation for two years , extendable at the discretion of the competent authority, and will be governed by the New Pension Scheme (NPS) . All posts carry All India transfer liability; candidates must be willing to serve at any of CCRUM's 24 Institutes/Centers across India. CCRUM Recruitment 2026 : How to Apply Online Step-by-Step

  • 1All applications for CCRUM Bharti 2026 must be submitted online only through the official CCRUM website. No documents or application forms need to be sent to CCRUM by post or mail.
  • 2Visit www.ccrum.res.in or www.ccrum.ayush.gov.in and go to the "Advertisement/Vacancy" tab.
  • 3Create a new account (Step I): Enter your full name (as per Matriculation Certificate), father's name, mother's name, gender, date of birth, mobile number, valid email ID, and Government ID proof. Verify via OTP.
  • 4Complete your profile (Step II): Log in with your Reference Number and Password. Upload photograph, signature, caste certificate, disability certificate (if applicable), basic qualification certificates, and other relevant documents.
  • 5Apply for the post (Step III): Select the post(s) you wish to apply for, fill in post-specific information, and upload post-specific documents.
  • 6Pay the fee: You will be redirected to the payment gateway. Pay via Debit Card, Credit Card, Net Banking, or UPI. Download the payment acknowledgement slip.
  • 7Download your application form in PDF format from the Dashboard for future reference.
  • 8Important: Details entered during account creation (Step I) cannot be modified at any later stage. Candidates applying for multiple posts must use the same login reference number and pay the fee separately for each post.

CCRUM Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for CCRUM Recruitment 2026?

Q2How many vacancies are there in CCRUM Bharti 2026?
A
CCRUM has notified 179 vacancies across 23 posts in Group A, B, and C categories under Advertisement No. 1/2026. Several of these vacancies are backlog positions from previous years.
Q3What is the age limit for CCRUM Recruitment 2026?
A
Age limits are set as per CCRUM norms. Candidates should refer to the official notification for post-wise age criteria and relaxation details.
Q4Is there negative marking in the CCRUM 2026 exam?
A
Yes. There is a 0.25 mark deduction for each wrong answer in the CBT for Group A, B, and C posts. However, for the MTS post, there is no negative marking.
Q5What is the selection process for CCRUM Group A posts?
A
Group A selection involves an online CBT of 70 marks followed by an interview of 30 marks. Candidates are shortlisted for interview at a ratio of 1:5 (1 vacancy), 1:4 (2 vacancies), or 1:3 (3 or more vacancies).
Q6Where will the CCRUM 2026 exam be held?
A
The online CBT will be conducted at exam centers in New Delhi/Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Guwahati. CCRUM reserves the right to add or cancel centers.
Q7Can I apply for more than one post in CCRUM Recruitment 2026?
A
Yes. Candidates can apply for multiple posts using the same reference number/login. Each post requires a separate application and separate fee payment. Do not create multiple accounts.
Q8What is the age limit for CCRUM Group A Research Officer posts?
A
The upper age limit for Research Officer posts is 40 years. Government servants are eligible for up to 5 years of age relaxation as per Central Government orders.
