BSI JRF Vacancy Details 2026

BSI JRF Recruitment 2026 covers a total of 6 Junior Research Fellow posts in Botany. The notification does not provide a category-wise or centre-wise vacancy breakup; all 6 posts are open across BSI's regional centres, and selected candidates may be posted at any of them based on organizational requirements. Place of work includes any BSI Regional Centre/Office located at Itanagar, Shillong, Gangtok, Howrah, Kolkata, Prayagraj, Noida, Dehradun, Jodhpur, Pune, Hyderabad, Vijaya Puram, Coimbatore, or Solan (Yercaud). Appointed JRFs may be transferred to any other BSI office based on organizational needs.

BSI JRF Eligibility Criteria 2026

Candidates wondering whether they qualify for BSI JRF Recruitment 2026 can check the essential and desirable qualifications below. Essential: Master's degree (M.Sc.) in Botany from a UGC-recognized university with at least 55% aggregate marks at the time of submitting the application. Desirable: M.Sc. in Botany with specialization in Plant Taxonomy or Plant Systematics (any plant group), working knowledge of computers, strong analytical abilities, and proficient technical writing skills. Candidates who have qualified CSIR-JRF NET are exempted from the written examination. However, they must still apply, select their preferred examination centres, and appear for the interview if shortlisted. BSI JRF Fellowship Amount and Duration 2026

Candidates checking the BSI JRF salary can note that selected candidates will receive a consolidated monthly fellowship of ₹37,000 for the first two years, enhanced to ₹42,000 from the third year onwards, along with HRA as admissible under central rules. The fellowship follows a 2+1+2 year structure: Initial appointment as JRF for 2 years. Following a performance assessment, suitable candidates are promoted to Senior Research Fellow (SRF) for a 3rd year; otherwise, the fellowship may be terminated. The SRF tenure may be extended for an additional 2 years, subject to satisfactory progress and institutional requirements. This fellowship is purely contractual and confers no right to regularization or absorption in BSI. Fellows are not entitled to any other benefits during or after their tenure.

BSI JRF Selection Process 2026

Selection for BSI JRF Recruitment 2026 involves a written test followed by an interview for shortlisted candidates. Candidates who have qualified CSIR-JRF NET are exempted only from the written test stage and go directly to the interview if shortlisted. A physical (offline) written test consisting of one Multiple Choice Question (MCQ) paper of 3 hours duration will be conducted across all Regional Centres of BSI. The exact date and time will be communicated in advance and posted on the official website. Online interviews for shortlisted candidates will be hosted through five regional centres: Central National Herbarium (Howrah), Eastern Regional Centre (Shillong), Western Regional Centre (Pune), Southern Regional Centre (Coimbatore), and Northern Regional Centre (Dehradun).
