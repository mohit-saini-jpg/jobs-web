DU PG Admission Round 2 Allotment 2026 - Check Status, Dates & Fee Payment Deadline

The University of Delhi (DU) has released the DU PG Round 2 allotment 2026 on the official CSAS (PG) portal, pgadmission.uod.ac.in. Candidates who applied for postgraduate programmes through CUET PG 2026 can now log in to check whether they have received a fresh seat or an upgraded allotment. Those allotted a seat must accept it, complete document verification, and pay the admission fee within the prescribed deadlines, with the final fee payment window closing on June 27, 2026.

This article breaks down the complete Round 2 schedule, the step-by-step process to check your allotment, the document and fee requirements, and what happens next in the DU PG admission cycle.

DU PG Round 2 Allotment 2026 - Quick Overview

Before diving into the details, here is a snapshot of what the Round 2 allotment means for DU PG aspirants and what immediate action is required.

The Round 2 list includes candidates who were not allotted a seat in Round 1, those who withdrew or were rejected earlier, and existing allottees seeking an upgrade to a higher preference. Seats vacated after Round 1 acceptance and fee payment have been added back to the pool for this round.

How to Check DU PG Round 2 Allotment Result 2026

This section explains exactly how to log in and view your individual Round 2 seat allotment on the official DU CSAS PG portal.

Follow these steps to check your allotment status:

  • Step 1: Visit the official DU PG admission portal at pgadmission.uod.ac.in.
  • Step 2: Log in to your CSAS (PG) dashboard using your CUET PG application number and password.
  • Step 3: Click on the "PG Admission" or "Admission List" section on the homepage.
  • Step 4: Locate the Round 2 (second) allotment/merit list link and open it.
  • Step 5: Search your application number using the Ctrl+F search function, or check your dashboard directly for your personalised allotment status.
  • Step 6: Review your allotted college, programme, and category, and download the result for your records.

Always rely only on the official portal for your allotment status rather than third-party websites, to avoid acting on incorrect information.

What to Do After Receiving Your DU PG Round 2 Allotment

This section covers the exact sequence of actions candidates need to complete to secure their allotted seat in Round 2.

  • Accept the allotment: Log in to the CSAS (PG) dashboard and click "Accept Allocation" before the June 24 deadline.
  • Submit documents for verification: Upload the required documents on the portal so that the allotted college or department can verify and approve your application by June 25.
  • Pay the admission fee: Once verification is approved, a payment link is generated on the dashboard. Complete the fee payment by June 27 to confirm your seat.
  • Decide on upgrades: If you already hold a seat from Round 1 and are not satisfied with your Round 2 allotment, you can choose to retain your current seat and wait for the next round instead of accepting a new one.

Documents Required for DU PG Round 2 Verification

This section lists the standard set of documents candidates should keep ready for the verification stage, as incomplete submissions can lead to non-approval of the allotted seat.

  • CUET PG 2026 scorecard/admit card
  • Class 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates
  • Bachelor's degree mark sheets/provisional certificate and degree certificate
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC-NCL/EWS/PwBD/KM, as applicable), issued within the valid timeframe
  • Children/Widows of Armed Forces Personnel (CW) candidates are required to submit their ECC (Eligibility-cum-Confirmation) certificate on the CSAS portal
  • Valid photo identity proof
  • Passport-size photograph and signature as uploaded during registration

Ensure all scanned documents are clear and legible, since verifying officers reject blurry or low-resolution uploads, which can hold up seat approval.

DU PG Round 2 Cutoff 2026

This section explains what the Round 2 cutoff represents and how it differs from the Round 1 cutoff.

Delhi University released the second cutoff for DU PG 2026 programmes alongside the Round 2 allotment list on June 22. The Round 2 cutoff reflects updated vacancy positions after Round 1 seat acceptance and fee payment, and it can move up or down compared to the first cutoff depending on the number of seats vacated, category-wise demand, and the overall difficulty level of CUET PG 2026. Candidates should check their specific department's cutoff on the official portal rather than relying on last year's figures, since cutoffs vary year to year based on the applicant pool.

What's Next After DU PG Round 2 Allotment

This section outlines the stages that follow Round 2 in the overall DU PG Admission 2026 cycle.

Delhi University conducts the PG admission process in multiple stages: three merit lists, followed by spot rounds and a mop-up round to fill any remaining vacant seats. Candidates not allotted a seat in Round 2, or those still waiting for their preferred department, remain in contention for the subsequent round. Certain specialised programmes including MA Music, MPEd, BPEd, and MFA, along with seats reserved under the CW (Children/Widows of Armed Forces Personnel) and Sports categories are filled through a separate, performance-based selection process in a later round rather than through the standard merit-based CSAS allotment.
