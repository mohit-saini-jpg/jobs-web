The CSIR NET City Slip 2026 (also called the Exam City Intimation Slip) for the June session is being released by the National Testing Agency (NTA) at csirnet.nta.nic.in. Candidates can download it using their application number and date of birth to find out which city their CSIR UGC NET exam centre will be located in. The Joint CSIR-UGC NET June 2026 exam is scheduled for July 17 and 18, 2026, conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT) across two shifts.

This article explains exactly how to download the CSIR NET City Slip 2026, what information it contains, how it differs from the admit card, and answers the most common questions candidates are searching for right now.

What Is the CSIR NET City Intimation Slip 2026?

Before downloading anything, it helps to understand what this document actually is and why NTA releases it separately from the admit card.

The CSIR NET City Intimation Slip is an advance notice issued by NTA that tells candidates only the city where their exam centre is located - not the exact venue address. It is released purely so that candidates have enough time to arrange travel and accommodation before the exam date. The precise exam centre address, reporting time, and other exam-day specifics are shared later through the official CSIR NET Admit Card, which NTA typically issues 3-4 days before the exam.

How to Download CSIR NET City Slip 2026: Step-by-Step Process

This section walks you through the exact steps to access and download your city intimation slip once it is live on the NTA portal.

Follow these steps to download the CSIR NET City Slip 2026:

  • Visit the official website - csirnet.nta.nic.in.
  • Locate and click on the link that reads "Download CSIR NET Exam City Intimation Slip."
  • Log in using your application number and date of birth.
  • Your city slip will appear on the screen - check the "City of Examination" column carefully.
  • Download the slip and take a printout for future reference and safekeeping.

Keep your application number and date of birth handy, as you will need these same credentials again later to download the admit card.

What Information Does the CSIR NET City Slip Contain?

Understanding what to check on your city slip helps you avoid errors or last-minute confusion. Here's what the document typically includes.

  • Examination City: The city where your allotted exam centre is located, helping you plan travel in advance.
  • Candidate Details: Basic identity information such as name, roll number, and application number for verification.
  • Instructions: Guidelines to be followed on exam day and other important rules set by NTA.
  • Important Dates: Reminders regarding the exam date and reporting time.
  • Contact Information: Helpline details to reach out to in case of any discrepancy on the slip.

Note that the exact centre address is not mentioned on the city slip - that detail is reserved for the admit card.

Why the CSIR NET City Slip Matters

A short explanation of why candidates shouldn't ignore this document even though it isn't required at the exam hall.

The city intimation slip may seem like a minor formality, but it plays a practical role in exam preparation. Since CSIR NET exam centres are spread across multiple cities in India, candidates allotted a centre in a different city need adequate lead time to:

  • Book train, bus, or flight tickets in advance at reasonable prices.
  • Arrange accommodation if the exam city is far from their hometown.
  • Plan the mode and route of travel to avoid last-minute stress on exam day.

Troubleshooting: What If You Face Issues Downloading the Slip?

If the download process doesn't work smoothly, here are the basic checks candidates should make before assuming a technical fault on NTA's end.

  • Ensure your internet connection is stable and working properly.
  • Double-check that you are entering the correct application number and date of birth.
  • Clear your browser cache or try an alternate browser if the page fails to load.
  • If the issue persists, contact the NTA helpdesk using the contact details available on the official website.
