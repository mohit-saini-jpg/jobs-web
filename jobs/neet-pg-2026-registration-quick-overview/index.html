What Is NEET PG 2026?

Before applying, candidates should understand what the exam is for and why it matters for their postgraduate medical journey.

NEET PG is the single national eligibility-cum-ranking examination for admission to MD, MS, and PG Diploma courses for the 2026-27 academic session. No separate state-level or institute-level entrance exam is valid for these postgraduate medical admissions, making NEET PG mandatory for all eligible Indian and foreign national candidates seeking a PG medical seat in India.

NEET PG 2026 Registration Link: How to Apply

This section explains where and how candidates can access the application form.

The NEET PG 2026 registration link has been activated on the official NBEMS website. Candidates can also access the form through the alternate portal, nbe.edu.in. The Information Bulletin was released at 4:00 PM on July 1, 2026, and candidates are strongly advised to read it thoroughly before starting the application, since it contains eligibility rules, the exam scheme, and fee details.

NEET PG 2026 Registration: Step-by-Step Process

This section walks candidates through the complete application procedure from registration to final submission.

Follow these steps to complete the NEET PG 2026 application form:

  • Visit the official website, natboard.edu.in, and click on the NEET PG 2026 registration link.
  • Register by entering your name, date of birth, gender, email ID, and mobile number.
  • Generate your User ID and password to log in.
  • Fill in the application form with personal, academic, and internship details.
  • Upload the required documents in the prescribed format and size.
  • Select your preferred test states (see the test state rule below).
  • Pay the NEET PG 2026 application fee online.
  • Review all details carefully and submit the form.
  • Download and print the confirmation page for future reference.

Candidates are advised not to rush to fill the form immediately after release. NBEMS has specifically recommended reading the instructions and information bulletin carefully before starting.

NEET PG 2026 Eligibility Criteria

This section explains who can apply for NEET PG 2026 based on academic and internship requirements.

To register for NEET PG 2026, candidates must meet the following eligibility conditions prescribed by NBEMS and the National Medical Commission (NMC):

  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must hold an MBBS degree or a provisional pass certificate recognised under the NMC Act, 2019, and the Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2023.
  • Internship Requirement: Candidates must have completed one year of compulsory rotatory internship, or be likely to complete it on or before September 30, 2026.
  • Medical Registration: Applicants must hold a valid permanent or provisional registration certificate issued by the NMC or a State Medical Council.

Candidates found ineligible at any stage even after appearing for the exam or securing a result will have their candidature cancelled.

Documents Required for NEET PG 2026 Registration

This section lists the documents and file specifications candidates must keep ready before starting the form.

Candidates should arrange the following documents in advance:

  • Recent passport-size photograph on a plain white background (not older than 3 months, under 80 KB)
  • A real-time photo captured via webcam
  • Scanned signature
  • Scanned left-hand thumb impression
  • MBBS degree certificate or provisional pass certificate
  • Internship completion certificate (or proof of likely completion by September 30, 2026)

Photograph Guidelines: NBEMS has issued strict rules for the photo upload selfies are not allowed, and candidates must not wear a stethoscope, cap, glasses, or spectacles while taking the photo. A neutral facial expression is required, and ears must be clearly visible.

Key Changes in NEET PG 2026 Registration

This section highlights the new rules introduced this year that candidates must be aware of before applying.

NBEMS has introduced several important changes for NEET PG 2026:

  • Test State Instead of Test City: Candidates must now select three preferred test states instead of specific cities. The first choice must be the state where the candidate's address is located, and the remaining two should ideally be neighbouring states.
  • No First-Come-First-Served (FCFS) Advantage: NBEMS has clearly stated that test state/city allotment is not on an FCFS basis, so applying early does not guarantee any allotment advantage.
  • Mandatory Aadhaar Verification: Aadhaar is used by NBEMS solely to verify candidate identity during the application and examination process.

NEET PG 2026 Exam Pattern

This section outlines the format of the exam so candidates know what to expect on test day.

NEET PG 2026 will be conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT) in a single shift, in English only. The exam will comprise 180 multiple-choice questions, each with four response options, to be answered within 3 hours and 30 minutes.

Important Instructions and Restrictions

This section covers the do's and don'ts candidates must follow to avoid application rejection.

  • Fill the form only on a computer/desktop; NBEMS advises against using mobile devices.
  • Verify all non-editable details carefully before final submission, as errors could lead to rejection.
  • After submission and payment, candidates can only edit their applications during the correction window (July 25–28, 2026). No new registration or payment is allowed during this period.
  • The following details cannot be changed after submission: Name, Aadhaar number, Email, Mobile Number, Nationality, Correspondence Address, and Test State Preference.
  • Once submitted, application forms cannot be withdrawn.
  • No applications will be accepted after the registration window closes on July 21, 2026, under any circumstances.
