GMAT Exam Pattern: Quantitative Reasoning

The GMAT Quantitative Reasoning section tests your ability to solve math-based problems using logic, arithmetic, and elementary algebra. It includes 21 Problem Solving questions and must be completed in 45 minutes, with no calculator allowed.

This section is not about advanced mathematics. Instead, it checks how well you can apply basic quantitative concepts to interpret data, solve word problems, and make accurate decisions under time pressure.

What to expect in Quant

  • 21 questions.
  • 45 minutes.
  • Problem Solving only.
  • Topics include arithmetic, algebra, number properties, ratios, and word problems.
  • No calculator is allowed.

How to prepare for Quantitative Reasoning? Focus on solving questions quickly and accurately, especially from arithmetic and algebra topics. Regular timed practice helps build speed and reduces careless errors in the final exam.

GMAT Exam Pattern: Verbal Reasoning

The GMAT Verbal Reasoning section measures how well you understand written material, analyze arguments, and identify logical conclusions. It contains 23 multiple-choice questions and gives you 45 minutes to complete the section.

This section mainly tests reading comprehension and critical reasoning skills, so success depends on careful reading, logical analysis, and the ability to identify the author’s point, tone, and assumptions.

What to expect in Verbal

  • 23 questions.
  • 45 minutes.
  • Reading Comprehension and Critical Reasoning.
  • Five answer choices per question.
  • Focus on comprehension, inference, argument evaluation, and reasoning.

How to prepare for Verbal? Practice active reading, identify the main idea quickly, and learn how to break down arguments into claims, evidence, and assumptions. Consistent practice with official-style passages is the best way to improve.

GMAT Exam Pattern: Data Insights

The GMAT Data Insights section checks how well you can analyze data from tables, charts, graphs, and multiple sources to solve business-style problems. It includes 20 questions, must be completed in 45 minutes, and contributes to the total GMAT score.

This section combines quantitative and verbal reasoning, so it tests both number skills and interpretation skills. The question formats include Data Sufficiency, Graphics Interpretation, Table Analysis, Two-Part Analysis, and Multi-Source Reasoning.

What to expect in Data Insights

  • 20 questions.
  • 45 minutes.
  • Five question types.
  • Uses tables, charts, graphs, text, and mixed data.
  • Tests analysis, interpretation, and reasoning across multiple formats.

How to prepare for Data Insights? Work on reading data accurately, comparing sources quickly, and eliminating answer choices using logic. This section rewards careful interpretation more than memorization, so practice with mixed-format questions is essential.

Scoring and Additional Features

  • All three sections contribute equally to the total score.
  • No negative marking.
  • You can send your scores to up to 5 programs for free within 48 hours after the test.
  • Official score report is available within 1–3 business days.

Preparation Tips for GMAT Exam 2026

  • Focus on accuracy since there is no negative marking but time is limited.
  • Practice with official GMAT practice tests from mba.com.
  • Strengthen data interpretation skills for the Data Insights section.
  • Build speed in Quantitative Reasoning by mastering arithmetic and algebra shortcuts.
  • Read diverse passages regularly to improve Verbal Reasoning performance.
