KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026: Eligibility Criteria

Candidate must be an Indian citizen. Candidate must be mentally and physically fit to discharge the duties of the post. A male candidate with more than one living spouse, and a female candidate married to such a person, is not eligible for appointment unless specifically exempted by the government.

KREIS Teacher Educational Qualification (Subject-wise)

Kannada Language Teachers: Graduation with Kannada as an optional subject in the Arts stream from a recognized university, or the four-year "Kannada Pandith" course from the Karnataka State Pre-University Education Board, together with a Kannada B.Ed. degree; or a Bachelor's degree with Kannada as an optional subject plus a postgraduate degree in Kannada. English Language Teachers: Graduation with English as an optional subject in the Arts stream, or a postgraduate diploma in English from a Regional Institute of English in South India, together with a B.Ed. with English as the primary method subject; or a postgraduate degree in English with Kannada studied as one subject up to degree/PUC/SSLC level, or a Karnataka Pre-University Education Board/Kannada Sahitya Parishat Kannada proficiency certificate (Kaav/Jaan/Ratna), or a Diploma in Kannada. KREIS Teacher Selection Process 2026

Selection to Teacher posts is based purely on a competitive written examination; there is no interview. Competitive Written Examination - Offline OMR-based test consisting of Paper-I (General Knowledge) and Paper-II (Subject-specific paper). Document Verification - As per merit and category-wise reservation. Minimum qualifying marks in the competitive exam is 35% for general category candidates; candidates scoring below this are disqualified for the selection process. Negative marking: Each question has 4 options; 0.25 marks will be deducted for every wrong answer.

KREIS Teacher Kannada Language Test

Candidates who have not studied Kannada as a first or second language up to SSLC level, or an equivalent level recognized by the state government, or who have not passed a Kannada test conducted by a competent authority, will be required to appear for a qualifying Kannada Language Test after the main exam. This test carries 150 marks, and candidates must secure a minimum of 50 marks to qualify; marks scored are not counted in the final merit list, and candidates scoring below the minimum become ineligible for appointment.

KEA KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1How many total vacancies are there in KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026?
A
There are 1087 total vacancies - 767 posts under the Rest of the State (RPC) cadre and 320 posts under the Kalyana-Karnataka (KK) local cadre.
Q2What is the last date to apply for KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026?
A
The online application window opens on 15 July 2026 and closes on 13 August 2026, with the fee payment deadline on 14 August 2026.
Q3Is TET or CTET mandatory for KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026?
A
Yes, candidates applying for Kannada, English, Hindi, Mathematics, Science, and Social Science Teacher posts must hold a valid Karnataka TET (Paper-2) or CTET certificate. This requirement is not specified for Computer Science, Physical Education, and Drawing Teacher posts in the notification.
Q4What is the age limit for KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026?
A
The minimum age is 18 years. The maximum age is 45 years for General category, 48 years for Category 2A/2B/3A/3B, and 50 years for SC/ST/Category-1, with additional relaxations applicable for specific categories.
Q5What is the application fee for KREIS Teacher Recruitment 2026?
A
The fee is ₹750 for General and 2A/2B/3A/3B candidates, ₹500 for SC/ST/Category-1/Ex-servicemen/Third gender candidates, and ₹250 for special pay (PWD) candidates. An additional ₹100 applies per extra post if applying for more than one.
Q6Is there an interview in the KREIS Teacher selection process?
A
No, selection is based purely on the competitive written examination followed by document verification. There is no interview.
Q7Is there negative marking in the KREIS Teacher exam?
A
Yes, 0.25 marks will be deducted for each wrong answer in the OMR-based competitive exam.
Q8What is the pay scale for KREIS Teacher posts?
A
Most Teacher posts carry a pay scale of ₹54,175 - ₹99,400, while Graduate and Diploma Drawing Teacher posts carry a pay scale of ₹49,050 - ₹92,500, as per the 2024 Revised State Pay Scale.
Q9Is the Kannada language test compulsory for all candidates?
A
No. It applies only to candidates who have not studied Kannada as a first or second language up to SSLC or an equivalent level. It is qualifying in nature (minimum 50 out of 150 marks) and marks are not added to the final merit.
Q10Are Computer Science and Drawing Teacher posts available in both cadres?
A
No. Computer Science Teacher posts are notified only under the RPC cadre, while Graduate and Diploma Drawing Teacher posts are notified only under the Kalyana-Karnataka cadre.
