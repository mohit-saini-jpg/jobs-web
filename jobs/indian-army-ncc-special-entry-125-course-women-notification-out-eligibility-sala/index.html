Indian Army NCC Special Entry 125 Course (Women) : Job Overview

The Indian Army has invited online applications from unmarried women holding an NCC 'C' Certificate, and Wards of Battle Casualties of Indian Army personnel, for Short Service Commission (Non-Technical) under the NCC Special Entry Scheme, 125th Course (April 2027) - SSCW (NCC)-125. This article covers complete details on eligibility, vacancies, selection process, training, and salary as per the official notification.

Eligibility Criteria

The candidate must be: (i) a citizen of India, or (ii) a subject of Nepal, or (iii) a person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, or specified East African countries (Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia) and Vietnam, with the intention of permanently settling in India, provided a certificate of eligibility has been issued by the Government of India (not required for Gorkha subjects of Nepal). 19 to 25 years as on 01 January 2027 (born not earlier than 02 January 2002 and not later than 01 January 2008, both dates inclusive). The date of birth recorded in the Matriculation/Secondary School Examination Certificate at the time of application will be treated as final; no subsequent change request will be entertained. Salary and Pay Scale

Stipend during training: ₹56,100 per month (fixed at OTA, Chennai for the full 49-week duration). Military Service Pay (MSP): ₹15,500 per month, fixed, for officers from Lieutenant to Brigadier. Approximate CTC on commissioning: ₹17–18 lakh per annum, excluding free medical cover and one home-town visit per year. Additional allowances include Dearness Allowance, Transport Allowance, Children Education Allowance (₹2,250/month per child, for two eldest children), Hostel Subsidy (₹6,750/month per child), Dress Allowance (₹20,000/year), and various field/hardship allowances depending on posting.

Tenure and Release Options

Officers are liable to serve for 10 years, extendable by a further 4 years, subject to eligibility and organizational requirements. Permanent Commission may be considered in the 10th year of service. Officers may apply for release during the 5th year of service; Army HQ's decision on such applications is final. Release options are also available after the 10th and 14th years of service. Reserve liability: Officers released before completion of contractual service must serve the Army Reserve for 5 years, plus 2 years on a voluntary basis, or up to age 37 (whichever is earlier).

Selection Process

Shortlisting of applications: Based on bonus marks out of a maximum of 100 - up to 50 marks for NCC 'C' Certificate grading (50 for 'A' Grade, 25 for 'B' Grade) and up to 50 marks for graduation performance (0 to 50 marks on a sliding scale from below 60% to 85%+ aggregate). Vacancy-to-call-up ratio: Candidates are shortlisted for SSB based on this ratio, decided by the Directorate General of Recruiting. SSB Centre and date selection: Shortlisted candidates choose their SSB centre and date on a first-come-first-served basis; choices cannot be changed later except under exceptional circumstances. SSB interview: Conducted over 5 days at Prayagraj, Bhopal, Bengaluru, or Jalandhar. Candidates undergo a two-stage process - Stage I, and Stage II (for those who clear Stage I).   • 1Visit www.joinindianarmy.nic.in and click "Officer Entry Application/Login," then "Registration" (skip if already registered).
  • 2After registration, click "Apply Online" under the Dashboard, then click "Apply" against Short Service Commission NCC Special Entry Course.

  • 6No changes can be made to the application after it closes. Candidates must submit only one application - multiple applications will result in cancellation.

Indian Army NCC Special Entry 125 Course (Women) : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1Who is eligible for the Indian Army NCC Special Entry Scheme 125th Course for women?
A
Unmarried female graduates aged 19–25 years (as on 01 January 2027) holding a minimum 'B' Grade in the NCC 'C' Certificate exam, along with 2–3 years of service in the Senior Division/Wing of NCC, are eligible. Wards of Battle Casualties of Indian Army personnel are also eligible, without the NCC 'C' Certificate requirement.
Q2How many vacancies are there in this recruitment?
A
There are 6 vacancies in total - 5 under the General category and 1 reserved for Wards of Battle Casualties of Indian Army personnel.
Q3What is the last date to apply for SSCW (NCC)-125?
A
The online application closes on 21 August 2026 at 1500 hrs. Applications opened on 21 July 2026 at 1500 hrs.
Q4What is the stipend during training?
A
Candidates receive a stipend of ₹56,100 per month during the 49-week training period at Officers Training Academy, Chennai.
Q5What is the salary after commissioning?
A
On commissioning as a Lieutenant, the approximate CTC is ₹17–18 lakh per annum, excluding free medical cover and one home-town visit per year.
Q6Is the NCC 'C' Certificate mandatory for this entry?
A
Yes, for the general category - a minimum 'B' Grade in the NCC 'C' Certificate exam is required, and it must be obtained on or before the date of application. It is not required for Wards of Battle Casualties.
Q7Where will the SSB interview be conducted?
A
SSB interviews will be conducted at Prayagraj (Uttar Pradesh), Bhopal (Madhya Pradesh), Bengaluru (Karnataka), and Jalandhar (Punjab).
Q8Can a candidate apply for both SSCW (NT) and NCC Special Entry in the same course?
A
No. Candidates can appear for only one SSB interview - either as a CDSE candidate for SSCW (NT) (Women)-125 Course or for NCC (Special) Entry-125 Course (Women) - and must submit an undertaking to this effect in the online application.
