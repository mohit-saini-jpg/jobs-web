Selection Process

1Merit-based (Marks scored in qualifying examination), followed by Document Scrutiny and Medical Test
2Candidates who have already undergone Apprenticeship Training earlier or are currently pursuing Apprenticeship in any industry.
3Candidates with 1 year or more of job experience.
4Candidates who have passed their qualifying degree/diploma/ITI before June 2022.
5Candidates who possess qualifications higher than the prescribed qualification for the applied discipline.
6Candidates whose qualifying institution is not functional and operational in Uttar Pradesh or Madhya Pradesh.
7Candidates whose qualifications were obtained through Part-time, Correspondence, or Distance Learning mode.
8OBC candidates belonging to the Creamy Layer.
9Candidates who submit multiple applications - multiple applications will lead to disqualification.
10Candidates who are unable to produce a valid Medical Fitness certificate as per Mines Rules 1955.
11Not pre-registering on the NATS portal (for Graduate/Diploma) or NAPS portal (for Trade/ITI) before applying - without a valid Enrolment ID,
12Applying for more than one discipline - multiple applications will lead to disqualification of candidature.
13Entering incorrect percentage/marks scored or maximum marks in the online form - candidature will be cancelled if found incorrect during scr
14Not checking that the qualifying institution is functional and operational in Uttar Pradesh or Madhya Pradesh.
15Uploading blurred or unclear photograph or signature in black ink.
16Not having an active email ID and mobile number valid for at least one year for future communication.
17Rounding off the passing percentage - NCL will not consider rounding off (49.99% will be treated as less than 50%).
18Not taking a printout of the finally submitted online application for future reference.

How to Apply

  • 1Valid NATS/NAPS Enrolment ID (registration reference).
  • 2Scanned colour photograph and scanned signature in black ink (as per portal specifications).
  • 3Date of Birth as per Matriculation certificate.
  • 4Academic details - total marks scored and maximum marks in qualifying degree/diploma/ITI trade.
  • 5Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC/EWS) in prescribed format (Annexure-1), if applicable.
  • 6PwBD disability certificate as per Rights of Persons with Disabilities Rules 2017 (Annexure-2), if applicable.
  • 7Address for correspondence and permanent address.
  • 8Online applications are open from 01-06-2026 to 15-06-2026 through NATS/NAPS portals only.
  • 9A candidate may apply for any one notified position only - multiple applications will lead to disqualification.
  • 10Information pertaining to Date of Birth, Caste Category, and Qualification once entered in the online application shall be considered final - no alteration will be permitted at later stages.
  • 11Stipend for Graduate and Diploma Apprentices is inclusive of the Government of India share paid through DBT; Trade Apprentice stipend will be paid directly by NCL.
  • 12Stipend will be paid directly into the AADHAAR-linked bank account of the Apprenticeship Trainee as per the signed contract.
  • 13Apprenticeship Training is purely temporary and statutory in nature - it does not confer any right or claim for regular employment in NCL.
  • 14Apprentices are eligible for 11 days Casual Leave and 15 days Medical Leave per year of training.
  • 15Engagement hours are between 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  • 16Apprentices will be covered under the Workmen Compensation Act, 1923 for any accident during training.
  • 17No accommodation, fooding/lodging, or transport will be provided during the training period.
  • 18No TA/DA will be paid for appearing in document scrutiny or post-scrutiny processes.
  • 19Canvassing in any manner at any stage will lead to cancellation of candidature.
  • 20Any dispute will be subject to courts/tribunals of Singrauli, MP only.

FAQs

Q1What is the last date to apply for NCL Apprentice Recruitment 2026-27?
A
The last date to apply online is 15 June 2026 through the NATS and NAPS portals.
Q2How many vacancies are announced in NCL Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 1607 apprentice vacancies have been announced across Graduate, Diploma, ITI Trade, and Paramedical disciplines.
Q3What is the selection process for NCL Apprentice Trainee 2026?
A
Selection is based on merit (marks in qualifying exam), followed by document verification and medical examination. No written test is conducted.
Q4What is the stipend for NCL Apprentice Trainees?
A
Stipend ranges from ₹9,600 to ₹13,700 per month depending on the qualification and discipline.
Q5Who is eligible to apply for NCL Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
Indian candidates who have completed Degree/Diploma/ITI/Paramedical courses from recognized institutes in Uttar Pradesh or Madhya Pradesh after June 2022 are eligible.
Q6What is the age limit for NCL Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must be between 18 to 26 years as on 01 June 2026, with age relaxation for reserved categories as per rules.
Q7Is there any application fee for NCL Apprentice 2026?
A
No, there is no application fee. Candidates can apply free of cost through NATS/NAPS portals.
Q8Can candidates with prior job experience apply for NCL Apprentice posts?
A
No, candidates with one year or more of work experience or prior apprenticeship training are not eligible.
Q9How to apply for NCL Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must register and apply through NATS (for Graduate/Diploma) or NAPS (for ITI Trade) portals online.
Q10Is NCL Apprenticeship a permanent job?
A
No, it is a one-year training program under the Apprentice Act, 1961, and does not guarantee permanent employment.
