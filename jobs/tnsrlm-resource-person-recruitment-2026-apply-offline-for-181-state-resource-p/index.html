  • 1Unlike most government recruitment processes, the TNSRLM PRI-CBO Phase II application must be submitted physically - either by post or in person - to the Project Director of the DMMU in the district the candidate is applying for.   • 2Identify the district you wish to apply for from the 15 notified districts
  • 3Prepare your application with all relevant personal, educational, and experience details
  • 4Attach self-attested copies of all supporting documents (educational certificates, experience certificates, SHG membership proof if applicable, age proof, and caste certificate if applicable)
  • 5Address the application envelope clearly to the Project Director, District Mission Management Unit (TNSRLM) of the respective district

TNSRLM Resource Person Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1Has TNSRLM officially released the SRP, DRP, and BRP Recruitment Notification for PRI-CBO Phase II?
A
Yes. Tamil Nadu State Rural Livelihoods Mission (TNSRLM) has officially released the recruitment notification for State Resource Persons (SRPs), District Resource Persons (DRPs), and Block Resource Persons (BRPs) under PRI-CBO Convergence Universalization – Phase II, covering 15 new districts across Tamil Nadu. The last date to apply is 29 June 2026.
Q2How many vacancies are available under TNSRLM PRI-CBO Phase II?
A
TNSRLM has notified 161 BRP positions assigned across blocks in 15 districts, along with SRP positions at the rate of 1 per every 3 districts and DRP positions at 1 per district. The total SRP and DRP counts are determined by the district empanelment ratio specified in the notification.
Q3What is the last date to apply for TNSRLM SRP, DRP, and BRP 2026?
A
The last date to submit applications - either by post or in person - to the Project Director, DMMU, TNSRLM, of the concerned district is 29 June 2026. Applications received after this date will not be entertained under any circumstances.
Q4What is the educational qualification required for each post?
A
For SRP and DRP, a Post Graduate degree in Social Work, Sociology, Rural Development, Women Studies, or any Social Science stream is required. For BRP, an Under Graduate degree in the same streams is sufficient. All posts require proficiency in Tamil and English.
Q5What is the age limit for TNSRLM SRP, DRP, and BRP?
A
For SRP and DRP, the age limit is 30 to 45 years as on 01.06.2026. For BRP, the age limit is 25 to 45 years as on 01.06.2026. The notification does not specify a general age relaxation category beyond the lower age threshold difference.
Q6How much is the monthly honorarium for each post?
A
The anticipated maximum monthly honorarium is ₹25,000 for SRPs, ₹18,000 for DRPs, and ₹10,500 for BRPs — all calculated for up to 25 days of engagement per month. Travel allowance is paid separately at ₹250/day (SRP), ₹120/day (DRP), and ₹60/day (BRP).
Q7What is the selection process for TNSRLM SRP and DRP posts?
A
SRP and DRP selection involves three stages: a Written Test (50 marks), a Focus Group Discussion (10 marks), and a Micro Teaching cum Interview (40 marks), for a total of 100 marks. Final selection is made on the basis of combined scores across all three stages.
Q8What is the selection process for TNSRLM BRP posts?
A
BRP selection has two stages totalling 100 marks: a Written Test with MCQs (50 marks) and a Micro Teaching cum Interview (50 marks). The interview component assesses communication skill, teaching skill, subject knowledge, computer knowledge, and training experience - 10 marks each.
Q9Is this recruitment open to male candidates?
A
No. All three posts - SRP, DRP, and BRP - are open to female candidates only. The notification states a preference for candidates who are SHG (Self Help Group) members, though this is preferred and not strictly mandatory.
Q10Which 15 districts are covered under TNSRLM PRI-CBO Phase II?
A
The 15 districts covered are: Ariyalur, Chengalpattu, Cuddalore, Kancheepuram, Krishnagiri, Madurai, Mayiladuthurai, Nagapattinam, Namakkal, Sivagangai, Tenkasi, Tiruvallur, Tiruvannamalai, Tiruvarur, and Villupuram.
