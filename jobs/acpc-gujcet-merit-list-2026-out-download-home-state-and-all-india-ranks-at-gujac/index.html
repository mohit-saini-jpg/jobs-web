What is the ACPC GUJCET Merit List 2026?

The ACPC GUJCET Merit List 2026 is the official ranking of candidates who have registered for degree engineering admissions in Gujarat. It is published by ACPC in two stages a provisional list for candidate review and a final list after grievance resolution and is the basis for seat allotment across more than 78,000 engineering seats in Gujarat.

ACPC handles counselling for over 141 institutes across Gujarat, including government engineering colleges, grant-in-aid institutions, DA-IICT, Nirma University, PDEU, and more than 100 self-financing colleges affiliated mainly with Gujarat Technological University (GTU). There are 16 government engineering colleges and 4 grant-in-aid colleges participating in the process.

How to Download ACPC GUJCET Merit List 2026 - Step-by-Step

The GUJCET 2026 merit list is accessible individually through the student login on the official ACPC portal. Follow these steps to check and download your rank:

  • Open a browser and go to the official ACPC admission portal: gujacpc.admissions.nic.in
  • On the homepage, look for the link labelled "Provisional Merit List 2026 - Degree Engineering" in the latest announcements or notifications section.
  • Click the link to open the student login page.
  • Enter your ACPC Application Number and Date of Birth in the respective fields.
  • Click Submit or Login.
  • Your merit list entry will load on screen showing your rank, category rank, score details, and other information.
  • Save or take a screenshot of the page for your records. If the portal is slow due to high traffic, wait a few minutes and try again.

Merit Formula for Home State Seats

ACPC uses a composite weighted formula to prepare the home state merit list. Understanding this formula is critical because it determines how much your board marks and GUJCET score each contribute to your final rank.

Merit Marks = 60% weightage on Class 12 PCM Theory Percentile + 40% weightage on GUJCET 2026 Percentile

This means Class 12 board marks carry more weight than your GUJCET exam score. A student with strong board performance but an average GUJCET score can still secure a better rank than a student with a high GUJCET score but weaker board marks.

Merit for All India Quota Seats

For candidates who applied with their JEE Main rank, a separate All India merit list is prepared. The 5% All India Quota (AIQ) seats in government and grant-in-aid institutions are filled purely on the basis of JEE Main 2026 All India Rank (AIR).

Seat Distribution Based on Merit List

  • Home State Merit Rank is used for 95% of sanctioned intake seats in government and grant-in-aid institutions, and for 50% of seats in unaided (self-financed) institutions.
  • All India Rank is used for 5% of sanctioned intake seats in government and grant-in-aid institutions.

Provisional vs Final GUJCET Merit List 2026 - Key Difference

Many students are confused about the difference between the two lists. Here is a clear explanation:

Provisional Merit List (Released June 12, 2026): The provisional merit list is the initial ranking published for candidate review. It is a review checkpoint only no seat is allocated at this stage. Candidates should log in and carefully verify their name, category, Class 12 marks, and GUJCET details. If any discrepancy is found (incorrect board marks, wrong category such as SC/ST/SEBC, or incorrect EWS status), candidates must raise a grievance within the announced window.

Final Merit List (Expected June 17, 2026): After ACPC processes all grievances and corrections, the final merit list is published. Only students who appear in the final merit list can participate in counselling and actual seat allotment rounds. No corrections are accepted after the grievance window closes.

How to Raise an Objection Against the GUJCET Merit List 2026

If you find an error in your provisional merit list entry such as incorrect board marks, wrong category, or incorrect EWS status follow these steps:

  • Log in to the official ACPC portal at gujacpc.admissions.nic.in
  • Open the Candidate Activity Board and check your provisional merit details.
  • Identify the discrepancy in your name, category, Class 12 marks, or GUJCET details.
  • Submit a correction request through the online grievance portal.
  • Upload supporting documents if required.
  • Submit your objection before the announced deadline.
  • Keep checking the portal for updates on your correction request.

No corrections are accepted once the grievance window closes. Candidates are strongly advised to verify all details immediately after the provisional list is published.

GUJCET 2026 ACPC Counselling - What Happens After the Merit List?

The GUJCET 2026 counselling process is fully online. Candidates do not need to visit any centre for document submission or verification. The entire process from merit list to seat allotment is conducted through gujacpc.admissions.nic.in.
