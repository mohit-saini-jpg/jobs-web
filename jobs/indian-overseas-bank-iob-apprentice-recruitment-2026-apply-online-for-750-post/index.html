IOB Apprentice Recruitment 2026-27 Eligibility Criteria

Candidates applying for IOB Apprentice Recruitment 2026-27 must be Citizens of India. Merely applying, being shortlisted, or appearing for document verification and personal interaction does not guarantee an offer of engagement in the Bank. Candidates must hold a Degree (Graduation) in any discipline from a University recognized by the Government of India, or an equivalent qualification recognized by the Central Government. The result must be declared on or before 31.07.2026. Candidates registered under the National Apprenticeship Training Scheme (NATS) must have had their graduation result declared between 01.03.2022 and 31.07.2026, both dates inclusive, and must produce mark sheets and provisional or degree certificates when required. IOB Apprentice Selection Process 2026-27

Selection for IOB Apprentice Recruitment 2026-27 will be based on an online examination, a test of the local language wherever applicable, and personal interaction if decided by the Bank. Satisfying the eligibility criteria alone does not guarantee a call for the online examination. The total duration of the examination is 90 minutes. The examination will be conducted online using the candidate's own front camera-enabled desktop, laptop, tablet, or smartphone, and candidates must display the same ID proof uploaded on the apprenticeship portal. Candidates must score a minimum aggregate percentage as decided by the Bank, with a 5 percent relaxation available for SC/ST/OBC/PwBD candidates. There is no minimum qualifying mark for individual subjects. Important Instructions for IOB Apprentice Recruitment 2026-27

Candidates should not have undergone apprenticeship earlier with IOB or any other organization, or been terminated from apprenticeship training due to their own fault. Candidates with one year or more of training or job experience after completing the educational qualification are not eligible. Apprentices are not treated as "employees" of the Bank and are not entitled to employee benefits. Apprentices are entitled to one day of casual leave per month, totaling 12 days per year, with a maximum of 4 days availed at a time.   • 6Provide the NATS/NAPS enrolment number while filling the online application form.
  • 7Apply for engagement from only one State/UT, preferably the home State/UT.
  • 8Pay the requisite application fee/intimation charges online between 07.07.2026 and 22.07.2026.
  • 9No other mode of application or payment will be accepted. Applications once submitted cannot be withdrawn, and fees once paid will not be refunded under any circumstances.
