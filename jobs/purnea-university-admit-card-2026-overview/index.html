Purnea University Admit Card 2026 - Overview

Purnea University, Purnia has issued the admit cards for undergraduate semester examinations for the current exam session. These hall tickets are compulsory for entry to the examination hall and must be downloaded and printed by every eligible student. The admit card contains complete details of the exam centre, subject-wise schedule and important instructions.

The university has also released updated exam date sheets for different semesters, including CBCS 3rd semester exams, so students should verify their exact paper dates along with the hall ticket. This article explains the exam schedule, download process, eligibility, and key instructions in a detailed yet simple manner.

Purnea University Exam Date Sheet 2026

Purnea University has published detailed exam date sheets for various UG semesters. These include end‑semester examinations for first semester students as well as CBCS 3rd semester students.

UG First Semester Exam Schedule (BA, BSc, BCom)

For the 2025-29 batch, first semester UG exams are scheduled as per the official timetable issued by the examination department. Papers are held on multiple days, usually in two shifts (morning and afternoon), covering core, compulsory and elective subjects.

Students should:

  • Check the date-wise and subject-wise schedule from the official PDF.
  • Note whether their paper falls in the first sitting (morning) or second sitting (afternoon).
  • Cross‑check the subject code printed on the date sheet with the one on their admit card.

BA/BSc/BCom CBCS 3rd Semester Date Sheet (December 2025 Session)

For CBCS 3rd semester (session 2024-28), the date sheet specifies:

  • Exam dates from late April to early May 2026.
  • Two sittings each day - 10:00 am to 1:00 pm and 2:00 pm to 5:00 pm.
  • Different days assigned for MJC, MIC, MDC, AEC and SEC papers across Science, Commerce and Arts streams.

Because the schedule is dense and subject‑specific, it is best practice to keep a printed copy of the date sheet and mark your own subjects clearly.

Exams & Courses Covered Under Purnea University Admit Card 2026

The current admit card notification covers multiple UG programmes under Purnea University. These include:

  • BA (Bachelor of Arts) - all streams and subject combinations notified by the university.
  • BSc (Bachelor of Science) - including different subject groups in science.
  • BCom (Bachelor of Commerce) - honours and general where applicable.

The hall tickets are issued semester‑wise, so the same student might receive different admit cards for different semesters if appearing in more than one examination block. Make sure you are downloading the admit card for the correct course, semester and session.

Step‑by‑Step Download Process

  • Visit the official website Open your browser and go to the Purnea University website: purneau.ac.in.
  • Open the Examination / Admit Card section On the homepage, look for links such as “Examination”, “Student Corner”, “Admit Card” or “UG End Semester Admit Card”.
  • Select the correct admit card link Click on the link related to “UG Semester Admit Card 2026” or the specific semester you are appearing in (e.g., “UG 1st Semester Admit Card”, “CBCS 3rd Semester Admit Card”).
  • Proceed to Samarth login (if redirected) You may be redirected to the Samarth portal (purneauniversityadm.samarth.edu.in). On that page, log in using your registered credentials such as User ID / registration number and password.
  • Choose course and semester After login, select your course (BA/BSc/BCom), semester, and session from the dropdown if required.
  • Generate and verify admit card Click on “Search”, “Submit” or “Download Admit Card”. Your hall ticket will appear on the screen. Check all details like name, course, exam centre and subject list.
  • Download and print Download the admit card as a PDF and take a neat printout on A4 paper. Keep multiple copies for safety and ensure the print is clear and readable.

If your admit card is not showing despite filling the exam form, contact your college examination department immediately.

Details Mentioned on Purnea University Admit Card 2026

Once you download the hall ticket, verify that all the information is correct. The admit card generally contains:

  • Full name of the student
  • Photograph and signature (if provided)
  • Father’s / mother’s name
  • Roll number and registration number
  • Course name (BA/BSc/BCom) and semester
  • Academic session / batch (for example, 2025-29 for first semester)
  • List of papers with subject codes
  • Exam dates, day, sitting (1st/2nd) and timing
  • Name and address of the exam centre
  • Important instructions and rules to be followed during the exam

Any mismatch, spelling error, wrong subject or incorrect category should be reported to the college or university exam section at the earliest so that corrections can be made before the exam.

Who Can Download Purnea University Admit Card 2026?

Not every student will find an admit card in the system; only those who meet certain conditions are eligible.

You are normally eligible to download the admit card if:

  • You are a registered student of Purnea University in a UG course like BA, BSc or BCom.
  • You have filled the respective semester / end‑semester exam form within the prescribed dates.
  • Your exam form and fee payment have been accepted by your college and forwarded to the university.
  • There is no pending issue regarding your registration, attendance or eligibility.

Students whose exam forms are incomplete, rejected or pending fee verification may not see their admit cards; they should immediately check with the college office.

Important Instructions for Purnea University UG Exams 2026

This section answers common “what to do / what not to do” doubts before the exam.

  • Carry mandatory documents Always carry a printed admit card and a valid photo ID (Aadhaar card, college ID, etc.) to the exam centre. Without these, entry is likely to be denied.
  • Report early to the exam centre Reach the centre at least 30-45 minutes before the reporting time mentioned on the admit card to complete verification and seating formalities.
  • Follow the date sheet carefully Check your exam date sheet every day, especially if there are two sittings, so you don’t confuse morning and afternoon papers.
  • Avoid prohibited items Do not bring mobile phones, smart watches, Bluetooth devices, calculators (if not allowed) or any electronic gadgets into the exam hall. These can lead to disqualification.
  • Comply with exam rules Listen carefully to invigilators, read instructions on the question paper and answer booklet, and fill in your roll number and other details correctly.
  • Use the opportunity wisely End‑semester exams are important for your CGPA and progression to the next semester. Prepare a realistic study schedule aligned with your date sheet and use the time between papers for revision.
