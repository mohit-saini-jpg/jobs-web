How to Apply

  • 1Date of Birth as per Matriculation / Secondary School Leaving Certificate
  • 2Educational qualification details (Class 12 pass certificate or appearing status)
  • 3Aadhaar Card (strongly advised as primary ID; other options: Voter Card/EPIC, PAN Card, Passport, Driving License, School Photo ID, or any Photo ID issued by State/Central Government)
  • 4Passport-size photograph (uploaded) and live photograph captured via webcam
  • 5Signature: signed three times (one below the other) on plain white paper with black ink, scanned and uploaded
  • 6URN (Universal Registration Number) - generate once, valid for all UPSC exams for lifetime
  • 7Fee payment: Rs. 100/- for General/OBC/EWS Male via Net Banking / Debit / Credit / RuPay / UPI; NIL for Female, SC/ST, and Wards of JCOs/NCOs/ORs studying in Military/Sainik Schools
  • 8Service preferences (1 to 4) to be indicated carefully in the Examination Specific Module
  • 9Original Matriculation / Secondary School Examination Certificate showing date of birth
  • 10Original 10+2 pass certificate or marks sheet (for candidates who have passed); bonafide certificate from school/college (for appearing candidates)
  • 11Fee exemption certificate from Principal (for Wards of JCOs/NCOs/ORs studying in Military/Sainik Schools)
  • 12NOC from Commanding Officer (for Armed Forces / Naval Sailors candidates)
  • 13Any other documents as required by the respective Services HQ
  • 14Applications once submitted cannot be withdrawn. No correction/alteration/modification in any field of the application is allowed after submission under any circumstances.
  • 15The URN Profile provides a one-time edit facility; changes to the URN profile do not reflect in applications already submitted and apply only to future applications.
  • 16Candidates must indicate service preferences carefully (1 to 4) in the Examination Specific Module; no addition/alteration is entertained after submission. It is mandatory to exercise at least one preference.
  • 17All candidates who qualify the written examination must register on www.joinindianarmy.nic.in with the same email ID as provided to UPSC to receive SSB centre allotment. Those already registered should not register again.
  • 18e-Admit Cards are available only on the Commission's website for download. No admit card is sent by post or email. Candidates must enter the venue at least 30 minutes before commencement; entry is closed 30 minutes prior.
  • 19Face authentication is mandatory at the examination venue for all candidates.
  • 20Use Black Ball Pen only for all writing/marking in OMR sheets. Any omission/mistake in encoding Roll Number or Test Booklet Series Code will render the answer sheet liable for rejection.
  • 21Negative marking applies: 1/3rd of marks deducted for each wrong answer. Marking more than one answer is also treated as wrong. Questions left blank carry no penalty.
  • 22Mobile phones, smart/digital watches, pagers, Bluetooth devices, calculators, and any electronic equipment are strictly banned inside the examination premises even in switched-off mode. Infringement entails disciplinary action including permanent/specified-period ban from UPSC examinations and possible prosecution under the Public Examinations (Prevention of Unfair Means) Act, 2024.
  • 23Candidates must undertake not to marry until they complete their full training. Marriage during training leads to discharge and recovery of all Government expenditure.
  • 24Candidates appear before SSB at their own risk and are not entitled to compensation for any injury sustained during SSB tests. Parents/guardians must sign a certificate to this effect.
  • 25Candidates who fail to report to the Academy within 7 days of the specified reporting date without prior written intimation will have their candidature liable for cancellation.
  • 26Candidates previously removed from NDA/INAC for lack of officer-like qualities or on disciplinary grounds are not eligible. Those withdrawn on medical grounds or who left voluntarily remain eligible, provided they satisfy medical and other prescribed conditions.
  • 27Representations on question papers and provisional answer keys can be submitted only through the Commission's website within 5 days of the examination (Day 3 to Day 7 up to 6:00 PM). No representation by email/post/hand or any other mode is accepted.
  • 28Government strives to have a workforce that reflects gender balance; women candidates are encouraged to apply.
  • 29All revisions/corrigendum (if any) will be hosted on the UPSC website only. In case of any dispute between English and Hindi versions of the notification, the English version will prevail.
