What Is HSTES LEET 2026?

HSTES LEET (Haryana State Technical Education Society Lateral Entry Entrance Test) is an annual Online Computer-Based Test (OCET) conducted by HSTES for admissions into the 2nd year (3rd Semester) of B.Tech/B.E. programmes under the Lateral Entry Scheme in Haryana engineering colleges. It is the primary gateway for diploma holders and eligible B.Sc. graduates who wish to skip the first year and directly enter the second year of a three-year B.Tech lateral course.

How to Download HSTES LEET 2026 Admit Card

Downloading the Haryana LEET 2026 hall ticket is a straightforward process done entirely online. No offline or alternative mode has been provided by HSTES for admit card distribution. Follow the steps below to access and save your hall ticket before the exam date.

  • Visit the official website: techadmissionshry.gov.in
  • Click on the "Download Admit Card" link on the homepage.
  • Enter your Application Number / User ID and Password as registered during the application process.
  • Click Submit to log in.
  • Your HSTES LEET 2026 Admit Card will appear on the screen.
  • Verify all details carefully (name, roll number, exam centre, date, and timings).
  • Download the admit card PDF and take at least two printed copies for safekeeping.

Details to Verify on Your HSTES LEET 2026 Hall Ticket

Once you download the Haryana LEET admit card, it is essential to cross-check every piece of information printed on it. Any error must be reported to HSTES immediately, as discrepancies at the exam centre can lead to denial of entry.

  • Candidate's full name
  • Roll Number / Application Number
  • Allotted exam centre name and address
  • Exam date and reporting time
  • Exam shift
  • Candidate's photograph and signature
  • Category and eligibility details

What to Carry to the HSTES LEET 2026 Exam Centre

Candidates must come prepared with the right documents to avoid being turned away at the examination hall. Along with the admit card, at least one original and valid government-issued photo ID is mandatory.

  • Printed copy of the HSTES LEET 2026 Admit Card (colour or black-and-white printout on A4 paper)
  • One original valid photo ID proof - Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport, PAN Card, or Driving Licence

Important: Candidates will not be permitted to enter the examination hall without both documents.

HSTES LEET 2026 Exam Pattern

Understanding the exam structure in advance helps candidates allocate their preparation time efficiently. The HSTES LEET 2026 is conducted as an online CBT with 90 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) to be completed in 90 minutes, with no negative marking.

HSTES LEET 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Before appearing for the exam, candidates should have already verified their eligibility during the application stage. The two qualifying pathways accepted by HSTES are:

  • A Diploma in Engineering/Technology with a minimum of 45% marks (40% for reserved categories)
  • A B.Sc. degree with a minimum of 45% marks (40% for reserved categories), provided the candidate passed Class 12 with Mathematics as a subject

For Kashmiri Migrants, an additional relaxation of 10% is provided in the minimum marks requirement.

B.Sc. graduates must also have passed Engineering Graphics/Engineering Drawing and Engineering Mechanics of the first year engineering programme, along with second year subjects.

HSTES LEET 2026 Result and Counselling Process

After the examination, HSTES will prepare an inter-se merit list based on each candidate's OCET 2026 score. This section explains what happens after the exam and how the seat allotment process works.

  • Result Date: July 8, 2026
  • The result will be published as a rank-wise merit list on the official HSTES portal.
  • Candidates appearing on the merit list will be eligible for the online counselling process.
  • Counselling begins: July 9, 2026
  • HSTES will conduct two rounds of online off-campus counselling for seat allotment.
  • Counselling fee can be paid online (debit card, credit card, net banking) or offline via e-challan at designated branches of Axis Bank and PNB.

Tie-Breaking Criteria for Equal Marks

If two or more candidates score equal marks in OCET 2026, the inter-se merit rank is decided in the following order:

  • Higher marks in Mathematics in OCET
  • Higher marks in Physics in OCET
  • Higher aggregate in Engineering subjects in OCET
  • Senior in age (date of birth)
  • Alphabetical order of candidate's name, then father's name, then mother's name

Counselling Categories

Both rounds of counselling are open to candidates from all reservation categories, including General, SC, BC-A, BC-B, ESM (Ex-Servicemen), FF (Freedom Fighter), PH (Persons with Disabilities), KM (Kashmiri Migrant), HOGC, TFW (Tuition Fee Waiver), and RoHC (Rest of Haryana Category), with 25% horizontal reservation for female candidates.

Tips for Exam Day

A few simple precautions on the day of the exam can make a significant difference. These guidelines will help candidates report smoothly and avoid last-minute stress.

  • Report early - arrive at the exam centre well before the reporting time mentioned on your admit card.
  • Do not carry mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators, or any electronic devices inside the exam hall.
  • Since there is no negative marking, attempt every single question - guessing on unknown questions carries no penalty.
  • Practice on a computer interface before the exam, as the LEET is a computer-based test (OCET).
