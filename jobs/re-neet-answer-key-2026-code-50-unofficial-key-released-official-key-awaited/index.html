Why Was the Re NEET UG 2026 Exam Conducted?

This section explains the background behind the Re NEET 2026 examination and why candidates had to appear for a fresh exam.

The original NEET UG 2026 examination was held on May 3, 2026, for approximately 22.79 lakh MBBS and BDS aspirants. However, NTA subsequently cancelled that exam after a confirmed paper leak traced to Sikar, Rajasthan. The CBI initiated an investigation and arrested more than five individuals involved in the leak. As a result, NTA rescheduled the examination for June 21, 2026, and all candidates who had appeared for the May 3 exam were issued fresh admit cards without any additional registration fee.

Re NEET UG 2026 Exam Day Overview

Here is a quick snapshot of how the Re NEET 2026 exam was conducted on June 21, 2026.

NTA conducted the Re NEET UG 2026 examination in offline pen-and-paper mode across 5,440 exam centres in 551 cities across India and 14 cities abroad. The exam was held in a single shift from 2:00 PM to 5:15 PM IST. The question paper was distributed in four codes Code 50, Code 60, Code 70, and Code 80. The syllabus and difficulty level remain identical across all four codes; only the arrangement of questions differs.

Based on initial student reactions and expert analysis, the Re NEET 2026 paper was rated moderate overall. Biology offered the strongest scoring opportunities and was largely NCERT-aligned. Chemistry was balanced but featured several Physical Chemistry questions involving lengthy calculations. Physics emerged as the most challenging section, acting as the key differentiator among candidates.

Re NEET 2026 Official Answer Key - NTA Release Date and Process

This section covers what candidates need to know about the upcoming official NTA provisional answer key and the objection process.

The official Re NEET 2026 Provisional Answer Key by NTA is expected to be released on neet.nta.nic.in approximately 3 to 5 days after the June 21 exam that is, around June 24 to June 26, 2026. Along with the provisional answer key, NTA will also release candidates' OMR response sheets for self-verification.

NTA Answer Key Release Process:

  • Step 1 - Provisional Answer Key Released: NTA publishes the provisional answer key with OMR sheets on neet.nta.nic.in.
  • Step 2 - Objection Window Opens: Candidates can challenge any answer in the provisional key by paying the prescribed fee per question.
  • Step 3 - Review of Objections: NTA subject experts review all valid challenges.
  • Step 4 - Final Answer Key Published: After reviewing objections, NTA releases the final answer key, which cannot be challenged.
  • Step 5 - Result Declared: The Re NEET 2026 result and ranks are prepared and declared based on the final answer key.
