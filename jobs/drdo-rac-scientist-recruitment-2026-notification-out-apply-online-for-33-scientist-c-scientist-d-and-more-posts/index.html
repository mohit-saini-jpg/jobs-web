Selection Process

  • Screening of received applications will be carried out at multiple stages. Final selection is purely based on merit of marks scored in the Final Personal Interview.

  • Stage 1 – Administrative Screening: Verification of identity

  • age

  • essential & higher qualifications

  • length of suitable experience and CCA Declaration (wherever applicable).

  • Stage 2 – Technical Screening (Level-1): For all Administratively Eligible candidates - Technical Screening by holding a Meeting of Technical Screening Committee (an independent body including Technical Experts from Industry/Academia/DRDO other than RAC) using Bio-data based Technical Screening at Level-1 by: (a) Relevance of suitable experience w.r.t. advertised qualifications; (b) Relevance of Desirable/Higher Qualification; (c) Any other criteria suggested by Technical Screening Committee. Predefined Ratio for Preliminary Online Interview: Up to 24 candidates for 1 post; up to 48 candidates for 2 posts.

  • Stage 3 – Technical Screening (Level-2 / Preliminary Online Interview

  • if required): If number shortlisted for Final Personal Interview is large

  • further shortlisting via Preliminary Online Interview of short duration (10–15 minutes). Predefined Ratio for Final Personal Interview: Up to 12 candidates for 1 post; up to 24 candidates for 2 posts.

  • Stage 4 – Final Personal Interview (in-person at RAC

  • Delhi): Final selection is purely on merit of marks scored. Candidates must score a minimum of 75% marks (75 out of 100) in the Final Personal Interview for consideration for selection. The Final Personal Interview will be held in-person only before the Selection Board at RAC

  • Delhi on the scheduled date - no online/virtual interview will be considered.

  • Note: If administrative screening does not result in minimum 6 eligible candidates against one vacancy

  • the subsequent selection process for that vacancy may be cancelled. If technical screening does not result in minimum 6 candidates per vacancy for Final Personal Interview

  • the number of vacancies will be reduced accordingly.

Physical Eligibility

Details

The competition level for DRDO RAC Scientist Recruitment 2026 is expected to be very high, particularly for Scientist C posts in Electronics & Communication Engineering, Computer Science & Engineering, and Mechanical Engineering - disciplines with a large pool of eligible candidates and growing interest in DRDO's prestigious research environment. DRDO provides one of the best career growth structures for scientists under the Flexible Complimenting Scheme (FCS), making it a highly sought-after employer. | The multi-stage shortlisting process (Administrative → Technical Level-1 → Level-2/Preliminary Online Interview → Final Personal Interview in Delhi) is rigorous. Only up to 12 candidates per vacancy reach the Final Personal Interview, and even among those, a minimum of 75% marks in the interview is mandatory for selection. For Scientist D and E posts requiring 7–11 years of experience, the competition narrows to experienced professionals, but the specificity of required experience (e.g., Optics/Photonics, Radiation Physics, Chemical Engineering for underwater applications) naturally limits the eligible pool, making those posts relatively less competitive. Niche posts like Oceanography, Pharmacology, and Remote Sensing will see very limited candidates, improving selection chances for well-qualified applicants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is the last date to apply?

The online application window closes on 19 June 2026 at 1600 hrs (IST).

Q2. How do I apply?

Apply online only via the RAC website: https://rac.gov.in. Applications by any other mode will not be accepted.

Q3. Which posts are advertised?

Direct recruitment for Scientist E (Level 13), Scientist D (Level 12) and Scientist C (Level 11) in DRDS cadre and encadered posts under the Lateral Recruitment Scheme. See notification for discipline-wise item numbers.

Q4. What are the essential academic requirements?

First Division (60% or equivalent) in the prescribed degree: BE/B.Tech/First Division M.Sc. or equivalent as specified for each post. CGPA/CPI conversions must be supported by university rules (6.75/10 = 60%).

Q5. What is the required post-qualification experience?

Scientist E: 11 years; Scientist D: 7 years; Scientist C: 3 years — experience counted only after the essential qualification and as on 19 June 2026. Experience below Pay Level 10 (7th CPC) is not counted.

Q6. Is there an application fee?

Yes. General/OBC/EWS (male) candidates pay Rs. 100 online. SC/ST/Divyang/Women candidates are exempt.

Q7. What is the selection process?

Multi-stage: Administrative screening → Technical Screening (Level‑1) → Technical Screening Level‑2/Preliminary Online Interview (if needed) → Final Personal Interview (in-person at RAC, Delhi). Final selection is based on interview marks; minimum 75/100 required in the Final Personal Interview.

Q8. Can serving government/PSU employees apply?

Yes, but they must upload a signed CCA Declaration during online submission and produce NOC from Cadre Controlling Authority (CCA) at the Final Personal Interview stage.
