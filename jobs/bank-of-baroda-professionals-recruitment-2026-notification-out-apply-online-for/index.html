Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Application Fee Details

Bank of Baroda has set a two-tier, non-refundable application fee for this recruitment. Unreserved (UR), EWS, and OBC candidates must pay Rs 850 (inclusive of GST), plus applicable payment gateway charges, while SC, ST, PWD, Ex-servicemen/DESM, and Women candidates pay Rs 175 (inclusive of GST), plus payment gateway charges. The fee can be paid online using a Debit Card, Credit Card, Internet Banking, or UPI. It is payable irrespective of whether an online test is eventually conducted and irrespective of whether the candidate is shortlisted for an interview, and once paid, it will not be refunded under any circumstances. | In-Hand Salary Calculator 2026

Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Age Limit

Age DetailsFixed Term Contractual Basis
Details
  1. Eligibility for every post is assessed as on 01 June 2026, the cut-off date fixed by the bank, and depends on a candidate's age, educational qualification, and post-qualification work experience in banks, financial institutions, or NBFCs. Age limits rise with seniority - from 28-38 years for the entry-level AVP I posts up to 36-52 years for the Vice President posts - and the maximum age shown applies to General category candidates
  2. Age relaxation is available to specific categories, though the bank has specified that it applies only to posts where reservation roster points exist. Since OBC is the only category with roster points in this recruitment (in the AVP II-SPSM, AVP II-Relationship Manager, and AVP I-PSM posts), the 3-year OBC relaxation is the one most likely to apply in practice. There is also no reservation for Ex-servicemen in the Officers' Cadre for this recruitment
  3. Every post requires at least a graduation in any discipline, mostly paired with a postgraduate management or finance qualification, a chartered accountancy, or an equivalent professional certification. Experience requirements range from a minimum of 5 years for the AVP I grade up to a minimum of 15 years for the Vice President posts, with a defined number of those years required specifically in the relevant function
  4. A few additional conditions apply across all posts. Educational qualifications must be from an institution recognised by the Government of India, a government body, or the AICTE. Experience gained in the clerical cadre is not counted towards eligibility, and any single stint of post-qualification work experience shorter than six months (as on the cut-off date) will also not be counted. Only one application is allowed per candidate; if multiple applications are submitted, only the latest valid one will be retained and the fee paid for any other registration will be forfeited. Bank of Baroda has also stated that only candidates willing to serve anywhere in India should apply, since posting will depend entirely on the bank's requirement at any of its offices or branches across the country
  5. On citizenship, a candidate must be an Indian citizen, or a subject of Nepal or Bhutan, or a Tibetan refugee who came to India before 1 January 1962 intending to settle permanently, or a person of Indian origin who migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, or specified East African countries with the same intention - though candidates in these latter categories must hold a certificate of eligibility issued by the Government of India
  6. Note: Eligible candidates should have the required post-qualification experience (e.g., 5+ years for AVP I or 15+ years for VP roles) in banks/NBFCs/financial institutions, be willing to work anywhere in India, and maintain a clean credit history (CIBIL 680+ with no defaults). Candidates should not apply if they don’t meet the age/experience criteria as on 01 June 2026, have only clerical or very short (<6 months) relevant experience, or are not open to posting anywhere in India

Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : Salary Details & Pay Scale

Details
  1. These are fixed-term contractual roles, and the tenure depends on the grade. For AVP I and AVP II, the initial contract runs for 5 years from the date of joining and can be renewed at the bank's discretion, subject to satisfactory performance, for one additional period of up to 5 years. For VP and DVP, the initial contract is for 3 years and can be extended in increments of 1 year each, provided the total engagement does not exceed 5 years. In every case, the engagement automatically ends at contract expiry unless renewed, and it cannot continue beyond the candidate reaching 60 years of age
  2. Remuneration is not fixed in the notification - the bank states it will be decided based on the selected candidate's qualifications, experience, overall suitability, last drawn salary, and the prevailing market benchmark for the position, and the place of posting will depend on the bank's requirement at any of its offices or branches in India
  3. A distinctive eligibility condition for this recruitment is credit history: candidates must have a CIBIL score of 680 or above at the time of joining, and the bank has explicitly stated that candidates who have defaulted on loan or credit card repayments, or against whom adverse reports exist with CIBIL or other credit-rating agencies, will be deemed ineligible for appointment - a check the bank can apply at any stage, including after joining

Bank of Baroda Professionals Recruitment 2026 : How to Apply Online Step-by-Step

  • 1Visit the Bank of Baroda website www.bankofbaroda.bank.in (or directly www.bankofbaroda.bank.in/Career.htm) and go to the Career section's "Current Opportunities" page.
  • 2Register using a valid personal email ID and contact number that you can keep active throughout the recruitment process, since all call letters and updates will be sent there.
  • 3Fill in the online application form carefully - no field can be edited once you click Submit - and ensure your name is spelt exactly as it appears on your certificates and mark sheets.
  • 4Upload your bio-data, scanned photograph (200×230 pixels, 20-50 KB), and signature (140×60 pixels, 10-20 KB) in JPG/JPEG format, along with PDF copies of your eligibility documents.
  • 5Pay the applicable application fee online using a Debit Card, Credit Card, Internet Banking, or UPI through the integrated payment gateway.
  • 6After successful payment, download and print the system-generated e-receipt and application form for your records; if a transaction fails, register again and make the payment afresh rather than assuming the first attempt succeeded.
  • 7Complete the process well before 06 July 2026, since the bank has advised against waiting until the last date to avoid login failures caused by heavy website traffic.
