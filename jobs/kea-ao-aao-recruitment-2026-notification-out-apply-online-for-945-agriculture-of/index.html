KEA AO AAO Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the difference between the KK Cadre and RPC Cadre in this recruitment?
A
The KK Cadre (Inland Source / ಒಳಮೂಲ) refers to posts filled through direct recruitment from the open market within the State cadre. The RPC Cadre (Rural Pay Commission / ಉಳಿಕೆ ಮೂಲ) refers to posts that were earlier unfilled in the Rural Pay Commission and are now being recruited through competitive examination. Both cadres have the same posts (Agriculture Officer and Assistant Agriculture Officer) and the same eligibility criteria, but different vacancy numbers and different exam dates (KK Cadre exam: 25.07.2026; RPC Cadre exam: 26.07.2026).
Q2Can I apply for both the KK Cadre and RPC Cadre?
A
Yes. Both are separate notifications with separate application numbers. Candidates who meet the eligibility criteria may apply for both cadres by submitting separate online applications and paying the fee separately for each notification. The exam dates are different, so appearing for both is possible.
Q3Can I apply for both Agriculture Officer and Assistant Agriculture Officer posts in a single application?
A
Yes. Within the Agriculture Department, candidates may apply for both the Agriculture Officer (Rs. 85 pay level) and the Assistant Agriculture Officer (Rs. 85 pay level) posts through a single application by paying the fee once. For each additional post under the same programme appearing in the same examination, Rs. 100/- per post must be paid as additional fee. Marks scored in the single examination will be automatically considered for both posts and the department will prepare separate final rank lists.
Q4Is B.Sc. Agriculture from a distance education university eligible?
A
No. The degree must be from a university / institute recognized by ICAR or AICTE. Degrees from institutions offering UGC / AICTE accredited distance education programmes or open university technical-level programmes will not be accepted. The degree must be a regular, full-time programme from a recognized institution.
Q5What is the minimum qualifying marks required in the written examination?
A
Candidates must score a minimum of 35 marks (out of 300) in the competitive written examination to qualify. Candidates who fail to secure this minimum cut-off will be disqualified from the selection process regardless of their overall rank.
Q6Is the Kannada Language Test mandatory for all candidates?
A
The Kannada Language Test is mandatory for candidates who have not studied Kannada as the first or second language up to SSLC level. Candidates who have studied in Kannada medium from Class 1 to Class 10, or who have passed SSLC / equivalent with Kannada as the first or second language, are exempted from the test. Those who must appear in the test must score a minimum of 50 marks out of 150 to be eligible for selection.
Q7Is there negative marking in the KPSC Agriculture Officer written examination?
A
Yes. This is an Offline OMR-based multiple-choice examination. Each wrong answer will attract a negative deduction of 0.75 marks. If a candidate shades an answer or voids a previously shaded answer after the 5-minute grace period following the final bell, 0.75 marks per such question will also be deducted. Candidates are advised to attempt questions carefully.
Q8What is the upper age limit for OBC (Category 2A/2B/3A/3B) candidates?
A
OBC candidates belonging to Category 2A, 2B, 3A, and 3B have an upper age limit of 43 years as on the last date of submission of application. This includes the one-time relaxation of 5 years already provided per Government Order dated 29.01.2026. No further general age relaxation is applicable beyond this for OBC candidates unless they fall under another specific relaxation category (e.g., Ex-Serviceman).
Q9Is Rural Candidate (Grameen) reservation available and what proof is required?
A
Yes. Rural reservation is available for candidates who have studied and passed from Class 1 to Class 10 in rural areas of Karnataka, as per Government Order No. 59 Shashana 2000 dated 06-01-2001. General merit candidates seeking Rural reservation must submit a Form-1 certificate from the Tahsildar, attested by the Head Master and Field Education Officer of the relevant school. SC / ST / OBC / PwD candidates seeking Rural reservation must submit a Form-2 certificate in the same manner. Both certificates must be submitted before the last date of application.
Q10Can candidates currently employed in a Government job apply for this recruitment?
A
Yes, candidates currently in Government service can apply. However, if selected, they must produce a No Objection Certificate (NOC) / Service Permission Certificate / Service Certificate from the employing recruitment authority before the date of appointment. Service details must be accurately entered in the online application. Failure to produce the NOC / service certificate will result in disqualification at the time of appointment. Candidates should not enter false service information as it will lead to cancellation of candidature.
