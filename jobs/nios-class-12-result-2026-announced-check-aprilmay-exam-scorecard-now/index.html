NIOS Class 12 Result 2026 - Overview

The National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) declared the Sr Secondary (Class 12) result for the April/May 2026 examination on June 4, 2026. This covers the public examination session that began on April 10 and ended May 6, 2026.

How to Check NIOS Class 12 Result 2026 Online

Checking your result is pretty straightforward. Just follow these steps:

  • Visit the official NIOS result portal at results.nios.ac.in
  • Click on the "Public Examination Result" section for 2026
  • Enter your enrollment number (this is your roll number)
  • Type the captcha code shown on the screen
  • Click "Submit"
  • Your scorecard will appear on the screen instantly
  • Download it and take a printout for future reference

Pro tip: Save a digital copy on your phone too. You'll need it for college admissions.

Alternative Ways to Get Your NIOS Class 12 Result

Sometimes the website gets crowded, or you just want a quicker option. Here are two alternatives:

1. Via DigiLocker

  • Go to digilocker.gov.in or download the DigiLocker app.
  • Create an account using your mobile number
  • Navigate to "HSC/SSC Mark Sheet"
  • Select NIOS Examination from the dropdown
  • Enter your registration number and year (2026)
  • Your NIOS Class 12 Result 2026 will appeardownload and save it

2. Via SMS

  • Type: NIOS12 space EnrollmentNumber
  • Example: NIOS12 130491254567
  • Send to: 5676750
  • You'll receive your result as a reply message

What's Inside Your NIOS Class 12 Scorecard?

Your marksheet isn't just a pass/fail stamp. It shows:

  • Subject-wise marks (theory + practical)
  • Total marks obtained
  • Qualifying status (Pass/Fail)
  • Grade (if applicable)
  • Enrollment number and student details.

Keep this safecolleges will ask for it during admission.

Important Notes for Students

Here are a few things you shouldn't miss:

  • Take at least two printouts of your scorecardone for admissions, one for backup
  • Verify your name, enrollment number, and subject details on the marksheet
  • If there's any error, contact NIOS immediately through their helpdesk
  • Don't share your enrollment number publiclyit's sensitive info
