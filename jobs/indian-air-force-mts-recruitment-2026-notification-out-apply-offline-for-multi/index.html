Eligibility Criteria - Who Can Apply

The general age requirement for the MTS post is 18 to 25 years , with the last date of receipt of application being the cut-off date for age calculation. Age Relaxation Available: Important: UR/OBC candidates applying against unreserved posts are not entitled to any age relaxation on account of experience. The post of MTS requires the following qualifications: Essential: Matriculation (Class 10 pass) or equivalent qualification from a recognized University or Board. Desirable: One year of experience as a Watchman, Lascar, Gestetner Operator, or Gardener from any recognized Organization or Institute. Meeting only the essential qualification does not guarantee a call for the written test. Applicants must be eligible Indian citizens as per the provisions applicable to Central Government recruitment.

Pay Scale and Benefits

One of the most important factors candidates consider before applying is the salary. Here is what the IAF MTS 2026 notification states about the pay structure. The MTS post carries a pay scale of Level-1 as per the 7th Central Pay Commission (CPC) Pay Matrix . This is the entry-level pay band for Group C civilian employees under the Government of India, and selected candidates will be entitled to all applicable central government allowances and benefits under relevant service rules. Note: Selected candidates will be subject to 'All India Service Liability', meaning they can be posted to any location across India as required by the Air Force. Selection Process - How Candidates Are Chosen

The IAF MTS recruitment follows a clearly defined selection process. Knowing each stage in advance helps candidates prepare effectively and avoid being eliminated at any step. All applications received will be examined for age limit compliance, minimum educational qualification, required documents, and certificates. Only eligible candidates will be shortlisted for the written test. Eligible shortlisted candidates will be called for a written test . The selection of candidates will be based purely on performance in the written test — no interview is conducted. Written Test Syllabus covers four areas: General Intelligence and Reasoning Numerical Aptitude General English General Awareness The question-cum-answer paper will be available in both English and Hindi.
