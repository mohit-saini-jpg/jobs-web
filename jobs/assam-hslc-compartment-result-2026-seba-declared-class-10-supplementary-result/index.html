Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026 - SEBA Declared Class 10 Supplementary Result

The Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026 has declared today, June 23, 2026, at 11:00 AM. The Board of Secondary Education, Assam (SEBA), now functioning under the Assam State School Education Board (ASSEB), will publish the supplementary result for Class 10 students who could not clear one or more subjects in the main HSLC examination held earlier this year. Once declared, the result is available online at sebaonline.org, where students can check their scores using their HSLC roll number and other admit card details.

How to Check Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026 Online

Many students are unsure of the exact process to check their compartment result. This section breaks down the process into simple, easy-to-follow steps.

Follow these steps to check your Assam HSLC (SEBA) Compartment Result 2026:

  • Visit the official website sebaonline.org.
  • Look for the link that reads "Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026" on the homepage.
  • Click on the link to open the result login window.
  • Enter your roll number and registration number from your SEBA admit card.
  • Enter the captcha code shown on the screen to verify your request.
  • Click submit to view your Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026 on the screen.
  • Download and take a printout of the result for future reference and school submission.

Note: Since a large number of students check the result at the same time, the website may slow down or become temporarily unresponsive. In such cases, students are advised to wait a few minutes and try again rather than refreshing repeatedly.

Who Was Eligible for the Assam HSLC Compartmental Exam 2026?

This section explains which students were eligible to appear for the compartmental examination, helping students understand the background of this result.

Students who failed in a maximum of three subjects and had obtained a minimum aggregate of 170 marks in the HSLC Examination 2026 were eligible to appear for the compartmental exam. Here are the key eligibility points:

  • Failed in up to 3 subjects in the main HSLC exam 2026
  • Secured a minimum aggregate of 170 marks overall
  • Applied for the compartmental exam through their respective school

Both theory and practical marks were considered for the compartmental exam, and Internal Assessment (IA) marks already secured were carried forward where applicable.

Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026: Passing Criteria

Understanding the passing requirements helps students correctly interpret their result once it is declared. This section outlines the minimum marks needed to clear the compartmental exam.

To qualify at the compartmental stage, candidates needed to score at least 30% marks in each subject they appeared for, with their total marks reaching 180 or above. Key points to note:

  • Minimum 30% marks required in each individual subject
  • Practical marks and internal assessment scores from the main HSLC 2026 exam are carried forward as-is and added to the compartmental theory score
  • The OMR system applies for objective sections in core subjects, and candidates must clear both the OMR and descriptive components separately to pass

What Happens After the Assam HSLC Compartment Result 2026 Is Declared?

This section explains the next steps for students depending on whether they clear the compartmental exam or not.

  • If you pass: Successful candidates will be issued a fresh mark sheet and certificate reflecting their updated scores, and they will be eligible for admission to Class 11 in the current academic year itself, without losing a year.
  • If you do not pass: Students unable to clear the compartmental exam will need to seek re-admission to their respective schools and reappear as regular candidates in the next HSLC examination.

Students who clear the compartment exam should collect their original certificate from their respective school once it is issued, as the digital result is typically provisional and meant for immediate reference and admission purposes.
