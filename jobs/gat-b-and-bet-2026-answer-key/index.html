How to calculate marks using the answer key

  • Open your downloaded answer key and recorded responses side by side.
  • For GAT B and BET, each question carries marks as per the pattern notified in the Information Bulletin (for example, 4 marks for each correct answer with either no negative marking or specified negative marking).
  • For every correct response, award yourself the full marks specified for that question.
  • If negative marking is applicable, deduct the specified marks for each incorrect answer according to the official scheme.
  • Total your marks across all questions to get your approximate score for GAT B or BET.
  • Treat this score as provisional; final scores will be based on the final answer key after resolution of all challenges.

GAT B and BET 2026 Exam Details and Eligibility (Brief)

This section captures evergreen queries like “What is GAT B?”, “What is BET?” and supports AI Overview context.

GAT B 2026 - exam pattern and eligibility

The Graduate Aptitude Test - Biotechnology (GAT B) is conducted by NTA for admission to DBT‑supported postgraduate programmes in Biotechnology and allied areas in participating institutions. The exam is a computer‑based test featuring multiple‑choice questions covering topics such as General Biotechnology, Life Sciences, and related subjects, with the detailed syllabus and pattern provided in the Information Bulletin.

Eligibility (in brief):

  • Candidates with a bachelor’s degree in Biotechnology or relevant life science disciplines, with minimum percentage requirements as specified by DBT and participating institutions, are eligible.
  • Final‑year students meeting the criteria may also apply, subject to conditions mentioned in the official bulletin.

BET 2026 - exam pattern and eligibility

The Biotechnology Eligibility Test (BET) is conducted for the award of DBT‑Junior Research Fellowship (DBT‑JRF) in Biotechnology and Life Sciences. BET 2026 is also a CBT‑based MCQ exam, designed to test advanced understanding of Biotechnology, with structured sections and a defined marking scheme notified by NTA/DBT.

Eligibility (in brief):

  • Candidates with a postgraduate degree in Biotechnology or relevant disciplines, fulfilling age and percentage conditions laid down by DBT, are eligible to appear for BET.
  • Detailed category‑wise and course‑wise eligibility is provided in the GAT B/BET 2026 Information Bulletin and should be strictly followed.

GAT B and BET 2026 Answer Key Challenge Process

How to raise objections to NTA answer key

NTA has opened an online window to allow candidates to challenge the provisional answer keys of both GAT B and BET 2026. Objections must be filed in online mode only, and challenges sent by email, post, or any other method will not be accepted.

Steps to challenge the answer key:

  • Visit exams.nta.nic.in/gat-bet and click on the “Challenge(s) regarding Answer Key” link for GAT B/BET 2026.
  • Log in using your Application Number and Password / Date of Birth.
  • Select the exam (GAT B or BET) and view the question IDs and option IDs as displayed by NTA.
  • Choose the question(s) you wish to challenge and select the alternate option you believe is correct.
  • Upload supporting documents or references in a single PDF file (within the size limit specified by NTA, such as 500 KB).
  • Submit your challenges and proceed to pay the non‑refundable processing fee of Rs 200 per question using debit card, credit card, net banking, or UPI.
  • Ensure you complete both submission and payment before the deadline (up to 11:50 PM on 24 May 2026).

Challenge window, fee, and final answer key

  • The answer key challenge facility for GAT B and BET 2026 is available from 23 May 2026 to 24 May 2026 (11:50 PM).
  • A fee of Rs 200 per question is applicable for every challenge filed, which is non‑refundable.
  • Challenges submitted without payment or through any medium other than the official online portal will not be considered.
  • If any challenge is found valid, NTA will revise the answer key accordingly, and the final answer key will be used for result preparation.
  • The decision of NTA regarding the challenges will be final, and no further correspondence will be entertained.

GAT B and BET 2026 Answer Key - Important Instructions

This section helps users avoid mistakes and aligns with “What should I do after answer key?” queries.

  • Always verify that you are viewing the correct exam (GAT B or BET) and the correct question paper as per your centre and session.
  • Use only the official NTA portals (exams.nta.nic.in/gat-bet and the linked Examination Services login) to download answer keys and response sheets.
  • Carefully read the marking scheme in the Information Bulletin before calculating your score, especially regarding negative marking, if any.
  • File challenges only if you are confident and have strong academic/reference support; remember that the challenge fee is non‑refundable.
  • Keep a copy of your downloaded answer key, response sheet, and payment receipt for challenge (if any) until the declaration of results and completion of the selection process.
