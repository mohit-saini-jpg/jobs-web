The Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) issues a verification slip to candidates who have completed document verification for the Diploma Common Entrance Test (DCET) 2026. What Is the DCET Verification Slip?

The verification slip is a document generated after your DCET application details and eligibility documents are checked by KEA. It typically confirms:

  • Your name, application number, and rank as recorded by KEA
  • Category and reservation claims that have been verified
  • Confirmation that you are cleared to proceed to option entry (choice filling) in counselling

Without this slip, candidates may not be able to proceed to the option entry stage of DCET counselling.

How Document Verification Works for DCET 2026

Reporting on DCET 2026 counselling describes two elements to this stage:

  • Document verification: candidates report to KEA-designated help centres for physical verification of original documents, or complete verification online where KEA permits it.
  • Verification slip and secret key: once verification is complete, candidates receive an acknowledgement in the form of the verification slip, along with a secret key used to log in for online option entry.

Some reports describe this as an online download from the candidate dashboard, similar to the process KEA uses for KCET. Since the two modes are described differently across sources, and this article could not independently verify which mode applies for your specific candidate category, please check your KEA login/dashboard or the official DCET notification for the mode that applies to you.

How to Download the Karnataka DCET 2026 Verification Slip

  • Go to the official KEA portal: cetonline.karnataka.gov.in (DCET candidates may also see a dedicated link under cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea or cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/dcet).
  • Locate the DCET 2026 verification slip / document verification link on the homepage.
  • Log in using your application number, date of birth, and password (or the secret key issued after in-person verification, if applicable).
  • Once logged in, the verification slip should display on screen.
  • Check every field carefully name, category, rank, and document status.
  • Download and save the slip, and keep a printed copy for counselling and reporting at the allotted college.

Documents Typically Required for DCET Verification

  • DCET 2026 admit card and rank card/result
  • Diploma marks card / provisional certificate (or equivalent for final-year students awaiting results)
  • Category and caste certificate, if claiming reservation
  • Income certificate, if applicable to your category
  • Domicile / study certificate as required by KEA
  • Valid photo ID

The exact document checklist for DCET 2026 should be confirmed from your KEA information bulletin, since requirements can vary by category (general, reserved, lateral entry, working professional).

What Happens After the Verification Slip

Based on current DCET 2026 counselling reporting, the sequence after verification is expected to be:

  • Option entry / choice filling for preferred colleges and courses
  • A mock seat allotment round, where candidates can add, delete, or modify their choices
  • Final seat allotment result, expected within about two days of the mock allotment result
  • Fee payment and reporting to the allotted college

Important Instructions

  • Use only the official KEA portal (cetonline.karnataka.gov.in) to download your verification slip; there is no alternate or third-party website for this.
  • Keep your application number, date of birth, and password/secret key ready before logging in.
  • If the portal is slow or not loading, this is commonly reported during high-traffic periods; try again after some time, or during early morning/late evening.
  • Do not proceed to option entry without reviewing your verification slip for accuracy.
  • Retain a printed copy of the verification slip for use during college reporting.
