SSC ASO Exam Pattern 2026 – Scheme of Examination

The SSC Assistant Section Officer Exam 2026 is conducted in two parts. Part-I is a Computer Based Examination (CBE) carrying 300 marks, and Part-II involves Evaluation of Service Records (APARs) carrying 100 marks. The final merit is prepared on the combined aggregate of both parts. There is no separate qualifying exam - this is a competitive examination. There is a negative marking of 0.25 marks (25%) for each wrong answer in both Paper-I and Paper-II. Candidates must attempt the examination only in the language (English or Hindi) opted in the application form. There will be no question paper in Braille. One hundred marks are reserved for evaluation of Annual Performance Appraisal Reports (APARs). Candidates shortlisted based on their Part-I aggregate performance will proceed to Part-II. SSC ASO Selection Process 2026

The SSC ASO selection process 2026 is a two-stage merit-based system. Candidates must clear the Computer Based Examination before their service records are evaluated. Final selection is strictly based on combined merit - no candidate has a right to selection merely by passing the exam. Stage 1 – Computer Based Examination (Part-I): Paper-I and Paper-II, each of 150 marks. The Commission may fix minimum qualifying marks individually for each paper. Stage 2 – APAR Evaluation (Part-II): Only candidates who qualify Part-I are eligible. Service records (APARs) are evaluated for 100 marks. Final Merit: Aggregate of Part-I (300 marks) + Part-II (100 marks) = 400 marks total. Names of finally selected candidates will be arranged in order of merit separately for each cadre. The exam has two parts. Part-I is a Computer Based Examination with two objective-type papers of 150 marks each (Paper-I and Paper-II), totalling 300 marks, with a duration of 3 hours each. Part-II is an evaluation of service records (APARs) for 100 marks. Total marks for final selection: 400.
