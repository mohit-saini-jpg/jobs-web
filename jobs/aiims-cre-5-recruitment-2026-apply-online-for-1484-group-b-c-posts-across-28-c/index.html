AIIMS CRE-5 Recruitment 2026 - Exam Pattern and Syllabus

The CRE-5 Computer Based Test is a structured, time-bound examination designed to assess both domain knowledge and general aptitude. Each section is sequential and time-locked - once a section closes, candidates cannot return to it. Candidates must attempt all possible questions within the active section's time window. Posts where the essential qualification is 10th/12th will be examined in bilingual (English and Hindi) medium. Posts requiring bachelor's or higher qualifications, or posts involving specific technical domain knowledge, will be examined in English only . In case of equal marks, ties are resolved in this order: (i) higher marks in the domain-specific section; (ii) fewer wrong answers/negative marks; (iii) older date of birth; (iv) alphabetical order of name, then father's name, then mother's name. Full syllabus details for all 57 groups are in Section IX of the official DRA

AIIMS CRE-5 Recruitment 2026 - Selection Process

CRE-5 follows a multi-stage recruitment process. Each stage is mandatory for progression to the next. Stage 1 - Online Application: Candidates register on www.aiimsexams.ac.in and submit applications by 03 July 2026. Stage 2 - Application Acceptance: Application status published on 9 July 2026. Stage 3 - Computer Based Test (CBT): Tentatively 25-27 July 2026 at examination centres across major states/UTs of India. Stage 4 - Skill Test (where applicable): Conducted after CBT for qualifying candidates in applicable groups. Venue: preferably Delhi/NCR. Date to be notified. Stage 5 - Choice & Preference: Qualified candidates are invited to exercise institute choices and indicate order of preference. Candidates who fail to exercise choices within the given period will not be considered for any institute. Stage 6 - Seat Allocation: Allocation is made in order of merit based on choices exercise

AIIMS CRE-5 Recruitment 2026 - Required Documents and Certificates

Candidates must have all certificates ready at the time of applying, as no additional time will be granted at document verification. AIIMS CRE-5 Recruitment 2026 : How to Apply Online Step-by-Step

  • 1Applications can only be submitted through the Online Registration Terminal (OTR) at www.aiimsexams.ac.in. Physical documents must not be sent; no correspondence regarding correction in registration will be entertained.
  • 2Visit the official website: www.aiimsexams.ac.in
  • 3Register through the OTR (One-Time Registration) portal if you have not already done so.
  • 4Log in and select the CRE-5 application link.
  • 5Fill in all details carefully - name, date of birth, category, qualification, experience, and NOC details if applicable.
  • 6Upload photographs, signature, and left thumb impression in the format specified under "Upload Image Instructions."
  • 7Select your examination centre preference (state/UT).
  • 8Pay the applicable examination fee via Debit Card, Credit Card, or Net Banking.
  • 9Submit the application and download the registration slip for your records.

  • 11Check your application status on 9 July 2026 at www.aiimsexams.ac.in.

AIIMS CRE-5 Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for AIIMS CRE-5 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for CRE-5 is 03 July 2026 up to 5:00 PM. No applications will be accepted after the portal closes at this time.
Q2What is the application fee for AIIMS CRE-5?
A
General and OBC candidates must pay ₹3,000 per group applied. SC, ST, and EWS candidates pay ₹2,400 per group. PwBD candidates are fully exempted from the fee. Fee is paid online and is non-refundable (except for SC/ST candidates who appear in the exam)
Q3What posts are covered under CRE-5?
A
CRE-5 covers 57 post groups comprising a wide variety of Non-Faculty Group B and Group C posts, including Junior Engineers, Pharmacists, Lab Technologists, Administrative Officers, Clerks, Nurses, OT Technologists, Radiographers, and many other technical and non-technical roles.
Q4How many institutes are participating in CRE-5?
A
A total of 28 AIIMS and Central Government Institutes/Bodies are participating in CRE-5, including all new AIIMS, ICMR institutes, CNCI Kolkata, Dr. RML Hospital, LHMC, Pasteur Institute of India, and others.
Q5When is the CRE-5 exam date?
A
The Computer Based Test (CBT) is tentatively scheduled for 25 to 27 July 2026. AIIMS reserves the right to conduct the examination at any other date with 10 days' advance notice.
Q6What is the selection process for CRE-5?
A
Selection involves a Computer Based Test (CBT) as the primary stage, followed by a Skill Test (for applicable post groups), choice/preference exercise by qualified candidates, seat allocation in order of merit, and document verification by the respective institute.
Q7What is the exam pattern for CRE-5?
A
The CBT consists of 100 MCQs of 400 marks to be completed in 90 minutes, divided into five sections of 20 questions each. There is a negative marking of 1/4 mark for each wrong answer. The paper covers 20 questions on General Knowledge, Aptitude & Computer, and 80 domain-specific questions.
Q8Is there any relaxation for PwBD candidates in age and qualifying marks?
A
Yes. PwBD candidates with benchmark disability (minimum 40%) receive an age relaxation of 10 years irrespective of their category. The qualifying marks for PwBD candidates are 30%, regardless of which category they belong to. Scribes and compensatory time are also available as per applicable government rules.
Q9Where can I check my application status after submission?
A
Application acceptance status will be published on 9 July 2026 at www.aiimsexams.ac.in. Candidates must log in with their credentials to check status. AIIMS does not respond to telephonic or email queries about application status before the scheduled date.
