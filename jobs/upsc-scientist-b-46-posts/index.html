FAQs

Q1What is UPSC Scientist-B Recruitment 2026 in IMD?
A
UPSC Scientist-B Recruitment 2026 is a Group A Gazetted recruitment drive to fill 46 Scientist-B posts in the India Meteorological Department (IMD), Ministry of Earth Sciences, through Advertisement No. 05/2026. The posts are in three disciplines – Atmospheric Sciences, General Meteorology, and Instrumentation – and will be filled via the UPSC Online Recruitment Application (ORA) system.
Q2How many vacancies are there for UPSC Scientist-B 2026 and in which disciplines?
A
UPSC has notified 46 total vacancies for Scientist-B posts in IMD under Advertisement No. 05/2026. These include 7 posts for Scientist-B (Atmospheric Sciences), 30 posts for Scientist-B (General Meteorology), and 9 posts for Scientist-B (Instrumentation) with reservation for UR/EWS/OBC/SC/ST and horizontal reservation for PwBD wherever applicable.
Q3What are the important dates for UPSC Scientist-B Recruitment 2026?
A
The advertisement was published around 22–23 May 2026, and online applications open from 23 May 2026 on the UPSC ORA portal. The last date to apply online is 12 June 2026 up to 18:00 hours, which is also the cut-off date for determining eligibility.
Q4What is the pay scale and nature of the UPSC Scientist-B posts?
A
All Scientist-B posts in IMD are permanent Group A Gazetted, Non-Ministerial posts in Level 10 of the Pay Matrix under the 7th Central Pay Commission. Selected candidates will be posted in IMD establishments across India and will be liable for all-India service postings as per government requirements.
Q5What are the essential educational qualifications for UPSC Scientist-B 2026?
A
For Scientist-B (Atmospheric Sciences), candidates need a Master’s degree in Engineering/Technology in relevant Atmospheric/Ocean/Hydrology/Atmospheric Physics or allied disciplines from a recognized university. For Scientist-B (General Meteorology), a Master’s degree in Mathematics, Physics, Atmospheric Science or Physics with Meteorology is required, while Scientist-B (Instrumentation) requires a Bachelor’s degree in Engineering/Technology in ECE, Mechanical, IT, Instrumentation, Mechatronics, Electrical, or Computer Science.
Q6What is the age limit for UPSC Scientist-B Recruitment 2026?
A
For Scientist-B posts in IMD, the maximum age is generally 35 years for UR/EWS candidates, with relaxations up to 38 years for OBC and 40 years for SC/ST candidates as per the advertisement and UPSC rules. Additional relaxations apply for eligible PwBD candidates and certain Central Government employees/Ex-Servicemen as per standard government norms.
Q7What is the application fee for UPSC Scientist-B posts and who is exempt?
A
Most UPSC recruitment advertisements, including Advt. No. 05/2026, prescribe an application fee of Rs. 25 for General/OBC/EWS male candidates, payable online or in cash at SBI. Women candidates and candidates belonging to SC, ST, and PwBD categories are fully exempted from paying the application fee for these posts.
Q8What is the selection process for UPSC Scientist-B Recruitment 2026?
A
UPSC may conduct Recruitment by Selection through Interview alone or through a Recruitment Test (RT) followed by Interview, depending on the number of applications received. In case of a Recruitment Test, only candidates who qualify the RT as per cut-off decided by UPSC will be called for interview, and the final merit will be based on performance in the interview (and RT where applicable).
