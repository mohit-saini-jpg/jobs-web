OJEE 2026 Exam Date Out

The Odisha Joint Entrance Examination Committee has announced that OJEE 2026 will be conducted over five days: 4 May, 5 May, 7 May, 8 May, and 9 May 2026. Different course-specific papers will be scheduled on different days and shifts within this window, so candidates must rely on their admit cards to know their exact exam date, timing, and centre. The exam will be held for a wide range of programmes such as B.Pharm, B.Sc Nursing, MCA, MBA, M.Tech, M.Arch, and various lateral entry courses.

OJEE 2026 Course-wise Exams Covered

OJEE 2026 is a common entrance platform for multiple professional courses at undergraduate, lateral entry, and postgraduate levels.

UG and Lateral Entry Courses

Through OJEE 2026, candidates can appear for entrance exams for the following undergraduate and lateral entry programmes:

  • B.Pharm
  • B.Sc Nursing
  • Lateral Entry to B.Tech (LE B.Tech)
  • Lateral Entry to B.Pharm (LE B.Pharm)

These exams are typically held in specific slots across the announced OJEE dates. Candidates choose their desired course(s) at the time of application, and their exam paper will be scheduled accordingly.

PG Courses

OJEE 2026 also facilitates admission to several postgraduate courses, such as:

  • MBA (Master of Business Administration)
  • MCA (Master of Computer Applications)
  • M.Tech (Master of Technology)
  • M.Arch (Master of Architecture)

The exact list of programmes and specializations is confirmed in the official OJEE information brochure for the 2026 session.

OJEE 2026 Application and Admit Card - Key Points

Many candidates searching for exam dates also want clarity on the application and admit card process, so this section adds strong practical value.

OJEE 2026 Application Process (Short Overview)

  • The OJEE 2026 application form will be available online on the official portals ojee.nic.in and odishajee.com.
  • Candidates must first complete a registration using a valid email ID and mobile number to generate their login credentials.
  • After registration, they have to fill in personal details, academic information, and course choices carefully as per eligibility.
  • Scanned images of recent passport-size photograph and signature need to be uploaded in the prescribed format and size.
  • The application fee must be paid online using the available digital payment options (debit card, credit card, net banking, UPI, etc.).
  • Once payment is successful, candidates should download and save the confirmation page for future reference.

How to Download OJEE 2026 Admit Card

Once admit cards are released, candidates will need to download them from the official website. A clear step-by-step guide helps users and also aligns well with AI Overviews.

  • Visit the official OJEE website: ojee.nic.in (or odishajee.com).
  • On the homepage, click on the link titled “OJEE 2026 Admit Card” or similar.
  • You will be redirected to the candidate login page.
  • Enter your application number and password/date of birth.
  • Enter the security pin (captcha) as shown and click on “Login” or “Submit”.
  • Your OJEE 2026 admit card will be displayed on the screen.
  • Check your name, course, exam date, time, and exam centre address carefully.
  • Download the admit card in PDF format and take a clear printout.
  • Preserve multiple copies of the printed admit card till the end of the admission process.

Candidates must carry the printed admit card plus a valid photo ID to the exam centre; otherwise, they may not be allowed to appear for the exam.

OJEE 2026 Exam Pattern (Brief)

While detailed pattern varies course-wise, a concise overview is useful for aspirants landing on a date-focused query.

  • OJEE is generally conducted in computer-based test (CBT) mode for most courses.
  • The question paper consists mainly of objective-type multiple-choice questions (MCQs).
  • The number of questions, total marks, and duration differ depending on the course (for example, B.Pharm vs MBA vs MCA vs M.Tech).
  • Each correct answer usually carries a fixed number of marks, and there may be negative marking in some papers as per OJEE rules.
  • The syllabus for each paper is aligned with the relevant qualifying examination level - such as 10+2 for B.Pharm and B.Sc Nursing, and graduation-level subjects for MBA, MCA, M.Tech and M.Arch.

Candidates should always refer to the official OJEE 2026 brochure for precise course-wise exam pattern, marking scheme, and detailed syllabus.

OJEE 2026 Eligibility Criteria (Short Recap)

Eligibility differs for each course; a high-level summary helps users decide quickly if they should apply.

  • B.Pharm and B.Sc Nursing: Candidates are generally required to have passed or be appearing in 10+2 or equivalent examination with relevant subjects (such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology) and minimum aggregate marks as prescribed by OJEE.
  • Lateral Entry to B.Tech/B.Pharm: Applicants usually need a diploma or equivalent qualification in the appropriate branch with a minimum percentage specified in the brochure.
  • MBA: Candidates should hold a bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognized university with at least the minimum qualifying marks mentioned by OJEE.
  • MCA: A bachelor’s degree with Mathematics at 10+2 or graduation level is typically required, along with minimum aggregate marks.
  • M.Tech/M.Arch: A relevant BE/B.Tech/B.Arch or equivalent degree with the required marks is usually mandatory.

Applicants must carefully check the official OJEE 2026 notification for detailed, course-specific eligibility, reservation policies, age criteria (if any), and document requirements.

OJEE 2026 - Important Instructions for Candidates

  • Note that OJEE 2026 will be conducted on multiple days (4, 5, 7, 8, and 9 May). Your specific exam date and shift depend on your chosen course and will be printed on your admit card.
  • Complete your application form carefully and within the stipulated deadline; incomplete or late applications are usually rejected.
  • Download your admit card as soon as it is released and verify all details. In case of any discrepancy, contact the OJEE authorities immediately.
  • Visit your exam centre location in advance if possible, especially if it is in an unfamiliar area, to avoid last-minute confusion.
  • Reach the exam centre well before reporting time, carrying your printed admit card, valid photo ID, and any other documents/instructions mentioned on the admit card.
  • Follow all exam-day rules strictly, including guidelines on permitted and prohibited items, to avoid any disciplinary issues.
