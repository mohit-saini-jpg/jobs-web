Northeast Frontier Railway NFR Act Apprentice Recruitment : Job Overview

Northeast Frontier Railway (NFR) has issued a Centralized Notification No. NFR/NAPS/ACT APP./2026 dated 14-07-2026 inviting online applications for engagement of Act Apprentices under the Apprentices Act 1961, for the training cycles 2025-2026 and 2026-2027. The recruitment covers 6777 training slots spread across eight Divisions and Workshops of NFR in Assam, West Bengal and Bihar. Candidates who have passed Matriculation and hold an ITI trade certificate in a notified trade can apply online. NFR Apprentice Selection Process 2026

Selection is purely merit-based, with no written examination. The merit list for each unit is prepared using the percentage of marks in Matriculation (minimum 50% aggregate) plus ITI marks in the relevant trade; the final panel is based on the average of the two. For Medical Laboratory Technician (Pathology) and (Radiology), merit is based on Matriculation marks plus 12th Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) marks. Calculation uses marks obtained in all subjects of Matriculation/12th, not select subjects, and the average ITI marks across all semesters (or the National Trade Certificate marks). Merit lists are prepared unit-wise, trade-wise and community-wise, in descending order of percentage, equal to the number of notified slots. Northeast Frontier Railway NFR Act Apprentice Recruitment : Important Instructions

Candidates must read the training slots against their ITI trade carefully and indicate Unit preferences clearly, since only one combination will be allotted. The candidate is responsible for the correctness of information filled in the online application; false information leads to immediate disqualification. Registered mobile number and email address must not be changed during the entire selection process. Reservation: 10% for EWS, 4% horizontal for PwBD, and 10% horizontal for Ex-Servicemen (ESM); PwBD and ESM slots are included within, not additional to, the notified total. If required numbers of SC candidates are unavailable, the balance is filled by ST candidates and vice versa; unfilled OBC/EWS slots are filled by UR candidates. A medical fitness certificate (Annexure-XI), signed by a Government-authorised doctor not below the rank of Assistant Surgeon, must be submitted at the time of Document Verification; DV will not be conducted without it. Training under the Apprentices Act 1961 does not, by itself, confer any right to appointment in Railway services. However, 20% of vacancies notified for erstwhile Group D (Level-1) posts are filled by giving preference to Course Completed Act Apprentices meeting eligibility conditions. No daily/conveyance/travelling allowance is paid for attending Document Verification. Canvassing in any form leads to disqualification, and no correspondence will be entertained on the matter. Candidates should be aware of and avoid unscrupulous elements promising selection; such instances should be reported to the SDGM/NFR and local police.

Northeast Frontier Railway NFR Act Apprentice Recruitment : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1How many vacancies are there in NFR Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
NFR has notified a total of 6777 Act Apprentice training slots across eight units for the 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 cycles. These figures are provisional and may increase or decrease based on administrative needs.
Q2When will the NFR Apprentice 2026 online application start and end?
A
The online application opens on 20-07-2026 at 09:00 hrs and closes on 19-08-2026 at 17:30 hrs.
Q3What is the age limit for NFR Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must be between 15 and 24 years of age as on the closing date, 19-08-2026, with age relaxation available for SC/ST, OBC, PwBD and Ex-Servicemen categories.
Q4What is the educational qualification for NFR Apprentice posts?
A
Candidates must have passed Matriculation with minimum 50% aggregate marks (relaxed for SC/ST/PwBD) and possess an NCVT/SCVT National Trade Certificate or Provisional Certificate in the notified trade. Medical Laboratory Technician (Pathology/Radiology) posts require 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry and Biology and minimum 50% marks in both Class 10 and Class 12.
Q5What is the application fee for NFR Apprentice 2026?
A
The application fee is ₹100/-, non-refundable. SC, ST, PwBD, EBC and women candidates are exempted from paying the fee.
Q6Is there a written exam for NFR Apprentice Recruitment?
A
No. Selection is purely merit-based, using the average of Matriculation percentage and ITI trade marks (or 12th Science marks for Medical Laboratory Technician posts). There is no written examination mentioned in the notification.
Q7What is the stipend for NFR Apprentices?
A
The minimum stipend for NTC/STC holders is ₹9,600 per month, with ₹10,560/month in the 2nd year and ₹11,040/month in the 3rd year where exemption is admissible, as per extant rules effective 11-09-2025.
Q8How many candidates are called for Document Verification?
A
Shortlisted candidates are called for Document Verification to the extent of 1.5 times the notified vacancies for each unit, trade and community category.
Q9Can a candidate apply for more than one unit or trade preference?
A
Candidates indicate their Unit-Department-Trade preferences in order of choice within a single application. Only one combination will finally be allotted based on merit and preference; submitting more than one separate application is not allowed and leads to disqualification.
Q10Does completing NFR Apprentice training guarantee a Railway job?
A
No. Training under the Apprentices Act 1961 does not by itself confer any right to appointment. However, 20% of vacancies notified for erstwhile Group D (Level-1) posts give preference to Course Completed Act Apprentices who meet other eligibility conditions and qualifying marks in the written examination for that recruitment.
