Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) Recruitment 2026?

GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) Recruitment 2026 is a state government recruitment drive under Advertisement No. 407/202627 to fill 184 Class‑3 Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) posts in the Directorate of AYUSH, Health & Family Welfare Department, Government of Gujarat.

Q2. How many vacancies are released for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) 2026?

A total of 184 vacancies have been notified for the Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda), Class‑3 post, distributed among Unreserved, EWS, SEBC, SC and ST categories, with women’s reservation included within each category and separate PwD vacancies covered under Notification No. 401/202526.

Q3. What are the important dates for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) Recruitment 2026?

Online applications for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) 2026 will be accepted on the OJAS portal from 15 May 2026 (2:00 PM) to 29 May 2026 (11:59 PM), and the last date for online fee payment is 01 June 2026 (11:59 PM), while the crucial date for age calculation is 29 May 2026.

Q4. What is the eligibility and educational qualification for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) 2026?

Candidates must have either a B.Pharm (Ayurveda) degree from a recognized university or a D.Pharm (Ayurveda) with at least 2 years’ experience as Pharmacist/Compounder in a Government, Local Body, Government Undertaking, Board or Corporation, along with basic computer knowledge and adequate knowledge of Gujarati or Hindi.

Q5. What is the age limit for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) Recruitment 2026?

For GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) 2026, the general male age limit is 18 to 35 years as on 29 May 2026, with higher upper‑age limits for women, reserved categories, PwD candidates, and Ex‑servicemen as per Government of Gujarat rules.

Q6. What is the salary for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) posts?

Selected candidates will receive a fixed salary of Rs. 40,800 per month for the first 5 years, after which, on satisfactory service, they will be placed in the regular Level‑5 pay scale of Rs. 29,200 – 92,300 under the 7th CPC.

Q7. What is the exam pattern for GSSSB Pharmacist/Compounder (Ayurveda) 2026?

The selection is through a single‑stage objective MCQ exam (CBRT/OMR) of 210 questions for 210 marks in 3 hours, divided into Part A (Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, Constitution, Current Affairs, Comprehension) for 90 marks and Part B (Ayurveda technical subjects) for 120 marks, with 1/4 negative marking and minimum 40% qualifying marks separately in each part.

Q8. Which Ayurveda topics are covered in the technical section (Part B) of the exam?

Part B of the paper includes Ayurveda Parichaya, Swasthavritta, Rachana Sharira, Kriya Sharira, Aushadh Nirman Siddhant, Dravyaguna, Rasashastra, General Introduction of Treatment, Bhaishajya Kalpana, and Pharmacy Management, collectively carrying 120 questions for 120 marks.
