NEST Response Sheet 2026 (Released) - Download PDF, Check Answers & Raise Objections

The NEST 2026 Response Sheet and Provisional Answer Key have been officially released at nestexam.in. Candidates who appeared for the National Entrance Screening Test (NEST) 2026 can now download the question paper with provisional answers in PDF format no login required. The objection window is open from June 10 to June 12, 2026, and the final result is scheduled for June 24, 2026.

What Is the NEST Response Sheet 2026?

The NEST 2026 Response Sheet (also referred to as the provisional answer key) is the official document released by the exam authority containing the correct answers to all questions asked in the examination. Candidates can use this document to cross-check their attempted answers and calculate their expected score before the official result is announced.

NEST (National Entrance Screening Test) is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to the 5-year Integrated M.Sc. programme at two premier institutes:

  • NISER - National Institute of Science Education and Research, Bhubaneswar
  • UM-DAE CEBS - University of Mumbai - Department of Atomic Energy Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences, Mumbai

Downloading and reviewing the response sheet is a critical step for candidates as it gives them an early indication of their performance and helps them identify any answer that may be incorrect and worth challenging.

Candidates should take note of all key dates related to the NEST 2026 provisional answer key, the objection process, and the result declaration. Missing the objection deadline means losing the chance to challenge any incorrect answers.

How to Download NEST 2026 Response Sheet / Provisional Answer Key

Downloading the NEST 2026 Response Sheet is a straightforward process and does not require candidates to log in with any credentials. The provisional answer key is available as a direct PDF on the official website. Follow the steps below:

  • Visit the official NEST website at nestexam.in
  • Look for the link titled "NEST 2026 Questions with Provisional Answer Keys" on the homepage or notifications section
  • Click on the link the PDF will open directly in your browser
  • Download and save the PDF for offline reference
  • Compare each of your attempted answers against the officially provided correct answers

The NEST 2026 provisional answer key covers all subjects tested in the examination. It provides correct answers for every question across all sections. The NEST exam typically tests candidates on the following subjects:

How to Use the NEST 2026 Response Sheet to Calculate Your Score

Understanding how to calculate your probable score using the answer key can help you set realistic expectations before the official result. Here is how NEST typically marks responses:

  • Each correct answer earns +3 marks
  • There is negative marking of -1 mark for each wrong answer
  • Unattempted questions carry 0 marks

Cross-reference every question in the downloaded PDF against your own responses to tally your correct answers, wrong answers, and unattempted questions. This self-assessment is only an approximation your final score depends on the official final answer key after all valid objections are reviewed.

How to Raise Objections Against NEST 2026 Provisional Answer Key

If a candidate believes that a particular answer given in the provisional answer key is incorrect, they can formally challenge it during the objection window. The objection window for NEST 2026 is open from June 10, 2026 (10:00 AM) to June 12, 2026 (11:30 PM).

Step-by-Step Process to Submit an Objection

  • Visit the official NEST website at nestexam.in
  • Click on the Login option
  • Enter your Application Number / Login ID and Password
  • Enter the CAPTCHA code as displayed
  • Identify the question(s) you wish to challenge
  • Upload supporting evidence this could include references from standard textbooks, academic papers, or other credible sources
  • Submit the objection
  • Save or print the acknowledgement receipt for future reference

Important Points About the Objection Process

  • According to the official notification, no objection fee has been mentioned for challenging the provisional answer key.
  • All objections must be submitted within the deadline. Late submissions will not be accepted.
  • Subject experts appointed by the exam authority will review each valid objection submitted.
  • Based on the review, a Final Answer Key will be prepared before the result is announced.
  • The NEST 2026 result will be computed using this final, verified answer key.

What Happens After the Objection Window Closes?

Once the objection deadline of June 12, 2026 passes, the exam authority will begin the process of reviewing all submitted challenges. Subject matter experts will examine the questions flagged by candidates and assess the validity of the supporting evidence provided.

If an objection is found to be valid, the answer key will be updated accordingly, and marks will be adjusted for all candidates who attempted that question. The final answer key will then be used to compute scores for all candidates, and the NEST 2026 result will be published on June 24, 2026.

Candidates who qualify the examination will be able to download their scorecards starting June 25, 2026. The official notification states that scorecards are issued only to those who clear the qualifying cutoff.

NEST 2026 Scorecard Details

The NEST 2026 scorecard will serve as the official document reflecting a candidate's performance in the exam. Key information to expect on the scorecard includes:

  • Candidate's name and roll number
  • Subject-wise marks
  • Total score
  • Qualifying status

Only candidates who meet the minimum qualifying criteria will receive their scorecards. This scorecard will be required at the time of the admission process at NISER Bhubaneswar and UM-DAE CEBS Mumbai.

About NEST Exam 2026

NEST (National Entrance Screening Test) is jointly conducted by NISER, Bhubaneswar, and UM-DAE CEBS, Mumbai, for admission to the five-year Integrated M.Sc. programme in sciences. The exam is held once a year and is considered one of the most competitive science entrance tests in India.
