What's NTA (National Testing Agency)?

National Testing Agency (NTA) is an autonomous organization under the Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education, Government of India. Established in 2017, NTA conducts entrance examinations for various undergraduate and postgraduate programs across Indian universities.

Key Facts About NTA:

  • Established: 2017
  • Purpose: Conduct fair, transparent, and computer-based entrance tests
  • Exams Conducted: CUET, JEE (Main), NEET, CBSE, and 50+ other entrance exams
  • Headquarters: New Delhi, India
  • Website: https://nta.ac.in

Why CUET Matters:

  • CUET (Common University Entrance Test) is the single gateway for admission to:
  • Delhi University (DU) - 75+ programs
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU)
  • ** Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU)**
  • 40+ Central and State Universities
  • Over 2,500+ undergraduate programs across India

Without CUET, you cannot get admission to these prestigious universities for B.A., B.Sc., BBA, and other undergraduate courses.

Common Mistakes While Challenging CUET UG 2026 Answer Key

Many students lose their objection fee due to these common mistakes:

1. Not Verifying Original Response

  • Mistake: Challenging without checking your actual response sheet
  • Solution: Download and compare your response sheet with the answer key first

2. Wrong Question Number

  • Mistake: Entering incorrect question number from the booklet
  • Solution: Check question number from both the Question Booklet and Response Sheet

3. Missing Supporting Evidence

  • Mistake: Submitting objection without proper justification
  • Solution: Provide clear explanation with reference to:
  • Standard textbooks
  • Official NTA syllabus
  • NTA answer key guidelines

4. Exceeding Time Limit

  • Mistake: Submitting objection after June 14, 2026 (deadline)
  • Solution: Submit within 3-5 days (June 9-14, 2026)

5. Incorrect Answer Choice

  • Mistake: Selecting wrong option number (A, B, C, D)
  • Solution: Verify option number from your actual response sheet

6. Multiple Objections for Same Question

  • Mistake: Submitting more than one objection for the same question
  • Solution: Submit only one objection per question

7. Payment Issues

  • Mistake: Not confirming payment receipt
  • Solution: Save payment confirmation receipt and transaction ID

8. Incomplete Form

  • Mistake: Leaving mandatory fields empty
  • Solution: Fill all required fields before submission
