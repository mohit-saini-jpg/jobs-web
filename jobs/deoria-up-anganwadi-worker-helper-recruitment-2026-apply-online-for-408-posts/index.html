Deoria UP Anganwadi Worker & Helper Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

The Office of the District Programme Officer, Deoria has released a recruitment notification for the posts of Anganwadi Karyakatri (Worker) and Anganwadi Sahayika (Helper) under the Integrated Child Development Services scheme. A total of 408 vacancies (146 Karyakatri and 262 Sahayika) are open across various projects/blocks in Deoria district. Only female candidates are eligible to apply. The online application process will run through the upanganwadibharti.in portal, with the last date to apply being 31 July 2026.

Deoria Anganwadi Recruitment 2026: Eligibility Criteria

Only female candidates are eligible to apply for both Anganwadi Karyakatri and Sahayika posts. Applicants must be residents of the same Gram Sabha (village) for rural posts, or the same Ward for urban posts, to which they are applying. The selection process for both posts will be conducted as per the procedure laid down in Government Order No. 1/1092245/2025/3313/58-1-2025(1917687) dated 17.09.2025, issued by the Bal Vikas Vibhag and Pushtahar, UP. Anganwadi Karyakatri: Minimum qualification is Intermediate ( 12th pass). Anganwadi Sahayika: Minimum qualification is Intermediate or equivalent. The merit list will be prepared based on marks/percentage obtained from Intermediate up to the highest qualification of Postgraduate. High School marks are not included while preparing the merit list for either post.

Deoria Anganwadi Recruitment 2026: Selection Process

Selection for both Anganwadi Karyakatri and Sahayika posts will be entirely merit-based, conducted online through the departmental portal. No written examination or interview will be conducted for these posts. If more than one eligible candidate is available in the same category for a post, a merit list will be prepared among all such eligible candidates. For Sahayika posts, the minimum qualification considered is Intermediate or equivalent, and the maximum qualification considered is Postgraduate, for merit preparation. Merit calculation formula: The percentage of marks obtained by a candidate is divided by 10 to arrive at the marks used for merit. For example, a candidate scoring 45% in Intermediate will be awarded 4.5 marks; a candidate scoring 59% will be awarded 5.9 marks.
