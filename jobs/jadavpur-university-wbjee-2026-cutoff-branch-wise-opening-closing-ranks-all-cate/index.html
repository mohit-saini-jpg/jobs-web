What Is the WBJEE Cutoff for Jadavpur University?

The WBJEE cutoff for Jadavpur University refers to the opening and closing ranks within which seats get allotted during each round of counselling. A lower rank number means a higher score so a closing rank of 202 in CSE (Open) means only the top 202 ranked candidates can be considered for that seat.

The cutoff is not a single number. It varies by branch, category, and counselling round. The WBJEE Board releases these figures after every round of seat allotment, so candidates are advised to track the official portal closely once counselling begins.

Factors That Determine the WBJEE Cutoff at Jadavpur University

Understanding why cutoffs change each year helps candidates better interpret the data above. The key factors include:

Reservation Category. Candidates belonging to SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, and EWS categories are assessed against separate merit lists with relaxed rank thresholds, as mandated by West Bengal government reservation norms.

Performance Trends in WBJEE. If the overall difficulty level of the exam changes as was the case in recent years rank distributions shift, causing cutoffs to rise or fall accordingly.

Number of Applicants and Seat Matrix. An increase in qualified candidates competing for a fixed number of seats naturally pushes cutoffs higher. JU's seat intake does not change significantly year to year, so demand pressure remains steady.

Choice Filling During Counselling. The opening and closing ranks are also influenced by how many candidates lock their choices for a particular branch. High demand branches like CSE and IT will always show tighter cutoffs.

Round of Counselling. Cutoffs typically relax slightly in later rounds as unfilled seats become available. Candidates who don't make it in Round 1 should not abandon hope and must participate in all rounds.
