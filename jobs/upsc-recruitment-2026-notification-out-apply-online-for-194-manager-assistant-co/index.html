How to Apply

  • 1Candidates must apply online only at https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/. The UPSC online portal has four modules: Account Creation, Universal Registration (URN), Common Application Form (CAF), and Post/Vacancy-specific module. The first three modules can be filled at any time; the fourth (vacancy-specific) module must be filled during the application window. Candidates who have already registered and have a URN can directly log in and fill the vacancy-specific module. Candidates are strongly advised to use Aadhaar Card as the ID document for seamless verification. Applications once submitted are final - no correction/alteration/modification is permitted after submission. Candidates must take a printout of the finally submitted application and carry it along with original documents if called for interview.
  • 2Documents to be Uploaded at the time of Online Application:
  • 3Live photograph captured via webcam/camera during CAF filling (mandatory)
  • 4Scanned recent passport-size photograph (as per instructions on UPSC website)
  • 5Scanned signature (three signatures one below the other on plain white paper, black ink)
  • 6Matriculation/10th Standard certificate or marksheet as proof of Date of Birth
  • 7Degree/Diploma certificate as proof of educational qualification; if not available, provisional certificate + all year marksheets
  • 8Experience Certificate(s) in prescribed format from Head(s) of Organisation(s) - mentioning duration, basic pay, consolidated pay, and nature of duties
  • 9Caste/Community certificate (SC/ST/OBC) in prescribed proforma from Competent Authority
  • 10OBC Non-Creamy Layer declaration in prescribed format
  • 11EWS certificate in prescribed proforma
  • 12PwBD Disability Certificate from Medical Board constituted by Central/State Government
  • 13Age relaxation certificate for Ex-Servicemen/Central/UT Government Employees in prescribed proforma
  • 14Order/letter for equivalent qualification (if applicable) citing Authority with number and date
  • 15Name change documents (if applicable - marriage certificate/divorce decree/affidavit as per situation)
  • 16Documentary support for any other claim made (Professional Registration, Publications, GATE/NET, etc.)
  • 17Printout of the finally submitted Online Recruitment Application must be retained - candidates will need to bring it to the interview along with original certificates. Candidates are NOT required to send printouts or documents by post or by hand to UPSC.
  • 18Apply Online at: https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/
  • 19For queries: Helpline No. 011-24041001 or 011-40303444 | Email: [email protected] (10:00 AM to 5:30 PM on all working days during the application window).

Important Instructions

Online applications open from 23-05-2026 and close on 12-06-2026 at 1800 Hrs - candidates must apply well in advance without waiting for the closing date. All candidates must enter correct and active email addresses - all correspondence including interview schedules will be communicated only through the registered email ID. Applications once submitted are final - no correction/alteration/modification in any field is allowed after submission. No withdrawal of applications is permitted. The date for determining eligibility in every respect is the closing date for submission of online recruitment application (12-06-2026). Filling wrong information may lead to rejection through computer-based shortlisting apart from debarment by the Commission. Face authentication will be mandatory for all candidates at the examination venue/Interview Hall - candidates must enter well in time for face authentication and frisking. Mobile phones (even in switched-off mode), pagers, smart watches, pen drives, cameras, Bluetooth devices, or any electronic/communication device are strictly banned at the examination/interview venue. Violation leads to disciplinary action including ban from future examinations/recruitment. Candidates must not bring valuables or bags to the venue - no arrangement for safe-keeping will be made and UPSC will not be responsible for any loss. The Commission will contribute towards interview travelling expenses at Second Class Mail railway fare rate by shortest route - candidates will be informed of details when called for interview. Canvassing in any form will disqualify a candidate. Summoning for interview conveys no assurance of selection - appointment orders will be issued by the respective Ministry. UPSC may grant higher initial pay to candidates adjudged meritorious in the interview. The Commission does not enter into correspondence with candidates about reasons for non-selection - no interim enquiry about results will be entertained. Prescribed proforma for various certificates are available at https://upsc.gov.in under Recruitment → Forms of Certificates.

FAQs

Q1What is the last date to apply for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for UPSC Recruitment 2026 under Advertisement No. 05/2026 is 12 June 2026 till 06:00 PM (1800 Hrs). Candidates are advised to complete the registration and fee payment process well before the closing date to avoid server issues or technical delays. Applications submitted after the deadline will not be accepted under any circumstances.
Q2How many vacancies are available in UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has announced a total of 194 vacancies through Advertisement No. 05/2026. These vacancies are available for various Group A and Group B Gazetted posts across multiple Central Government Ministries and Departments. The number of vacancies may increase or decrease depending on departmental requirements.
Q3What is the educational qualification required for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
The educational qualification for UPSC Recruitment 2026 varies depending on the post applied for. Candidates with qualifications such as BE/B.Tech, Bachelor’s Degree, Master’s Degree, Ph.D., CA, Engineering Degree, or equivalent qualifications in relevant fields are eligible to apply. Applicants must carefully check the post-wise eligibility criteria mentioned in the official notification before submitting the application form.
Q4What is the application fee for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates belonging to the General, OBC, and EWS male categories need to pay an application fee of Rs. 25/- through online payment methods such as debit card, credit card, UPI, or internet banking. However, SC/ST/PwBD candidates and all women candidates are exempted from paying the application fee. Once paid, the fee is non-refundable under any condition.
Q5What is the age limit for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
The age limit for UPSC Recruitment 2026 differs according to the post and department. In general, the upper age limit ranges between 30 years and 45 years for unreserved candidates, while age relaxation is available for SC, ST, OBC, PwBD, Ex-Servicemen, and Government employees as per Government of India rules. Candidates should verify the specific age criteria for their chosen post.
Q6How can I apply online for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates can apply online for UPSC Recruitment 2026 through the official UPSC Online Recruitment Application (ORA) portal. Applicants must first complete account registration, generate a Universal Registration Number (URN), fill out the Common Application Form, and then submit the post-specific application form. After successful submission, candidates should save and print the application form for future reference.
Q7What is the selection process for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
The selection process for UPSC Recruitment 2026 may include shortlisting, Recruitment Test (RT), and Interview, depending on the number of applications received for each post. In some cases, UPSC may directly conduct interviews if the number of eligible candidates is manageable. Final selection will be based on performance and fulfillment of eligibility requirements.
Q8Which departments are included in UPSC Advertisement No. 05/2026?
A
UPSC Advertisement No. 05/2026 includes vacancies in several prestigious government departments and ministries. These include DGCA, PESO, Department of Posts, Indian Meteorological Department (IMD), Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), Ministry of Defence, Ministry of MSME, Ministry of Home Affairs, and many others. Candidates can choose posts based on their qualification and experience.
Q9Is work experience mandatory for UPSC Recruitment 2026?
A
Work experience requirements vary depending on the post applied for in UPSC Recruitment 2026. Some posts, especially technical and senior-level positions, require 1 to 10 years of relevant experience, while certain posts like Scientist-B and Assistant Director positions may not require prior work experience. Candidates should read the post-wise eligibility section carefully before applying.
Q10Where can I download the UPSC Recruitment 2026 Notification PDF?
A
Candidates can download the official UPSC Recruitment 2026 Notification PDF from the UPSC website. The notification contains complete details regarding eligibility, vacancies, reservation, age limit, pay scale, application process, and important instructions. Reading the notification carefully before applying can help candidates avoid mistakes during the online application process.
