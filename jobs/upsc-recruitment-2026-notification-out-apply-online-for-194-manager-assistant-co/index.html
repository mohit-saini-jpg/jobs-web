Age Limit

DetailsAge limits are post-specific and are calculated as on the closing date for submission of online recruitment application i.e. 12-06-2026. The age limits shown in the advertisement are the normal limits for UR/EWS candidates. Standard age relaxations are applicable for SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), and PwBD (10 years) for vacancies reserved/suitable for them. OBC/SC/ST/PwBD candidates applying against unreserved posts are not entitled to age relaxation.

Qualification / Eligibility

DetailsCandidates must fulfill all eligibility conditions as on the closing date of online application i.e. 12-06-2026 (1800 Hrs). Selection Process

1Selection will be made by Recruitment by Selection - through Interview or Recruitment Test followed by Interview as decided by UPSC on a post-by-post basis. In the event of a large number of applications
2UPSC will adopt shortlisting criteria including desirable qualifications
3higher educational qualifications
4higher experience
5experience before/after essential qualification
6or by conducting a Recruitment Test. The summoning of candidates for interview conveys no assurance of selection. Appointment orders will be issued by the respective Ministry.
7Stage 1 - Application Scrutiny and Shortlisting
8All applications are scrutinized for eligibility. If applications are large in number
9UPSC may shortlist based on: (a) Desirable Qualifications; (b) higher educational qualifications; (c) higher relevant experience; (d) counting experience before/after essential qualification; (e) invoking experience even where not mentioned as EQ/DQ; or (f) by holding a Recruitment Test. Candidates should mention all qualifications and experience in the relevant field over and above the minimum qualifications.
10Stage 2 - Recruitment Test (RT) followed by Interview (wherever applicable)
11If selection is made by Recruitment Test followed by Interview
12candidates must achieve minimum suitability at both stages. Minimum interview marks: UR – 50
13OBC – 45
14SC/ST/PH – 40 (out of 100 total interview marks). Minimum marks for Recruitment Test shall be decided by UPSC on a case-by-case basis.
15Stage 3 - Medical Examination
16Selected candidates must be in sound bodily health and must undergo medical examination and satisfy medical authority as required by the Government.

How to Apply

  1. 1Candidates must apply online only at https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/. The UPSC online portal has four modules: Account Creation, Universal Registration (URN), Common Application Form (CAF), and Post/Vacancy-specific module. The first three modules can be filled at any time; the fourth (vacancy-specific) module must be filled during the application window. Candidates who have already registered and have a URN can directly log in and fill the vacancy-specific module. Candidates are strongly advised to use Aadhaar Card as the ID document for seamless verification. Applications once submitted are final - no correction/alteration/modification is permitted after submission. Candidates must take a printout of the finally submitted application and carry it along with original documents if called for interview.
  2. 2Documents to be Uploaded at the time of Online Application:
  3. 3Live photograph captured via webcam/camera during CAF filling (mandatory)
  4. 4Scanned recent passport-size photograph (as per instructions on UPSC website)
  5. 5Scanned signature (three signatures one below the other on plain white paper, black ink)
  6. 6Matriculation/10th Standard certificate or marksheet as proof of Date of Birth
  7. 7Degree/Diploma certificate as proof of educational qualification; if not available, provisional certificate + all year marksheets
  8. 8Experience Certificate(s) in prescribed format from Head(s) of Organisation(s) - mentioning duration, basic pay, consolidated pay, and nature of duties
  9. 9Caste/Community certificate (SC/ST/OBC) in prescribed proforma from Competent Authority
  10. 10OBC Non-Creamy Layer declaration in prescribed format
  11. 11EWS certificate in prescribed proforma
  12. 12PwBD Disability Certificate from Medical Board constituted by Central/State Government
  13. 13Age relaxation certificate for Ex-Servicemen/Central/UT Government Employees in prescribed proforma
  14. 14Order/letter for equivalent qualification (if applicable) citing Authority with number and date
  15. 15Name change documents (if applicable - marriage certificate/divorce decree/affidavit as per situation)
  16. 16Documentary support for any other claim made (Professional Registration, Publications, GATE/NET, etc.)
  17. 17Printout of the finally submitted Online Recruitment Application must be retained - candidates will need to bring it to the interview along with original certificates. Candidates are NOT required to send printouts or documents by post or by hand to UPSC.
  18. 18Apply Online at: https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/
  19. 19For queries: Helpline No. 011-24041001 or 011-40303444 | Email: [email protected] (10:00 AM to 5:30 PM on all working days during the application window).

Important Instructions

Online applications open from 23-05-2026 and close on 12-06-2026 at 1800 Hrs - candidates must apply well in advance without waiting for the closing date. All candidates must enter correct and active email addresses - all correspondence including interview schedules will be communicated only through the registered email ID. Applications once submitted are final - no correction/alteration/modification in any field is allowed after submission. No withdrawal of applications is permitted. The date for determining eligibility in every respect is the closing date for submission of online recruitment application (12-06-2026). Filling wrong information may lead to rejection through computer-based shortlisting apart from debarment by the Commission. Face authentication will be mandatory for all candidates at the examination venue/Interview Hall - candidates must enter well in time for face authentication and frisking. Mobile phones (even in switched-off mode), pagers, smart watches, pen drives, cameras, Bluetooth devices, or any electronic/communication device are strictly banned at the examination/interview venue. Violation leads to disciplinary action including ban from future examinations/recruitment. Candidates must not bring valuables or bags to the venue - no arrangement for safe-keeping will be made and UPSC will not be responsible for any loss. The Commission will contribute towards interview travelling expenses at Second Class Mail railway fare rate by shortest route - candidates will be informed of details when called for interview. Canvassing in any form will disqualify a candidate. Summoning for interview conveys no assurance of selection - appointment orders will be issued by the respective Ministry. UPSC may grant higher initial pay to candidates adjudged meritorious in the interview. The Commission does not enter into correspondence with candidates about reasons for non-selection - no interim enquiry about results will be entertained. Prescribed proforma for various certificates are available at https://upsc.gov.in under Recruitment → Forms of Certificates.
