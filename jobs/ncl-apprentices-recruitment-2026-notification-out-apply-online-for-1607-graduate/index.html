Selection Process

1Selection will be made purely on the basis of merit drawn from qualifying examination marks
2followed by document scrutiny and a mandatory medical test. No written examination will be conducted by NCL for this recruitment.
3Stage 1 – Merit List Preparation
4For Graduate and Diploma positions
5merit is drawn on the basis of marks scored in the qualifying Graduation/Diploma examination. For ITI Trade positions
6merit is based on the average of passing percentage in Matriculation and the respective National/State Trade Certificate. Candidates are arranged in descending order of marks. In case of a tie
7date of birth (older candidate preferred) and then alphabetical order of name will be applied.
8Stage 2 – Document Scrutiny
9Shortlisted candidates based on merit will be called to appear with all original certificates for scrutiny. Date and venue will be notified on nclcil.in and through NATS/NAPS portal or email. Failure to appear or produce valid documents within the stipulated date and time will result in cancellation of candidature.
10Stage 3 – Medical Examination
11Final offer for Apprenticeship Training is subject to clearing the Medical Test as per prescribed standards under the Mines Rules 1955. A fitness certificate from the medical officer of a Government Hospital (rank not less than Assistant Civil Surgeon) in the prescribed format (Annexure-3) must be produced. The Medical Fitness certificate must be issued after the date of release of this Notification.

After successful registration, a registration number (Enrolment ID) will be generated, following which the candidate must apply for the NCL positions through the respective portal.
  2. 2Documents/Details required at the time of Online Application:
  3. 3Valid NATS/NAPS Enrolment ID (registration reference).

Important Instructions

Online applications are open from 01-06-2026 to 15-06-2026 through NATS/NAPS portals only. A candidate may apply for any one notified position only - multiple applications will lead to disqualification. Information pertaining to Date of Birth, Caste Category, and Qualification once entered in the online application shall be considered final - no alteration will be permitted at later stages. Stipend for Graduate and Diploma Apprentices is inclusive of the Government of India share paid through DBT; Trade Apprentice stipend will be paid directly by NCL. Stipend will be paid directly into the AADHAAR-linked bank account of the Apprenticeship Trainee as per the signed contract. Apprenticeship Training is purely temporary and statutory in nature - it does not confer any right or claim for regular employment in NCL. Apprentices are eligible for 11 days Casual Leave and 15 days Medical Leave per year of training. Engagement hours are between 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Apprentices will be covered under the Workmen Compensation Act, 1923 for any accident during training. No accommodation, fooding/lodging, or transport will be provided during the training period. No TA/DA will be paid for appearing in document scrutiny or post-scrutiny processes. Canvassing in any manner at any stage will lead to cancellation of candidature. Any dispute will be subject to courts/tribunals of Singrauli, MP only. Dates mentioned in the notification are tentative and subject to alteration/amendment.
