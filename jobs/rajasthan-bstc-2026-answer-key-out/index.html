Raising Objections on Rajasthan BSTC Answer Key 2026

Objection process and important dates

After releasing the provisional answer key, the exam authority usually provides a specific window during which candidates can submit objections against any answer they consider incorrect. This process ensures transparency and gives candidates a fair opportunity to highlight any discrepancies.

Common steps for raising objections:

  • Visit the official Pre D.El.Ed website and log in with your application number and password or date of birth.
  • Click on the “Provisional Answer Key Objection” or “Grievance” link.
  • Select the question number for which you want to raise an objection.
  • Choose the answer option you believe is correct and provide a valid justification.
  • Upload supporting documents or reference material, if required.
  • Pay the prescribed objection fee per question, if applicable.
  • Submit the form before the last date mentioned in the official notification.

Candidates should carefully read the instructions on the portal, as objections filed without proper proof or within the wrong format may be rejected.

Final answer key and result announcement

Once the objection window closes, the exam authority reviews all submitted objections with subject experts. If any mistake is found in the provisional answer key, corrections are made and a final answer key is released. The result of BSTC 2026 is then prepared based on this final answer key, and no further challenges are usually allowed.

The BSTC 2026 result will be published in online mode, and candidates will be able to check their marks, rank, and qualifying status using their login details. After the result, the counselling schedule and seat allocation process for D.El.Ed institutes will be notified separately.
