What Does RV/RW Mean in a DAVV Result?

RV/RW stands for Revaluation and Rechecking. These results apply only to students who applied for revaluation or rechecking of their answer sheets after the original result was declared. If your course is not listed under RV/RW, your original semester result was likely published earlier in a separate notification, and you should check the regular result list for your course and semester instead.

What Does "1YDC" Mean?

DAVV commonly uses course-code suffixes such as 1YDC, 2YDC, 3YDC, and 4YDC to denote the duration/degree-course category of a programme (One-Year, Two-Year, Three-Year, and Four-Year Degree Course batches respectively). The exact eligibility basis for the "1YDC" MA batch is not specified in the notification shared here; students should refer to their admission/enrollment documents or contact their college for the precise programme structure applicable to them.

How to Check DAVV Result 2026 Online

  • Visit the official DAVV result portal: https://www.dauniv.ac.in/results.
  • Find your course name from the live results list (e.g., "M.A. Pre-History Sem 1" or "Master of Arts - Sociology (1YDC) - Semester II").
  • Click on the Mark List, Pass List, or Grade List link next to your course, as applicable.
  • Use your roll number or enrollment number to search for your name within the PDF (for Pass List/Grade List downloads) or enter your roll number directly (for Mark List lookups, where the portal supports individual search).
  • Download and save the result, and take a printout for your records.

Details Usually Mentioned on a DAVV Result

  • Student name
  • Roll number / enrollment number
  • College/institute name
  • Course and semester
  • Subject-wise marks or grades
  • SGPA/CGPA (for grade lists)
  • Overall result status (Pass/Fail/ATKT/Supplementary)

Important Instructions for Students

  • Results published online, including Mark Lists, Pass Lists, and Grade Lists, are generally provisional until the official marksheet or consolidated result is issued by the university.
  • Cross-check your name, roll number, and enrollment number carefully after downloading your result.
  • Keep a downloaded copy or printout safe until you receive your original marksheet/grade card.
