Assam CEE 2026 Provisional Answer Key Released What You Need to Know Right Now

The Assam CEE Answer Key 2026 is officially out. The Assam Science and Technology University (ASTU) released the provisional answer key on June 14, 2026, after 6:00 PM on its official website, astu.ac.in. Candidates who appeared for the Assam Combined Entrance Examination held on the same day from 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM can now download their response sheets and cross-check each answer against the official key.

This is a provisional answer key, meaning it may contain errors. ASTU has opened a structured objection window that runs until June 16, 2026, at 6:00 PM, giving candidates a two-day window to flag any discrepancies. Challenges must be submitted through the dedicated CEE-2026 candidate login portal at astu.ac.in, with a processing fee of ₹500 per question.

The final answer key and subsequently the Assam CEE 2026 result will be published in June 2026 after all valid challenges are reviewed by subject experts.

How to Calculate Your Probable Score Using the Assam CEE Answer Key 2026

Before results are officially declared, candidates can use the provisional answer key to estimate their performance. This helps in gauging college admission possibilities and planning accordingly.

The Assam CEE marking scheme works as follows:

  • +4 marks for every correct response
  • -1 mark for every incorrect response (negative marking applies)
  • 0 marks for unattempted questions

To calculate your probable score:

  • Download the provisional answer key PDF from astu.ac.in.
  • Count the total number of questions you answered correctly multiply this figure by 4.
  • Count the total number of questions you answered incorrectly this is the number of marks to deduct (1 mark per wrong answer).
  • Subtract the wrong-answer deductions from your correct-answer score.
  • The resulting figure is your estimated probable score.

Example: If you attempted 90 questions, got 70 correct and 20 wrong your probable score = (70 × 4) - (20 × 1) = 280 - 20 = 260 marks.

How to Challenge the Assam CEE 2026 Provisional Answer Key

If you believe any answer in the official provisional key is incorrect, ASTU provides a formal process to raise objections. Challenges are only accepted within the specified window and through the official portal. Here is the complete process:

Step 1: Go to the ASTU official website at www.astu.ac.in.

Step 2: Click on the "CEE-2026 Login Portal" link.

Step 3: Log in using your registered Application Number and Password.

Step 4: Navigate to the "Raise Objections / Challenge Answer Key" section.

Step 5: Select the question number you wish to challenge and enter the answer you believe is correct.

Step 6: Pay the processing fee of ₹500 per question using a debit card, credit card, or internet banking.

Step 7: Verify the payment amount before final submission if the fee paid is insufficient, the challenge will be automatically cancelled with no refund.

Step 8: Submit your objection and save the confirmation page for future reference.
