AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026: Check Date, Direct Link at polycet.ap.gov.in

The AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026 is scheduled to be released by the State Board of Technical Education and Training, Andhra Pradesh (SBTET AP) on July 7, 2026, at the official counselling portal, polycet.ap.gov.in. Candidates who registered for AP POLYCET 2026 counselling and completed the web options entry will be able to check their allotted college and branch by logging in with their hall ticket number, ICR form number, and date of birth. This article covers the release date, how to check and download the seat allotment letter, the reporting schedule, and everything candidates need to do after allotment.

AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result Release Date

As per the revised AP POLYCET 2026 counselling schedule issued by SBTET AP, the Round 1 seat allotment result is scheduled for release on July 7, 2026. This follows the closing of the web options editing window on July 5, 2026. Candidates are advised to keep checking the official portal, since counselling authorities sometimes revise timelines by a day or two based on the volume of option entries received.

AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result Status

As of now, the AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026 is awaited and has not yet been officially declared. It is expected to go live on the counselling portal on July 7, 2026. This page will be updated with the direct link and step-by-step checking process as soon as SBTET AP activates the result.

Selection Stage Covered

This result covers the Round 1 (Phase 1) seat allotment stage of AP POLYCET 2026 counselling, which comes after registration, certificate verification, and web options entry, and before self-reporting and physical reporting at the allotted college.

Score Card and Merit List Availability

A separate merit list is not published for seat allotment; allotment is generated directly against each candidate's AP POLYCET 2026 rank card, which was released earlier. Candidates should refer to their rank card details while checking the seat allotment result.

Cut Off Marks and Qualifying Marks

SBTET AP has not officially released college-wise or branch-wise cut off marks for the Round 1 seat allotment, as allotment depends on the dynamic combination of rank, category, and web options rather than a fixed cut off. The qualifying marks to be included in the AP POLYCET 2026 rank list are as follows:

  • OC, BC, and EWS candidates: Minimum 36 out of 120 marks (30%)
  • SC and ST candidates: No minimum qualifying marks; rank is allotted purely on merit

Candidates looking for previous years' rank-vs-college trends should treat them only as a rough reference, since actual cut offs vary every year based on seat availability and demand.

How to Check AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result 2026

Candidates can follow the steps below to check and download their AP POLYCET 2026 seat allotment letter once it is released:

  • Visit the official AP POLYCET counselling portal, polycet.ap.gov.in.
  • Click on the "Round 1 Seat Allotment Result" or "Seat Allocation" link on the homepage.
  • Log in using your ICR Form Number, Hall Ticket Number, and Date of Birth.
  • Your AP POLYCET Round 1 Seat Allotment Result will be displayed on the screen.
  • Pay the prescribed allotment fee of Rs 800 online to download the allotment letter.
  • Download the seat allotment letter and take a printout for reporting at the college.

Details Mentioned on the AP POLYCET Seat Allotment Letter

The AP POLYCET Round 1 seat allotment letter typically carries the following details:

  • Candidate's name, hall ticket number, and rank
  • Category and reservation details
  • Name of the allotted polytechnic college
  • Allotted diploma branch or course
  • Fee to be paid at the time of reporting
  • Reporting deadline at the allotted college

What to Do After the AP POLYCET Seat Allotment Result

Candidates allotted a seat in Round 1 must complete the following steps within the announced deadlines to confirm their admission:

  • Download and verify all details on the seat allotment letter.
  • Complete online self-reporting on the counselling portal confirming acceptance of the allotted seat.
  • Physically report to the allotted polytechnic college between July 7 and July 10, 2026.
  • Submit original documents and pay the remaining admission fee at the college.

Candidates who are not satisfied with their Round 1 allotment can wait for the subsequent rounds, including the internal sliding round and the spot round for seats that remain vacant after Round 1 reporting.

Documents Required for Reporting at the Allotted College

Candidates should carry the following original documents along with photocopies while reporting at the allotted college:

  • AP POLYCET 2026 Seat Allotment Letter
  • AP POLYCET 2026 Hall Ticket
  • AP POLYCET 2026 Rank Card
  • Memo of marks of SSC or its equivalent examination
  • Transfer certificate and study certificate
  • Income and caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Special category certificates such as NCC, PH, CAP, or Sports (if applicable)
  • Fee payment receipt or bank challan

Document verification is generally already completed during the Help Line Centre stage of counselling, so candidates whose certificates were verified earlier are not required to undergo re-verification at the college, unless specifically instructed by the institute.

AP POLYCET 2026 Counselling and Selection Process

The complete AP POLYCET 2026 counselling process includes the following stages:

  • Online registration and counselling fee payment
  • Certificate verification (online or at Help Line Centres)
  • Web options entry for colleges and branches
  • Round 1 seat allotment based on rank, category, and options filled
  • Self-reporting and physical reporting at the allotted college
  • Subsequent rounds, including sliding and spot rounds, for vacant seats

Important Instructions for Candidates

  • Keep your login credentials, including ICR Form Number and Hall Ticket Number, ready before checking the result.
  • Both online self-reporting and physical reporting at the college are mandatory; missing either step may lead to cancellation of the allotted seat.
  • Check the official portal regularly, as counselling dates can be revised by the authority.
  • Retain multiple copies of the seat allotment letter for future reference during the admission process.
  • Candidates dissatisfied with the Round 1 allotment should wait for the sliding and spot rounds rather than dropping out of counselling.
