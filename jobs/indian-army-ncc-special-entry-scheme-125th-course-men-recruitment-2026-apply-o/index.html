Indian Army NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course Eligibility 2026

Candidates wondering about eligibility for the NCC Special Entry Scheme 125th Course can note that the entry is open only to unmarried male graduates who meet specific age, nationality, and NCC certification requirements. Wards of Battle Casualties have a slightly relaxed set of conditions, explained separately below. A candidate must be a citizen of India, a subject of Nepal, or a person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, or specified East African countries with the intention of permanently settling in India, subject to a certificate of eligibility from the Government of India (not required for Gorkha subjects of Nepal). Salary and Pay Scale After Commissioning

Selected candidates commissioned as Lieutenant draw pay as per Level 10 (₹56,100 – ₹1,77,500) in the Defence Pay Matrix, along with Military Service Pay of ₹15,500 per month. The approximate CTC on commissioning is ₹17–18 lakh per annum , excluding free medical cover and one home-town travel benefit per year. During the 49-week training period at OTA Chennai, cadets receive a fixed stipend of ₹56,100 per month . On successful commissioning, this training period is not counted as commissioned service, though admissible allowance arrears for the training period are paid to cadets.

Indian Army NCC Special Entry Scheme 125th Course (Men) : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for the NCC Special Entry Scheme 125th Course 2026?
A
The online application closes on 20 August 2026 at 1500 hrs. Applications opened on 20 July 2026 at 1500 hrs.
Q2How many vacancies are there in the NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course?
A
There are 70 total vacancies - 63 for the General category and 7 reserved for Wards of Battle Casualties of Indian Army personnel.
Q3What is the age limit for NCC Special Entry Scheme 125th Course?
A
Candidates must be 19 to 25 years old as on 01 January 2027, i.e., born between 02 January 2002 and 01 January 2008 (both dates inclusive).
Q4What educational qualification is required for NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course eligibility?
A
A Bachelor's Degree from a recognised university along with a minimum 'B' Grade in the NCC 'C' Certificate examination. Final-year students can also apply if they complete their degree before the course starts.
Q5Is there an application fee for NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course?
A
The official notification does not specify any application fee for this entry.
Q6What is the salary during training and after commissioning?
A
Cadets receive a stipend of ₹56,100 per month during the 49-week training at OTA Chennai. After commissioning, the approximate CTC is ₹17–18 lakh per annum as a Lieutenant (Pay Level 10), excluding free medical cover and annual home-town travel.
Q7What is the selection process for NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course?
A
The process includes Application, Shortlisting (based on bonus marks for NCC 'C' Certificate and graduation performance), SSB Interview, Medical Examination, Merit List, and finally the Joining Letter for training at OTA Chennai.
Q8Where will the SSB interview be conducted?
A
SSB interviews will be conducted at Selection Centres in Prayagraj, Bhopal, Bengaluru, and Jalandhar, likely in November/December 2026, for a maximum duration of five days.
Q9Can married candidates apply for this NCC Special Entry Scheme?
A
No, only unmarried male candidates (including unmarried Wards of Battle Casualties) are eligible to apply for this entry.
Q10What is the official website to apply online for NCC Special Entry Scheme 125 Course Bharti 2026?
A
Candidates must apply only through www.joinindianarmy.nic.in. No other website or offline mode is valid for this recruitment.
