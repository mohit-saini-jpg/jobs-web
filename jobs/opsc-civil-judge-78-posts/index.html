FAQs

Q1What is the last date to apply online for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application form for OPSC Civil Judge (Junior Division) Recruitment 2026 is 8 June 2026 up to 5:00 PM through the official OPSC portal.
Q2How many vacancies are released in OPSC Civil Judge 2026 notification?
A
The Odisha Public Service Commission has announced a total of 78 vacancies for Civil Judge (Junior Division) in Odisha Judicial Service, including 26 posts reserved for women and 3 posts for persons with benchmark disabilities.
Q3What is the age limit for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must be between 23 and 42 years of age as on 1 April 2025, with upper age relaxations up to 5 years for SC, ST, SEBC, women and certain defence personnel, 10 years for PwBD and 7 years for eligible law clerk or research assistant experience holders.
Q4What qualifications are required for the Odisha Judicial Service Civil Judge exam 2026?
A
Applicants must hold a graduate degree in Law (LLB) from a recognized university and have at least three years’ practice as an advocate as on 1 April 2025, with law‑clerk or research experience to judges or constitutional authorities also countable under specified conditions.
Q5Is Odia language knowledge compulsory for OPSC Civil Judge 2026?
A
Yes, candidates must be able to read, write and speak Odia and must have passed HSC or an equivalent examination with Odia as a subject or possess a Middle English School standard Odia language certificate from an approved board.
Q6What is the application fee for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?
A
General, SEBC and other non‑exempt candidates must pay Rs. 700 as examination fee online, while SC, ST and PwBD candidates are fully exempt; the fee is refundable only to those who actually appear in the preliminary examination.
Q7What is the selection process for Odisha Judicial Service Civil Judge 2026?
A
The selection process comprises three stages: a preliminary objective‑type written examination for screening, a descriptive main written examination, and a 100‑mark interview, with final merit based on combined mains and interview scores only.
Q8Is there negative marking in the OPSC Civil Judge preliminary exam 2026?
A
Yes, the preliminary examination consists of 100 multiple‑choice questions of 1 mark each, and there is negative marking of 25 percent of the mark assigned to a question, meaning 0.25 mark is deducted for every wrong answer.
