How to Apply

  • 1H.S.C. or equivalent certificate (age proof)
  • 2Bachelor's Degree of Law (LLB) certificate issued by the concerned University
  • 3All mark lists in support of LLB examination
  • 4Odia test pass certificate (if not passed HSC/equivalent with Odia as a subject)
  • 5Caste Certificate for SEBC/SC/ST (in prescribed format, from competent authority of Odisha; SEBC certificate must not be more than 3 years old before the date of application submission)
  • 6Certificate of practice (3 years minimum as advocate as on 01-04-2025) duly certified by Principal Judicial Officer of a Court or an Advocate of that Court with minimum 10 years' standing endorsed by Principal Judicial Officer
  • 7Certificate of Provisional Enrolment/Registration with the concerned State Bar Council
  • 8Permanent Disability Certificate (PwBD candidates – minimum 40% permanent disability; temporary disability certificate not accepted)
  • 9Ex-Serviceman Release Certificate (if applicable)
  • 10No Objection Certificate from competent authority (Government Servants)
  • 11Character certificate from Principal/Proctor/Dean/Professor of institution last studied
  • 12Four recent passport size photographs (unsigned and unattested, within 06 months)
  • 13Any proof of identity
  • 14Third Gender/Transgender candidates must upload Identity Certificate/Card issued by Competent Authority
  • 15Only candidates possessing the requisite qualifications, within the prescribed age limit and fulfilling all eligibility conditions strictly by the closing date will be considered eligible.
  • 16No hard copy/offline applications will be accepted – online submission only.
  • 17Examination fee is non-refundable in general; it will be refunded only to those who appear in the Preliminary Written Examination.
  • 18Applications without payment of examination fees (except SC/ST/PwD candidates) will be outrightly rejected.
  • 19SC/ST/SEBC reservation and relaxation are admissible only to SC/ST/SEBC of Odisha by birth. OBC certificates will not be accepted in lieu of SEBC certificates.
  • 20SEBC candidates must submit Non-Creamy Layer Caste Certificate not more than 3 years old as on the date of submission.
  • 21Community (caste status) once mentioned in the online application form shall not be changed subsequently under any circumstances.
  • 22PwBD candidates must upload Permanent Disability Certificate only; temporary disability certificates will not be accepted.
  • 23Government servants must obtain NOC from their Head of Office well in advance and produce it at document verification stage.
  • 24Women candidates must submit caste certificates by birth (showing "daughter of…"); caste certificates by virtue of marriage (showing "wife of…") are not acceptable.
  • 25Any complaint on the conduct of examination must be sent to the Grievance Wing by email ([email protected]) within 7 days of completion of the examination.
  • 26Candidates submitting forged/manipulated documents will be debarred from appearing in OPSC examinations for 3 years.
  • 27Mobile phones and electronic gadgets are banned inside examination premises.
  • 28Candidates are advised to visit https://opsc.gov.in regularly for updates. For technical support during online registration, candidates may contact OPSC Technical Support at Telephone No. 0671-2304707 (10:30 AM to 1:30 PM & 2:00 PM to 5:30 PM on working days).
  • 29All decisions of the Commission regarding eligibility, conduct of examinations, selection, and other matters are final and binding.
