Qualification / Eligibility

Details
  1. Understanding the Indian Navy SSC officer eligibility 2026 is the most critical step before filling the application. Eligibility differs by branch - both in terms of educational qualification streams and date-of-birth range. Candidates must meet conditions of nationality as laid down by the Government of India
  2. The minimum educational requirement for most branches is a BE/B.Tech degree with minimum 60% marks in the relevant discipline. Here is a branch-wise summary of qualification requirements:
  3. Executive Branch (GS(X)/Hydro): BE/B.Tech in any discipline with minimum 60% marks. Pilot: BE/B.Tech in any discipline with minimum 60% marks. Candidates must have 60% aggregate in Class X and XII and minimum 60% marks in English in Class X or Class XII. Naval Air Operations Officer (Observers): BE/B.Tech in any discipline with minimum 60% marks; 60% aggregate in Class X and XII and minimum 60% marks in English in Class X or Class XII. Air Traffic Controller (ATC): Same as Pilot / Observers entry. Logistics: (a) BE/B.Tech in any discipline with First Class; OR (b) MBA with First Class; OR (c) B.Sc/B.Com/B.Sc (IT) with First Class along with PG Diploma in Finance/Logistics/Supply Chain Management/Material Management; OR (d) MCA/M.Sc (IT) with First Class. Naval Armament Inspectorate Cadre (NAIC): BE/B.Tech with minimum 60% marks in streams including Mechanical, Aeronautical, Electrical & Electronics, Electronics, Micro Electronics, Industrial Production, Instrumentation & Control, Electronics & Communication, Industrial Engineering, Aero Space, Control Engineering, Information Technology, Production, Instrumentation, Applied Electronics & Instrumentation, Electronics & Tele Communication, Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Computer Application, Metallurgy, Metallurgical, Chemical, Material Science; OR Post Graduate degree in Electronics/Physics. Candidates must have 60% aggregate marks in Class X and XII and minimum 60% marks in English in Class X or Class XII. Education: Multiple streams accepted including MSc/MA in Maths/Operational Research/Physics/Applied Physics/Meteorology/Oceanography/Atmospheric Sciences, MA (History) with min 55%, BE/B.Tech in Mechanical/Production/Electrical/Electronics/Communication/Electronics and Instrumentation, ME/M.Tech in Electronics/Communication/Controls/Radar/Microwave/Optical Fibre Communication/Digital Signal Processing/VLSI etc., ME/M.Tech in Thermal/Production/Machine Design/Mechatronics/Meteorology/Oceanography/Atmospheric Sciences (with Physics and Maths at graduation). Minimum 60% marks in Class X, XII, and minimum 60% marks in English in Class X or XII. Engineering Branch (GS): BE/B.Tech with minimum 60% marks in Marine Engineering, Instrumentation Engineering, Production Engineering, Industrial Engineering & Management, Aero Space Engineering, Automobiles Engineering, Control Engineering, Instrumentation & Control, Metallurgy Engineering, Mechatronics Engineering, Aeronautical Engineering, Mechanical/Mechanical with Automation. Submarine Tech Engineering: Same as Engineering Branch (GS). Electrical Branch (GS): BE/B.Tech with minimum 60% marks in Electrical Engineering, Electronics Engineering, Electrical & Electronics, Electronics & Tele Communication, Applied Electronics & Instrumentation, Instrumentation, Tele Communication, Electronics & Communication, Power Electronics, Power Engineering, Instrumentation & Control, Electronics & Instrumentation, Applied Electronics and Communication (AEC). Submarine Tech Electrical: Same as Electrical Branch (GS). Naval Constructor: BE/B.Tech with minimum 60% marks in Mechanical/Mechanical with Automation, Civil Engineering, Aeronautical Engineering, Aero Space Engineering, Marine Engineering, Ship Technology, Naval Architecture, Metallurgy, Ship Building, Ship Design, Ocean Engineering

FAQs

Q1Has Indian Navy officially released the SSC Officer Recruitment 2026 notification?
A
Yes, the Indian Navy has officially released the Short Service Commission (SSC) Officer Recruitment 2026 notification for the Jun 2027 (AT 27) Course. A total of approximately 275 vacancies (tentative) are available across 12 branches/cadres including Executive, Pilot, Naval Air Operations Officer, ATC, Logistics, NAIC, Education, Engineering, Electrical, Naval Constructor, and Submarine Tech branches.
Q2What is the last date to apply for Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026 is 27 July 2026. The application window opens on 25 June 2026.
Q3What is the official website to apply for Indian Navy SSC Officer 2026?
A
Candidates must apply online at the official Indian Navy website: www.joinindiannavy.gov.in.
Q4What is the age limit for Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026?
A
The age limit varies by branch. As a general range, candidates born between 02 July 2002 and 01 January 2008 are eligible for most branches. For Pilot and Observer entries, the date of birth range is 02 July 2003 to 01 July 2008. Refer to the branch-wise vacancy table for exact date-of-birth requirements.
Q5What is the educational qualification for Indian Navy SSC Officer 2026?
A
Most branches require a BE/B.Tech degree with minimum 60% marks in relevant engineering/technology streams. The Logistics branch also accepts MBA, B.Sc/B.Com with PG Diploma, or MCA/M.Sc (IT) with First Class. The Education branch accepts MSc/MA/ME/M.Tech in specified subjects. Exact stream-wise requirements differ by branch.
Q6Is the Indian Navy SSC recruitment open for women candidates in 2026?
A
Yes, the Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026 is open to both men and women for most branches. However, some branches have a capped number of vacancies for women candidates, and a few entries such as Submarine Tech Engineering (Ser 9) and Submarine Tech Electrical (Ser 11) are open only for men. All candidates must be unmarried at the time of application.
Q7What is the starting salary of an Indian Navy Sub Lieutenant in 2026?
A
The initial gross salary of a Sub Lieutenant is approximately Rs 1,20,000/- per month along with applicable allowances. Pilot/NAOO and Submarine allowance of Rs 31,250/- is admissible after successful completion of training.
Q8What is the selection process for Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026?
A
The selection process involves: (1) Shortlisting based on normalised marks in qualifying degree, (2) SSB Interview at designated SSB Centres, (3) Medical Examination for SSB-recommended candidates, and (4) Preparation of final merit list based on SSB marks and medical clearance.
Q9What is the tenure of Short Service Commission in Indian Navy?
A
Selected candidates will be granted Short Service Commission initially for 12 years, extendable by a maximum of 02 years, subject to service requirement, performance, medical eligibility, and willingness of candidates.
Q10Can final-year students apply for Indian Navy SSC Officer Recruitment 2026?
A
Yes, candidates who are in the final year of their qualifying degree can apply. However, they must fulfil the required educational qualifications before joining INA, Ezhimala by June 2027. Candidates failing to meet the stipulated educational criteria will not be permitted to join the Academy.
