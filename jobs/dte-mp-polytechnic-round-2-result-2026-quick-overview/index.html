How to Check DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 Seat Allotment Result 2026

Checking your Round 2 seat allotment result on the DTE MP official portal is a simple process. Follow the step-by-step guide below to log in, view your allotment status, and download your MP Polytechnic seat allotment letter 2026 without any confusion.

  • Visit the official DTE MP counselling website: dte.mponline.gov.in
  • On the homepage, click on the "Online Counselling" or "Diploma Courses" section for Polytechnic admissions 2026-27.
  • Click on the "Round 2 Seat Allotment Result" or "Print Allotment Letter" link.
  • Enter your login credentials: MP Polytechnic Application Number Date of Birth Password (set during registration)
  • MP Polytechnic Application Number
  • Date of Birth
  • Password (set during registration)
  • Submit the captcha code and click "Login".
  • Your Round 2 seat allotment status will appear on the screen - check your allotted college, branch, and category.
  • If a seat has been allotted, pay the non-refundable partial tuition fee online to confirm your seat.
  • Download the MP Polytechnic Round 2 allotment letter and take 2-3 printouts for future use.

Important: Candidates must download the seat allotment letter within the stipulated deadline. Failure to download and pay the fee within the deadline will result in forfeiture of the allotted seat.

What to Do After Checking DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 Result 2026

Once you have checked your MP Polytechnic Round 2 seat allotment result, the next steps are equally important to complete your admission successfully. Missing any of the following steps could lead to cancellation of your allotted seat.

If You Have Been Allotted a Seat in Round 2

  • Pay the Partial Tuition Fee: Log in to dte.mponline.gov.in and pay the required non-refundable partial tuition fee online to confirm your seat. Payment can be made via Paytm, credit/debit card, or net banking.
  • Download the Allotment Letter: After fee payment, download your Round 2 seat allotment letter and take at least 2-3 printouts.
  • Report to the Allotted College: Visit your allotted Polytechnic college within the reporting window with all required original documents.
  • Complete Document Verification: Submit your original documents at the college for verification during the admission process.

If You Have Not Been Allotted a Seat in Round 2

  • Wait for the announcement of College Level Counselling (CLC), which is conducted by DTE MP for seats remaining vacant after all centralized rounds.
  • Keep checking dte.mponline.gov.in for any additional rounds or notifications.
  • Ensure your registration details and documents are updated on the portal for any future rounds.

Documents Required to Report to the College After Round 2 Allotment

All candidates allotted a seat in the DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 must carry the following documents in original as well as self-attested photocopies when reporting to the allotted institute. Missing any document may delay or cancel the admission process.

  • DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 Seat Allotment Letter (printed copy)
  • Class 10 (High School) Marksheet and Pass Certificate
  • Class 10 Admit Card / Roll Number Card (if required)
  • Domicile Certificate of Madhya Pradesh
  • Category Certificate (OBC / SC / ST / EWS, if applicable)
  • Income Certificate (if applicable, for fee concession categories)
  • Aadhaar Card (original and photocopy)
  • Recent Passport-Size Photographs (4-6 copies)
  • Character Certificate from the last attended school/institution
  • Migration Certificate (if studied outside MP board)
  • Transfer Certificate (TC) from the last school/institution
  • Fee payment receipt generated from the DTE MP portal

Important: As per DTE MP norms, the Transfer Certificate (TC) and Migration Certificate (MC) are generally retained by the allotted institution after submission. Candidates should clarify this with their allotted college at the time of reporting.

Who Is Eligible to Participate in DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 Counselling 2026?

Not all candidates need to participate in Round 2. Understanding your eligibility status helps you take the right action without any confusion. Round 2 of the MP Polytechnic counselling 2026 is open to the following candidates.

  • Candidates who appeared in Round 1 but were not allotted any seat due to lower merit rank or unavailability of preferred choices.
  • Candidates who were allotted a seat in Round 1 but did not report to the college or chose not to accept the seat.
  • Candidates who wish to upgrade their allotment to a better college or branch than what was allotted in Round 1 (subject to seat availability).
  • Candidates who registered in Round 1 do not need fresh registration - they can directly participate in Round 2 choice filling using their existing credentials.

DTE MP Polytechnic Round 2 Counselling 2026 - Complete Process Explained

The MP Polytechnic Round 2 counselling follows a structured online process. Candidates who are new to Round 2 or need a refresher on how the process works can follow the step-by-step breakdown below.

Step 1 - Log in to the DTE MP Counselling Portal

Candidates who participated in Round 1 can directly log in at dte.mponline.gov.in using their application number, date of birth, and password. No fresh registration is required for Round 2 if you were registered in Round 1.

Step 2 - Choice Filling for Round 2

After logging in, fill in your preferences for colleges and branches in order of priority. The Round 2 choice filling window opened on 21 June 2026. Candidates must lock their choices before the deadline; unlocked choices are not considered for allotment.

Step 3 - Seat Allotment Based on Merit and Choices

The DTE MP authority processes all registered choices against available seats using the merit rank from the MP Polytechnic Merit List 2026. The Round 2 allotment is released on 23 June 2026. Seats are allocated based on merit rank, category, and choice order.

Step 4 - Fee Payment and Allotment Letter Download

Candidates allotted a seat must pay the non-refundable partial tuition fee online and download the allotment letter from the portal. This confirms the seat reservation.

Step 5 - Reporting to the Allotted Institute

After downloading the allotment letter and paying the fee, candidates must physically report to the allotted Polytechnic college within the prescribed reporting window, along with all original documents for verification and final admission.
