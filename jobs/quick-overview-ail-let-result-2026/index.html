Quick Overview - AIL LET Result 2026 : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 13/07/2026

AIL LET Result 2026 is scheduled to be declared today, July 13, 2026, by the Army Institute of Law (AIL), Mohali, on the official website ail.ac.in. Candidates who appeared for the AIL LET 2026 entrance exam on July 5, 2026 can log in with their registered credentials to check their score, rank, and qualifying status. This article covers everything available in official notifications and reliable reports about the AIL LET 2026 result, scorecard download steps, eligibility, counselling, and more. Wherever specific information has not been officially confirmed, it is stated clearly rather than assumed.

Educational Qualification

  • Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination from the Punjab School Education Board, or an equivalent examination recognised by Punjabi University, Patiala.
  • Minimum aggregate marks required in the qualifying examination: 45%.

Category Eligibility (Wards of Army Personnel)

Applicants under the Wards of Army Personnel category must fall under one of the recognised sub-categories defined by AIL (for example, wards of serving, retired, or deceased Army personnel). The exact sub-category definitions were not detailed in the sources reviewed for this article; candidates should refer to the official AIL LET 2026 information bulletin on ail.ac.in for the precise category descriptions.

Age Limit

No specific age limit for AIL LET 2026 was found in the official notification or the sources reviewed. Candidates should verify this directly on ail.ac.in, as the requirement may be tied to the age criteria of the qualifying 10+2 examination board rather than a separate AIL LET age bar.

Minimum Qualifying Marks

As per available information, the minimum qualifying mark in AIL LET is 25% of the total marks, applicable across all categories. This is a baseline threshold; qualifying it does not guarantee a seat, as final admission depends on merit rank, category, and seat availability. The official category-wise cutoff for 2026 will be released by AIL, Mohali, along with or shortly after the result. Candidates should confirm the final cutoff on ail.ac.in once declared.

How to Check and Download AIL LET Result 2026

  • Visit the official website: ail.ac.in.
  • Look for the "AIL LET Result 2026" or "Candidate Login" link on the homepage.
  • Enter your registration/application number and password (or date of birth), the same credentials used to download your admit card.
  • Click "Submit" to view your result on the candidate dashboard.
  • Your AIL LET 2026 scorecard will display your score, rank, and qualifying status.
  • Download and save the scorecard, and take a printout for counselling and admission purposes.

Details Mentioned on the AIL LET 2026 Scorecard

  • Candidate's name
  • Application number and exam roll number
  • Section-wise performance
  • Overall marks obtained
  • All India rank and category rank
  • Qualifying status

After downloading the scorecard, candidates should verify that all details are correct. In case of any discrepancy, they should contact the AIL LET helpdesk immediately.

Merit List and Tie-Breaking Rules

Army Institute of Law will prepare a merit list based on AIL LET 2026 scores. The final course-wise merit list is expected to be published after the result declaration on July 13, 2026. If two or more candidates secure the same marks, the institute uses the following tie-breaking method as per available reports:

  • The candidate with higher marks in the qualifying (10+2) examination is ranked higher.

Further tie-breaking criteria beyond this, if any, were not specified in the sources reviewed. Candidates should check the official information bulletin for the complete tie-breaking policy.

AIL LET 2026 Counselling Process

Candidates shortlisted as per the AIL LET 2026 merit list must participate in the counselling round. Based on available information, the process includes:

  • Counselling will be conducted in online mode (not offline), reportedly via a Google Meet link shared over email.
  • Candidates must send scanned copies of the required original documents via email to [email protected].
  • Candidates must pay the admission-cum-course fee online through the payment link provided on the official website.
  • Shortlisted candidates should carry the required documents to prove their eligibility during subsequent verification.
  • If seats remain vacant after the initial round, further rounds of counselling may be conducted, and cutoffs may be relaxed in later rounds.

The exact fee amount for admission/counselling was not confirmed in the sources reviewed for this article. Candidates should check the official fee structure on ail.ac.in before making any payment.

Documents Required for AIL LET 2026 Counselling

  • AIL LET 2026 admit card and scorecard/result printout
  • Class 10 and Class 12 mark sheets and certificates
  • Proof of category (Wards of Army/Navy/Air Force Personnel, where applicable)
  • Valid photo ID proof
  • Passport-size photographs (as specified in the official bulletin)

This list reflects commonly required documents for such counselling processes; candidates must refer to the official AIL LET 2026 counselling notice for the complete and exact document checklist, as it was not fully itemised in the sources reviewed.

Important Instructions for Candidates

  • Use only the login credentials issued during registration/admit card download to check the result.
  • Cross-check every detail on the scorecard immediately after downloading it.
  • Report any discrepancy in the scorecard to the AIL LET helpdesk without delay.
  • Keep printed and digital copies of the scorecard safe for counselling and admission.
  • The institute has reportedly not confirmed acceptance of result re-verification requests; candidates should confirm this directly with AIL if relevant.
  • Candidates awaiting confirmation of their qualifying (Class 12) exam result should apply to their examination board to send a confidential result directly to the Principal, AIL, as per reported guidance. It remains the candidate's responsibility to ensure this reaches the institute.
