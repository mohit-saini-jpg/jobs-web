IOB Chief Financial Officer: Roles & Responsibilities

Financial Reporting & Compliance: Overseeing the statutory auditing process, reporting audit results as per SEBI requirements, monitoring critical financial parameters (Non-Interest Income, ROA, ROE etc.), coordinating with NFRA and RBI on auditor-related issues, and handling Whole Bank Long Form Audit Report (WBLFAR) matters. Tax Compliance: Optimization of the Bank's tax liability, GST and TDS compliance. Accounting Systems: Developing and maintaining accounting systems per Accounting Standards and RBI Guidelines, Internal Financial Controls over Financial Reporting (IFCOFR), and implementation of IFRS/IND AS. Management Reporting: Coordinating with the Financial Control Department for review of regional and Whole Bank performance. International Banking Activities: Supporting the International Banking Group on accounting and tax compliance as per local regulators. Other Activities: Ove

IOB Chief Financial Officer Selection Process 2026

Selection will be through Personal Interview/Discussion . The Bank may carry out a preliminary screening of applications to shortlist eligible candidates based on qualifications, suitability, and experience. Total marks allocated for the Personal Interview: 100 . Minimum qualifying marks: 50 (45 for OBC/SC/ST/PwBD candidates). In case multiple candidates score the same cut-off marks, ranking will be done according to age, in descending order. The Bank may change the mode of selection depending on the number of applications received. Merely fulfilling the minimum qualification and experience criteria does not automatically entitle a candidate to an interview call; the Bank may consider additional qualifications, greater experience, and level of responsibilities handled while shortlisting.

IOB Chief Financial Officer: Joining, Bond & Probation

The selected candidate, at the time of joining, must execute a Bond of Rs. 3 lakhs to serve the Bank for a stipulated period of 3 years. The Bond will be invoked if the candidate resigns before the expiry of the bond period from the date of joining. On joining, the selected candidate will be on probation for a period of 1 year and subjected to continuous assessment. Confirmation depends on qualifying the assessment as per standards determined by the Bank. If the candidate fails to achieve the minimum stipulated standards, their services may be terminated. Place of Posting: Central Office, Chennai.
