How To Apply

  • Applications must be submitted online only through the BPSC Online Application Portal (bpsconline.bihar.gov.in), accessible on the official website https://bpsc.bihar.gov.in. Hard copy applications will not be accepted under any circumstances. The online portal will be active from 07-05-2026 to 31-05-2026. New users must complete One Time Registration (OTR) on the BPSC portal - only one OTR per candidate is permitted. Multiple OTR registrations can lead to rejection of candidature.
  • All supporting documents must be uploaded in PDF format (max 100 KB per document) at the time of filling the online application. A valid and working email ID and mobile number are mandatory and must be kept active until final results are published. Candidates must upload a live webcam photograph during application - the background must be white or light-coloured with sufficient light. Hindi and English signatures must both be scanned and uploaded (jpg/jpeg format, max 15 KB, dimensions 220x100 pixels).
  • Documents Required to be Uploaded at the Time of Online Application:
  • Matriculation / 10th Certificate (age proof and name verification)
  • Graduation degree / Provisional Certificate (educational qualification proof)
  • SC / ST Caste Certificate (if applicable) - issued by competent State Government authority
  • EBC / BC Creamy Layer Exemption Certificate (if applicable)
  • EWS Income & Asset Certificate (if applicable) - issued by competent authority as per Annexure-I (Proforma-I)
  • Permanent Residential Address Proof (for Bihar domicile/reservation claims)
  • Freedom Fighter descendant proof (for Pota/Poti/Nati/Natini - grandfather's freedom fighter certificate)
  • PwBD / Divyang Certificate (if applicable) - issued by Civil Surgeon/CMO of Government hospital
  • Age relaxation proof (if claiming upper age relaxation)
  • The BPSC OTR portal is accessible at: https://bpsconline.bihar.gov.in/candidate/login. Candidates facing any grievances with OTR or application may use the Grievance Tab on the portal.
  • Apply Online at: https://bpsconline.bihar.gov.in

Important Instructions

  • Only Indian citizens with valid Bihar domicile (for reservation benefits) are eligible to apply; candidates from outside Bihar can apply under UR category only. Candidates must possess the prescribed minimum qualification (Graduation) on the closing date of application. No hard copy applications will be accepted - online submission only via BPSC OTR portal. Examination fee is non-refundable; incomplete applications without fee will be rejected. Only one OTR registration per candidate is allowed; multiple OTR registrations can lead to rejection. All documents must be uploaded in PDF format at the time of online application - hard copy submissions to the Commission office are not required. Married women must provide their father's (not husband's) caste/creamy layer certificate for reservation claims. For PwBD reservation, only PwBD certificate issued by Civil Surgeon/CMO of a Government hospital will be accepted as provisionally valid. Bihar Government employees are allowed maximum 5 attempts at competitive examinations after regular appointment. Candidates who have been directed to not appear by UPSC/State PSC/other selection bodies due to misconduct are not eligible to appear in this examination. Submitting wrong/incomplete/misleading information in the online application will make the candidate solely responsible for adverse consequences. All updates/corrigendum/errata will be published only on https://bpsc.bihar.gov.in - candidates must check regularly. Negative marking of 1/3 mark applies for each wrong answer in the Preliminary Examination. Candidates must keep 4 copies of their recent photograph safely for use when requested by the Commission. The detailed syllabus is available on the BPSC website at https://bpsc.bihar.gov.in. All decisions of the Commission regarding eligibility, selection, and other matters are final and binding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Recruitment 2026?

BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Recruitment 2026 is a part of the Integrated 72nd Combined Competitive Examination conducted by Bihar Public Service Commission to fill 44 Sugar Cane Officer posts in the Sugarcane Industry Department, Government of Bihar.

Q2. How many vacancies are there for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer 2026?

A total of 44 vacancies have been announced for the Sugar Cane Officer post, distributed across categories UR, EWS, EBC, BC, SC and ST as per Bihar Government reservation rules.

Q3. What are the important dates for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Recruitment 2026?

As per the 72nd CCE schedule, the notification is dated 05/06 May 2026, the online application starts from 06–07 May 2026, and the last date to apply online is 30–31 May 2026 (exact window to be confirmed from the official PDF).

Q4. What is the eligibility criteria for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer 2026?

Candidates must possess a Bachelor’s Degree from a recognized University, with preference for Agriculture or allied disciplines, and must satisfy all other conditions of the 72nd CCE notification such as age, domicile and category criteria.

Q5. What is the age limit for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Recruitment 2026?

For Sugar Cane Officer, the minimum age is 20/21/22 years (depending on specific post rules) and the maximum age is 37 years for General (Male), 40 years for OBC and all Female candidates, and 42 years for SC/ST candidates, with relaxations as per Bihar Government norms.

Q6. What is the application fee for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer 2026?

The examination fee for each BPSC exam (including the 72nd CCE) is Rs. 100/- per examination, and candidates without Aadhaar must pay an additional Rs. 200/- as biometric fee, making the total Rs. 300/- for non‑Aadhaar applicants.

Q7. What is the selection process for BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Recruitment 2026?

The selection process comprises three stages: Preliminary Examination (objective and qualifying), Main Examination (descriptive and merit‑deciding) and Interview, conducted under the overall framework of the BPSC 72nd CCE.

Q8. Is there negative marking in BPSC Sugar Cane Officer Prelims?

Yes, in line with BPSC CCE rules, there is negative marking of 1/3rd mark for each wrong answer in the Preliminary Examination for Sugar Cane Officer under the 72nd CCE.
