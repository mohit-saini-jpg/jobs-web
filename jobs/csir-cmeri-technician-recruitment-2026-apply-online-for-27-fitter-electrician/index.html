CSIR CMERI Technician Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

CSIR-Central Mechanical Engineering Research Institute (CSIR-CMERI), Durgapur, has released Advertisement No. 01/2026 (No. 8/2/2026(1)-Rct dated 4 July 2026) inviting online applications for 27 posts of Technician (1) [Group-II] across seven trades at CSIR-CMERI Durgapur (West Bengal) and its Extension Centre, the Centre of Excellence for Farm Machinery, Ludhiana (Punjab). Eligible candidates can apply online at www.cmeri.res.in from 4 July 2026 (10:00 AM IST) to 3 August 2026 (5:00 PM IST).

Educational Qualification & Desirable Experience (Trade-wise)

For all seven trades, the essential qualification follows a common pattern based on SSC/10th Standard with Science subjects and minimum 55% marks, combined with an ITI/trade certificate or equivalent work experience. Desirable experience is trade-specific and is summarized below. Essential Qualification: SSC / 10th Standard or equivalent with Science subjects, minimum 55% marks, and an ITI certificate or national/state trade certificate in Fitter from a recognized institution; OR the same qualification with 2 years’ full-time apprentice trainee experience in Fitter from a recognized institution; OR the same qualification with 3 years’ work experience in Fitter in a Ministry/Department/Organization/PSU/Autonomous Body under the Government of India/State/UT. Desirable Experience: Ability to read and interpret technical drawings and schematics; proficiency in fitting, assembling, and mainta

Exam Pattern & Selection Process

Selection is a three-stage process: screening of applications, a qualifying Trade Test, and a Competitive Written Examination. This section explains the marking scheme and qualifying criteria for the written stage. Online applications received within the stipulated last date are screened by a duly constituted Screening Committee, which shortlists candidates for a Trade Test in the relevant trade. The Trade Test is qualifying in nature ; its syllabus will be notified separately on the official website. Candidates who qualify in the Trade Test are called for the Competitive Written Examination. Paper-II and Paper-III are evaluated only for candidates who secure the minimum threshold marks (to be determined by the Selection Committee) in Paper-I.
