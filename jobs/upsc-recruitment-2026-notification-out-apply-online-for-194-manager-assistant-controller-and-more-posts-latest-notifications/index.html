Selection Process

  • Selection will be made by Recruitment by Selection - through Interview or Recruitment Test followed by Interview as decided by UPSC on a post-by-post basis. In the event of a large number of applications
  • UPSC will adopt shortlisting criteria including desirable qualifications
  • higher educational qualifications
  • higher experience
  • experience before/after essential qualification
  • or by conducting a Recruitment Test. The summoning of candidates for interview conveys no assurance of selection. Appointment orders will be issued by the respective Ministry.
  • Stage 1 - Application Scrutiny and Shortlisting
  • All applications are scrutinized for eligibility. If applications are large in number
  • UPSC may shortlist based on: (a) Desirable Qualifications; (b) higher educational qualifications; (c) higher relevant experience; (d) counting experience before/after essential qualification; (e) invoking experience even where not mentioned as EQ/DQ; or (f) by holding a Recruitment Test. Candidates should mention all qualifications and experience in the relevant field over and above the minimum qualifications.
  • Stage 2 - Recruitment Test (RT) followed by Interview (wherever applicable)
  • If selection is made by Recruitment Test followed by Interview
  • candidates must achieve minimum suitability at both stages. Minimum interview marks: UR – 50
  • OBC – 45
  • SC/ST/PH – 40 (out of 100 total interview marks). Minimum marks for Recruitment Test shall be decided by UPSC on a case-by-case basis.
  • Stage 3 - Medical Examination
  • Selected candidates must be in sound bodily health and must undergo medical examination and satisfy medical authority as required by the Government.

How To Apply

  • Candidates must apply online only at https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/. The UPSC online portal has four modules: Account Creation, Universal Registration (URN), Common Application Form (CAF), and Post/Vacancy-specific module. The first three modules can be filled at any time; the fourth (vacancy-specific) module must be filled during the application window. Candidates who have already registered and have a URN can directly log in and fill the vacancy-specific module. Candidates are strongly advised to use Aadhaar Card as the ID document for seamless verification. Applications once submitted are final - no correction/alteration/modification is permitted after submission. Candidates must take a printout of the finally submitted application and carry it along with original documents if called for interview.
  • Documents to be Uploaded at the time of Online Application:
  • Live photograph captured via webcam/camera during CAF filling (mandatory)
  • Scanned recent passport-size photograph (as per instructions on UPSC website)
  • Scanned signature (three signatures one below the other on plain white paper, black ink)
  • Matriculation/10th Standard certificate or marksheet as proof of Date of Birth
  • Degree/Diploma certificate as proof of educational qualification; if not available, provisional certificate + all year marksheets
  • Experience Certificate(s) in prescribed format from Head(s) of Organisation(s) - mentioning duration, basic pay, consolidated pay, and nature of duties
  • Caste/Community certificate (SC/ST/OBC) in prescribed proforma from Competent Authority
  • OBC Non-Creamy Layer declaration in prescribed format
  • EWS certificate in prescribed proforma
  • PwBD Disability Certificate from Medical Board constituted by Central/State Government
  • Age relaxation certificate for Ex-Servicemen/Central/UT Government Employees in prescribed proforma
  • Order/letter for equivalent qualification (if applicable) citing Authority with number and date
  • Name change documents (if applicable - marriage certificate/divorce decree/affidavit as per situation)
  • Documentary support for any other claim made (Professional Registration, Publications, GATE/NET, etc.)
  • Printout of the finally submitted Online Recruitment Application must be retained - candidates will need to bring it to the interview along with original certificates. Candidates are NOT required to send printouts or documents by post or by hand to UPSC.
  • Apply Online at: https://upsconline.nic.in/ora/
  • For queries: Helpline No. 011-24041001 or 011-40303444 | Email: [email protected] (10:00 AM to 5:30 PM on all working days during the application window).

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is the last date to apply for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

The last date to submit the online application for UPSC Recruitment 2026 under Advertisement No. 05/2026 is 12 June 2026 till 06:00 PM (1800 Hrs). Candidates are advised to complete the registration and fee payment process well before the closing date to avoid server issues or technical delays. Applications submitted after the deadline will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Q2. How many vacancies are available in UPSC Recruitment 2026?

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has announced a total of 194 vacancies through Advertisement No. 05/2026. These vacancies are available for various Group A and Group B Gazetted posts across multiple Central Government Ministries and Departments. The number of vacancies may increase or decrease depending on departmental requirements.

Q3. What is the educational qualification required for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

The educational qualification for UPSC Recruitment 2026 varies depending on the post applied for. Candidates with qualifications such as BE/B.Tech, Bachelor’s Degree, Master’s Degree, Ph.D., CA, Engineering Degree, or equivalent qualifications in relevant fields are eligible to apply. Applicants must carefully check the post-wise eligibility criteria mentioned in the official notification before submitting the application form.

Q4. What is the application fee for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

Candidates belonging to the General, OBC, and EWS male categories need to pay an application fee of Rs. 25/- through online payment methods such as debit card, credit card, UPI, or internet banking. However, SC/ST/PwBD candidates and all women candidates are exempted from paying the application fee. Once paid, the fee is non-refundable under any condition.

Q5. What is the age limit for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

The age limit for UPSC Recruitment 2026 differs according to the post and department. In general, the upper age limit ranges between 30 years and 45 years for unreserved candidates, while age relaxation is available for SC, ST, OBC, PwBD, Ex-Servicemen, and Government employees as per Government of India rules. Candidates should verify the specific age criteria for their chosen post.

Q6. How can I apply online for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

Candidates can apply online for UPSC Recruitment 2026 through the official UPSC Online Recruitment Application (ORA) portal. Applicants must first complete account registration, generate a Universal Registration Number (URN), fill out the Common Application Form, and then submit the post-specific application form. After successful submission, candidates should save and print the application form for future reference.

Q7. What is the selection process for UPSC Recruitment 2026?

The selection process for UPSC Recruitment 2026 may include shortlisting, Recruitment Test (RT), and Interview, depending on the number of applications received for each post. In some cases, UPSC may directly conduct interviews if the number of eligible candidates is manageable. Final selection will be based on performance and fulfillment of eligibility requirements.

Q8. Which departments are included in UPSC Advertisement No. 05/2026?

UPSC Advertisement No. 05/2026 includes vacancies in several prestigious government departments and ministries. These include DGCA, PESO, Department of Posts, Indian Meteorological Department (IMD), Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), Ministry of Defence, Ministry of MSME, Ministry of Home Affairs, and many others. Candidates can choose posts based on their qualification and experience.
