How to Apply

  • 1The application process has two mandatory steps - Registration and Application Submission - which are separate operations but corollary to each other. Completing registration without submitting the application form means the application is incomplete and will be rejected.
  • 2Visit the OSSSC official website at www.osssc.gov.in.
  • 3On the Home page, click "Apply Online" if you are a New User (not registered earlier for any OSSSC recruitment). If you are a Registered User (already registered for any earlier OSSSC post and have a User ID), click the "Re-registration" option under the Applicant Menu to re-register for this post.
  • 4Read the instructions carefully by clicking "How do I register/re-register/apply" on the Home page before filling the Registration Form.
  • 5Complete the Registration Form first. Keep the following ready before starting: scanned signature in jpg/jpeg/png format between 20 KB and 50 KB; scanned colour passport-size photograph in jpg/jpeg/png format between 20 KB and 100 KB; valid personal email ID and mobile/cell phone number (to be kept active throughout the recruitment process).
  • 6After successful registration, log in and fill the Online Application Form with all required details and upload documents as per the guidance of the online system.
  • 7Indicate district preferences (up to the districts available) in order of priority.
  • 8Pay the examination fee of ₹500 (if applicable) using Net Banking/Credit Card/Debit Card/UPI through the Payment Gateway. After successful payment, print the Confirmation Page.
  • 9Submit the online application. Incomplete applications will be summarily rejected. No Admission Letter will be issued to candidates whose application is found incomplete.
  • 10Keep the User ID, Password, and registration number safely - these will be required for downloading the Admission Letter and future communications from OSSSC.

OSSSC Nursing Officer Recruitment 2026 - 5989 Vacancies : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026 is 13 July 2026. The online system will be automatically disabled after this date and no application will be accepted thereafter.
Q2How many vacancies are there in OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
The OSSSC has notified 5989 vacancies for the post of Nursing Officer across all 30 districts of Odisha under the Health & Family Welfare Department.
Q3What is the educational qualification required for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
Candidates must have passed 10+2, hold a Diploma in GNM or BSc Nursing from a recognised institution approved by the Indian Nursing Council, and possess a valid Registration or Renewal Certificate from the Nursing Council of Odisha as on the date of submission of the online application form.
Q4What is the age limit for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
Candidates must be between 21 and 42 years of age as on the date of advertisement (12 June 2026). SC/ST/SEBC/Women candidates get a 5-year relaxation (upper limit becomes 47 years), PwD candidates get a 10-year relaxation (upper limit becomes 52 years), and contractual Nursing Officers under OSH&FW Society/Scheme and ASHAs get an upper limit of 45 years.
Q5What is the application fee for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
The examination fee is ₹500 for all candidates except those belonging to SC, ST, and Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) categories, who are exempted from paying any fee. The fee can be paid via Net Banking, Credit Card, Debit Card, or UPI.
Q6What is the pay scale for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
The Nursing Officer post carries a pay scale of ₹29,200–92,300 at Pay Matrix Level-8, Cell-01 as per Odisha Revised Scale of Pay (ORSP) Rules 2017. The starting basic pay is ₹29,200 per month.
Q7What is the selection process for OSSSC Nursing Officer 2026?
A
Selection is based on a Written Test of 100 marks (MCQ type, OMR/CBRE mode, 2 hours duration) followed by Document Verification and preparation of a Provisional Select List. There is no interview. Minimum qualifying marks are 30% for ST/SC/PwDs/Ex-Servicemen/Sportsperson and 35% for all others.
Q8What is the exam pattern for OSSSC Nursing Officer Written Test?
A
The Written Test has 100 questions for 100 marks in 2 hours. The paper includes 60 questions from nursing syllabus subjects, 25 questions on practical skills, 10 questions on Arithmetic (High School level), and 5 questions on English (High School level). There is negative marking of 0.25 marks per wrong answer.
