ITAT Selection Process 2026-27

Recruitment is through a Written Examination, Skill Test, followed by a Personal Interview. The written examination and skill test will be conducted at 9 stations: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore, Guwahati, Lucknow, Ahmedabad and Hyderabad. Candidates must opt for two centres of choice in order of preference. If a sufficient number of candidates is not received for a particular station, ITAT has the sole discretion to cancel that centre, and the candidate will be required to appear at the second centre chosen by them. The Personal Interview carries 75 marks . Only candidates shortlisted after the Skill Test will be called for the Personal Interview; the date and venue will be notified later. The Skill Test will be evaluated only for candidates who secure the minimum qualifying marks in the Written Examination. The overall merit list will be drawn on the basis of combined merit

Q3What is the age limit for ITAT Senior Private Secretary and Private Secretary posts?
A
The age limit for all candidates shall not exceed 35 years, with standard age relaxation applicable for Government servants and SC/ST/OBC candidates as per Central Government instructions.
Q4What is the pay scale for ITAT Senior Private Secretary and Private Secretary?
A
Senior Private Secretary carries Pay Level-8 (₹47,600 to ₹1,51,100), and Private Secretary carries Pay Level-7 (₹44,900 to ₹1,42,400), both as per the 7th CPC Pay Matrix.
Q5What is the selection process for ITAT Recruitment 2026-27?
A
Selection consists of a Written Examination (General English and General Knowledge & Reasoning), a Skill Test (English Shorthand @120 WPM and English Typing @45 WPM), and a Personal Interview. The Skill Test is evaluated only for candidates who clear the qualifying marks in the Written Examination, and the final merit list is based on combined Skill Test and interview marks.
Q6What is the required shorthand and typing speed for ITAT Private Secretary posts?
A
Candidates must have English Shorthand speed of 120 WPM and English Typing speed of 45 WPM, as tested in the Skill Test.
Q7Can a candidate apply for both Senior Private Secretary and Private Secretary posts?
A
Yes. Candidates wishing to apply for both posts need to submit only a single application, since a single examination is conducted for both posts, and the post offered depends on the candidate's rank in the consolidated merit list.
Q8What are the minimum qualifying marks in the ITAT written examination?
A
General category candidates need a minimum of 40% in each paper and 50% aggregate; OBC/EWS candidates need 35% in each paper and 45% aggregate; SC/ST/PWD candidates need 30% in each paper and 40% aggregate.
Q9Is there a probation period after selection to ITAT posts?
A
Yes. Selected candidates shall be on probation for a period of 2 years from the date of joining the post.
Q10How will ITAT communicate admit cards and results to candidates?
A
All communication regarding admit cards, discrepancies in applications, call letters for Skill Test/Personal Interview and final results will be sent by email only, to the address provided in the application. General notifications will also be uploaded on the official ITAT website, https://www.itat.gov.in.
