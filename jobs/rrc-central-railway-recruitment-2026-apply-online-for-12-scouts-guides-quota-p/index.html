RRC Central Railway Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

Railway Recruitment Cell, Central Railway (RRC/CR) has issued Employment Notice No. RRC/CR/S&G/02/2026, dated 11/07/2026, inviting online applications against the Open Advertisement Scouts and Guides Quota for the year 2026-27. The recruitment covers 2 posts in Level 2 and 10 posts in Level 1 (2 posts each for Mumbai, Bhusawal, Nagpur, Pune and Solapur divisions). The posts are open to candidates of all communities, and there is no reservation applicable for this recruitment. Applications must be submitted online at www.rrccr.com.

Eligibility (Nationality)

A candidate for appointment must be: A citizen of India, or A citizen of Nepal, or A subject of Bhutan, or A Tibetan refugee who came to India before 1st January 1962 with the intention of permanently settling in India, or A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia or Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India. Candidates belonging to the last four categories must possess a certificate of eligibility issued by the Government of India. Such candidates may sit for the exam, but the appointment offer is given only after the certificate is issued. Applicants can apply for both Level 1 and Level 2, provided they meet the eligibility criteria for each. Selection Process

Selection is based on a Written Test (CBT/TAB mode) followed by marks awarded on certificates during document verification. There is no interview. Candidates who score a minimum of 40% marks in the Written Test, to the extent of 2.5 times the number of vacancies, will be called for the next stage. 40 Objective Questions carrying 40 marks (CBT/TAB mode) 1 Essay Type Question carrying 20 marks, based on the Scouts and Guides Organisation, its activities, and General Knowledge Negative marking applies to objective questions: 1/3rd of the marks allotted is deducted for every wrong answer Test duration: 60 minutes Question paper is set in English and Hindi All certificates, including those used for minimum eligibility and for awarding marks, must be uploaded on the portal. Certificates not uploaded at the time of application will not be entertained during document verification.

Syllabus for Written Test

The Written Test syllabus for both Level 1 and Level 2 covers: History of Scouting/Guiding; Law and Promise; Organization at District and State level; Training Centres at District and State level; knowledge of relevant books; Scouting for Boys/Guiding for Girls in India; motto, left-hand shake, salute, prayer, flag song, and National Anthem; National and Scouting flags; social services; hiking; proficiency badges; stages in Scouting/Guiding; Thinking Day; progressive training of Scouts/Guides/Rovers/Rangers; pioneering; handicrafts; Indian Railway Jamborette; National Jamboree; Jamboree on the Air; National-level organisation and training centres; WAGGGS/WOSM; Uprashtrapati Award Competition; Prime Minister Shield Competition; Community Development Programmes; aims and methods of Scouting; and General Knowledge.
