GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026 : Job Overview

The Gujarat Subordinate Service Selection Board (GSSSB) has released the GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026 notification (Advertisement No. 433/2026-27) for 82 X-Ray Assistant, Class-3 vacancies under the Health and Family Welfare Department. Candidates aged 18 to 33 years with HSC (Physics, Chemistry, English) qualification can apply online at ojas.gujarat.gov.in from 20 July 2026 (2:00 PM) to 3 August 2026 (11:59 PM). Selected candidates will draw a fixed monthly pay of ₹26,000 for the first five years.

GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Eligibility Criteria 2026

Applicants often ask what qualifications are needed for the GSSSB X-Ray Assistant post - the answer covers both educational qualification and age limit, detailed below. Candidates applying for GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026 must have passed the Higher Secondary School Certificate (HSC/12th) examination with Physics, Chemistry, and English as subjects, conducted by the Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board. This qualification standard has been fixed as per the Health and Family Welfare Department's resolution dated 18 November 2024. In addition, candidates must possess basic computer knowledge as prescribed under the Gujarat Civil Services Classification (General) Rules, 1967, and adequate knowledge of Gujarati and/or Hindi . GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Application Fee 2026

The application fee for GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026 differs for unreserved and reserved category candidates and must be paid online only. The fee must be paid online through Debit Card, Internet Banking, UPI, or Wallet after the application is confirmed on OJAS, and the last date to pay the exam fee is 6 August 2026 (11:59 PM) . Candidates who appear for the examination will get the fee refunded through the same payment mode used, but no refund is given to candidates who do not appear for the exam. GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Selection Process 2026

Selection for the GSSSB X-Ray Assistant post is based on a single-stage computer-based/OMR written examination followed by document verification, so candidates do not need to appear for multiple test stages. The exam consists of objective-type Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) split into Part-A and Part-B. The combined duration for Part-A and Part-B is 3 hours (180 minutes) , and the exam follows negative marking - 1/4th mark is deducted for every wrong answer. A minimum qualifying standard of 40% applies independently to both Part-A and Part-B for all categories, meaning candidates must clear both sections separately to remain in contention. After the exam, GSSSB will release a provisional answer key, invite objections, and then publish the final answer key followed by the provisional result. GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Salary and Pay Scale 2026

Selected candidates for GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026 will initially be appointed on a fixed pay before moving to the regular pay matrix. During the first five years , appointees will receive a fixed monthly pay of ₹26,000 as per the Finance Department's resolutions. After five years, subject to satisfactory service, candidates will be placed in the regular Pay Level-2 of the 7th Pay Commission, ranging from ₹19,900 to ₹63,200 per month , along with other applicable benefits under the relevant government resolutions. This regularization is subject to the outcome of pending SLP No. 14124/2012 and SLP No. 14125/2012 before the Supreme Court.

Documents Required for GSSSB X-Ray Assistant Recruitment 2026

Candidates must keep all original certificates ready before applying and during the later document verification stage. Required documents include the HSC/12th mark sheet showing Physics, Chemistry, and English, the school leaving certificate for age proof, category certificate (SC/ST/SEBC/EWS, where applicable, obtained within the validity period specified in the notification), computer knowledge certificate (if available), disability certificate (for PwD candidates, in the format prescribed by the Health and Family Welfare Department circular dated 15 December 2025), ex-servicemen identity card and discharge book (if applicable), and a recent passport-size photograph with white background, taken within one year of the last date of application.
