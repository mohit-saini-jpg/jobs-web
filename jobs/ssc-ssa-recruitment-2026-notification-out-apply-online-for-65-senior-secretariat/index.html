SSC SSA Exam Pattern 2026 - Scheme of Examination

The SSC Senior Secretariat Assistant exam 2026 is conducted in two parts. Part-I is a Computer Based Examination (CBE) of 200 marks with four subjects, each carrying 50 marks and 50 questions. Part-II is an evaluation of service records (APARs) worth 100 marks. The final rank of the candidate is determined by adding both Part-I and Part-II scores. All questions are of Objective Type Multiple Choice format. There is a negative marking of 0.25 marks for each wrong answer. Candidates must attempt the examination in the language (English or Hindi) opted in the application form, except the "General English" section of the first subject, which must be attempted in English only. No Braille question paper will be provided. One hundred marks are earmarked for evaluation of Annual Performance Appraisal Reports (APARs). SSC SSA UDC Selection Process 2026

The SSC SSA / UDC selection process 2026 is a two-stage merit-based system. Candidates must first clear the Computer Based Examination and are then shortlisted for APAR evaluation. The final rank is based on the combined aggregate of both stages. This is a competitive examination - success does not confer any right to selection unless the cadre authority is also satisfied about the candidate's suitability. Stage 1 - Computer Based Examination (Part-I): 200 marks across 4 objective-type subjects. The Commission may fix minimum qualifying marks at its discretion. Stage 2 - Evaluation of Service Records (Part-II): 100 marks based on APAR evaluation. Only Part-I qualifiers are considered. Final Merit: Aggregate of Part-I (200 marks) + Part-II (100 marks) = 300 marks total. Names of selected candidates will be arranged in order of merit for inclusion in the Select List of SSA/UDC of CSCS cadr

SSC SSA Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the SSC SSA Recruitment 2026 and who is it for?

The SSC SSA Recruitment 2026 refers to the Senior Secretariat Assistant / Upper Division Clerk Grade Limited Departmental Competitive Examination, 2025, conducted by the Staff Selection Commission. It is open exclusively to serving Lower Division Clerks (JSA/LDC) of the Central Secretariat Clerical Service (CSCS) cadre under DoPT — not for general public candidates.
Q2How many vacancies are available in SSC UDC Vacancy 2026?

A total of 65 vacancies are available under the Central Secretariat Clerical Service (CSCS) cadre of DoPT. Category-wise breakup (UR/SC/ST/PwBD) has not been specified in the current notification and will be communicated in due course.
Q3What is the last date to apply for SSC SSA Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application is 16 July 2026 (up to 11:00 PM / 2300 hours). The printed application hard copy must reach SSC (Northern Region), New Delhi through the Head of Department by 23 July 2026 (6:00 PM).
Q4What is the eligibility condition for SSC SSA UDC Limited Departmental Exam 2025?
A
Candidates must be regularly appointed Lower Division Clerks in the CSCS cadre of DoPT with not less than five years' approved and continuous service in the LDC grade as on 01 August 2025. If appointed through a competitive exam, the results must have been declared at least five years before the crucial date and the candidate must have at least four years of approved service.

Q6Where will the SSC SSA exam 2026 be held?
A
The Computer Based Examination will be held tentatively in August 2026 at centres in Delhi-NCR only. No request for change of centre will be entertained under any circumstances. Candidates serving at Indian missions abroad must appear in India at their own expense.
Q7What is the exam pattern for SSC SSA / UDC Limited Departmental Exam 2026?
A
Part-I is a Computer Based Examination of 200 marks covering four subjects — Comprehension and General English (50 marks), Parliamentary Procedure (50 marks), General Knowledge (50 marks), and Noting and Drafting (50 marks) — with a duration of 3 hours. Part-II covers APAR evaluation for 100 marks. Total marks for final selection: 300.

The SSC SSA / UDC post is at Pay Level-4 in the 7th Pay Commission Pay Matrix, with a pay range of Rs. 25,500 to Rs. 81,100 per month. It is a Group C Non-Gazetted post under DoPT, and all applicable central government allowances are additionally admissible.

Yes. A candidate must have passed the typewriting test held by UPSC or SSC for confirmation in the LDC grade on or before the date of notification, unless they have been formally exempted. Candidates who are exempted must quote the exemption order number and date in the application form.
