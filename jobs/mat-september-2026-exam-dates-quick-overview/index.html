What Is MAT and Who Conducts It?

Candidates new to MBA entrance exams often ask what MAT actually is and why it matters. This section explains the exam's background, its recognition, and why it remains a popular choice among management aspirants.

The Management Aptitude Test (MAT) is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the All India Management Association (AIMA), a body that has been administering the test since 1988. MAT is officially recognized by the Government of India and serves as an entry gateway to hundreds of B-schools across the country offering MBA and PGDM programmes. Unlike single-attempt exams such as CMAT, MAT is conducted four times a year - in February, May, September, and December - giving candidates repeated opportunities to appear and use their best score for admission within the same academic session.

MAT September 2026 Registration: How to Apply Online

Many first-time applicants are unsure how the online registration process works. This section breaks the MAT application process into simple, sequential steps so candidates can complete registration without errors.

Candidates can complete the MAT September 2026 application entirely online through the official AIMA MAT website. The steps are:

  • Visit the official website - mat.aima.in
  • Create a new account using your name, email ID, mobile number, and date of birth
  • Fill in the application form with personal details, academic qualifications, and your preferred exam mode (PBT or CBT)
  • Upload the required documents in the prescribed format and size
  • Pay the application fee online via debit card, credit card, or net banking
  • Review and submit the application form carefully, as no correction window is typically provided afterward
  • Download and print the confirmation page/application form for future reference

Candidates are advised to complete registration well in advance of the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues on the portal.

MAT September 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Before registering, candidates should confirm whether they meet the basic eligibility requirements. This section outlines the qualification and other criteria set for MAT 2026.

  • Educational Qualification: A bachelor's degree in any discipline (BA, B.Com, B.Sc, B.Tech, etc.) from a recognized university
  • Final-Year Students: Candidates in their final year of graduation, awaiting results, are also eligible to apply
  • Minimum Percentage: There is no minimum percentage requirement prescribed by AIMA, though individual participating institutes may set their own criteria
  • Age Limit: There is no minimum or maximum age restriction for appearing in MAT
  • Number of Attempts: No limit - candidates can appear in all four MAT sessions (February, May, September, December) in a year, and even in both PBT and CBT modes within the same session

Understanding the exam structure helps candidates prepare a targeted study plan. This section explains the sections, number of questions, duration, and marking scheme for MAT.

MAT is an objective-type, multiple-choice test conducted over 120 minutes, with no sectional time limit, allowing candidates flexibility in managing their time across sections.

MAT Test Modes and Test Cities

Candidates can choose between two formats depending on their comfort level, and this section clarifies the differences and city availability.

MAT September 2026 will be conducted in two modes:

  • Paper-Based Test (PBT): A traditional offline test using OMR-based answer sheets
  • Computer-Based Test (CBT): An online, computer-delivered test format

AIMA conducts the exam across 54 test cities in India. Since test cities may differ between PBT and CBT modes, candidates should check centre availability carefully before selecting their preferred mode and city during registration.

MAT Score Validity and Colleges Accepting MAT Scores

A key concern for aspirants is how widely MAT scores are accepted and for how long they remain valid. This section addresses both points.

MAT scores are valid for one year from the date of declaration and are accepted by 600+ B-schools across India for MBA and PGDM admissions. Some of the well-known institutes accepting MAT scores include:

  • Jaipuria Institute of Management, Noida
  • Institute of Public Enterprise (IPE), Hyderabad
  • SIES College of Management Studies, Mumbai
  • IBA, Bangalore
  • Rajagiri Business School, Kochi
  • NISM, Mumbai

As part of the base registration fee, AIMA also sends the MAT scorecard to a set number of institutes chosen by the candidate during the application process; additional college applications are made separately by the candidate using their best available score.
