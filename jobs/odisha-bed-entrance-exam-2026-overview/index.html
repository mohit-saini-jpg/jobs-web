Eligibility Criteria for Odisha B.Ed 2026

  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree in Arts/Science/Commerce with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved categories like SC/ST/SEBC as per norms). Engineering/Technology graduates need 55% aggregate, subject to conditions in the brochure.
  • Subject Combinations: Specific requirements for school subjects apply as per the SAMS prospectus.
  • Age Limit: Upper age limits follow previous patterns with relaxations for SC/ST/SEBC/Women/PwD; check the exact cut-off and details in the official brochure.
  • Nationality & Domicile: Indian citizen, ordinarily a permanent resident/domicile of Odisha as specified.

How to Apply Online for Odisha B.Ed 2026

  • Visit https://te.samsodisha.gov.in and register as a new student (use mobile/email for OTP verification).
  • Log in with credentials and select the B.Ed 2026 course to access the CAF.
  • Fill in personal, academic, category, domicile, and qualification details; choose exam centres and preferences.
  • Upload scanned documents (photo, signature, certificates) in specified formats/sizes.
  • Pay the application fee online via debit/credit card, net banking, or UPI.
  • Review, submit, and print the confirmation/application page.

Application Fee (approximate, confirm on portal/payment stage):

  • General/Unreserved/SEBC: ₹500
  • SC/ST/PwD: ₹300

Exam Pattern for Odisha B.Ed Entrance Exam 2026

  • Mode: Offline, OMR-based pen-and-paper
  • Questions/Marks: 100 MCQs for 100 marks
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Marking: +1 for correct answers; negative marking (if applicable) per brochure
  • Sections: General English, General Awareness, Reasoning Ability, Teaching Aptitude, and subject-specific (Arts/Science) components.

Syllabus Overview

  • The syllabus aligns with prior years and is detailed in the SAMS brochure:
  • General English: Comprehension, grammar, vocabulary, error spotting, synonyms/antonyms, idioms.
  • General Awareness: Current affairs, Indian polity/history/geography, general science, education/social issues.
  • Reasoning: Analogies, series, coding-decoding, puzzles, logical reasoning.
  • Teaching Aptitude: Learner psychology, classroom management, teacher roles, communication.
  • Subject-Specific: Graduation-level topics in chosen stream.
