How to Check the DU CSAS UG First Seat Allotment List 2026

Follow these steps to check your allotment status:

  • Visit the official DU CSAS UG portal at ugadmission.uod.ac.in.
  • Log in using your CUET UG 2026 application number and the password created during CSAS registration.
  • Go to the seat allocation or dashboard section, which will display your First Allotment result.
  • Check the allotted college name, programme, and category against your submitted preferences.
  • Download and save the allotment result/letter for your records; you may also print it for physical verification at the college.

If you do not see an allotment reflected, or the portal is slow to load, this is commonly due to heavy traffic immediately after the list is released; candidates are advised to retry after some time rather than repeat login attempts rapidly.

Eligibility for DU CSAS UG Seat Allotment 2026

  • Candidates must have appeared for and qualified in CUET UG 2026, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).
  • Candidates must have completed CSAS UG 2026 Phase 1 (Registration) and Phase 2 (Preference Filling) before the respective deadlines.
  • Programme-specific eligibility depends on the CUET UG subject combination taken by the candidate; DU maps eligible CUET subjects to each undergraduate programme, and only matching combinations are considered during allotment.

DU CSAS UG Application and Acceptance Fee 2026

  • DU charges a one-time, non-refundable CSAS UG registration/application fee, payable online during Phase 1 of registration.
  • After seat allotment, candidates who accept a seat are required to pay an online acceptance/admission fee before the college confirms admission.
  • Payments must be made only through the official payment gateway on the CSAS portal (ugadmission.uod.ac.in) - offline or cash payments at the college are not accepted for this step.

Note: The exact fee amount (registration fee and acceptance fee, including any category-wise variation) is not confirmed in the sources reviewed for this article. Some reports indicate that overall undergraduate programme fees at DU range roughly between Rs. 8,000 and Rs. 30,000 annually depending on the college and programme, but this is a third-party estimate, not an official fee notification, and should be verified on the CSAS dashboard payment screen or the official Bulletin of Information.

DU CSAS UG Selection and Allotment Process

Seat allocation under CSAS is determined by the following factors, in combination:

  • The candidate's CUET UG 2026 score/rank.
  • Category of the candidate (General, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, EWS, PwBD, and other applicable reservation categories as per Government of India norms).
  • Availability of seats in each college-programme combination.
  • The order of college-programme preferences locked by the candidate during Phase 2.

The system allots the highest-ranked preference for which a candidate is eligible at the time of each round. After receiving an allotment, candidates choose one of the following options:

  • Freeze: Accept the seat permanently. This exits the candidate from further allocation rounds and is treated as final.
  • Upgrade: Accept the current seat provisionally while remaining eligible to be moved to a higher-ranked preference in a later round.
  • Reject: Decline the allotted seat entirely, which removes the candidate from the CSAS UG 2026 process.

How to Accept the DU CSAS UG First Allotment 2026 (Step-by-Step)

  • Log in to your dashboard at ugadmission.uod.ac.in using your CUET UG 2026 credentials.
  • Open the seat allocation section and review the allotted college, programme, and category.
  • Choose Freeze, Upgrade, or Reject based on your preference (see the Selection Process section above for what each option means).
  • If accepting, proceed to the fee payment gateway on the same portal and complete payment via net banking, debit card, credit card, or UPI.
  • Save the payment confirmation and registration/acceptance receipt as a PDF.
  • Upload/carry the required documents (see above) for verification, either online through the portal or physically at the allotted college as instructed.
  • Wait for the college to verify and approve your application; monitor your CSAS dashboard and registered email for status updates.

Important Instructions and Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Name, date of birth, and category are auto-fetched from the CUET database and generally cannot be edited after Phase 1 submission; contact the DU admission helpdesk immediately if these details are incorrect.
  • Any mismatch between your CSAS profile and your original documents can lead to rejection at document verification - for example, an OBC-NCL candidate incorrectly selecting the General category, or a spelling difference between Class 12 records and the CUET application.
  • Fee payment alone does not confirm your seat - document verification and college approval are separate, mandatory steps.
  • All fee payments must be made only through the official CSAS portal gateway; do not pay any amount directly at a college counter for this step.
  • Missing the acceptance or payment deadline results in forfeiture of the allotted seat, with no reinstatement for that round.
  • Regularly check the CSAS dashboard and registered email, since all official communications are sent through these channels only.

What Happens If You Are Not Allotted a Seat in Round 1?

Candidates who do not receive a seat in the First Allotment List automatically remain eligible for the Second CSAS Allocation List, expected on July 25, 2026, without any separate registration or action. The second round fills seats that become vacant when Round 1 allottees:

  • Do not accept their allotted seat within the deadline,
  • Choose Upgrade and are later moved to a better seat (vacating the earlier one), or
  • Reject their Round 1 allotment.

These vacated seats are redistributed in Round 2 using the same merit-and-preference logic as Round 1. Candidates who remain unallotted after multiple regular rounds may also explore Sports, ECA (Extra-Curricular Activities), and Kashmiri Migrant category admissions, if eligible, subject to availability, as well as any subsequent spot or mop-up rounds announced by the University.
