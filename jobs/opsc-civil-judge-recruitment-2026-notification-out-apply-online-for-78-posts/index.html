Syllabus

Details

Preliminary Written Examination (100 Marks, OMR MCQ, 1½ Hours, Negative marking 25%): | The paper covers the Constitution of India; CPC 1908; CrPC 1973 and Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita 2023; Indian Evidence Act 1872 and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam 2023; IPC 1860 and Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023; Limitation Act; Transfer of Property Act; Law of Contract; Law of Succession; Specific Relief Act; Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005; and any other relevant legal topics as specified by the High Court of Orissa from time to time. | Main Written Examination – Paper 1 (General English, 150 Marks): Translation and retranslation of ten lines each (one passage English to Odia and one Odia to English); Short Essay of about 150 words; Precis writing of 300 words; one passage of about 500 words with 5 questions. | Main Written Examination – Paper 2 (Procedural Laws, 150 Marks): Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 and Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita 2023; Code of Civil Procedure 1908; Indian Evidence Act 1872 and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam 2023. | Optional Papers (150 Marks each – candidate to choose any 3): Law of Crime & Law of Torts; Personal Laws (Hindu Law and Mohammedan Law); Law of Property (Transfer of Property Act, Specific Relief Act, Indian Limitation Act); Law of Contract (Indian Contract Act, Sales of Goods Act, Partnership Act, Negotiable Instrument Act); Jurisprudence and the Constitution of India.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is the last date to apply online for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?

The last date to submit the online application form for OPSC Civil Judge (Junior Division) Recruitment 2026 is 8 June 2026 up to 5:00 PM through the official OPSC portal.

Q2. How many vacancies are released in OPSC Civil Judge 2026 notification?

The Odisha Public Service Commission has announced a total of 78 vacancies for Civil Judge (Junior Division) in Odisha Judicial Service, including 26 posts reserved for women and 3 posts for persons with benchmark disabilities.

Q3. What is the age limit for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?

Candidates must be between 23 and 42 years of age as on 1 April 2025, with upper age relaxations up to 5 years for SC, ST, SEBC, women and certain defence personnel, 10 years for PwBD and 7 years for eligible law clerk or research assistant experience holders.

Q4. What qualifications are required for the Odisha Judicial Service Civil Judge exam 2026?

Applicants must hold a graduate degree in Law (LLB) from a recognized university and have at least three years’ practice as an advocate as on 1 April 2025, with law‑clerk or research experience to judges or constitutional authorities also countable under specified conditions.

Q5. Is Odia language knowledge compulsory for OPSC Civil Judge 2026?

Yes, candidates must be able to read, write and speak Odia and must have passed HSC or an equivalent examination with Odia as a subject or possess a Middle English School standard Odia language certificate from an approved board.

Q6. What is the application fee for OPSC Civil Judge Recruitment 2026?

General, SEBC and other non‑exempt candidates must pay Rs. 700 as examination fee online, while SC, ST and PwBD candidates are fully exempt; the fee is refundable only to those who actually appear in the preliminary examination.

Q7. What is the selection process for Odisha Judicial Service Civil Judge 2026?

The selection process comprises three stages: a preliminary objective‑type written examination for screening, a descriptive main written examination, and a 100‑mark interview, with final merit based on combined mains and interview scores only.

Q8. Is there negative marking in the OPSC Civil Judge preliminary exam 2026?

Yes, the preliminary examination consists of 100 multiple‑choice questions of 1 mark each, and there is negative marking of 25 percent of the mark assigned to a question, meaning 0.25 mark is deducted for every wrong answer.
