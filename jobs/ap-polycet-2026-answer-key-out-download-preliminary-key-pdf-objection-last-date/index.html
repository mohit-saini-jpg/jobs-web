Using the AP POLYCET 2026 preliminary answer key, candidates can estimate their marks before the result is declared. You can explain the marking process in an easy way:

  • AP POLYCET 2026 consists of 120 multiple-choice questions.
  • Each question carries 1 mark.
  • There is generally no negative marking in AP POLYCET (candidates should confirm with the official brochure for the current year).

Steps to calculate your score:

  • Take your AP POLYCET 2026 question paper and OMR sheet (or carbon copy, if provided).
  • Note your question booklet code (Set A, Set B, Set C, or Set D).
  • Open the preliminary answer key PDF and go to the table corresponding to your set.
  • For each question, compare your marked option with the answer given in the key (1, 2, 3, 4 usually correspond to options A, B, C, D).
  • Award yourself 1 mark for every correct response.
  • Do not deduct marks for incorrect or unattempted questions (if there is no negative marking).
  • Add up all correct responses to get your approximate score out of 120.

This estimated score helps you understand your chances of qualifying and getting admission into your preferred polytechnic course and college.
