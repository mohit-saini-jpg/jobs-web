How to Apply

  1. 1Step 1: Visit the official APSSB website at www.apssb.nic.in and navigate to the CHSLE 2026 online application link (active from 20 July 2026).
  2. 2Step 2: Register with your basic details and create a login. First-time applicants must complete the registration process before accessing the application form.
  3. 3Step 3: Fill in the online application form carefully. Enter all personal details, educational qualifications, category, and post preference. Candidates must clearly indicate their preference of post and department - preference once submitted is final and cannot be changed.
  4. 4Step 4: Upload the following scanned documents:
  5. 5Passport size photograph (50-100 KB, JPG/JPEG/PNG)
  6. 6Signature on white paper with black ink pen (10-50 KB, JPG/JPEG/PNG)
  7. 7Step 5: Pay the examination fee online (₹150 for APST / ₹200 for UR). PwD candidates are exempted.
  8. 8Step 6: Submit the application before 10 August 2026 (5:00 PM). Application details cannot be edited after the closing date. Take a printout of the submitted application for future reference.
  9. 9Important: If an applicant submits multiple forms for the same post, only the latest application will be considered. Incomplete applications or those filled with wrong/irrelevant information will be summarily rejected.

Important Instructions

Candidature is purely provisional at all stages. The Board can cancel it at any stage if eligibility conditions are not met. Candidates must carry a valid photo identity proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID/Driving License/PAN Card) along with the admit card to the examination centre. Details on ID must match the admit card. Post and department preference indicated during application is final and cannot be changed. Merit list will be prepared based on the preference order. There is no provision for re-evaluation or re-checking of answer scripts. Electronic gadgets (calculators, mobile phones, laptops, smart watches etc.) are strictly prohibited inside the examination hall. Individual admit cards will not be sent by post. Candidates must download them from www.apssb.nic.in. APST = Arunachal Pradesh Scheduled Tribe; PRC = Permanent Resident Certificate; UR = Unreserved. The Board reserves the right to cancel/modify/withdraw any post, question, or the entire examination process without prior notice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Q1. What is the last date to apply for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for APSSB CHSLE 2026 is 10 August 2026 by 5:00 PM. The application link will be disabled after this time
Q2 Q2. How many total vacancies are available under APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
A total of 207 Group C vacancies have been announced across 9 posts and multiple departments of the Government of Arunachal Pradesh.
Q3 Q3. Which post has the highest number of vacancies in APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Stockman (Post Code 24/26) has the highest vacancy count with 89 posts in the Animal Husbandry & Veterinary department.
Q4 Q4. What is the application fee for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
The examination fee is ₹150 for APST candidates and ₹200 for Unreserved candidates. Persons with Disabilities (PwD) are exempted from paying any fee.
Q5 Q5. Is there negative marking in APSSB CHSLE 2026 written exam?
A
No. There is no negative marking in the APSSB CHSLE 2026 written examination. However, candidates must score a minimum of 33% in aggregate to be eligible for selection.
Q6 Q6. Which post codes require a Diploma in Computer Application?
A
Post Codes 17/26 (DEO), 20/26 (JSA), 22/26 (LDC), and 23/26 (Record Keeper) require a minimum 6-month Diploma in Computer Application from an AICTE/APCTE recognised institution.
Q7 Q7. Will the Skill Test marks be added to the final merit list?
A
No. The Stage II Skill Test is qualifying in nature only. Its marks will not be counted in the final merit list. The merit list is prepared solely on the basis of Stage I written exam marks.
Q8 Q8. Can I change my post and department preference after submission?
A
No. The preference of post and department once submitted is final and cannot be changed under any circumstances. Candidates should carefully choose their preferences before submitting the form.
Q9 Q9. What is the age limit for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Age limit varies by post — between 18 to 28 years for Record Keeper (23/26), 18 to 32 years for DEO, JSA, LDC, Agriculture Field Assistant, and 18 to 35 years for Field Assistant, Fishery Demonstrator, Laboratory Assistant, and Stockman. Age relaxation applies for APST (5 years), PwBD (10 years), and Ex-Servicemen as per rules.
Q10 Q10. Where do I download the admit card for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Admit cards will be available for download from the official APSSB website www.apssb.nic.in. Individual admit cards will not be sent by post. Candidates must regularly check the website for updates.
