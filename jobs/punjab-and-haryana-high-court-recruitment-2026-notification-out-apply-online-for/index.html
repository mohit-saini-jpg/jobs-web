Important Instructions

All particulars including category once filled by candidates are sacrosanct; no change will be permitted at any stage of the recruitment process. Candidates wishing to apply for both posts must apply separately for each post with separate fee payment for each. Merely satisfying the eligibility criterion does not entitle any candidate to be called for interview; the High Court may apply shortlisting criteria if a large number of applications are received. E-Admit Cards will be uploaded on www.highcourtchd.gov.in; Admit Cards will NOT be sent by post. Issuance of a provisional E-Admit Card does not confirm eligibility. Photograph on E-Admit Card must be duly attested (Gazetted Officer / Self-attested) and must exactly match the photograph uploaded during online application; non-matching leads to disallowance at interview. No mobile phone, electronic device, watch, ornaments (jewellery), or other baggage is allowed inside the interview centre. No TA/DA will be paid for appearing in the interview or testimonials checking. Online application without proper photograph and signature will be summarily rejected. Retain 5 copies of the uploaded photograph for use at different stages. Candidates employed on contract or ad hoc basis in government offices are NOT considered government employees; they must apply as non-departmental candidates and need not obtain NOC. Regular Government/Semi-Government/PSU employees must obtain NOC from Head of Department; failure to produce NOC at testimonials checking will lead to cancellation of candidature. Fee once paid is non-refundable; re-check all details before making payment. In case of any discrepancy between advertisements published in newspapers and the official website, the contents on www.highcourtchd.gov.in will prevail. The High Court shall not be responsible for non-delivery of SMS or email due to network issues, switched-off mobile phones, or emails going to spam; candidates must visit www.highcourtchd.gov.in and check their email accounts regularly. Application found incomplete or with false information can be rejected at any stage, even after completion of selection process. The cut-off date to apply is sacrosanct; no candidate will be allowed to apply after 30-06-2026 under any circumstance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Q1. What is the last date to apply for Punjab and Haryana High Court Safai Sewak and Mali Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date for online registration is 30-06-2026 (11:59 P.M.). The online application window opened on 09-06-2026.
Q2 Q2. How many total vacancies are notified in Punjab and Haryana High Court Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 167 vacancies are notified — 75 posts of Safai Sewak (Sweeper) and 92 posts of Mali (Gardener) on the establishment of the High Court of Punjab and Haryana at Chandigarh.
Q3 Q3. What is the educational qualification required for Safai Sewak and Mali posts?
A
Knowledge of Hindi and regional language is the prescribed qualification for both posts. For Mali, preference will be given to candidates with a Diploma in Gardening from a recognized institute and/or 3 years' experience as a Gardener. For Safai Sewak, preference will be given to candidates with minimum 3 years' experience as a Safai Sewak (Sweeper).
Q4 Q4. What is the age limit for Punjab and Haryana High Court Safai Sewak and Mali Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must be between 18 and 35 years of age as on 30-06-2026. PwD candidates (min. 40% disability — Low Vision, Deaf & Dumb, Intellectually Disabled MILD IQ) get 10 years' relaxation. Ex-Servicemen get relaxation equal to their defence service period plus 3 years.
Q5 Q5. What is the application fee for Punjab and Haryana High Court Safai Sewak and Mali Recruitment 2026?
A
General and out-of-state SC/ST/BC candidates pay Rs. 700/-. SC/ST/BC candidates of Punjab, Haryana, U.T. Chandigarh, Ex-Servicemen, and PwD (min. 40% disability) candidates pay Rs. 600/-. Dependents of Ex-Servicemen disabled/deceased during active operation are exempted from fee on uploading requisite proof. Online transaction charges are levied extra and fee is non-refundable.
Q6 Q6. Can a candidate apply for both Safai Sewak and Mali posts?
A
Yes. A candidate wishing to apply for both posts must submit separate online applications with separate fee payments for each post.
Q7 Q7. What is the selection process for Safai Sewak and Mali posts in Punjab and Haryana High Court?
A
Selection is based on Interview. Depending on the volume of applications received, the High Court may apply a suitable shortlisting criteria before the interview, which will be communicated to candidates at the appropriate time. The decision of the High Court on all selection matters is final and binding.
Q8 Q8. What documents must be produced at testimonials checking for Punjab and Haryana High Court Safai Sewak and Mali Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must produce the printout of the application form, Date of Birth proof (Matriculation Certificate or Birth Certificate), qualification certificate(s), caste certificate (for reserved category), NOC in Annexure II format (for government employees), Affidavit (Annexure III) about close relatives in the judiciary, Affidavit (Annexure IV) about previous government employment, Disability Certificate (for PwD), and Discharge Book (for Ex-Servicemen).
Q9 Q9. Will TA/DA be paid for attending the interview?
A
No. No Travelling Allowance or Daily Allowance will be paid to any candidate for appearing in the interview or for testimonials checking.
Q10 Q10. Where can candidates check updates and E-Admit Card download links for Punjab and Haryana High Court Recruitment 2026?
A
All updates, interview dates, E-Admit Card download links, results, notices, and corrigendum will be published only on the official website of the High Court of Punjab and Haryana at www.highcourtchd.gov.in. Candidates must visit the website regularly and also check their registered email accounts (including spam folder) for important notifications.
