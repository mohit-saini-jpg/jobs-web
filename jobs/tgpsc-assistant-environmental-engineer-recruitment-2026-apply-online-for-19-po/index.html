Eligibility Criteria & Job Requirements

Option 1: Bachelor's Degree in Civil / Mechanical / Chemical / Bio-Technology / Mining / Environmental Engineering from a recognized University in India (established under Central/Provincial/State Act or recognized by UGC), OR qualified in Section A and B of AMIE (India) Civil Examination . Option 2: B.Tech / B.E. in any branch with a Post Graduate Degree in Environmental Engineering from a recognized University in India (established under Central/Provincial/State Act or recognized by UGC), OR qualified in Section A and B of AMIE (India) Civil Examination. Distance Education: Degrees obtained through Open Universities / Distance Education mode must be recognized by UGC / AICTE / Distance Education Bureau. The onus of proof rests with the applicant. Qualification Date: The result of the required qualification must have been declared by the University as on the date of Notification i.e., J

TGPSC Selection Methodology

Written Examination (Objective Type): The exam will be held either in OMR-based Offline mode or Computer Based Recruitment Test (CBRT) mode. The Commission will notify the mode through web note / press note and Hall Ticket. Paper I – General Studies and General Abilities: 150 Questions | 150 Marks | 150 Minutes | Bilingual (English and Telugu). Paper II – Concerned Subject (Environmental Engineering): 150 Questions | 300 Marks | 150 Minutes | English Only. Total Marks: 450 Marks. Qualifying Marks: OC / Sportspersons / EWS – minimum 40%; BCs – minimum 35%; SCs / STs / PwD – minimum 30%. Normalization: For CBRT conducted in multiple sessions, scores will be normalized and results announced on percentile basis. Tie-Breaking Order: Local status (Telangana locals first) → Date of Birth (older gets priority) → Marks in subject paper → Date of passing qualifying exam → Percentage in qualifying

Q1What is the total number of vacancies for this recruitment?
A
There are a total of 19 vacancies for the post of Assistant Environmental Engineer in Telangana Pollution Control Board, including carried forward vacancies from previous recruitments.

A
As per G.O.Ms.No.86, G.A.(Ser.A) Department, Dt. 18/05/2026, the upper age limit has been raised by 10 years - from 34 years to 44 years, reckoned as on 01/07/2026.
Q3Is a B.Tech in any branch eligible for this post?
A
Yes. Candidates with B.Tech/B.E. in any branch are eligible only if they also hold a PG Degree in Environmental Engineering from a recognized university.
Q4Is local reservation applicable for this post?
A
No. Local reservation is not applicable as this is a State Level post and selection is made on state-wide merit.
Q5Are SC/ST candidates eligible for OC vacancies?
A
Yes. Applicants belonging to SC, ST, BC, EWS, and Differently Abled who avail upper age relaxation will also be considered for Open Category (OC) vacancies.
Q6What is the exam pattern and total marks?
A
The exam has two papers - Paper I (General Studies, 150 marks) and Paper II (Concerned Subject, 300 marks) - totaling 450 marks, with 150 questions each and 150 minutes per paper.
Q7Can I raise objections against the answer key?

Yes. After publication of the provisional key, candidates can raise objections online within the stipulated period by paying ₹500/- per question. Fee is reimbursed if the objection is found genuine.
