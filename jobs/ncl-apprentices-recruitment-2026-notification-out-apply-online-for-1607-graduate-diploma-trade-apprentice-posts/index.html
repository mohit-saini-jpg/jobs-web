Selection Process

  • Selection will be made purely on the basis of merit drawn from qualifying examination marks
  • followed by document scrutiny and a mandatory medical test. No written examination will be conducted by NCL for this recruitment.
  • Stage 1 – Merit List Preparation
  • For Graduate and Diploma positions
  • merit is drawn on the basis of marks scored in the qualifying Graduation/Diploma examination. For ITI Trade positions
  • merit is based on the average of passing percentage in Matriculation and the respective National/State Trade Certificate. Candidates are arranged in descending order of marks. In case of a tie
  • date of birth (older candidate preferred) and then alphabetical order of name will be applied.
  • Stage 2 – Document Scrutiny
  • Shortlisted candidates based on merit will be called to appear with all original certificates for scrutiny. Date and venue will be notified on nclcil.in and through NATS/NAPS portal or email. Failure to appear or produce valid documents within the stipulated date and time will result in cancellation of candidature.
  • Stage 3 – Medical Examination
  • Final offer for Apprenticeship Training is subject to clearing the Medical Test as per prescribed standards under the Mines Rules 1955. A fitness certificate from the medical officer of a Government Hospital (rank not less than Assistant Civil Surgeon) in the prescribed format (Annexure-3) must be produced. The Medical Fitness certificate must be issued after the date of release of this Notification.

How To Apply

  • Candidates must apply online only. Graduate and Diploma Apprentice candidates must first enrol on the NATS portal at https://nats.education.gov.in and Trade Apprentice candidates must enrol on the NAPS portal at https://www.apprenticeshipindia.gov.in. After successful registration, a registration number (Enrolment ID) will be generated, following which the candidate must apply for the NCL positions through the respective portal.
  • Documents/Details required at the time of Online Application:
  • Valid NATS/NAPS Enrolment ID (registration reference).
  • Scanned colour photograph and scanned signature in black ink (as per portal specifications).
  • Date of Birth as per Matriculation certificate.
  • Academic details - total marks scored and maximum marks in qualifying degree/diploma/ITI trade.
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC/EWS) in prescribed format (Annexure-1), if applicable.
  • PwBD disability certificate as per Rights of Persons with Disabilities Rules 2017 (Annexure-2), if applicable.
  • Address for correspondence and permanent address.
  • Candidates must save and preserve a hard copy as well as soft copy of the finally submitted online application for future reference. No paper-based or postal applications will be accepted.
  • Apply Online (Graduate/Diploma) at: https://nats.education.gov.in
  • Apply Online (Trade/ITI) at: https://www.apprenticeshipindia.gov.in
  • For assistance, candidates may email the HRD Department, NCL at [email protected] or visit Menu > Career > Apprenticeship Training section on the NCL official website www.nclcil.in.

Important Instructions

  • Online applications are open from 01-06-2026 to 15-06-2026 through NATS/NAPS portals only. A candidate may apply for any one notified position only - multiple applications will lead to disqualification. Information pertaining to Date of Birth, Caste Category, and Qualification once entered in the online application shall be considered final - no alteration will be permitted at later stages. Stipend for Graduate and Diploma Apprentices is inclusive of the Government of India share paid through DBT; Trade Apprentice stipend will be paid directly by NCL. Stipend will be paid directly into the AADHAAR-linked bank account of the Apprenticeship Trainee as per the signed contract. Apprenticeship Training is purely temporary and statutory in nature - it does not confer any right or claim for regular employment in NCL. Apprentices are eligible for 11 days Casual Leave and 15 days Medical Leave per year of training. Engagement hours are between 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Apprentices will be covered under the Workmen Compensation Act, 1923 for any accident during training. No accommodation, fooding/lodging, or transport will be provided during the training period. No TA/DA will be paid for appearing in document scrutiny or post-scrutiny processes. Canvassing in any manner at any stage will lead to cancellation of candidature. Any dispute will be subject to courts/tribunals of Singrauli, MP only. Dates mentioned in the notification are tentative and subject to alteration/amendment.
