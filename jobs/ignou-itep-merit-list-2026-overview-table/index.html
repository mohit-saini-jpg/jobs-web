IGNOU ITEP Result Status

The IGNOU ITEP merit list 2026 for the BSc B.Ed programme has been officially released. The document lists selected candidates by rank, along with a separate waiting list for candidates who have not secured a direct seat in the first round.

Selection Stage Covered

This release covers the merit-based selection stage of ITEP admission. Selection to ITEP is based on candidates' performance in the relevant national-level entrance test, followed by preparation of a category-wise merit list by IGNOU. Admission offers are extended in order of rank, category, and seat availability.

  • Category-wise merit list (Unreserved, OBC-(NCL), SC, ST, EWS, and PwBD as applicable)
  • Main rank list for directly selected candidates
  • Extended waiting list for candidates awaiting seat allotment

Score Card Availability and Cut Off Marks

As of now, IGNOU has not released a separate scorecard for ITEP entrance test candidates along with this merit list. Only the rank-based merit list and waiting list have been published.

Cut off marks have not been officially released by IGNOU for the ITEP BSc B.Ed July 2026 session. Selection is currently based purely on candidate rank within each category, as reflected in the merit list. Candidates should rely only on official IGNOU communication for cut off information, and should not treat any unofficial figures as final.

How to Check the IGNOU ITEP Merit List 2026

Candidates can follow these steps to check their name and rank in the IGNOU ITEP BSc B.Ed merit list:

  • Visit the official IGNOU website or the direct merit list link.
  • Open the ITEP BSc B.Ed July 2026 merit list PDF.
  • Use the search function (Ctrl+F) to look up your application number or name.
  • Check your category, programme code, and rank or waiting list number.
  • Download and save a copy of the merit list PDF for future reference.

Details Mentioned on the IGNOU ITEP Merit List

The official merit list PDF includes the following details for each candidate:

  • Application Number
  • Candidate Name
  • Father's Name
  • Category (Unreserved, OBC-(NCL), SC, ST, GEN-EWS, PwBD, etc.)
  • Programme Code (BSCBED)
  • Rank or Waiting List (WL) Number

What to Do After Checking the Merit List

Candidates whose names appear in the selected rank list should watch for an official offer of admission communicated by IGNOU, typically through the candidate's registered email address. Those on the waiting list should continue monitoring official updates, as seats vacated in later rounds are usually offered to waitlisted candidates in order of rank.

  • Regularly check the registered email ID used during application.
  • Keep the application number and login details handy.
  • Do not make any payment based on unofficial calls, messages, or links.
  • Watch the official IGNOU website for admission and document verification updates.

Documents Required for Admission

While IGNOU has not published a separate document checklist specifically for this merit list release, candidates called for admission to ITEP are generally expected to keep the following documents ready, as is standard for IGNOU programme admissions:

  • Secondary (10th) and Senior Secondary (12th) mark sheets and certificates
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC-(NCL)/EWS/PwBD), where applicable
  • Recent passport-size photographs
  • Valid photo identity proof
  • Entrance test admit card and result-related documents, if issued

Candidates should confirm the exact document list once IGNOU issues its official admission or document verification notice, as requirements have not yet been separately announced for this round.

Selection Process

Admission to IGNOU's ITEP BSc B.Ed programme follows a merit-based selection process:

  • Candidates appear for the relevant national-level entrance test.
  • IGNOU prepares a category-wise merit list based on entrance test rank.
  • Top-ranked candidates in each category are offered admission directly.
  • Remaining seats, if any, are filled from the waiting list in order of rank as vacancies arise.
  • Selected candidates complete document verification and fee payment within the timeline communicated by IGNOU.

Important Instructions

  • Waitlisted candidates should avoid assuming automatic selection and should wait for an official offer.
