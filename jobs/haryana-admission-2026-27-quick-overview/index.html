Haryana Admission 2026-27 Quick Overview : Important Dates & Timelines

Notification DatePost Date: 11/07/2026

Haryana UG / PG 2nd / 3rd / 4th Year Admission 2026 notification has been released by the Department of Higher Education, Haryana (DGHE) on 10 July 2026. Students currently studying in Government, Government-Aided, and Self-Finance colleges across Haryana who are moving into the 2nd, 3rd, or 4th year of their Undergraduate (UG) and Postgraduate (PG) programmes for the 2026-27 academic session must complete their admission/promotion process through the Centralized Online Admission Portal. This article explains the important dates, fee structure, application process, and instructions exactly as stated in the official DGHE notice.

Students must note the following dates carefully, as fees increase progressively after the free admission window closes.

After 24 July 2026, every additional day of delay adds to the late fee, so students are advised to complete admission well before the first deadline.

Eligibility

  • The student must currently be enrolled in a Govt., Govt.-Aided, or Self-Finance college in Haryana and be eligible for promotion to the 2nd, 3rd, or 4th year of a UG or PG programme for the 2026-27 session.
  • Detailed academic eligibility (minimum marks, pass criteria, subject combination, etc.) is decided as per the norms of the affiliating university, and Principals are responsible for ensuring compliance. The DGHE notice does not list university-wise eligibility criteria, so students should confirm this with their respective college or affiliating university.

Payment Mode - Govt. CollegesOnline and Offline

For offline payment, the student submits the fee at the college, and the fee receipt is generated through the admission portal only. If a student pays online, the late fee (if applicable) is automatically deducted from the total admission fee; in offline collection, the college submits the late fee amount to DGHE headquarters separately after admissions close.

How to Apply: Haryana UG PG Admission Online Form 2026

  • Visit the official Centralized Online Admission Portal at admissions.highereduhry.ac.in.
  • Log in using your student credentials for the respective college/programme.
  • Check and verify your course/programme, subject allocation, and fee details as updated by your college.
  • Complete the Anti-Ragging Undertaking on the portal (linked with antiragging.in) - this is mandatory as per Supreme Court and UGC/AICTE regulations.
  • Pay the admission fee - online for Govt. colleges, or offline at the college counter for Aided/Self-Finance colleges.
  • Submit the form before 24 July 2026 to avoid any late fee.
  • Save/download the confirmation or fee receipt generated by the portal for future reference.

Important Instructions

  • Admission through the Centralized Online Admission Portal is mandatory for all Govt., Govt.-Aided, and Self-Finance colleges - promotion to 2nd, 3rd, and 4th year will be processed only through this portal.
  • Colleges must first update the Programme/Course Fee on the admission portal for the 2026-27 session before students can proceed.
  • Complete admission before 24 July 2026 to avoid late fees.
  • Late fee amount stays with the State Government; colleges are required to submit collected late fees to DGHE headquarters.
  • Submitting the Anti-Ragging Undertaking online is compulsory for all admitted students.
  • Timely submission of the Registration Return to the affiliating university is the college's responsibility.
  • For any admission-related query, students can contact the helpdesk at [email protected] or call the toll-free number 1800-180-2133.
