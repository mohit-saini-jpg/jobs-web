Important Instructions

THIS IS NOT A REGULAR EMPLOYMENT. Engagement of Young Professionals is purely on a contractual basis for consultancy purposes. Young Professionals are not entitled to any benefit or compensation available to regular NSIC employees, including DA, HRA, CGHS, Medical reimbursement, or Provident Fund. The engagement is on a full-time basis. The selected candidate cannot take up any other assignment in any other company during the period of engagement. Candidates may apply for only one post. If more than one application is submitted, only the first application (by system-generated date and time) will be considered and the remaining will be summarily rejected. Prescribed qualifications and experience must have been acquired on or before 08-06-2026. Qualifications or experience acquired after the cut-off will not be considered. Incomplete applications will be summarily rejected. Once submitted, no request to add or modify information will be entertained under any circumstances. NSIC reserves the right to upgrade eligibility criteria and increase or decrease the number of vacancies in any category without assigning any reason. Call letters and all future communications will be sent by email only. Candidates must ensure their registered email ID is active for at least 3 months. NSIC bears no responsibility for non-delivery of emails. Shortlisting for interview is at NSIC's sole discretion — fulfilling eligibility criteria does not guarantee a call for interview. The selected candidate must join within 15 days of the offer of engagement. Failure to join within this period will result in automatic withdrawal of the offer. Income Tax and other applicable taxes will be deducted at source. NSIC will issue TDS certificates. GST, as applicable, shall be admissible to Young Professionals. The Young Professional is responsible for paying their own Income Tax as applicable. NSIC or the Competent Authority may terminate the contract at any time by giving 30 days' notice or paying 30 days' compensation in lieu thereof, without assigning any reason. Antecedent/police verification of selected candidates will be conducted as per MHA guidelines. A negative police verification report will result in immediate termination of the contract without any notice. No abbreviations should be used in the application where full forms are indicated — applications with abbreviations in such fields are liable for rejection. NSIC reserves the right to cancel, modify, withdraw, or postpone this recruitment notice at any time without assigning any reason. All corrigenda and updates will be published on www.nsic.co.in only — candidates should check the website regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1Q1. What is NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
The NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026 is a contractual hiring initiative by the National Small Industries Corporation Limited (NSIC) under the Ministry of MSME, Government of India. It aims to engage qualified professionals across multiple domains including IT, Finance, Marketing, Engineering, Legal, and Digital Media. The recruitment is purely on a contract basis and does not provide permanent government employment benefits.
Q2Q2. How many vacancies are available in NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 17 Young Professional vacancies have been announced under NSIC Recruitment 2026. These posts are distributed across 12 specialised domains, including Software Development, Digital Marketing, Company Secretary, Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and MSME Business Outreach. The number of vacancies may be revised depending on organizational requirements.
Q3Q3. What is the eligibility criteria for NSIC Young Professional posts?
A
Candidates must hold a relevant graduation, post-graduation, or professional qualification depending on the post applied for. In addition, a minimum of 2 years of work experience in the relevant field is mandatory. Applicants must also ensure that their qualifications and experience are acquired on or before the cut-off date mentioned in the notification.
Q4Q4. What is the age limit for NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
The upper age limit for NSIC Young Professional posts is 40 years as on 08 June 2026. Age relaxation is applicable for reserved categories such as SC, ST, OBC-NCL, and PwBD candidates as per Government of India rules. However, candidates must still meet the minimum experience requirement along with age eligibility.
Q5Q5. What is the salary offered in NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
Selected candidates will receive a consolidated monthly pay ranging from Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 70,000, depending on the post and experience. There are no additional allowances such as DA, HRA, or medical benefits. However, performance-based annual increments of up to 5% may be granted, subject to a maximum ceiling.
Q6Q6. What is the selection process for NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
The selection process consists of shortlisting of applications followed by an interview or interaction. Candidates are shortlisted based on their qualifications, experience, and domain relevance. Final selection is made through interview performance, and only shortlisted candidates are called via email for further stages.
Q7Q7. Is NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026 a permanent job?
A
No, this recruitment is strictly contractual in nature. Selected candidates will be engaged initially for one year, extendable up to a maximum of five years based on performance. It does not offer permanent government job status, pension, or regular employee benefits.
Q8Q8. What is the application process for NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must apply online through the official NSIC website (www.nsic.co.in) under the Careers section. The process includes registration, filling the application form, uploading documents, and submitting the form online. After submission, candidates receive a system-generated application number for future reference.

The last date to submit the online application for NSIC Young Professional Recruitment 2026 is 08 June 2026. Candidates are advised to complete the application process well before the deadline to avoid technical issues. No applications will be accepted after the closing date.
