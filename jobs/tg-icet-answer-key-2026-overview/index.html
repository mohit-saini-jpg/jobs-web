How to Calculate Score Using TG ICET Answer Key 2026

The TG ICET 2026 provisional answer key lets candidates estimate their tentative scores well before result declaration. You can explain the calculation method in simple steps:

  • Download the answer key, master question paper and your response sheet from the official portal.
  • For each question, compare the option you marked in your response sheet with the correct option in the preliminary key.
  • Count the total number of correct responses.
  • Apply the official marking scheme given in the TG ICET information brochure (for example, if each correct answer carries one mark and there is no negative marking, tentative score = number of correct answers).

Make it clear that the tentative score is only for reference and that the final score and rank will be based on the result prepared after evaluating objections and publishing the final key.

TG ICET 2026 Answer Key - Objection Window & Fee

Candidates who find any discrepancy in the TG ICET Answer Key 2026 can raise objections during the official objection window. As per the schedule, objections against the provisional answer key can be submitted from 30 May 2026 to 5 June 2026.

A processing fee of ₹500 per question is applicable while raising each objection, payable through the prescribed payment modes on the portal. After scrutinising the objections and supporting documents, the authorities will release the final answer key, which will be used for result preparation.

You can describe the generic steps for raising objections as:

  • Log in at icet.tgche.ac.in using your TG ICET credentials.
  • Click on the “Objections on Preliminary Key” link.
  • Select the question number and exam session/set where you find an error.
  • Enter your proposed correct answer and justification with reference.
  • Upload documentary proof (standard textbooks, official sources) if asked.
  • Pay the objection fee and submit the form within the deadline.

This reinforces transparency and gives users a practical guide without over‑claiming.

TG ICET 2026 Exam - Eligibility & Key Details (Brief)

This section satisfies broader informational intent for users who are also searching about the exam.

TG ICET 2026 Eligibility Snapshot

TG ICET is conducted for admission to MBA and MCA programmes offered by universities and colleges in Telangana. In general terms:

  • For MBA: Candidates should hold a bachelor’s degree of at least three years duration from a recognised university with minimum marks as specified in the notification.
  • For MCA: Candidates usually require a bachelor’s degree with Mathematics at 10+2 level or at the degree level, with minimum marks as prescribed.

Always advise readers to refer to the official TG ICET 2026 notification/brochure for detailed, category‑wise eligibility, reservations and age criteria.

Exam Pattern & Courses Through TG ICET

TG ICET 2026 is held in computer‑based test mode and typically assesses Analytical Ability, Mathematical Ability and Communication Ability through objective questions. The exam acts as a common entrance for:

  • MBA courses in universities and affiliated colleges in Telangana
  • MCA courses in participating institutions across the state

Mention that exact pattern (number of questions, duration, section‑wise distribution) should be confirmed from the latest official brochure to avoid outdated details.
