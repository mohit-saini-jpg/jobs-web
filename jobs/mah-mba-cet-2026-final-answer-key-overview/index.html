How to Download MAH MBA CET 2026 Final Answer Key for Second Attempt

Step-by-step process to download the final answer key

Candidates can follow these simple steps to download the final answer key:

  • Visit the official website of Maharashtra State CET Cell: cetcell.mahacet.org.
  • On the home page, navigate to the “MAH-MBA/MMS-CET 2026” link under the list of examinations.
  • Open the MAH MBA/MMS CET 2026 page and scroll to the “Notifications” or “Latest Announcements” section.
  • Click on the link mentioning “Final Answer Key / Objection Redressal - MAH MBA/MMS CET 2026 Second Attempt” or a similar title.
  • The final answer key PDF will open in a new tab; download or save it for reference.

How to check your answers using the final key

Once you download the final answer key:

  • Note your Question IDs / serial numbers and the options you selected in the exam.
  • Compare each question’s official final option with the option you marked.
  • Mark each response as correct or incorrect based on the final key.
  • Use the official marking scheme to estimate your probable raw score.

This process gives you a fair idea of your performance before the MAH MBA CET 2026 result is declared.

Objection Redressal and Changes in MAH MBA CET 2026 Final Answer Key

Before the final answer key, the CET Cell had released the provisional answer key and opened a window for candidates to submit objections with valid proof. Subject experts evaluated all the challenges and accepted only those objections that were found academically valid and backed by credible references. The questions affected by accepted objections have been updated in the final answer key, along with the corrected options.

After the publication of this final key, the objection process stands closed. That means candidates can no longer raise new objections or request changes. The final key is binding and will directly determine each candidate’s marks and percentile.

How to Calculate MAH MBA CET 2026 Score Using Final Answer Key

Marking scheme for MAH MBA CET 2026

MAH MBA CET generally follows a straightforward marking pattern:

  • 1 mark is awarded for every correct answer.
  • No marks are deducted for incorrect answers.
  • No marks are awarded or deducted for questions not attempted.

Candidates should always verify the exact marking scheme in the official information brochure for the current session, but this pattern is typically followed.

Example of calculating your tentative raw score

To estimate your score using the final answer key:

  • Step 1: Count the total number of correct answers as per the final key.
  • Step 2: Count the total number of incorrect and unattempted questions (these carry 0 marks).
  • Step 3: Your tentative raw score = number of correct answers × 1.

For example, if you attempted most questions and, after comparison, you find 165 correct responses, your estimated score will be 165 marks. The CET Cell will later convert this raw score to a percentile based on the performance of all candidates.

MAH MBA CET 2026 Result and Next Steps After Final Answer Key

Once the final answer key is released, the next major event is the declaration of MAH MBA CET 2026 result. The CET Cell will use the final answer key to compute each candidate’s raw marks and then convert these into percentile scores. These percentile scores will be reflected on the MAH MBA CET 2026 scorecard.

Candidates will have to log in with their application number and password on the official website to download the scorecard. The scorecard will show your overall percentile, which plays a crucial role in MBA/MMS admissions across Maharashtra.

What happens after the result - CAP rounds and admissions

After the MAH MBA CET 2026 result is out, the State CET Cell will launch the Centralised Admission Process (CAP) for MBA/MMS programmes. During CAP:

  • Candidates must register online and upload required documents.
  • They will fill and lock their preferences for participating institutes and courses.
  • Seats will be allotted based on MAH MBA CET percentile, category, and preferences.
  • Candidates must confirm their seats by reporting to the allotted institute within the given deadlines.

Your estimated score from the final answer key can help you identify realistic target institutes before CAP registration opens.

MAH MBA CET 2026 Eligibility

Although the final answer key is mainly for current test-takers, many aspirants who will appear in future sessions also search for eligibility details. A short recap:

  • Educational qualification: Candidates should hold a Bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognised university with the minimum percentage prescribed by the authorities, with relaxation for reserved categories.
  • Final-year candidates: Students appearing in the final year of their graduation are usually eligible, provided they pass and submit proof within the stipulated timeline.
  • Nationality: The exam is primarily for Indian nationals; NRI/OCI/PIO/Foreign nationals and Children of Indian Workers in Gulf Countries may have separate rules and quotas as per official notifications.
  • Age limit: Typically, there is no upper age limit specified for MAH MBA CET.

For accurate and updated criteria, aspirants must always refer to the latest official MAH MBA/MMS CET brochure.

Important Instructions for MAH MBA CET 2026 Second Attempt Candidates

Candidates who took the second attempt of MAH MBA CET 2026 should keep these points in mind:

  • Use only the officially released final answer key to estimate your score; ignore unofficial keys now.
  • Do not expect any changes after the final key, as the objection process is closed.
  • Keep checking the official website and your registered email/SMS for result, scorecard, and CAP schedule updates.
  • Keep your application details, admit card, and identity documents safe for future verification.
  • Start shortlisting institutes based on your tentative score and previous years’ cut-offs to act quickly once CAP begins.
