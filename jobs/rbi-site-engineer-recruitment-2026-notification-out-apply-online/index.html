RBI Site Engineer Eligibility Criteria 2026

Eligibility for RBI Site Engineer Recruitment 2026 is reckoned as on 01 June 2026 and includes nationality, age, educational qualification, and work experience requirements specific to each post. All three posts require a B.Tech (or equivalent) in the relevant stream along with extensive government-sector construction experience. RBI Site Engineer Recruitment 2026 : How to Apply Online Step-by-Step

  • 1Candidates can apply for RBI Site Engineer Recruitment 2026 only through the official website www.rbi.org.in between 29 June 2026 and 20 July 2026. The process requires registration, document upload, a live photograph capture, and online fee payment before final submission.
  • 2Visit www.rbi.org.in, go to Opportunities@RBI > Current Vacancies > Vacancies, and select "Lateral Recruitment of Site Engineers on Full-Time Contract Basis in RBI."
  • 3Click "Online Application Form" and select "Click here for New Registration"; enter your name, contact details, and email ID to generate a Provisional Registration Number and Password.
  • 4Log in and fill in personal, educational, and experience details, using "Save and Next" to verify entries before final submission.
  • 5Upload your scanned photograph (20-50KB, JPG), signature (10-20KB, JPG), left thumb impression, and handwritten declaration as per the prescribed specifications.
  • 6Capture and upload a live photograph using a webcam or mobile phone QR scan during registration.
  • 7Upload supporting PDF documents (20KB-500KB each) for age, category, educational qualification, and work experience proof.
  • 8Click "Complete Registration" only after verifying all details, since no changes are allowed afterward.
  • 9Proceed to the "Payment" tab and pay the applicable fee via debit card, credit card, net banking, IMPS, or mobile wallet, then click "Submit."
  • 10Candidates should submit only one application per post; in case of multiple submissions, only the last completed application with the higher Registration ID (RID) will be considered, and fees paid against one RID cannot be adjusted to another.
