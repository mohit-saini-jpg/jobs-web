NIEPID Recruitment 2026 - Selection Process

NIEPID follows a structured selection process depending on post level and category. For Regular Group 'A' Posts (e.g., Lecturer in Rehabilitation Psychology): Screening of applications by a duly constituted Screening Committee Shortlisting (minimum 1:5 ratio per vacancy) Written Examination (if more than 20 applications received) Personal Interview For Regular Group 'B' & 'C' Posts (Stenographer, LDC/Typist, Physiotherapy Assistant): Screening of applications Merit-based selection as per criteria approved by the Ministry (interviews dispensed as per DoPT guidelines) For Contractual Posts: Screening of applications Written examination (if more than 20 applications received for a post) Personal Interview before appropriate committee For CDEIC Contractual Posts: Selection on contractual basis for 12 months, extendable based on satisfactory performance

NIEPID Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is the last date to apply for NIEPID Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit applications for all regular, contractual, and CDEIC posts under Employment Notice No. 03/2026 is 01 August 2026. Applications received after this date will not be considered.
Q2How many vacancies are available in NIEPID Recruitment 2026?
A
There are 6 regular posts (Lecturer in Rehabilitation Psychology – 2, Stenographer – 1, LDC/Typist – 2, Physiotherapy Assistant – 1) along with multiple contractual and CDEIC positions across NIEPID headquarters and its regional/composite centres.
Q3What is the application fee for NIEPID 2026?
A
The application fee is ₹500/- per post (non-refundable Demand Draft) for General/OBC/EWS candidates. No fee is charged from SC/ST/Women/PH category candidates.
Q4What is the selection process for NIEPID Lecturer post?
A
Selection for the Lecturer in Rehabilitation Psychology (Group A) involves application screening by a Screening Committee, shortlisting at a 1:5 ratio, and a Personal Interview. If more than 20 applications are received, a written examination is conducted before the interview.
Q5What qualifications are needed for the Lecturer in Rehabilitation Psychology post?
A
Candidates need a two-year M.Phil. in Clinical/Rehabilitation Psychology from a reputed institution (or Ph.D. in relevant Psychology), valid RCI/MCI registration, and a minimum of 2 years of teaching/research experience. The upper age limit is 45 years.
Q6Is RCI registration mandatory for all posts?
A
No. RCI/MCI registration is mandatory only for specific posts - including Lecturer in Rehabilitation Psychology (regular) and several contractual/CDEIC posts such as Assistant Professor (PMR), (Speech), (Clinical Psychology), Clinical Psychologist, Occupational Therapist, ASLP, and others. Posts like Stenographer, LDC/Typist, and Pharmacist do not require RCI registration.
