Physical Eligibility

DetailsSince this is a deputation vacancy from Central Government, competition is limited to serving Central Government Officers at Level 8/10 of the Pay Matrix — a well-defined and relatively focused pool. With 60 vacancies across 20 RPOs spread pan-India, interested officers have the opportunity to express preference for up to 3 RPO stations. The desirable criteria strongly favour officers with prior experience in Sections dealing with Administration & Establishment, RTI, Finance, Audit, and public dealing — making CSS (Central Secretariat Service) officers and those from similarly structured Departments particularly well-placed. Officers with clean vigilance and service records and those who have handled passport or public services-related work will have an edge. | The 2-year deputation period (extendable as per rules) and the mandatory requirement of serving the full term should be carefully considered by officers before applying. Serving at an RPO involves significant public dealing and field-level administrative work — distinct from typical Secretariat/Ministry desk work — which officers should factor into their decision.

Important Instructions

This is a deputation vacancy — only serving Central Government Officers holding analogous posts in Level 8/10 of the Pay Matrix on regular basis are eligible. It is mandatory to serve the full deputation term. Premature relieving will not be considered except in rare or exceptional circumstances. Applications must be forwarded through proper channel — applications not sent through proper channel will not be considered. The forwarding Ministry/Department must verify and certify that particulars furnished by the officer are correct and must confirm that if selected, the officer shall be relieved on immediate basis. Only applications complete in all aspects will be considered — incomplete applications will be rejected. ACRs/APARs for the last 5 years must be duly attested on each page by a Group A Officer. Officers must state their preference of up to 3 RPO stations from the list of 20 in Annexure-II. Advance copies may be mailed to [email protected] — but this alone does not constitute a valid application. Pay and allowances will be admissible as per DoPT O.M. No. 6/8/2009-Estt. (Pay II) dated 17.06.2010 and amendments. Selected officers may be required to travel within India. The last date for forwarding applications through proper channel is 17-07-2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Q1. What is MEA Section Officer Deputation Recruitment 2026 for Regional Passport Offices?
A
The MEA Section Officer Deputation Recruitment 2026 is a vacancy circular to fill 60 Section Officer or equivalent posts in 20 Regional Passport Offices (RPOs) across India on deputation basis, issued under Circular No. CDR-II/575/02/2025 dated 22-05-2026. These are not permanent appointments, but temporary deputation postings for eligible Central Government officers.
Q2 Q2. How many vacancies are there and in which RPOs under MEA Section Officer Deputation 2026?
A
There are 60 vacancies in total, with 3 Section Officer posts in each of the 20 Regional Passport Offices such as Amritsar, Bengaluru, Bhubaneswar, Chennai, Cochin, Coimbatore, Guwahati, Jalandhar, Kozhikode, Madurai, Mumbai, Nagpur, Panaji, Pune, Srinagar, Surat, Trichy, Trivandrum, Vijayawada, and Visakhapatnam. The exact distribution is provided in Annexure-II of the vacancy circular.
Q3 Q3. Who is eligible to apply for MEA Section Officer RPO Deputation 2026?
A
Only serving Central Government officers are eligible. They must be working in any Ministry, Department or Office of the Central Government and holding an analogous post in Level 8 or Level 10 of the Pay Matrix on a regular basis. Applications from State Government, PSU, autonomous bodies, or private sector employees will not be considered.
Q4 Q4. What is the age limit for MEA Section Officer Deputation Recruitment 2026?
A
The maximum age limit is 56 years as on 01-01-2026 for appointment on deputation. There is no separately prescribed minimum age other than being a serving eligible Central Government officer, but any officer who has attained more than 56 years on 01-01-2026 is ineligible.
Q5 Q5. What is the period of deputation and can it be curtailed?
A
The initial period of deputation is 2 years, which is extendable as per extant rules of the Government of India. Serving the full deputation term is mandatory, and premature relieving will not be considered except in rare or exceptional circumstances, so officers should apply only if they are ready to complete the full tenure.
Q6 Q6. What are the desirable qualifications and experience for MEA Section Officer RPO posts?
A
Desirable attributes include good managerial and interpersonal skills, strong noting and drafting abilities, and the capacity to liaise with other Ministries, Departments and organizations. Preference is given to officers who have worked as Section Officer or equivalent in Central Government offices and handled Administration & Establishment, Policy, Court Cases, RTI, Finance & Accounts, Audit, and public dealing.
Q7 Q7. What is the pay scale and allowances for MEA Section Officer Deputation in RPOs?
A
The posts are in Level 8 or Level 10 of the 7th CPC Pay Matrix, depending on the officer’s present analogous grade. Pay and allowances during deputation will be governed by DoPT’s O.M. No. 6/8/2009-Estt. (Pay II) dated 17.06.2010 and subsequent amendments, which regulate deputation pay, allowances, and related benefits.
Q8 Q8. How can eligible officers apply for MEA Section Officer Deputation 2026?
A
Interested officers must apply in the prescribed pro-forma (Annexure-I) and forward their applications through proper channel via their Ministry/Department/Cadre Controlling Authority so that they reach MEA on or before 17-07-2026. An advance copy of the application can be sent by email to [email protected], but only applications duly forwarded through proper channel will be considered.
