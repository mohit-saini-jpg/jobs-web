NBEMS Selection Process 2026

Candidates wondering about the NBEMS selection process can note that it differs by post category. For Junior Accountant, Stenographer, and Junior Assistant, selection happens in two stages: a Computer Based Test (CBT) followed by a Computer Knowledge/Skill Test. There is no interview for Group B and C posts. The CBT consists of 200 objective-type questions worth 200 marks, to be completed in 180 minutes (3 hours). Each wrong answer carries a negative marking of 0.25 marks. The minimum qualifying marks are 40% for SC/ST/PwBD/Ex-Servicemen candidates and 50% for all others. Candidates who clear Stage-I are called for the Stage-II skill test, which carries 100 marks over a 75-minute duration. Five candidates per vacancy in each category are shortlisted based on Stage-I merit. NBEMS Service Bond and Document Requirements

Selected candidates must execute a Penalty Bond agreeing to serve NBEMS for a minimum continuous period of two years, coinciding with the probation period. If a candidate resigns before completing this two-year period, they are liable to pay three months' last-drawn salary as liquidated damages. Candidates also need to keep at least 5 colour passport-size photographs, the confirmation page, application printout, and e-admit card for future reference, along with original documents for verification when called.

NBEMS Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1What is NBEMS Recruitment 2026?

Q5What is the eligibility for NBEMS Deputy Director (Medical) post?
A
For the Deputy Director (Medical) post, the candidate must be below 35 years of age and must hold a recognized Postgraduate Medical qualification approved under the Indian Medical Council Act-1956. Desirable qualifications include training in medical education technology and knowledge of modern evaluation techniques such as MCQ generation and MEQ analysis.
Q6What is the qualification required for NBEMS Junior Assistant post?
A
For the Junior Assistant post, candidates must have passed the Senior Secondary Examination (12th of 10+2) from a Board/University recognized by Central/State Govt./UT Administration, must have proficiency in computers and basic software (Windows, LAN architecture), and must qualify the examination as may be prescribed by NBEMS.
Q7What is the pay scale for NBEMS Junior Assistant 2026?
A
The Junior Assistant post is placed at Pay Matrix Level 2 under the 7th CPC, with a basic pay range of approximately ₹19,900 to ₹63,200. In addition to basic pay, employees receive Dearness Allowance, HRA, Transport Allowance, and other Central Government benefits.
Q8What is the age limit for NBEMS Stenographer post?
A
The age limit for the NBEMS Stenographer post is 18 to 27 years. Age relaxation for SC/ST/OBC/PwBD and other reserved categories is applicable as per Government of India norms.
