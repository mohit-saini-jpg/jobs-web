Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What is the total number of vacancies for UKSSSC Pashudhan Prasar Adhikari 2026?

A total of 120 vacancies have been announced — 108 posts are for direct recruitment (सीधी भर्ती) and 12 posts are reserved exclusively for departmental employees (विभागीय कार्मिकों) of the Animal Husbandry Department, Uttarakhand. The number of vacancies is subject to change based on revised requisitions from the concerned department.

Q2. What is the educational qualification required to apply for Pashudhan Prasar Adhikari?

For direct recruitment candidates, a Bachelor's Degree in any one of the following subjects from a university established by law in India is required: Biology (जीव विज्ञान), Agriculture (कृषि), or Animal Husbandry (पशुपालन). For departmental employees applying under the 12 reserved posts, Intermediate (10+2) with Biology or Agriculture subjects along with 5 years of continuous departmental service is sufficient.

Q3. What is the age limit for UKSSSC Pashudhan Prasar Adhikari 2026, and what is the cut-off date?

The decisive cut-off date for age calculation is 01 July 2026. Candidates must be minimum 21 years and maximum 42 years of age as on this date, meaning they must have been born not after 01 July 2005 and not before 02 July 1984. Age relaxation of 5 years is available for SC/ST/OBC (Uttarakhand), 5 years for Freedom Fighter dependants, and 10 years for Persons with Disabilities (Uttarakhand) for Group-C posts. Q4. What is the application fee, and is it refundable?

The examination fee is Rs. 300/- for Unreserved / OBC candidates, Rs. 150/- for Uttarakhand SC/ST/EWS and Divyang candidates, and Rs. 00/- (Nil) for Orphan (Anath) candidates. Fee must be paid online via Credit Card, Debit Card, Net Banking, or UPI. The fee is non-refundable under any circumstances. Bank drafts and cheques are not accepted.

Q5. What is the exam pattern and syllabus for the Pashudhan Prasar Adhikari written examination?

The written examination consists of one objective type (MCQ) paper of 100 questions carrying 100 marks, to be completed in 2 hours. The paper is divided into three parts: Part A (Biology – 30 marks, compulsory for all), and Part B (Biology – Botany and Zoology, 70 marks) or Part C (Agriculture and Animal Husbandry, 70 marks) — candidates must attempt any one of Part B or C. There is negative marking of ¼ (0.25) marks for each wrong answer. The exam may be conducted in offline OMR mode or online

Q7. What are the minimum qualifying marks required to pass the written examination?

Candidates belonging to Unreserved (UR), OBC, and EWS categories must score a minimum of 45% marks in the written examination. Candidates belonging to SC and ST categories must score a minimum of 35% marks. Candidates who do not meet these minimum qualifying marks will not be considered in the written competitive examination and will be disqualified from further stages.

Q8. What happens after the written examination? Is there an interview or physical test?

There is no interview or physical test in this recruitment. After the written examination result is declared, candidates in the merit list will be called for mandatory Document Verification (अभिलेख सन्निरीक्षा). All reservation/qualification claims are verified at this stage. Candidates who fail to appear for document verification or whose documents are found incorrect/invalid will have their candidature cancelled.
