What Is JAC Delhi Counselling 2026?

This section explains what JAC Delhi is and which institutes participate, for readers who are new to the counselling process.

JAC Delhi, or the Joint Admission Committee, Delhi, conducts the centralised counselling process for admission to B.Tech and B.Arch programmes at government-funded technical universities in the National Capital Territory of Delhi. Admissions are carried out on the basis of candidates' ranks obtained in the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE Main) conducted by the National Testing Agency.

Five institutions participate in JAC Delhi 2026: Netaji Subhas University of Technology (NSUT), Delhi Technological University (DTU), Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (IGDTUW), Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology Delhi (IIIT-Delhi), and Delhi Skill and Entrepreneurship University (DSEU). A notable change in 2026 is that DSEU has rejoined the JAC Delhi counselling process after independently conducting its own admissions in 2025, adding over 1,040 seats across its nine Delhi campuses. This has pushed the total 2026 seat pool to approximately 7,855 seats, a significant increase from 2025's 6,491 seats.

How to Check JAC Delhi Round 2 Seat Allotment Result 2026

This section walks candidates through the exact steps to view and download their Round 2 allotment letter online.

Candidates can check their result by following these steps:

  • Visit the official website, jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in.
  • Click on the "Seat Allotment Result" or "View Seat Allotment" link under eServices.
  • Log in using your JEE Main 2026 application number and password.
  • Your allotment letter will be displayed showing the allotted institute, programme, category, and reporting instructions.
  • Download and print the allotment letter, as it will be required at the time of physical reporting.

The allotment letter includes the candidate's Roll No., Name, Father's Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Category, Subcategory, Rank Details, and Allotment Details, along with important instructions.

Next Steps After Round 2 Allotment: Freeze, Float, or Withdraw

This section explains the choices candidates must make once they see their Round 2 result, since each option affects future rounds differently.

Candidates who receive a seat must formally submit their "willingness" by choosing one of the available options. Broadly, the choices are:

  • Freeze: Accept the allotted seat as final. You will not be considered for upgradation in later rounds, but your admission is confirmed.
  • Float: Accept the current seat but remain eligible to be considered for a better-preferred course or institute in subsequent rounds.
  • Withdraw: Candidates withdrawing on or before July 12, 2026 (10:30 PM) via the JAC Delhi portal will receive a Seat Acceptance Fee refund as per the refund policy. No refund is given after this deadline, and withdrawn candidates become ineligible for all remaining JAC Delhi 2026 rounds.

Following willingness submission, candidates satisfied with their seat must complete document verification and fee payment. For Round 2, this must be completed by July 3, 2026, failing which the allotted seat is automatically cancelled with no extensions.

Seat Acceptance and Document Verification Process

This section outlines the mandatory post-allotment steps required to confirm admission.

After the Round 2 result, allotted candidates must:

  • Upload the required scanned documents in the prescribed format.
  • Submit their willingness (Freeze or Float).
  • Pay the applicable seat acceptance fee through the payment link on the login portal.
  • Report for document verification as per the schedule; this verification is followed by an update to the admission confirmation status on the candidate's login.
  • Once admission is confirmed, candidates can download the provisional admission letter from their login.

Note: candidates who have already verified documents and received a provisional letter in an earlier round need not report again in subsequent rounds, and those who get upgraded need not report to the new institute for fresh document verification.

JAC Delhi Registration and Seat Acceptance Fee

This section lists the fee structure candidates need to keep ready during counselling.

The JAC Delhi 2026 registration fee is Rs. 1,500, payable via credit card, debit card, or net banking. This fee is non-refundable regardless of whether a candidate receives a seat allotment. A separate seat acceptance fee applies once a seat is allotted, and this amount is adjustable against the final tuition fee after admission is confirmed. Balance fees, along with hostel and mess charges, must be paid directly to the allotted institute as per its own schedule.

JAC Delhi Round 2 Cutoff Trends 2026

This section explains how the Round 2 cutoff differs from Round 1 and what factors influence it.

Round 2 brings seats vacated through Round 1 withdrawals and upgrades back into the allotment pool, which typically relaxes closing ranks compared to Round 1. Factors that influence the Round 2 cutoff include:

  • Seat availability: Seats freed by Round 1 withdrawals re-enter the pool and relax closing ranks.
  • Category-wise applicant volume: More registrations in a given category push closing ranks lower.
  • Branch demand: CSE and IT branches remain highly competitive even in Round 2.
  • Quota: Seats are split 85% for Delhi region candidates and 15% for outside-Delhi applicants, which significantly affects branch-wise closing ranks.

Candidates should compare their JEE Main 2026 category-wise rank (not their Common Rank List rank) against the closing rank for their target branch and institute, as published in the official Opening Rank–Closing Rank (OR-CR) PDFs on the JAC Delhi website.

Documents Required for JAC Delhi 2026 Reporting

This section lists the documents candidates should keep ready for verification, since incomplete documentation can lead to seat cancellation.

Candidates should carry both originals and photocopies of:

  • JEE Main 2026 admit card and rank/score card
  • Class 10 and Class 12 mark sheets and certificates
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC-NCL/EWS/PwD), where applicable
  • Delhi region proof (based on the school from which Class 12 was passed)
  • Passport-size photographs
  • JAC Delhi registration confirmation slip and allotment letter

Note: OBC-NCL and EWS certificates must generally be issued on or after April 1, 2026, to be valid for JAC Delhi admissions. Candidates should also confirm that "Delhi" region status is based strictly on the Delhi PIN code of the school from which Class 12 was passed, not on domicile or permanent address.
