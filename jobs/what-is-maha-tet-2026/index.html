What Is MAHA TET 2026?

This section covers a quick overview of the Maharashtra Teacher Eligibility Test 2026, who conducts it, and why the admit card is a critical document for every appearing candidate.

The Maharashtra Teacher Eligibility Test (MAHA TET) is a state-level teacher recruitment examination conducted on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra by the Maharashtra State Board of Examination (MSCE), Pune. It assesses the eligibility of candidates who wish to teach in government and aided primary and upper primary schools across Maharashtra.

The Maharashtra TET 2026 is being held for two levels:

  • Paper 1 - For candidates aspiring to teach Classes 1 to 5 (Primary Level)
  • Paper 2 - For candidates aspiring to teach Classes 6 to 8 (Upper Primary Level)

The MAHA TET qualifying certificate holds lifetime validity, making it a one-time milestone for aspiring teachers in Maharashtra.

MAHA TET 2026 Admit Card - Key Highlights

Before going through the download steps, here is a summary of everything candidates need to know about the MAHA TET Hall Ticket 2026.

The Maharashtra TET Admit Card 2026 is available only in online mode - no admit card will be dispatched via mail or post. Candidates must download and take a printout of their hall ticket and carry it on the day of the exam. Without a valid admit card, entry to the examination hall will not be permitted.

  • Admit Card Release Date: 16th June 2026
  • Exam Date: 28th June 2026 (Sunday)
  • Paper 1 Shift: 10:30 AM to 1:00 PM (Morning Shift)
  • Paper 2 Shift: 2:30 PM to 5:00 PM (Evening Shift)
  • Login credentials required: Login ID and Password

How to Download MAHA TET Admit Card 2026 - Step-by-Step Process

This section explains the exact steps to download the Maharashtra TET Hall Ticket 2026 from the official website. Candidates should keep their application number or registered mobile number ready before beginning the process.

Follow these steps to download your MAHA TET 2026 Admit Card:

  • Visit the official website: https://mahatet.in/
  • On the homepage, go to the "Important Notice" section.
  • Click on the link: "Mahatet (2026) - Click here to download admit card"
  • A login page will appear on your screen.
  • Enter your Username and Password.
  • Enter the Captcha Code as shown and click Login
  • Your MAHA TET Admit Card 2026 will appear on screen.
  • Download and save the PDF file.
  • Take a colour printout and keep it safely for exam day.

Understanding the exam pattern before the examination helps candidates plan their preparation effectively. Here is a quick breakdown of the MAHA TET 2026 exam pattern.

  • Both papers consist of 150 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Each question carries 1 mark (Total: 150 marks per paper)
  • Duration: 2 hours 30 minutes for each paper
  • No negative marking for incorrect answers

MAHA TET 2026 Exam Centres

The MAHA TET 2026 will be conducted at various exam centres across Maharashtra. The exam authority has allocated centres based on the medium of examination and the number of candidates.

Key highlights of centre allocation:

  • 103 Bengali medium candidates will appear from centres in Pune and Chandrapur districts.
  • 1,018 Kannada medium candidates are allotted centres in Thane (72), Solapur (634), and Sangli (312).
  • 49 Telugu medium candidates have been allotted centres in Pune (24) and Thane (25).
  • 32 Gujarati medium candidates have all been assigned to the Nandurbar exam centre.

Candidates can verify their exact exam centre details through their respective MAHA TET 2026 Admit Card.

Important Instructions for MAHA TET 2026 Exam Day

Following the official exam day guidelines ensures a smooth experience at the examination centre. Candidates are strongly advised to read all instructions mentioned on the admit card carefully.

  • Carry a printed copy of the MAHA TET 2026 Admit Card; digital copies on mobile will NOT be accepted.
  • Carry a valid original photo ID proof along with the admit card.
  • Report to the exam centre at least 30-45 minutes before the scheduled reporting time.
  • Electronic devices such as mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators, and earphones are strictly prohibited inside the hall.
  • No candidate will be permitted to enter the examination hall after the gate-closing time.
  • Candidates must not write or mark anything on the admit card.
