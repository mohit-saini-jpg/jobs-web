Eligibility Criteria - Who Can Apply for CSIR ASO 2026?

The eligibility for CSIR ASO Recruitment 2026 is highly specific and differs from standard government job notifications. Only candidates who appeared in UPSC's Civil Services Examination 2024 but were not finally recommended for any service - and whose details appear on the UPSC Pratibha Setu Portal - are eligible. General graduates not associated with CSE-2024 are not eligible for this recruitment. Candidates must hold a University degree and must be non-recommended candidates of UPSC Civil Services Examination 2024 (CSE-2024) , as per details available on the UPSC Pratibha Setu Portal. There is no branch or subject restriction on the degree - any graduate from a recognised university qualifies on academic grounds, provided the CSE-2024 condition is met. CSIR ASO Selection Process 2026

The CSIR ASO 2026 selection process involves two key stages - shortlisting based on CSE-2024 marks, followed by a Computer Proficiency Test (CPT). The final merit is determined by CSE-2024 total marks (Mains + Personality Test), subject to qualifying the CPT. Candidates who clear the CPT but score lower in CSE-2024 will rank below those with higher CSE-2024 scores, making UPSC marks the decisive factor. Application Screening: Applications are screened and candidates are shortlisted for CPT in the ratio of 1:5 (i.e., 5 times total vacancies per category, subject to a minimum of five). Shortlisting is based on total marks obtained in UPSC CSE-2024 Mains plus Personality Test. Computer Proficiency Test (CPT): Shortlisted candidates appear for a qualifying CPT of 100 marks and 1-hour duration. Qualifying the CPT is mandatory for further consideration. Computer Proficiency Test (CPT) - Pattern & Minimum Qualifying Marks

The Computer Proficiency Test for CSIR ASO 2026 is qualifying in nature and carries 100 marks with a duration of 1 hour. Candidates who fail to secure the prescribed minimum threshold marks will be eliminated from further consideration regardless of their CSE-2024 scores. The CPT will be held tentatively in Delhi, though CSIR reserves the right to conduct it in other cities as well. The CPT will assess proficiency in Word Processing (typing and document exercises), Spreadsheet preparation, and PowerPoint Presentation - skills essential to day-to-day office functioning. For PwBD candidates, Govt. of India / Staff Selection Commission (SSC) rules on CPT shall apply.

CSIR ASO Salary & Benefits 2026

Candidates selected as Assistant Section Officer in CSIR will be placed at Pay Level 7, Cell 1 of the 7th Pay Commission Pay Matrix, with a starting basic pay of ₹44,900 per month scaling up to ₹1,42,400. Beyond the base salary, CSIR employees receive a comprehensive benefits package aligned with Central Government norms. Officers appointed will be liable to serve anywhere in India across any of CSIR's 37 national laboratories, institutes, extension centres, or CSIR Headquarters at Anusandhan Bhawan, New Delhi. CSIR ASO Recruitment 2026 : Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1How many vacancies are there in CSIR Assistant Section Officer Recruitment 2026?
A
CSIR has announced 100 vacancies for the post of Assistant Section Officer under Advertisement No. E-I/RC/2026/2. These include 61 posts for ASO (General), 18 for ASO (Finance & Accounts), and 21 for ASO (Stores & Purchase). Additionally, 7 posts out of the 100 are reserved for Persons with Benchmark Disabilities.
Q2Who is eligible to apply for CSIR ASO Recruitment 2026?
A
Only candidates who are non-recommended candidates of UPSC Civil Services Examination 2024 (CSE-2024) and hold a University degree are eligible. Candidates must appear on the UPSC Pratibha Setu Portal. General graduates who did not appear in CSE-2024 are not eligible for this recruitment.
Q3What is the last date to apply for CSIR ASO 2026?
A
The last date for both online application submission and fee payment is 13 July 2026 at 05:00 PM. Applications must be submitted through https://www.csir.res.in or https://recruitment.csir.res.in.
Q4What is the salary for CSIR Assistant Section Officer?
A
Selected candidates will be appointed at Pay Level 7, Cell 1 of the 7th CPC Pay Matrix, with a basic pay range of ₹44,900 to ₹1,42,400 per month, along with DA, HRA, Transport Allowance, and other Central Government service benefits.
Q5What is the application fee for CSIR ASO 2026?
A
Candidates from UR, OBC (NCL), and EWS categories must pay ₹500 via SBI Collect. Women, SC, ST, PwBD, and Ex-Servicemen candidates are exempted from paying any application fee.
Q6What is the selection process for CSIR ASO Recruitment 2026?
A
The selection involves three stages: (1) shortlisting based on CSE-2024 Mains + Personality Test marks in a 1:5 ratio per category; (2) a qualifying Computer Proficiency Test (CPT) of 100 marks and 1-hour duration; and (3) final merit prepared on the basis of CSE-2024 total marks, subject to clearing CPT minimum threshold marks.
Q7What is the Computer Proficiency Test (CPT) for CSIR ASO?
A
The CPT is a 100-mark, 1-hour qualifying test covering Word Processing, Spreadsheet preparation, and PowerPoint Presentation. Minimum qualifying marks range from 30 (PwBD categories) to 40 (UR category) out of 100. The CPT is tentatively scheduled to be held in Delhi.
Q8What is the age limit for CSIR ASO 2026?
A
The upper age limit is 33 years as on 13 July 2026 (the closing date). Age relaxation is available: 5 years for SC/ST, 3 years for OBC (NCL), 10 years for PwBD (UR), 15 years for PwBD (SC/ST), 13 years for PwBD (OBC), and 5 years for CSIR departmental candidates.
Q9How will cadre be allotted - General, Finance & Accounts, or Stores & Purchase?
A
CSIR will prepare a single merit list. Cadre allocation (General / Finance & Accounts / Stores & Purchase) will be decided based on merit and the candidate's preferred cadre choice submitted in the online application form. Once allotted, the cadre is final and cannot be changed.
Q10What documents are required at the time of CSIR ASO document verification?
A
Candidates must carry original documents including proof of date of birth, educational qualification certificates (10th onwards), CSE-2024 mark sheets (Prelims and Mains + Personality Test), caste/community certificate (if applicable), PwBD certificate (if applicable), employment certificate, and a No Objection Certificate from employer (if currently in government service). Self-attested copies of all uploaded documents must also be brought.
