Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. How many vacancies are there in India Post West Bengal Circle LDCE Postal Assistant & Sorting Assistant Recruitment 2026?

India Post West Bengal Circle has notified 85 tentative vacancies for the posts of Postal Assistant (CO/RO & Post Office) and Sorting Assistant (RMS) for the vacancy year 2026 (01.01.2026 to 31.12.2026).

Q2. Who is eligible to apply for India Post WB Circle LDCE Postal Assistant & Sorting Assistant 2026?

Only eligible Departmental officials of West Bengal Circle (excluding Postal Accounts Office/Wing) who meet the service conditions as on 01.01.2026 can apply for this LDCE; it is not open to outsiders or GDS of other circles.

Q3. What are the important dates for India Post West Bengal Circle LDCE Recruitment 2026?

The last date to submit applications at Divisional Office is 29 May 2026, applications must reach Regional Office by 04 June 2026, Circle Office by 09 June 2026, and the LDCE exam will be held on 21 June 2026 at 10:00 AM.

Q4. What posts are filled through India Post WB Circle LDCE 2026?

This LDCE fills promotional vacancies for Postal Assistant (Circle Office & Regional Offices), Postal Assistant (Post Office) and Sorting Assistant (Railway Mail Service) under West Bengal Circle for the vacancy year 2026.

Q5. What is the exam pattern for India Post West Bengal Circle LDCE Postal Assistant & Sorting Assistant 2026?

The selection is based on a Written Examination (Paper‑I, MCQ, 100 marks, 120 minutes) followed by a Data Entry Skill Test (DEST – Paper‑III, 25 marks, 15 minutes), conducted as per the revised LDCE pattern; the earlier local language Paper‑II has been removed.

Q6. What are the minimum qualifying marks in India Post WB Circle LDCE 2026?

Minimum qualifying marks in Paper‑I are 40% for General/OBC/EWS and 33% for SC/ST/PwBD, while in DEST (Paper‑III) candidates must secure 75% for General/OBC/EWS and 60% for SC/ST/PwBD, where applicable.

Q7. How are Category A and Category B officials defined for India Post WB LDCE 2026?

Category A includes officials in Level‑3 with 3 years’ service, Level‑2 with 5 years’ service (including Level‑1) or Level‑1 with 5 years’ service. Category B includes officials in Level‑1/2/3 with 8 years’ total regular service including regular GDS service (5 years if regular GDS service existed on or before 17.06.2022) and is considered only if sufficient Category A candidates are not available.

Q8. How is merit prepared for India Post WB Circle LDCE Postal Assistant & Sorting Assistant 2026?

The final merit list is prepared purely on marks obtained in Paper‑I, subject to candidates qualifying the DEST; DEST marks are not added to Paper‑I, and cadre/division allocation is done on a merit‑cum‑preference basis as per options filled in Annexure‑III.
