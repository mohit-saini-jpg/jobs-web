What is the JIPMAT Answer Key 2026?

The JIPMAT 2026 answer key is an official document published by NTA that lists the correct answers to all questions asked in the entrance examination. It is released in two phases first the provisional answer key, and then the final answer key. Understanding both phases is essential for every candidate.

  • Provisional Answer Key: Released shortly after the exam. Candidates can download it, compare responses, and raise objections if they believe any answer is incorrect.
  • Final Answer Key: Published after NTA reviews all objections submitted during the challenge window. The final answer key and result are released together on the same day by NTA.

The answer key allows candidates to estimate their probable score well before the official result is declared, helping them plan their next steps for IIM Jammu and IIM Bodh Gaya admissions.

How to Download JIPMAT Answer Key 2026

Candidates can access the JIPMAT 2026 provisional answer key through the official login portal. The steps below walk you through the complete download process.

Visit the official website at exams.nta.nic.in/JIPMAT and click on the link that reads "Download Question Paper and Challenge Answer Key" or "JIPMAT 2026 Answer Key." Enter your Application Number along with your Password or Date of Birth, then click the submit button to load your answer document. Save both the response sheet and the PDF file to your device for future reference.

Step-by-step guide:

  • Go to the official NTA JIPMAT portal: examinationservices.nic.in
  • Click on the "JIPMAT 2026 Answer Key Challenge" link
  • Log in using your Application Number + Password or Application Number + Date of Birth
  • Enter the captcha code and click Sign In
  • Inside your dashboard, click on "View Question Paper" to see your recorded responses
  • Download the answer key PDF and response sheet and save them locally
  • To raise objections, click on "Challenge of Question"

How to Download the JIPMAT 2026 Response Sheet

The response sheet is a personalized document that records every answer a candidate marked during the examination. It is essential for calculating your score and for raising objections.

The response sheet contains the question ID and answer ID for all answer options submitted during the exam. Candidates need both the response sheet and the answer key together to accurately compare their responses and identify any discrepancies worth challenging.

The response sheet is available inside the candidate login dashboard and is typically active for 2-3 days. Candidates are advised to download and save it immediately.

How to Raise Objections Against JIPMAT Answer Key 2026

If a candidate believes that any answer in the provisional answer key is incorrect, NTA provides a formal objection window to challenge it. This process ensures fair and accurate evaluation.

After accessing the JIPMAT login portal, candidates must click on the "raise objection" button. Each question and answer option is associated with a unique ID mentioned in both the question paper PDF and the response sheet. Candidates can check the section name alongside the question ID and the correct option ID. If the candidate is doubtful about the correct option, they can raise an objection and mark the answer they believe is correct, along with supporting remarks.

Objection Fee Details

Candidates must pay a non-refundable processing fee of ₹200 for every individual question they decide to challenge. The fee is not returned even if the subject experts agree with the submitted claim. Payment can be made online via Debit Card, Credit Card, or Net Banking.

Key Rules for Raising Objections

  • Objections must be submitted within the prescribed window (on or before June 12, 2026)
  • No objections are entertained after the last date. NTA facilitates the objection window for 2-3 days.
  • Supporting documents must be uploaded in PDF format where applicable
  • Save the confirmation page after submission for future reference
