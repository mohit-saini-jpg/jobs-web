What Is the CUET UG Final Answer Key 2026?

The final answer key is the definitive set of correct answers released by NTA after reviewing all candidate objections. This answer key is final and no further changes will be made by NTA in the key. It is the basis on which the official CUET UG 2026 scores and result will be calculated. Candidates can use it to cross-check their answers and estimate their marks before the official scorecard is published.

CUET UG 2026 Exam Schedule - When Was the Exam Held?

Before understanding the answer key, it helps to know the exam timeline. NTA conducted the Common University Entrance Test between May 11 and May 31, 2026 and on June 6 and June 7, 2026, in offline mode. Following the examination, NTA released the provisional answer key on June 9 and invited objections till June 11.

7 Questions Dropped from CUET UG 2026 - Full List

One of the most important updates in the final answer key is the dropping of seven questions. As part of the final answer key revision process, NTA dropped seven questions from the examination. These questions will not be counted in the score calculation for any candidate. The complete list of dropped questions is as follows:

  • Q. 226895775439 - General Aptitude Test (501)
  • Q. 226895775891 - Physics (322)
  • Q. 226895782651 - Hindi (102)
  • Q. 226895792545 - Physics (322)
  • Q. 226895792569 - Physics (322)
  • Q. 226895793708 - Sociology (326)
  • Q. 226895795791 - Core Mathematics & Applied Mathematics (319)

Candidates who attempted these questions will neither gain nor lose marks for them, as they have been removed from the scoring process entirely.

CUET UG 2026 Marking Scheme - How to Calculate Your Score

Understanding the marking scheme is essential for candidates who wish to estimate their raw scores. To calculate CUET 2026 marks obtained, candidates need to follow the below marking scheme: one mark for every correct answer. Negative marking applies for incorrect responses. Use the formula below:

Score Calculation Formula:

  • Correct Answer: +1 mark
  • Wrong Answer: -0.25 mark (negative marking)
  • Unattempted Question: 0 marks
  • Dropped Questions: No marks added or deducted

Keep in mind that questions dropped by NTA do not affect the total maximum marks for those specific subjects.

What Happened to Candidate Objections?

The objection review process is a critical step between the provisional and final answer keys. The final answer key has been prepared after reviewing the objections and challenges submitted by candidates against the provisional answer key. The objection window remained open until June 11, following which subject experts examined the representations received from candidates.

All valid objections were reviewed by subject experts, and corrections (including dropped questions) were applied uniformly across all candidates. No individual notifications were sent the revised final key itself reflects all accepted challenges.

CUET UG 2026 Result Date - When Will Results Be Declared?

With the final answer key now out, all eyes are on the result. The National Testing Agency will declare the CUET UG 2026 result by June 26. Some sources indicate NTA is expected to announce the CUET 2026 result by June 24 at cuet.nta.nic.in.

Results will be published at the official portal and candidates will need their login credentials to access their scorecards.

How to Check CUET UG 2026 Result - Steps to Download Scorecard

Once the result is declared, candidates can follow these steps to download their official scorecard:

  • Go to the official website: cuet.nta.nic.in
  • Click on the "CUET UG 2026 Result" tab on the homepage
  • Enter your Application Number, Password, and Captcha Code
  • Click the Submit button
  • The CUET UG 2026 scorecard will appear on your screen
  • Cross-check all the details on the scorecard
  • Save and download the PDF version of the scorecard

The CUET UG 2026 Scorecard will serve as an admission document at participating universities for the 2026-27 academic session.

What Comes After the CUET UG 2026 Result?

The release of the final answer key is the last major step before result declaration. Once the scorecards are released, the participating universities will begin announcing admission schedules, counselling processes, and cut-off requirements for various programmes.

Key post-result steps for candidates:

  • Check CUET UG Scorecard at cuet.nta.nic.in
  • Monitor University Cut-offs on individual university websites
  • Register for Counselling as per university-specific schedules
  • Complete Document Verification within the deadline
  • Pay Admission Fee to confirm your seat

Candidates who fail to complete the document verification and fee payment before the deadline will not be eligible for admissions.
