Age Limit

DetailsAge limits for posts in DSSSB Advertisement 03/2026 vary by post. Most Junior Scientific Assistant posts (FSL) have an age limit of 18 to 27 years. Teaching posts in the Directorate of Education have an upper age limit of 30 years. Technical posts in NDMC vary between 18–32 years and 18–37 years depending on the post. The eligibility for age will be determined as on the closing date of application, 15 July 2026. Age relaxation is available for SC/ST (5 years), OBC-Delhi (3 years), PwBD (10 years), Ex-Servicemen (military service period plus 3 years), and departmental candidates (up to 40 years for Group C posts in the same line). Contractual employees of the same department may also claim one-time age relaxation up to 5 years, subject to having worked at least 180 working days (or 120 days for teaching posts) in that particular year.

How to Apply

  • 1Candidates must apply online only through the official DSSSB portal at dsssbonline.nic.in. Before applying, candidates must complete a one-time registration on the portal. The same user ID and password can be used for all future DSSSB examinations - no separate registration is required for each post. However, only one online application is allowed per post code; if multiple applications are detected for the same post code, all will be rejected and the candidate may be debarred. Once an application is submitted, no corrections or modifications - including change of category - will be permitted under any circumstances.
  • 2Candidates are advised to apply well before the closing date of 15 July 2026 to avoid last-minute technical issues. The application fee of ₹100 must be paid through SBI e-Pay; no other payment mode is accepted. After successful submission, candidates should download and save a printout of their application for future reference. All supporting documents (educational certificates, caste certificates, experience certificates, etc.) will be required at the time of e-dossier upload, which will be called separately after the written examination. Candidates who fail to upload their e-dossier within the given time will have their candidature cancelled with no further opportunity.
  • 3Visit the DSSSB portal: https://dsssbonline.nic.in
  • 4Complete one-time registration (if not already registered).
  • 5Login and select the relevant post code from Advertisement No. 03/2026.
  • 6Fill in all details carefully - no changes can be made after submission.
  • 7Pay the application fee of ₹100 via SBI e-Pay (exempted for Women/SC/ST/PwBD/ESM).
  • 8Upload a recent colour passport photo (plain white/off-white background, clear face, eyes open) and signature.
  • 9Submit and download the application form printout.
  • 10Exam date will be announced separately on the DSSSB website.

Important Instructions

  • Reservation benefits are available for SC, ST, OBC (Delhi), EWS, PwBD, ESM, and Meritorious Sports Persons as per Government of NCT of Delhi and Central Government instructions. An important point for OBC candidates: only OBC (Delhi) candidates notified by the Government of NCT of Delhi are eligible for reservation. OBC candidates from outside Delhi will be treated as Unreserved (UR) candidates and must apply under the UR category.
  • OBC (Delhi) candidates must possess a valid OBC (Non-Creamy Layer) certificate issued on or after 01/04/2026 but not later than 15/07/2026 (the closing date). For EWS candidates, an Income & Asset certificate valid for financial year 2026–27, based on income of financial year 2025–26, is required. The reservation roster, earmarking of vacancies, and PwBD post suitability are determined entirely by the respective User Departments, not by DSSSB.
  • Only one registration is allowed per candidate on the DSSSB portal. Multiple registrations will lead to cancellation of all applications. Only one application per post code is allowed. Multiple applications for the same post code will result in cancellation and debarment. The eligibility cut-off date is 15 July 2026 - age, qualification, experience, and all other eligibility conditions will be determined as on this date. DSSSB does not verify eligibility at the application stage; candidature is accepted on a provisional basis. Verification happens at the e-dossier stage after the written examination. No calculator, mobile phone, Bluetooth device, earbud, camera, smartwatch, or any other electronic gadget is allowed in the examination hall. Dress code: light clothes with half sleeves, no big buttons, brooch, or badge; salwar/trouser; slippers or sandals with low heels. Shoes are not allowed. Exam centres will be in Delhi/NCR and other states as decided by DSSSB. DSSSB reserves the right to cancel, modify, or amend any part of the advertisement or examination at any stage. In case of any discrepancy between the English, Hindi, Urdu, and Punjabi versions of the advertisement, the English version will be treated as final. Candidates must regularly check the DSSSB website at https://dsssb.delhi.gov.in for updates and notifications/corrigenda.
  • In summary, DSSSB Advertisement No. 03/2026 offers 1,979 government job vacancies across 25 post codes in Delhi. The largest vacancy categories are TGT Computer Science (675 posts) in the Directorate of Education, Special Educator Primary (450 posts), JSA Cyber Forensic (81 posts) in the Forensic Science Laboratory, and IT Assistant Grade-A (125 posts) in the Information Technology Department. Candidates from a wide range of educational backgrounds - Class 12 pass to Master's degree holders - can find suitable posts in this advertisement. The application window is open from 16 June 2026 to 15 July 2026 and all candidates must apply online at dsssbonline.nic.in. The written examination, which will be a Computer Based Test, will be scheduled and notified separately on the DSSSB website at dsssb.delhi.gov.in.

FAQs

Q1. What is DSSSB Advertisement No. 03/2026 and how many vacancies are there?
DSSSB Advertisement No. 03/2026 (dated 29/05/2026) invites online applications for 1,979 vacancies across 25 post codes in various departments of the Government of NCT of Delhi.
Q2. When does the online application window open and close?
Online applications open on 16 June 2026 (12:00 noon) and close on 15 July 2026 (11:59 PM). The eligibility cut‑off date is 15 July 2026.
Q3. Where do I apply and download the official notification PDF?
Apply online at https://dsssbonline.nic.in. Notification PDF and details are on https://dsssb.delhi.gov.in (official notification: https://dsssb.delhi.gov.in/sites/default/files/DSSSB/circulars-orders/final_advt-03-2026_1.pdf).
Q4. Which departments and major posts are covered?
Departments include Forensic Science Laboratory, Directorate of Education, NDMC, Information Technology Department, Department of Delhi Archives, and Labour Department. Major posts: TGT (Computer Science) — 675, Special Educator (Primary) — 450, IT Assistant Grade-A — 125, JSA (Cyber Forensic) — 81, plus many technical and teaching roles.
Q5. What is the application fee and who is exempt?
Fee: ₹100 (paid via SBI e-Pay). Exemptions: Women, SC, ST, PwBD, and Ex-Servicemen. Fee is non‑refundable.
Q6. What are the educational and skill requirements?
Qualifications vary by post: from Matric/ITI/NCVT (technical posts) to Graduation/Diploma and Master’s degrees (scientific/teaching posts). Several posts require typing/key‑depression speed (e.g., 8,000 key depressions/hour) or B.Ed/CTET/RCI credentials for teaching posts. Check post‑wise requirements in the notification.
Q7. What is the age limit and are there relaxations?
Age limits vary by post (examples: JSA posts 18–27 yrs; most teaching posts up to 30 yrs; some technical posts up to 32–37 yrs). Age relaxations apply as per Central Government rules (SC/ST +5, OBC-Delhi +3, PwBD +10, Ex‑Servicemen service period +3, and others as specified).
Q8. What is the exam pattern and marking scheme?
Single-stage Computer Based Test (CBT) of 200 marks, 2 hours. Exam types: One Tier General, One Tier Technical, or One Tier Technical/Teaching depending on the post. Negative marking: 0.25 marks per wrong answer. Minimum qualifying marks: General/EWS 40%, OBC‑Delhi 35%, SC/ST/PwBD 30%.
Q9. How will reservation and OBC-Delhi rules work?
Reservation for UR/OBC (Delhi)/SC/ST/EWS/PwBD/ESM/MSP as per GNCTD rules. Only OBC candidates with valid OBC (Non‑Creamy Layer) certificates issued for Delhi (on/after 01/04/2026 and not later than 15/07/2026) are eligible for OBC‑Delhi reservation. EWS / income certificates must follow notification timelines.
Q10. What are the key application instructions and documents to keep ready?
Register once on dsssbonline.nic.in, then apply for relevant post code(s). Only one application per post code; multiple apps for same code may lead to rejection. Pay fee via SBI e‑Pay if applicable. Keep scanned recent passport photo, signature, educational certificates, caste/PwBD/EWS/ESM certificates, and experience documents ready for later e‑dossier upload. No corrections allowed after submission; save the application printout.
