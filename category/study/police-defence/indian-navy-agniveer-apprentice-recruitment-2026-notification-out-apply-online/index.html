Important Instructions

  • Only unmarried Indian male candidates are eligible. Agniveers cannot marry during their entire 4-year tenure; violation will result in dismissal from service. Agniveers are enrolled under the Navy Act 1957 for a period of 4 years. Indian Navy is not obliged to retain Agniveers beyond the engagement period. Up to 25% of each specific batch of Agniveers may be offered permanent enrolment in the Indian Navy as Sailors (Regular Cadre) / Artificers based on performance and organisational requirements. Selection for further enrolment is entirely at the discretion of the Indian Navy; Agniveers have no right to be selected. Agniveers will not be permitted to seek release at their own request prior to completion of the engagement period. Agniveers are not governed by Naval Pension Regulations/Rules and are not entitled to gratuity for the engagement period. Agniveers will not be eligible for Ex-Servicemen status. Candidates declaring incorrect domicile/gender and other details shall not be permitted to appear in the recruitment and their candidature will be cancelled. Candidates reporting 30 minutes after the reporting time for Stage I - INET shall not be allowed to appear in the examination. A candidate's selection pertaining to a particular batch is valid for that batch only. Qualified candidates whose names do not appear in the merit list cannot claim admission for the next batch. Mobile phones or any other communication devices are not allowed inside the examination premises. Infringement will entail disciplinary action including ban from future examinations. Candidates should not submit multiple applications. If more than one application is received, candidature will be cancelled. Minimum height standard is 157 cms. Permanent body tattoos are only permitted on the inner face of forearms (inside of elbow to wrist) and on the reversed side of palm/back (dorsal) side of hand. Tattoos on any other part of the body are not acceptable. Candidates are advised to get their ears cleaned for wax and tartar removed from teeth prior to medical examination. Visual Standards: Uncorrected Vision 6/12, 6/12 | Corrected Vision 6/6, 6/6 | Colour Perception: CP PASS. No enquiry will be entertained regarding this recruitment/enrolment after a period of six months post completion of the recruitment cycle for the respective batch. The terms and conditions given in the advertisement are guidelines only and are subject to change without notice. Further, orders issued by the Government as amended from time to time will apply for selected candidates.

FAQs

Q1. What is the last date to apply for Indian Navy Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
The last date to submit the online application for Agniveer (Apprentice) 01/2027 and 02/2027 Batch is 29 June 2026 (17:00 hrs). The correction window closes on 06 July 2026. Candidates are advised to apply well before the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues.
Q2. Can female candidates apply for Indian Navy Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
No. Only unmarried male candidates are eligible to apply for Agniveer (Apprentice) 01/2027 and 02/2027 Batch. Female candidates are not eligible for this recruitment.
Q3. What is the age limit for Agniveer (Apprentice) 01/2027 and 02/2027 Batch?
For the 01/2027 Batch, candidates must be born between 01 December 2004 and 31 May 2009 (both dates inclusive). For the 02/2027 Batch, candidates must be born between 01 May 2005 and 31 October 2009 (both dates inclusive). Candidates born between 01 Dec 2004 – 31 Oct 2009 are eligible to appear in INET 2/2026.
Q4. What is the educational qualification required for Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
Candidates must have passed Matriculation (Class 10th) with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks from a recognised Board, and must have completed a 3-year Diploma course from an AICTE-recognised Polytechnic Institute with a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate in any of the eligible Engineering or Electrical diploma trades.
Q5. What is the salary and Seva Nidhi package for Agniveer (Apprentice)?
Agniveers receive a monthly in-hand amount of ₹21,000 in Year 1, increasing to ₹28,000 in Year 4. Additionally, 30% of the monthly package is contributed to the Agniveer Corpus Fund, with a matching contribution by the Government of India. On completion of the 4-year engagement, Agniveers receive a one-time Seva Nidhi of ₹10.04 Lakhs. There is no gratuity or pension entitlement.
Q6. What is the selection process for Indian Navy Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
The selection process consists of: Stage I – INET (Indian Navy Entrance Test, computer-based, 100 questions, 1 hour); followed by Stage II comprising Physical Fitness Test (PFT), Written Examination, and Recruitment Medical Examination; and finally a Pre-Enrolment Medical Examination at INS Chilka before induction. All stages are mandatory and qualifying.
Q7. Is there negative marking in the Agniveer INET examination?
Yes. There is a penalty of 0.25 marks (one-fourth) deducted for every wrong answer in the INET examination. Candidates must pass in all sections as well as in aggregate. The Indian Navy reserves the right to determine the pass marks in each section and in aggregate.
Q8. What is the application fee for Indian Navy Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
The examination fee for Stage I – INET is ₹550/- plus 18% GST, payable online via net banking, credit card, debit card, or UPI. There is no separate fee for the Stage II process. Fee once paid is non-refundable under any circumstances. Sponsor candidates and NCC 'C' Certificate holders are exempted from Stage I – INET and are directly issued call-up letters for Stage II.
Q9. Can an Agniveer be permanently enrolled in the Indian Navy after the 4-year tenure?
On completion of the 4-year engagement, up to 25% of each specific batch of Agniveers may be offered an opportunity to apply for permanent enrolment in the Indian Navy as Artificers (Sailors, Regular Cadre) on completion of induction training. Selection is based on objective criteria including performance during the 4-year engagement period and is entirely at the discretion of the Indian Navy. Agniveers have no right to claim permanent enrolment.
Q10. What are the physical and medical standards for Agniveer (Apprentice) 2026?
The minimum height standard is 157 cms. For the Physical Fitness Test, male candidates must complete a 1.6 km run within 6 minutes 30 seconds, 20 squats, 15 push-ups, and 15 bent knee sit-ups. Visual standards require uncorrected vision of 6/12, 6/12 and corrected vision of 6/6, 6/6 with Colour Perception CP PASS. Permanent body tattoos are only permitted on the inner face of forearms and back of the hand; tattoos on any other part of the body will lead to disqualification. Candidates with gender reassignment surgery will be declared UNFIT.
