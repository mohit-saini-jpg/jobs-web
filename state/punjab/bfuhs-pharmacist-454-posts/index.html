How to Apply

  1. 1Visit the official website: www.bfuhs.ac.in
  2. 2Navigate to the Recruitment / Vacancies section
  3. 3Click on the link for Advt. No. BFU-26/20 – Pharmacist (Pharmacy Officer)
  4. 4Register and fill in the online application form carefully
  5. 5Select your category carefully - category once selected cannot be changed under any circumstances
  6. 6Mention your sub-category vis-à-vis Caste in the online form
  7. 7Upload all required documents including educational certificates, Punjab Pharmacy Council registration, and category certificates
  8. 8Pay the application fee online (non-refundable)
  9. 9Submit the application and download the confirmation receipt
  10. 10Category is final: The category once selected will not be changed under any circumstances. Wrong category selection will result in cancellation of candidature.
  11. 11Sportspersons claiming reservation must possess a Sports Gradation Certificate issued by the Director of Sports, Punjab, as per the latest instructions, available as on the last date of application.
  12. 12SC/BC certificate must be in accordance with Welfare Department Punjab instructions; ESM, Freedom Fighter, EWS, and Sports certificates must comply with the concerned department's instructions.
  13. 13Fictitious/bogus certificates: If any certificate is found fake at any stage, candidature will be cancelled and legal action will follow.
  14. 14Police Verification is mandatory before appointment - passing the written exam alone does not guarantee appointment.
  15. 15Government employees applying must obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their Head of Department and must not be under any disciplinary proceedings.
  16. 16The number of posts can be increased or decreased at any time as per departmental requirements.
  17. 17Reserved category posts are provisional and subject to change as per Punjab Government Policy Guidelines.
  18. 18No extra weightage for higher qualification.
  19. 19In case of equal marks, preference is given to the elder candidate.
  20. 20Nationality: Candidates must be Indian citizens or fall under prescribed categories (Nepal/Bhutan citizens, Tibetan refugees who came before 1.1.1962, or persons of Indian origin from specified countries with Punjab Government eligibility certificate).
  21. 21Candidates who are not registered with Punjab Pharmacy Council under the Pharmacy Act, 1948, as on 1 July 2026
  22. 22Candidates who do not hold a Diploma in Pharmacy, B.Pharm, or Pharm.D from a recognised institution
  23. 23Candidates who have not passed Matriculation with Punjabi as a subject and cannot qualify the Punjabi language test at 50%
  24. 24Candidates above 37 years of age as on 1 January 2026 (unless eligible for age relaxation under a specific category)
  25. 25Candidates previously disqualified by any recruiting board/government body where the disqualification period is still active
  26. 26Government employees with pending disciplinary proceedings who cannot obtain an NOC from their Head of Department
  27. 27Wrong category selection: This is irreversible - double-check your category (General/SC/BC/EWS/ESM etc.) before final submission
  28. 28Not mentioning sub-category: Candidates must specify their sub-category vis-à-vis Caste in the online form as instructed
  29. 29Applying without Punjab Pharmacy Council registration: Registration must be active and obtained before 1 July 2026
  30. 30Ignoring the Punjabi Language test: Many candidates underestimate this mandatory qualifier - failing at 50% disqualifies you regardless of your written exam performance
  31. 31Sports candidates without Gradation Certificate: The Sports Gradation Certificate must already be in hand as on the last date of application; no extra time is given
  32. 32Not claiming COVID experience correctly: Experience benefit is only for candidates who worked for the same nature of post under Punjab Health/Medical Education departments during March 2020–June 2021 - mis-claiming it can lead to disqualification

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for BFUHS Pharmacist Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for BFUHS Pharmacist (Pharmacy Officer) Recruitment 2026 is 1 July 2026. Applications must be submitted at bfuhs.ac.in.
Q2 How many posts are available in BFUHS Pharmacist Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 454 posts are available - comprising 348 fresh vacancies and 106 backlog posts — across multiple reservation categories under the Punjab Health and Family Welfare Department.
Q3 What is the age limit for BFUHS Pharmacist 2026?
A
The age limit is 18 to 37 years as on 1 January 2026. SC/BC candidates get 5 years relaxation, Physically Handicapped get 10 years, and government employees can apply up to 45 years.
Q4 What qualifications are required for BFUHS Pharmacist post?
A
Candidates must have passed Class 12 with Science, hold a Diploma in Pharmacy or B.Pharm or Pharm.D from a recognised institution, and be registered with the Punjab Pharmacy Council under the Pharmacy Act, 1948.
Q5 What is the application fee for BFUHS Pharmacist Recruitment 2026?
A
The fee is ₹2,360/- for all categories except SC (₹2,000 + 18% GST) and ₹1,180/- for SC category candidates (₹1,000 + 18% GST). The fee is non-refundable.
Q6 What is the salary for Pharmacist (Pharmacy Officer) in Punjab?
A
The initial pay is ₹29,200/- at Pay Level 5 under the 7th Central Pay Commission. Additional allowances are governed by Punjab Government and Department of Health service rules.
Q7 What is the selection process for BFUHS Pharmacist 2026?
A
Selection is based on a written exam (90 marks) + experience marks (10 marks) = 100 marks total, plus a mandatory Punjabi Language qualifying test (minimum 50% required). Final selection is on inter-se merit subject to Punjab Government reservation rules.
Q8 Is there a Punjabi language requirement for this post?
A
Yes. Candidates must pass a Punjabi Language qualifying test at Matriculation level with at least 50% marks. Failing this test disqualifies the candidate from the final merit list regardless of their performance in other papers.
Q9 Who conducts the written exam for BFUHS Pharmacist Recruitment?
A
The written examination is conducted by Baba Farid University of Health Sciences (BFUHS), Faridkot. Candidates scoring below 33% in the written test will be declared ineligible.
Q10 What is the official website to apply for BFUHS Pharmacist 2026?
A
Candidates must apply online at www.bfuhs.ac.in.
