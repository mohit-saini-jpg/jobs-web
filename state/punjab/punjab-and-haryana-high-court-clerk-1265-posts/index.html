Selection Process

1Online Written Examination / Computer Based Test (CBT) + Computer Proficiency Test (CPT) + Checking of Original Testimonials / Interaction
2Candidates who do not hold a Bachelor's degree (BA/BSc or equivalent) from a recognized university - it is the mandatory minimum educational qualification.
3Candidates who have not passed Matriculation with Hindi or Sanskrit as one of the subjects, AND have not done +2/BA/MA with Hindi as one of the subjects - both the degree AND the Hindi/Sanskrit condition must be fulfilled by 09-07-2026.
4Candidates who are not bonafide residents of Haryana - reservation benefits (category, age relaxation, fee concession) are available only to Haryana residents with valid Haryana Government-issued certificates. Non-Haryana reserved category candidates must apply under General category.
5Candidates who do not have proficiency in computers (Word Processing and Spread Sheets) - this is a mandatory qualification and is tested in the CPT.
6Candidates above 42 years of age as on 01.01.2026 (for General category), except those eligible for specific age relaxation as per the rules - overall age cap is 52 years for most relaxation categories.
7Candidates who have already married a person having a living spouse, or who (having a living spouse) have married another person - unless the Hon'ble High Court has specifically exempted such marriage under applicable personal law.
8In-service Government employees who cannot produce a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their Head of Office or Department when called - failure to produce NOC will result in cancellation of candidature.
9Candidates who cannot or will not commit to working anywhere in the State of Haryana - a mandatory undertaking to this effect must be given in the Online Application Form.
10Candidates who entered incorrect information in the application - all particulars are sacrosanct after submission and no change is permitted at any stage; errors cannot be corrected or explained later.
11Candidates who are unable to type the English Composition Subjective portion on computer and cannot qualify the CPT Typing Test at 30 WPM (unless eligible for CPT exemption under PwBD provisions).
12Not verifying Haryana domicile status before applying under a reserved category - benefits of reservation and fee concession are only for bonafide Haryana residents; applicants from other states' reserved categories must apply under General category.
13Not checking that both degree qualification AND Hindi/Sanskrit condition are fulfilled before applying - missing either condition will result in disqualification.
14Filling incorrect category or sub-category in the Online Application Form - all particulars are sacrosanct after submission and no change shall be permitted at any stage of the recruitment process.
15OBC/BC candidates from Creamy Layer applying under BC reservation - only Non-Creamy Layer candidates (as per Haryana Govt. Notification No. 40/13/2024-1SW dated 16.07.2024) are eligible for BC reservation; Creamy Layer candidates must apply as General.
16EWS candidates not having the correct certificate - EWS certificate must be valid for FY 2026-2027, based on gross annual income for FY 2025-2026, issued on or before 23-06-2026. EWS certificates issued for Central Government jobs will NOT be accepted.
17ESM and family member candidates not having a valid eligibility certificate from the concerned Zila Sainik Board - this must be valid as on the last date of application (09-07-2026).
18Uploading a photograph that is more than one month old or does not match the real-time photo captured during application or the photo to be pasted on the E-Admit Card - mismatch will result in denial of entry at the examination centre.

20Expecting the E-Admit Card to be sent by post - E-Admit Cards are uploaded on www.sssc.gov.in only; candidates must download and print them by logging in with Registration ID and Password.
21Not checking the SSSC website and registered email (including spam folder) regularly - all notifications, exam dates, admit card updates and results are communicated only through www.sssc.gov.in and email/SMS; SSSC is not responsible for any candidate failing to appear due to non-checking of the website.
22Waiting until the last date to apply - SSSC advises applying well before the closing date to avoid internet/website congestion issues; the cut-off date is sacrosanct and no relief will be granted after the deadline under any circumstance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the total number of vacancies in Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk Recruitment 2026?
A
The High Court of Punjab and Haryana has announced a total of 1265 Clerk vacancies under the Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk Recruitment 2026. This includes 1015 existing posts and 250 anticipated vacancies up to June 2026. The number of vacancies may change depending on administrative requirements at the time of final selection
Q2 What is the eligibility criteria for Haryana SSSC Clerk 2026?
A
Candidates must have a Bachelor’s degree (BA, BSc, or equivalent) from a recognized university along with basic computer proficiency in Word Processing and Spreadsheets. Additionally, applicants must have studied Hindi or Sanskrit at Matric level or higher. All qualifications must be completed before 09 July 2026.
Q3 What is the age limit for Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk Recruitment 2026?
A
The minimum age is 18 years and the maximum age is 42 years for General category candidates as on 01 January 2026. Reserved categories like SC, BC, PwBD, and others are eligible for age relaxation as per Haryana government rules. However, the upper age limit cannot exceed 52 years after all relaxations.
Q4 What is the last date to apply for Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk Vacancy 2026?
A
The online application process will start on 26 May 2026 at 4:00 PM and will remain open until 09 July 2026 at 4:00 PM. Candidates are advised to complete their application well before the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues. Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances.
Q5 What is the selection process for Haryana High Court Clerk Recruitment 2026?
A
The selection process consists of three stages: Computer Based Test (CBT), Computer Proficiency Test (CPT), and document verification. The final merit list is prepared based on marks obtained in the CBT only. The CPT is qualifying in nature and must be cleared to proceed further.
Q6 What is the exam pattern of Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk CBT 2026?
A
The CBT includes General Knowledge and English Composition sections with both objective and subjective questions. The exam carries 100 marks with a duration of 2 hours, and there is negative marking of 1/4 for incorrect answers. Candidates must score at least 33% in each section and 40% overall to qualify.
Q7 Is there any application fee for Haryana Clerk Recruitment 2026?
A
Yes, the application fee varies based on category and gender. General category males need to pay Rs. 1200, while females pay Rs. 875. Haryana reserved categories pay Rs. 710, and PwBD candidates are exempted from paying any fee.
Q8 Who can apply for Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk posts under reserved categories?
A
Only bonafide residents of Haryana are eligible to claim reservation benefits under SC, BC, EWS, ESM, and other categories. Candidates from other states must apply under the General category even if they belong to a reserved category. Valid and updated certificates are mandatory at the time of application.
Q9 What is the salary of Haryana High Court Clerk 2026?
A
The pay scale for Clerk posts will be as per the latest rules and instructions issued by the Government of Haryana. Along with basic pay, selected candidates will also receive allowances and benefits applicable to state government employees. The job is permanent and offers stable career growth.
Q10 How to apply online for Punjab and Haryana High Court Clerk Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates must apply online through the official website sssc.gov.in by completing registration, filling the application form, uploading documents, and paying the fee. It is important to keep scanned documents, a valid email ID, and a working mobile number ready before applying. The application is considered complete only after successful fee payment.
