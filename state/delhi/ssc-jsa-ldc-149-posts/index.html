Selection Process

1Paper-II (CBT) qualifying: Candidates must clear the minimum marks fixed for Paper-II to have their Paper-I evaluated.
2Paper-I (Descriptive) evaluation: Only those qualifying in Paper-II have their descriptive paper assessed.
3APAR evaluation (Part-II): Based on the aggregate of Paper-I + Paper-II, shortlisted candidates move to service-record evaluation.
4Final merit: Prepared on the combined total of Paper-I + Paper-II + Part-II (APAR) = 260 marks, cadre-wise, subject to reservation. Ties are resolved by Paper-I marks, then Paper-II part (a), then Paper-II total, then date of birth (older ranked higher), then alphabetical order.
5Paper-I (Short Essay): One essay of 400–500 words on any of the specified subjects.
6Paper-II (a) Language: Simple composition and applied grammar (in the chosen English/Hindi medium).
7Paper-II (b) General Knowledge: Current events, matters of everyday observation and experience, and general information about India.
8Application not forwarded by the Head of Department/Office (the single biggest cause of rejection).
9Multiple applications submitted for the same exam.
10Photograph with a cap/spectacles, dark background, blurred, or a pre-existing (scanned) photo instead of a live capture.
11Signature that is blurred, miniature, or below the size norm.
12Wrong OTR details - name, father's/mother's name, or date of birth not matching the matriculation certificate (OTR data is non-editable).
13Hard copy reaching SSC-NR after the deadline, or incomplete documents.
14Apply during off-peak hours - late at night or early morning - rather than on the last day.

How to Apply

  1. 1Generate OTR on the new website. Visit ssc.gov.in and click "Register Now." Any OTR created on the old site (ssc.nic.in) will not work on the new portal.
  2. 2Opt for Aadhaar-Based Authentication during OTR (recommended). Candidates who skip Aadhaar must upload the prescribed proof documents.
  3. 3Log in with your Registration Number and password, then open the JSA/LDC LDCE 2025 link under the "Live Exam" tab and click Apply.
  4. 4Fill the application - service details, cadre, crucial date, length of service, and age-relaxation code (if any). Information auto-filled from OTR is non-editable; use "Modify Registration" if a correction is needed.
  5. 5Capture your live photograph and upload your signature as per the specifications (see below).
  6. 6Upload the required documents (listed below) and Submit. Take a printout of the submitted application.
  7. 7Forward the hard copy through proper channel. Your Head of Department / Head of Office must forward the printed application along with service documents to: The Regional Director, Staff Selection Commission (Northern Region), Block No. 12, CGO Complex, Lodhi Road, New Delhi – 110003, so as to reach by 03-07-2026 (6:00 PM) - or 10-07-2026 for candidates abroad/A&N/Lakshadweep.
  8. 8Certified true copy of the first page of your Service Book (attested by HoD/HoO)
  9. 9Certified particulars of service (posts held, scale of pay, nature of appointment)
  10. 10Class 12th certificate / educational qualification proof (if applicable)
  11. 11Caste/Category certificate (SC/ST), if claiming reservation
  12. 12Disability certificate (Annexure-VII / VIII) for PwBD candidates
  13. 13A recent passport-size photo for the live capture reference, and a scanned signature
  14. 14Photograph: Captured live via webcam/phone, in a well-lit area with a plain background. No cap, mask, or spectacles. Face must fill the on-screen red rectangle. Pre-existing/scanned photos are rejected.
  15. 15Signature: JPEG/JPG, 10–20 KB, dimensions about 6.0 cm × 2.0 cm. Sign so the signature fills at least 80% of the box. Blurred or miniature signatures are rejected.
  16. 16Negative marking of 0.25 marks per wrong answer applies to Paper-II (CBT) only. There is no negative marking in the descriptive Paper-I.
  17. 17Candidates must attempt the exam in the language (English/Hindi) chosen in the application form.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Can a general candidate or private-sector employee apply for this exam?
A
No. This is a Limited Departmental Competitive Examination, open only to existing regular Group 'C' / MTS employees of the 12 participating Central Government cadres, with at least 3 years of service. It is not open to the general public or freshers.
Q2 How many vacancies are there in SSC JSA / LDC LDCE 2025?
A
A total of 149 vacancies across 12 departments - the largest shares being Central Passport Organisation (50), Armed Forces HQ Clerical Service (31), O/o Registrar General of India (17), and O/o Development Commissioner-MSME (13).
Q3 What are the application dates?
A
The online application is open from 05th June 2026 to 26th June 2026 (up to 11:00 PM). The forwarded hard copy must reach SSC-NR by 03rd July 2026.
Q4 Is there an application fee?
A
No application fee is prescribed for this departmental examination. (A ₹50-per-question charge applies only to optional answer-key challenges.)
Q5 Where will the exam be held and when?
A
The Computer Based Examination will be conducted in Delhi-NCR only, tentatively in July–August 2026.
Q6 What is the exam pattern?
A
Part-I has Paper-I (descriptive Short Essay, 100 marks) and Paper-II (objective CBT - Language 50 + General Knowledge 50 = 100 marks, with 0.25 negative marking). Part-II is APAR evaluation worth 60 marks. Final merit is out of 260 marks.
Q7 Do I need to send a physical copy of my application?
A
Yes. Apart from the online form, your Head of Department/Office must forward the printed application with service documents to SSC (Northern Region). Without proper-channel forwarding, the application is rejected.
