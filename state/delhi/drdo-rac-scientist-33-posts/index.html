Selection Process

1Administrative Screening → Technical Screening (Level-1 & Level-2 if required) → Final Personal Interview (in-person at RAC, Delhi)
2Candidates who do not have First Division (60%+) in the essential qualifying degree - this is a hard minimum requirement and no rounding off is permitted.
3Candidates with experience below Pay Level 10 (7th CPC) or equivalent - such experience will not be counted for computing the required years of experience.
4Candidates whose period of study (higher qualification like PhD) overlaps with the work experience claimed - overlapping periods will be removed from total experience.
5Candidates applying for Scientist D or E posts who are currently unemployed for more than one year - under current employment, the period of un-employment shall not exceed one year, else the candidature will be rejected.
6Candidates serving in Government/PSU who do not upload a signed CCA Declaration - without CCA Declaration their candidature will be rejected, and NOC will be mandatory at Final Personal Interview.
7Candidates from Private Sector who cannot produce CTC/Pay drawn proof/Form 16A for the claimed experience period - this is the sole criteria for establishing experience suitability from private sector.
8Candidates exceeding the maximum age of 35 years (Scientist C), 45 years (Scientist D/E), or 56 years inclusive of all relaxations.
9Candidates who cannot attend the Final Personal Interview in-person at RAC, Delhi - no online/virtual mode is available for Final Personal Interview under any circumstances.
10Candidates who do not meet the specific work experience fields mentioned in the advertised qualifications - the Technical Screening Committee assesses relevance of experience very strictly.
11Candidates applying for Scientist C (Encadered) posts from Reserved categories seeking age relaxation - there is no category-based age relaxation for Scientist C Encadered posts.
12Not checking whether your experience is at or above Pay Level 10 (7th CPC) equivalent - experience below this level is invalid and will not be counted.
13Miscounting experience years by including the period of higher qualification (PhD/M.Tech) that overlaps with work experience - the overlapping period will be deducted.
14Applying for multiple posts by submitting one application - candidates applying for more than one post must apply separately for each post and mention item numbers of all other posts where they have applied, along with the order of preference.
15Not highlighting desirable qualifications and experience in the Justification column under 'Other Section' in the online application form - this is crucial for Technical Screening Committee's assessment.
16Providing incorrect or non-verifiable experience details - the length of experience (date of joining along with Pay Scale/CTC and end date of each employment) should be available in attached experience documents.
17Not getting experience certificates issued by Admin/HR Head/Director/Principal/Dean/Registrar/Competent Authority - certificates issued by Group Head/Division Head/Project Head/Professor or Head of Department are not acceptable.
18Not converting CGPA to percentage before applying - CGPA/CPI of 6.75 on a 10-point scale equals 60%; candidates must provide conversion formula certificate from the university.
19Changing registered mobile number or email after application - vital information will be communicated only to the registered contact; applicants are advised not to change these.
20Locking/submitting the application without previewing it along with uploaded documents - no correction in data/application will be allowed and no document will be accepted once the application is finally submitted/locked.
21Uploading documents in language other than English or Hindi without a notarized/certified transcript - all certificates in other languages must be accompanied by notarized transcripts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply?
A
The online application window closes on 19 June 2026 at 1600 hrs (IST).
Q2 How do I apply?
A
Apply online only via the RAC website: https://rac.gov.in. Applications by any other mode will not be accepted.
Q3 Which posts are advertised?
A
Direct recruitment for Scientist E (Level 13), Scientist D (Level 12) and Scientist C (Level 11) in DRDS cadre and encadered posts under the Lateral Recruitment Scheme. See notification for discipline-wise item numbers.
Q4 What are the essential academic requirements?
A
First Division (60% or equivalent) in the prescribed degree: BE/B.Tech/First Division M.Sc. or equivalent as specified for each post. CGPA/CPI conversions must be supported by university rules (6.75/10 = 60%).
Q5 What is the required post-qualification experience?
A
Scientist E: 11 years; Scientist D: 7 years; Scientist C: 3 years — experience counted only after the essential qualification and as on 19 June 2026. Experience below Pay Level 10 (7th CPC) is not counted.
Q6 Is there an application fee?
A
Yes. General/OBC/EWS (male) candidates pay Rs. 100 online. SC/ST/Divyang/Women candidates are exempt.
Q7 What is the selection process?
A
Multi-stage: Administrative screening → Technical Screening (Level‑1) → Technical Screening Level‑2/Preliminary Online Interview (if needed) → Final Personal Interview (in-person at RAC, Delhi). Final selection is based on interview marks; minimum 75/100 required in the Final Personal Interview.
Q8 Can serving government/PSU employees apply?
A
Yes, but they must upload a signed CCA Declaration during online submission and produce NOC from Cadre Controlling Authority (CCA) at the Final Personal Interview stage.
Q9 Are online/virtual interviews allowed for the final stage?
A
No. The Final Personal Interview is strictly in-person at RAC, Delhi; no online/virtual final interviews are permitted.
Q10 What common mistakes should I avoid before submitting the application?
A
Applying without First Division (60%) in the essential degree. Including overlapping PhD/study periods as work experience. Not providing required pay/CTC proof for private-sector experience. Failing to upload signed CCA Declaration (where applicable). Submitting documents in other languages without notarized English/Hindi transcripts. Changing registered email/phone after submission (all communications will use registered contacts).
