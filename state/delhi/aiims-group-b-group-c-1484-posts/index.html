How to Apply

  1. 1Visit the official website: www.aiimsexams.ac.in
  2. 2Register through the OTR (One-Time Registration) portal if you have not already done so.
  3. 3Log in and select the CRE-5 application link.
  4. 4Fill in all details carefully - name, date of birth, category, qualification, experience, and NOC details if applicable.
  5. 5Upload photographs, signature, and left thumb impression in the format specified under "Upload Image Instructions."
  6. 6Select your examination centre preference (state/UT).
  7. 7Pay the applicable examination fee via Debit Card, Credit Card, or Net Banking.
  8. 8Submit the application and download the registration slip for your records.
  9. 9If you are working in a Government/PSU/Autonomous Body, upload the No Objection Certificate (NOC) from your current employer by 6 July 2026 - failing to do so will prevent you from appearing in the examination.
  10. 10Check your application status on 9 July 2026 at www.aiimsexams.ac.in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for AIIMS CRE-5 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for CRE-5 is 30 June 2026 up to 5:00 PM. No applications will be accepted after the portal closes at this time.
Q2 What is the application fee for AIIMS CRE-5?
A
General and OBC candidates must pay ₹3,000 per group applied. SC, ST, and EWS candidates pay ₹2,400 per group. PwBD candidates are fully exempted from the fee. Fee is paid online and is non-refundable (except for SC/ST candidates who appear in the exam)
Q3 What posts are covered under CRE-5?
A
CRE-5 covers 57 post groups comprising a wide variety of Non-Faculty Group B and Group C posts, including Junior Engineers, Pharmacists, Lab Technologists, Administrative Officers, Clerks, Nurses, OT Technologists, Radiographers, and many other technical and non-technical roles.
Q4 How many institutes are participating in CRE-5?
A
A total of 28 AIIMS and Central Government Institutes/Bodies are participating in CRE-5, including all new AIIMS, ICMR institutes, CNCI Kolkata, Dr. RML Hospital, LHMC, Pasteur Institute of India, and others.
Q5 When is the CRE-5 exam date?
A
The Computer Based Test (CBT) is tentatively scheduled for 25 to 27 July 2026. AIIMS reserves the right to conduct the examination at any other date with 10 days' advance notice.
Q6 What is the selection process for CRE-5?
A
Selection involves a Computer Based Test (CBT) as the primary stage, followed by a Skill Test (for applicable post groups), choice/preference exercise by qualified candidates, seat allocation in order of merit, and document verification by the respective institute.
Q7 What is the exam pattern for CRE-5?
A
The CBT consists of 100 MCQs of 400 marks to be completed in 90 minutes, divided into five sections of 20 questions each. There is a negative marking of 1/4 mark for each wrong answer. The paper covers 20 questions on General Knowledge, Aptitude & Computer, and 80 domain-specific questions.
Q8 Is there any relaxation for PwBD candidates in age and qualifying marks?
A
Yes. PwBD candidates with benchmark disability (minimum 40%) receive an age relaxation of 10 years irrespective of their category. The qualifying marks for PwBD candidates are 30%, regardless of which category they belong to. Scribes and compensatory time are also available as per applicable government rules.
Q9 Where can I check my application status after submission?
A
Application acceptance status will be published on 9 July 2026 at www.aiimsexams.ac.in. Candidates must log in with their credentials to check status. AIIMS does not respond to telephonic or email queries about application status before the scheduled date.
