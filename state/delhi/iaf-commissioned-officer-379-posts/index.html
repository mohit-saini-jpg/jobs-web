Selection Process

1Stage‑I: Officer Intelligence Rating Test and Picture Perception & Discussion Test on Day 1; this is a screening stage.
2Stage‑II: Psychological tests, Group Tests and Personal Interview over the next days for Stage‑I qualified candidates.
3CPSS: Computerised Pilot Selection System for Flying Branch candidates (once‑in‑a‑lifetime test; CPSS/PABT failures are ineligible for Flying Branch).
4English: Comprehension, error detection, sentence completion, synonyms/antonyms, cloze test, idioms & phrases, analogy, sentence rearrangement, one‑word substitution, homonyms.
5General Awareness: History, Geography, Sports, National & International Organisations, Art & Culture, Personalities, Environment & Ecology, Polity, Economy, Basic Science, Science & Technology, Current Affairs, Defence.
6Numerical Ability: Fractions, Time & Work, Average, Percentage, Profit & Loss, Ratio & Proportion, Interest, Time & Distance, Area & Perimeter, Probability, Number System, Mixture & Allegation, Squares/Cubes, HCF & LCM, Mensuration, Heights & Distances, Statistics.
7Reasoning & Military Aptitude: Verbal and Non‑Verbal Reasoning.
8Training Date & Duration: Training is scheduled to commence in the first week of July 2027; duration is approximately 62 weeks for Flying and Ground Duty (Technical) branches and 50 weeks for Ground Duty (Non‑Technical).
9Stipend during Training: Flight Cadets receive a fixed stipend of Rs. 56,100/- per month during one year of training (as per 7th CPC matrix).
10Pay on Commissioning: Flying Officer - Pay Level 10 (Rs. 56,100-1,77,500) plus applicable MSP and allowances as per branch and posting.
11Insurance: Officers are covered under Air Force Group Insurance Society (AFGIS), with Rs. 1.25 crore cover (on contribution) for death in service and Air Veterans Insurance Cover after retirement for eligible PC/SSC officers.

How to Apply

  1. 1Visit the official AFCAT website https://afcat.edcil.co.in and read the advertisement and instructions carefully.
  2. 2Click on the sign‑up/registration link and enter full name, mobile number and email ID, then verify via OTP on email and mobile.
  3. 3Agree to the declaration, submit the registration form and obtain the system‑generated User ID and Password sent to registered email and mobile.
  4. 4Login with User ID and Password, click on “Go to Application”, and fill personal details, communication details, qualification details, course preferences and other required fields.
  5. 5Upload scanned images of recent passport size colour photograph (100-200 KB), signature (80-150 KB), thumb impression (50-100 KB) and live image capture as per specified formats.
  6. 6Carefully verify all details using “Preview” and then proceed to pay the examination fee of Rs. 550/- + GST (for AFCAT entry only) through online payment gateway (Net Banking, Debit/Credit Card, UPI etc.).
  7. 7After successful payment and submission, download and print the final submitted application form for future reference.
  8. 8Recent passport size colour photograph (100-200 KB; JPG/JPEG).
  9. 9Signature in black ink on white paper (80-150 KB; JPG/JPEG).
  10. 10Thumb impression - Left thumb (male) / Right thumb (female) (50-100 KB; JPG/JPEG/PDF).
  11. 11Live image capture via device camera at the time of application.
  12. 12Printout of e‑Admit Card (AFCAT 02/2026).
  13. 13Original Aadhaar Card and a second photo ID (PAN/Passport/Driving Licence/Voter ID etc.).
  14. 14Two passport size colour photographs (same as uploaded).
  15. 15Original and photocopies of Matriculation and 10+2 certificates and mark sheets.
  16. 16Original/provisional Graduation/PG degree and mark sheets (for AFSB).
  17. 17Certificate from College Principal for final year candidates specifying aggregate percentage and likely result date.
  18. 18Original NCC ‘C’ Certificate (for NCC Special Entry) and GATE documents (for GATE Score Entry).
  19. 19NOC from employer for Govt/PSU/Armed Forces personnel, if applicable.
  20. 20Candidates must ensure eligibility for the branch(es) they apply for; incorrect information at any stage leads to cancellation of candidature.
  21. 21Use of unfair means or possession of prohibited items (mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, study material etc.) inside the exam hall will result in cancellation and debarment from future AFCATs.
  22. 22No request for change of exam centre/date/slot will be entertained; centres may be changed by IAF based on administrative reasons.
  23. 23Serving airmen must apply online like other candidates and also follow service procedures as per AFO 11/2015.
  24. 24Use/possession of narcotics is strictly banned; candidates may be tested for drugs during medicals and training, and those found positive are liable for termination.
  25. 25Permanent tattoos are permitted only on the inner forearms and back of hands (with conditions); non‑permissible tattoos or those with offensive/extremist content lead to rejection.
  26. 26Training is rigorous; candidates should maintain good physical fitness (running, push‑ups, rope climbing, swimming etc.) before reporting to Air Force Academy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is AFCAT 02/2026 and which posts are notified?
A
AFCAT 02/2026 is the Indian Air Force Common Admission Test for recruitment of Commissioned Officers in Flying Branch and Ground Duty (Technical & Non‑Technical) branches, including NCC Special Entry and GATE Score Entry for courses commencing in July 2027.
Q2 What are the AFCAT 02/2026 application dates?
A
For AFCAT 02/2026, online registration opens on 20 May 2026 (11:00 hrs) and closes on 19 June 2026 (23:30 hrs), and applications must be submitted only through the official AFCAT website.
Q3 What is the age limit for AFCAT 02/2026 Flying Branch?
A
For the Flying Branch, the age limit is 20 to 24 years as on 01 July 2027, with a relaxable upper age of 26 years for candidates holding a valid Commercial Pilot Licence issued by DGCA (India).
Q4 What is the age limit for Ground Duty (Technical and Non‑Technical) in AFCAT 02/2026?
A
For Ground Duty (Technical and Non‑Technical) branches (including GATE Score Entry), the age limit is 20 to 26 years as on 01 July 2027 for both men and women candidates.
Q5 What is the educational qualification required for AFCAT 02/2026 Flying Branch?
A
For the Flying Branch, candidates must be graduates with minimum prescribed marks and must have studied Physics and Mathematics at 10+2 level or possess a BE/B.Tech degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
Q6 What is the AFCAT 02/2026 exam fee?
A
For AFCAT entry, candidates must pay a non‑refundable exam fee of Rs. 550/- plus GST, while NCC Special Entry and GATE Score Entry candidates are exempt from paying any examination fee.
Q7 What is the pay scale of an AFCAT 02/2026 Flying Officer after commissioning?
A
On successful completion of training, an IAF Flying Officer receives a pay scale of Rs. 56,100 – 1,77,500 (Level 10 as per 7th CPC) plus Military Service Pay (MSP) of Rs. 15,500 per month, along with applicable allowances.
Q8 Is AFCAT 02/2026 open for both men and women candidates?
A
Yes, unmarried male and female Indian citizens can apply for Short Service Commission in Flying Branch and Ground Duty branches, and for Permanent Commission wherever notified, subject to eligibility conditions.
Q9 What is the selection process for AFCAT 02/2026?
A
The AFCAT 02/2026 selection process includes an online AFCAT written exam (for AFCAT entry), followed by AFSB interview (Psychological tests, Group Tests and Personal Interview), and a medical examination, after which a final merit list is prepared.
Q10 How can I apply online for Indian Air Force AFCAT 02/2026?
A
Eligible candidates must apply online only through the official AFCAT portal afcat.cdac.in , using a valid email ID and mobile number, and carefully filling personal, educational and branch preference details before the last date
