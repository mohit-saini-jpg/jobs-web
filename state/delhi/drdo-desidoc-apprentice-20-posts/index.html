Selection Process

1NATS registration first. Your application can be rejected outright if you do not have an active NATS enrolment number before you apply. Complete registration at the portal before filling the form.
2Email ID accuracy. All communication from DESIDOC will come by email only. A single typo in your email address means you will miss the shortlist notice, interview call, and offer letter.
3Photo and document self-attestation. Attach self-attested photocopies, not originals. Unsigned or un-attested copies may be treated as incomplete.
4Envelope superscription. Write the name of the discipline on the outside of the envelope. Envelopes without this may be missorted or delayed.
5Deadline buffer. The 21-day window runs from the advertisement date on the DRDO website, not from the date you discover the ad. Apply well in advance to avoid postal delays.
6Category certificate format. SC/ST/OBC certificates must be in the prescribed Central Government format. Certificates in state government formats or outdated formats may cause your reservation claim to be treated as General category.

How to Apply

  1. 1Register on the NATS portal at http://www.mhrdnats.gov.in or http://nats.education.gov.in and note your enrolment number.
  2. 2Download the application form from the DRDO website at www.drdo.gov.in (look in the "What's New" section).

  4. 4SC/ST/OBC candidates must attach a valid caste certificate in the prescribed Central Government format.
  5. 5Superscribe the envelope with the name of the discipline applied for.
  6. 6Submit by post or by hand to: Director, Defence Scientific Information & Documentation Centre (DESIDOC), Metcalfe House, Civil Lines, Delhi – 110 054, within 21 days of the advertisement date.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for DRDO DESIDOC Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
Applications must be submitted within 21 days from the date the advertisement is published on the DRDO website (www.drdo.gov.in). Check the "What's New" section of the website for the exact publication date.
Q2 How many vacancies are available?
A
A total of 20 seats are notified — 16 in Library & Information Science, 2 in Computer Science, and 2 in Photography/Videography. The number may increase or decrease based on organisational requirements.
Q3 What is the age limit for DESIDOC apprenticeship 2026?
A
The upper age limit is 28 years as on the date of the written test/interview. SC/ST candidates get a relaxation of 5 years and OBC candidates get 3 years.
Q4 Which pass-out years are eligible?
A
Only candidates who completed their qualifying course in 2024 or 2025 are eligible. Candidates who passed before 2024 and those holding post-graduate qualifications are not eligible.
Q5 What is the stipend for DESIDOC apprentices?
A
Graduate and B.E. holders receive ₹12,300 per month. Diploma and ITI holders receive ₹10,900 per month.
Q6 How do I apply for DESIDOC apprenticeship 2026?
A
First register on the NATS portal (mhrdnats.gov.in or nats.education.gov.in), then download the application form from www.drdo.gov.in, fill it with self-attested document copies, and send it by post or hand-deliver it to DESIDOC, Metcalfe House, Civil Lines, Delhi – 110 054.
Q7 Is there a prior experience restriction?
A
Yes. Candidates who have already completed one year or more of training or job experience after their qualifying qualification are not eligible for engagement as apprentices.
Q8 Will completing the training guarantee a job at DRDO or DESIDOC?
A
No. Completion of apprenticeship training does not confer any right to employment. Apprentices cannot claim any appointment in DESIDOC or DRDO after the training period ends.
Q9 What is the selection process?
A
Candidates are shortlisted on merit (marks percentage in the essential qualification) and then called for a written test and/or interview. Only selected candidates are informed through an offer letter or email.
