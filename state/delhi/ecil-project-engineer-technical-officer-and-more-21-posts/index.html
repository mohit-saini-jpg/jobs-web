Selection Process

1Candidates who do not meet the minimum 60% aggregate marks in B.E./B.Tech. (for PE-C and TO-C) or Diploma (for APE-C); SA-C requires only ITI pass.
2Candidates who do not have the required post-qualification experience — 3 years for PE-C, 1 year for TO-C and APE-C, 2 years for SA-C — in the relevant field; experience from internships, college projects, or academic institutions is NOT counted.
3Candidates who have crossed the upper age limit (33 years for PE-C; 30 years for TO-C, APE-C, SA-C as on walk-in date), except those eligible for age relaxation.
4Candidates seeking permanent government employment; this is strictly a temporary contract engagement for up to 4 years with no guarantee of regularization.
5Candidates unwilling to work anywhere in India or relocate; ECIL requires readiness to work at any location based on organizational needs.
6Candidates applying for more than one post category; only one category application is allowed.
7OBC candidates who belong to the Creamy Layer; only Non-Creamy Layer OBC candidates (with certificate not older than 1 year from date of selection explicitly mentioning NCL) are eligible for OBC reservation.
8Candidates whose qualification is through Distance/Correspondence mode or from institutions not recognized by appropriate statutory authorities.
9Current ECIL employees (on rolls) who have not obtained prior written permission letter from their respective Head of Division (HoD) before attending the walk-in.
10Candidates who cannot produce all original certificates at the time of walk-in; document verification is mandatory for registration and interview.
11Arriving late — registration closes sharp at 11:30 hrs and no exceptions are made; candidates must plan to reach the venue before 11:00 hrs to complete formalities comfortably.
12Not downloading the Application Form from www.ecil.co.in before attending; candidates must bring a duly filled application form and resume to the walk-in venue.
13Bringing experience certificates that do not mention duration (from and to dates) and post held; work experience without supporting documents will be excluded from experience calculation.
14Currently employed candidates not carrying their Appointment Order and first/recent pay slips; these are mandatory without fail for currently employed applicants.
15Not carrying a CGPA-to-percentage conversion certificate from the university/institution if the degree uses a grade/CGPA system; this is mandatory for eligibility determination.
16Applying for a post where your experience profile does not match the specific domain requirements (e.g., applying for VSAT/SATCOM-specific PE-C posts without any satellite communication experience).
17OBC candidates bringing an OBC certificate older than 1 year from the date of walk-in, or one that does not explicitly mention the Non-Creamy Layer clause; such certificates will not be accepted.
18Not carrying all original documents along with self-attested photocopies; document verification happens before the interview and incomplete documentation leads to rejection.
19Candidates at remote postings (Rawatbhata, Tarapur, Lunglie, Kaaru-Leh) not making prior accommodation/travel arrangements; ECIL advises candidates that document verification and interviews may take considerable time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What are the walk-in dates for ECIL Recruitment 2026?
A
The walk-in dates vary by zone: Kolkata (East Zone) on 15-06-2026; Hyderabad (HQ), Mumbai (West Zone), and New Delhi (North Zone) on 18-06-2026; and Chennai (South Zone) on 19-06-2026. Registration is between 09:00 hrs and 11:30 hrs at the respective venue.
Q2 How many total posts are notified in ECIL Walk-in Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 22 posts are notified across four post categories — Project Engineer on Contract (PE-C), Technical Officer on Contract (TO-C), Assistant Project Engineer on Contract (APE-C), and Senior Artisan on Contract (SA-C) — spread across five zones (Hyderabad, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, New Delhi).
Q3 What is the eligibility for ECIL PE-C (Project Engineer on Contract) post?
A
Candidates must hold a B.E./B.Tech. degree with at least 60% aggregate marks in the relevant discipline, along with a minimum of 3 years of post-qualification experience (apprenticeship up to 1 year also counted). The upper age limit for UR category is 33 years as on the walk-in date.
Q4 What is the salary / emoluments for ECIL Project Engineer on Contract (PE-C)?
A
PE-C receives Rs. 40,000/month in Year 1, Rs. 45,000/month in Year 2, Rs. 50,000/month in Year 3, and Rs. 55,000/month in Year 4. Additionally, a one-time lump sum attire allowance of Rs. 8,400/- is paid after completing at least 6 months of tenure.
Q5 Is ECIL Walk-in Recruitment 2026 a permanent job?
A
No. This is purely a temporary/contract engagement for an initial period of 1 year, extendable up to 4 years (including the initial term) depending on project requirements and satisfactory performance. It does not create any right to regularization or permanent employment in ECIL.
Q6 Can I apply for multiple posts in ECIL Walk-in Recruitment 2026?
A
No. Candidates must apply for one category of post only. Applying to multiple posts is strictly not allowed.
Q7 What documents must I carry to the ECIL walk-in?
A
You must carry the duly filled application form and resume, 10th certificate (DOB proof), qualification certificates and mark sheets, CGPA conversion certificate (if applicable), experience certificates (mentioning duration and post held), Appointment Order and recent pay slips (if currently employed), category certificate (EWS/OBC-NCL/SC/ST), and a government-issued photo identity proof — all in original along with one set of self-attested photocopies.
Q8 What is the selection method for ECIL Walk-in Recruitment 2026?
A
Selection is based on merit combining three criteria: Qualification (20% of aggregate percentage), Relevant Experience (maximum 30 marks — weightage varies by post), and Personal Interview (50 marks). Only candidates who clear document verification will be allowed to participate in the interview.
Q9 Is internship or project work counted as experience for ECIL posts?
A
No. Experience from Academic Institutions, Colleges, Internships, or Project work is explicitly NOT considered for post-qualification experience calculation. Only industry experience from registered organizations and relevant apprenticeship (up to a maximum of 1 year) will be counted.
Q10 Where can I get updates and corrigendum for ECIL Walk-in Recruitment 2026?
A
All updates, corrigendum (if any), and official information will be published only on the official ECIL website www.ecil.co.in. Candidates must not rely on other websites, social media handles, or third-party publishers for any recruitment-related information.
