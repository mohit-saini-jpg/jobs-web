Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for GUVNL Vidyut Sahayak JE Electrical 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application is 02 July 2026 up to 18:00 hrs. Applications must be submitted through www.mgvcl.com.
Q2 How many total vacancies are there in GUVNL Vidyut Sahayak 2026?
A
There are a total of 183 vacancies across MGVCL (22), DGVCL (27), UGVCL (26), PGVCL (86), and GETCO (22). Additional vacancies may arise within one year from the date of publication of the Second Tier result.
Q3 What is the educational qualification required?
A
Candidates must hold a B.E. (Electrical) / B.Tech. (Electrical) / B.E. Electrical and Electronics (EEE) degree through regular mode from a UGC/AICTE-approved university, with no ATKT in the final year or last two semesters and a minimum of 55% average marks in the final year or last two semesters.
Q4 What is the age limit for GUVNL Vidyut Sahayak 2026?
A
The maximum age is 35 years for Unreserved and 40 years for Reserved categories (including EWS) as on 10 June 2026. Additional relaxations of 5 years for females, 10 years for PwD, and 10 years for Ex-Armed Force personnel are available, with the overall cap at 45 years.
Q5 Where can candidates apply for MGVCL Recruitment 2026?
A
Candidates can submit their online applications through the official MGVCL career portal at www.mgvcl.com/career.
Q6 What is the application fee for GUVNL Vidyut Sahayak 2026?
A
The fee is ₹500/- (inclusive of GST) for Unreserved candidates and ₹250/- (inclusive of GST) for Reserved/PwD candidates. Payment is online only; the fee is non-refundable.
Q7 What is the salary for Vidyut Sahayak JE Electrical?
A
The fixed remuneration is ₹48,100/- per month in the first year and ₹50,700/- per month in the second year. On successful completion of the 2-year contractual period, candidates may be appointed as regular Junior Engineer – Electrical in the pay scale of ₹45,400–1,01,200/-.
Q8 What is the selection process for GUVNL Vidyut Sahayak 2026?
A
Selection is through a two-tier Computer Based Test (CBT). Tier 1 covers general aptitude subjects (100 marks). Tier 2 covers Electrical Engineering (100 marks). The final merit is based on Tier 2 marks. Shortlisted candidates then undergo document verification and pre-employment medical examination.
Q9 Is there negative marking in the exam?
A
Yes. There is a negative marking of 1/4 mark (0.25 marks) deducted for every wrong answer in both the First Tier and Second Tier examinations.
Q10 Can I change my company preference after applying?
A
No. The order of preference for companies (MGVCL, DGVCL, UGVCL, PGVCL, GETCO) once submitted is final and irrevocable. No request for revision, addition, or change will be entertained under any circumstances.
