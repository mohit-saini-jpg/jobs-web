Selection Process

1Scrutiny → Shortlisting → Personal Interview
2Application Scrutiny: All online applications are received and scrutinised by the Selection Committee of AIIMS Rajkot. Only eligible candidates are called for the next stage.
3Shortlisting: If the vacancy-to-candidate ratio exceeds 1:10, the Selection Committee may shortlist on the basis of an objective screening score (Appendix 1 criteria).
4Document Verification: Shortlisted candidates must produce original academic, experience, category, and registration certificates before the interview.
5Personal Interview: Eligible candidates appear before the Standing Selection Committee at AIIMS Rajkot in person. Candidates must make a 5-minute PowerPoint presentation on their work and achievements.
6Final Selection: The decision of the Competent Authority regarding selection is final and binding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for AIIMS Rajkot Faculty Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to apply online is 10th July 2026 by 17:00 Hours. The application must be submitted via the Google Form link and the signed copy emailed to [email protected].
Q2 How many vacancies are released in AIIMS Rajkot Faculty Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 87 vacancies have been released across four posts - Professor (26), Additional Professor (17), Associate Professor (15), and Assistant Professor (28) — spanning 37 departments. All SC, ST, and OBC vacancies except one are backlog vacancies.
Q3 What is the application fee for AIIMS Rajkot Faculty Recruitment 2026?
A
General and OBC candidates must pay Rs. 500. EWS candidates also pay Rs. 500. SC, ST, and PwBD candidates are fully exempted. Candidates applying on deputation basis are not required to pay any fee.
Q4 What is the selection process for AIIMS Rajkot Faculty posts?
A
Selection is through application scrutiny, shortlisting (if applicants exceed 1:10 ratio), document verification, and a personal interview before the Standing Selection Committee at AIIMS Rajkot. Candidates must deliver a 5-minute PowerPoint presentation at the interview.
Q5 Can candidates currently employed in government service apply?
A
Yes. Government employees must submit a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their present employer at the time of interview. Deputation candidates must route their application through their employer along with ACRs/APARs for the last 5 years and a Vigilance Clearance Certificate.
