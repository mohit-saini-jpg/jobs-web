Selection Process

1All questions are objective type multiple choice.
2No negative marking.
3Candidates must score a minimum of 33% in aggregate to qualify. Those scoring below 33% are ineligible for selection.
4There are no sectional cut-offs.
5Stage II (Skill Test) is qualifying in nature only - marks will not be added to the final merit list.
6General Awareness (50 marks): Current events, History, Polity, Constitution, Sports, Art & Culture, Geography, Economics, Everyday Science, Scientific Research, National/International Organisations. State-specific questions may also be asked.
7General Intelligence & Reasoning (50 marks): Analogies, similarities, differences, space visualization, problem solving, verbal and figure classification, arithmetical reasoning, number series, abstract ideas, analytical functions.
8Arithmetical & Numerical Ability (50 marks): Number Systems, Simplification, Decimals, Data Interpretation, Fractions, LCM, HCF, Ratio & Proportion, Percentage, Average, Profit & Loss, Simple & Compound Interest, Mensuration, Time & Work, Time & Distance, Tables & Graphs.
9English Language & Comprehension (50 marks): Error spotting, Fill in the blanks, Synonyms, Antonyms, Spelling errors, Idioms & Phrases, One word substitution, Sentence improvement, Active/Passive voice, Direct/Indirect narration, Cloze passage, Comprehension.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application for APSSB CHSLE 2026 is 10 August 2026 by 5:00 PM. The application link will be disabled after this time
Q2 How many total vacancies are available under APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
A total of 207 Group C vacancies have been announced across 9 posts and multiple departments of the Government of Arunachal Pradesh.
Q3 Which post has the highest number of vacancies in APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Stockman (Post Code 24/26) has the highest vacancy count with 89 posts in the Animal Husbandry & Veterinary department.
Q4 What is the application fee for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
The examination fee is ₹150 for APST candidates and ₹200 for Unreserved candidates. Persons with Disabilities (PwD) are exempted from paying any fee.
Q5 Is there negative marking in APSSB CHSLE 2026 written exam?
A
No. There is no negative marking in the APSSB CHSLE 2026 written examination. However, candidates must score a minimum of 33% in aggregate to be eligible for selection.
Q6 Which post codes require a Diploma in Computer Application?
A
Post Codes 17/26 (DEO), 20/26 (JSA), 22/26 (LDC), and 23/26 (Record Keeper) require a minimum 6-month Diploma in Computer Application from an AICTE/APCTE recognised institution.
Q7 Will the Skill Test marks be added to the final merit list?
A
No. The Stage II Skill Test is qualifying in nature only. Its marks will not be counted in the final merit list. The merit list is prepared solely on the basis of Stage I written exam marks.
Q8 Can I change my post and department preference after submission?
A
No. The preference of post and department once submitted is final and cannot be changed under any circumstances. Candidates should carefully choose their preferences before submitting the form.
Q9 What is the age limit for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Age limit varies by post — between 18 to 28 years for Record Keeper (23/26), 18 to 32 years for DEO, JSA, LDC, Agriculture Field Assistant, and 18 to 35 years for Field Assistant, Fishery Demonstrator, Laboratory Assistant, and Stockman. Age relaxation applies for APST (5 years), PwBD (10 years), and Ex-Servicemen as per rules.
Q10 Where do I download the admit card for APSSB CHSLE 2026?
A
Admit cards will be available for download from the official APSSB website www.apssb.nic.in. Individual admit cards will not be sent by post. Candidates must regularly check the website for updates.
