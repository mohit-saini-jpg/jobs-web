Selection Process

1Merit based on marks obtained in ITI / Diploma / Graduation. Preference to candidates from neighbouring villages within 16 km radius of plant site.
2Candidates who have already completed an apprenticeship under the Apprentices Act-1961 in any Government, PSU or private industrial organisation at any point in the past.
3Candidates who are currently undergoing apprenticeship training anywhere - they are explicitly ineligible.
4Candidates who absconded from a previous apprenticeship training - they are permanently barred.
5Candidates whose age as on 30/06/2026 is below the minimum or above the maximum prescribed for their apprentice type (except those eligible for age relaxation under reserved categories).
6Candidates who passed their ITI/Diploma/Graduation more than 5 years before 30/06/2026 (applicable for Diploma and Graduate Apprentices on NATS 2.0 portal).
7Candidates who completed their qualification through distance/part-time/correspondence mode - only Full-Time/Regular qualifications are accepted.
8Candidates who are not medically fit as per NPCIL's minimum physical standards (Height below 137 cm, Weight below 25.4 kg, Chest expansion below 3.8 cm, or suffering from Tuberculosis/contagious disease).
9Candidates who cannot obtain a Police Verification Certificate from their District Police Authority - this is mandatory for the highly prohibited Kalpakkam site.
10OBC candidates from the Creamy Layer - only Non-Creamy Layer OBC candidates are eligible for OBC reservation benefits.
11Candidates who have provided false information or suppressed relevant details - their application/training will be rejected forthwith and NPCIL will not entertain any correspondence.
12Not registering on the correct portal before applying - Trade Apprentices must register on NAPS 2.0 (apprenticeshipindia.gov.in), while Diploma and Graduate Apprentices must register on NATS 2.0 (nats.education.gov.in). Applying without this registration will make the candidature invalid.
13Not linking Aadhaar with bank account before registration on the portal - this is a mandatory prerequisite.
14Applying for a trade/discipline in which PwBD candidature is not identified - check the detailed PwBD eligibility table for each trade before applying.
15Diploma/Graduate candidates not providing the authenticated CGPA-to-percentage conversion formula from their institution - if not provided, NPCIL's standard conversion table will be applied, which may result in a lower equivalent percentage than expected.
16Sending an incomplete application or an application without proper enclosures - such applications will be summarily rejected without any communication.
17Missing the last date - applications received after 30/06/2026 for any reason will not be considered. Postal delays are not an excuse; apply well in advance.
18Not enclosing 4 passport-size photographs or forgetting to affix a photograph on the application form.
19Welder trade applicants not attaching 8th standard mark sheet (required additionally for this trade).
20Candidates living within 16 km of MAPS not attaching residential proof of permanent address - this is required to avail local preference in selection.
21Filling in the wrong category (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/UR) - category cannot be changed later and mismatch at document verification will lead to cancellation.

How to Apply

  1. 1Step 1: Register on the mandatory apprenticeship portal - NAPS 2.0 (for Trade Apprentices) or NATS 2.0 (for Diploma & Graduate Apprentices). Ensure Aadhaar is linked with your bank account before registering.
  2. 2Step 2: Download the Application Form (Annexure-1) from www.npcil.nic.in > HR Management > Opportunities. Verify your eligibility before filling the form.
  3. 3Step 3: Fill in the application form completely in Block Letters. Affix a recent passport-size photograph in the space provided. Sign the declaration.
  4. 4Step 4: Attach self-attested copies of all required documents (see list below).
  5. 5Step 5: Send the complete application by post to reach on or before 30/06/2026 at: Deputy Manager (HRM), Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited (A Government of India Enterprise), Madras Atomic Power Station, Kalpakkam – 603 102, Chengalpattu, Tamil Nadu.
  6. 6Birth Certificate issued by Competent Authority / SSC Mark Sheet as proof of Date of Birth
  7. 7Educational Qualification mark sheets (10th / 12th; and 8th mark sheet additionally for Welder trade)
  8. 8ITI / Diploma / Graduate Degree certificate and mark sheets of all semesters/years
  9. 9Other qualification/experience certificates, if any
  10. 10Caste Certificate issued by Competent Authority in prescribed Central Government format (for SC/ST/OBC candidates - valid as on 30/06/2026)
  11. 11Valid Income and Assets Certificate as on 30/06/2026 in prescribed format issued by Competent Authority (for EWS category)
  12. 12Disability Certificate from Competent Authority in prescribed format (for PwBD candidates; disability must be 40% or more)
  13. 13Police Verification Certificate issued by concerned District Police Authority
  14. 14Character/Conduct Certificate from the concerned institution
  15. 15Aadhaar Card (Compulsory)
  16. 164 recent passport-size photographs
  17. 17Residential proof of permanent address (if living within 16 km radius of MAPS, Kalpakkam)
  18. 18Copy of Apprentice Registration on NAPS 2.0 (https://www.apprenticeshipindia.gov.in) or NATS 2.0 (https://www.nats.education.gov.in)
  19. 19Proof from University/Institute converting CGPA/GPA/SGPA into percentage (for candidates awarded grades)
  20. 20THIS IS NOT AN EMPLOYMENT IN NPCIL. Engagement as an apprentice shall not create any obligation on NPCIL to offer regular employment, nor any obligation on the apprentice to accept employment under NPCIL after completion of training.
  21. 21The period of apprenticeship training will be for a maximum of One Year. The contract of apprenticeship terminates on expiry of the training period.
  22. 22Candidates who have already undergone or are currently undergoing apprenticeship under the Apprentices Act-1961 in any organization are strictly NOT eligible to apply.
  23. 23Preference in selection will be given to candidates from neighbouring villages within 16 km radius of the MAPS plant site in Kalpakkam. Merit-based selection from other candidates will follow only if trades/disciplines are not filled by local candidates.
  24. 24All selected apprentices must sign a contract of apprenticeship with NPCIL as per the Apprentices Act-1961 and Apprenticeship Rules-1992.
  25. 25Madras Atomic Power Station is a highly prohibited area. Engagement is subject to submission of Police Verification Certificate from the concerned District Police Authority.
  26. 26Candidature is provisional at all stages. If false information or suppressed facts are detected at any stage of the process or during training, the application/training shall be rejected forthwith and NPCIL will not entertain any correspondence.
  27. 27If it is detected that the candidate has provided false documents, impersonated, or affixed a non-human/irrelevant photograph, the applicant will be liable to be disqualified, prosecuted and permanently debarred from apprenticeship in NPCIL.
  28. 28Only Full-Time/Regular mode qualifications (ITI/Diploma/Graduation) are accepted. Distance/Part-Time/Correspondence qualifications are not eligible.
  29. 29Diploma and Graduate Apprentice applicants must have passed their qualifying examination within 5 years of the last date of application i.e. 30/06/2026 (applicable for NATS 2.0 portal eligibility).
  30. 30All SC/ST/OBC (NCL) candidates must produce a self-attested copy of valid caste certificate in the prescribed Central Government format from a Competent Authority along with originals at the time of document verification.
  31. 31NPCIL strives to have a workforce that reflects gender balance. Women candidates are encouraged to apply.
  32. 32In case of any discrepancy between Hindi and English versions of the notification, the English version shall prevail for all purposes.
  33. 33Management reserves the right to cancel or alter the number of seats of any particular trade/discipline or cancel the entire engagement process without assigning any reason.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date to submit the offline application for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice Recruitment 2026 is 30 June 2026.
Q2 How many vacancies are available in NPCIL MAPS Apprentice 2026?
A
A total of 158 apprenticeship seats are available, including Trade (124), Diploma (18), and Graduate (16) posts.
Q3 What is the mode of application for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
The application process is offline only, and candidates must send the filled form by post to NPCIL Kalpakkam.
Q4 Is there any application fee for NPCIL Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
There is no application fee required for applying to NPCIL MAPS Apprentice Recruitment 2026.
Q5 What is the selection process for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice 2026?
A
Selection is based on merit of marks obtained in ITI, Diploma, or Graduation, followed by document verification and medical fitness.
Q6 What is the stipend for NPCIL MAPS apprentices?
A
The monthly stipend ranges from ₹9,600 to ₹12,300 depending on Trade, Diploma, or Graduate apprentice category.
Q7 What is the age limit for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice Recruitment 2026?
A
The age limit is 18–24 years for Trade, 18–25 years for Diploma, and 21–28 years for Graduate apprentices as on 30 June 2026.
Q8 Is prior apprenticeship allowed for NPCIL MAPS recruitment?
A
Candidates who have already completed or are currently undergoing apprenticeship training are not eligible to apply.
Q9 Is NAPS/NATS registration mandatory for NPCIL Apprentice 2026?
A
Yes, NAPS 2.0 registration is mandatory for Trade apprentices and NATS 2.0 for Diploma/Graduate apprentices before applying.
Q10 What is the training duration for NPCIL MAPS Apprentice 2026?
A
The apprenticeship training duration is maximum one year at Madras Atomic Power Station, Kalpakkam.
