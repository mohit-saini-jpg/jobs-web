Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for ISRO URSC JRF Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date for receipt of online applications is 11 July 2026. This date is also the cut-off for determining age and qualification eligibility for all purposes.
Q2 How many JRF posts are available in ISRO URSC Recruitment 2026?
A
A total of 16 Junior Research Fellow positions have been notified under Advertisement No. URSC:02:2026 across Electronics, Computer Science, Optics, Mechanical, and allied disciplines. The number is provisional and may vary based on actual requirements.
Q3 What is the age limit for ISRO URSC JRF 2026?
A
The maximum age is 28 years as on 11 July 2026. SC/ST candidates get 5 years relaxation and OBC candidates get 3 years. Ex-Servicemen, PwBD, and certain women categories receive relaxation as per Government of India orders.
Q4 Is GATE or NET mandatory to apply for ISRO URSC JRF?
A
Yes, it is mandatory. Candidates must have qualified CSIR–UGC NET (including Lectureship), GATE, JAM, JEST, or a national-level examination conducted by Central Government departments or agencies such as DST, DBT, DAE, DOS, DRDO, MoE, IIT, IISc, IISER, NISER, etc. Possession of the postgraduate degree alone does not make a candidate eligible.
Q5 What is the minimum percentage required for ISRO JRF 2026?
A
For most positions, a minimum of 60% aggregate or CGPA/CPI of 6.5 on a 10-point scale in M.E./M.Tech./M.Sc.(Engg.) is required. For JRF-E13 via the M.Sc. route, the minimum is 65% aggregate or CGPA/CPI of 6.84.
Q6 What is the fellowship stipend for ISRO URSC JRF?
A
JRFs receive ₹37,000 per month. This may be enhanced to ₹42,000 per month upon promotion to Senior Research Fellow at the sole discretion of ISRO/DOS/URSC after two years. HRA is also paid if hostel accommodation is not provided. No DA is paid.
Q7 What is the selection process for ISRO URSC JRF Recruitment 2026?
A
Selection involves two stages: an initial screening based on academic performance and application parameters, followed by an Interview conducted in Bengaluru. Call letters for the interview are sent by e-mail only. The result is published on the ISRO website.
Q8 Can candidates awaiting final results apply for ISRO JRF 2026?
A
No. Only passed candidates are eligible to apply. Students waiting for their final results are not eligible and should not apply.
Q9 Where should I apply for ISRO URSC JRF 2026?
A
Applications must be submitted online only at www.isro.gov.in under the "CAREERS" section. No other mode of application is accepted. For clarifications, write to [email protected].
Q10 Does the JRF position lead to a permanent job at ISRO?
A
No. The JRF position does not confer any claim or right for regular appointment in URSC or in any ISRO/Department of Space centre. The maximum combined engagement as JRF and SRF together shall not exceed five years.
