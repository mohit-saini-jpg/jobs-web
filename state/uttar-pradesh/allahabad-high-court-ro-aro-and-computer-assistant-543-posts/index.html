Selection Process

1Stage-I: Preliminary (OMR) + Stage-II: Mains (Subjective) + Stage-III: Computer Knowledge Test (CBT)
2Candidates who do not possess a Graduation degree from a recognized University/Institute by the closing date of the application form.
3Candidates who do not have a Diploma/Degree in Computer Science, or 'O' Level certificate from NIELIT/DOEACC Society, or 'CCC' certificate - all three posts require this mandatorily.
4Candidates outside the eligible age band as on 01.07.2026 (below 18 / above 35 years for CA; below 21 / above 35 years for RO and ARO), except those entitled to notified relaxations.
5Candidates who do not have a minimum typing speed of 25 words per minute in English on a computer, as this is both an eligibility requirement and a Stage-III qualification criterion.
6Candidates not domiciled in Uttar Pradesh who wish to claim reservation benefits, as reservation is applicable only for U.P. domicile candidates; non-UP reserved category candidates will be treated as General/UR.
7OBC candidates from the creamy layer; only non-creamy layer OBC candidates with a valid, recent certificate from U.P. are entitled to OBC reservation benefits.
8Male candidates who have more than one wife living, or female candidates who have married a person already having a living wife - such candidates are specifically ineligible under the service rules.
9Persons dismissed from service by the Union Government, State Government, Local Authority, or any Government Corporation, as they are deemed ineligible under the applicable rules.
10Candidates who are not physically and mentally fit as per the medical standards, since a medical certificate from the CMO is mandatory before final appointment.
11Candidates currently in government service who cannot produce a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their Cadre Controlling Authority when required.
12Submitting more than one application form - only the last submitted application shall be considered and fee paid for earlier applications will not be refunded.
13Entering wrong category (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/UR) - category cannot be changed after submission and mismatch with certificates at document verification stage leads to cancellation.
14Uploading blurred, fabricated, hand-made or computer-generated photographs, or photographs without the applicant's name and date of capture printed on them - such applications are liable to be rejected.
15Writing full name in capital letters in the signature box instead of a full running-hand signature - unsigned application forms will be rejected outright.
16Providing the mobile number or email ID of a coaching centre or third party instead of your own - all communication including OTP, admit card and results will go to the registered contact details only.
17Not downloading and saving the Confirmation Page after fee payment - the Confirmation Page is generated only after successful payment and must be retained for future reference.
18Ignoring the correction window (24-25 June 2026) - only limited fields can be corrected during this period; requests for corrections after the correction window will not be entertained by NTA or the High Court in any manner.
19Candidates currently employed in Central/State Government not arranging the NOC in advance - the NOC must be produced as and when asked, failing which the selection may be cancelled.
20Not noting down the system-generated Application Number at the time of registration - this number is required for all future correspondence and subsequent logins.
21Attempting to disclose or share examination question paper contents - candidates are bound by a Non-Disclosure Agreement upon registration, and breach of NDA is a punishable offence leading to cancellation of candidature.

How to Apply

  1. 1Recent coloured passport-size photograph with applicant's name and date of capture printed on it (JPG/JPEG, 10 KB-200 KB); photo must show 80% face including both ears against white background - polaroid and computer-generated photos not acceptable
  2. 2Signature in running handwriting with Blue/Black pen on white paper (JPG/JPEG, 10 KB-100 KB); capital letters not accepted
  3. 3Left Hand Thumb Impression on white paper (JPG/JPEG, 10 KB-200 KB); right thumb may be used if left is unavailable
  4. 4Category Certificate (PDF, 50 KB-300 KB) - wherever applicable
  5. 5Class Xth or equivalent Certificate as proof of Date of Birth (PDF, 50 KB-300 KB)
  6. 6Employment Certificate - optional (PDF, 50 KB-300 KB)
  7. 7Live photograph captured via webcam or mobile phone at the time of filling the application form
  8. 8Candidates must appear in all three stages of the examination; it is mandatory to qualify each stage individually. No exemption from any stage shall be granted to any candidate including those belonging to the Physically Handicapped category.
  9. 9Final Merit List shall be prepared exclusively on the basis of aggregate marks in Stage-II and Stage-III. Candidates selected for RO post shall not be considered for ARO or CA; candidates selected for ARO shall not be considered for CA.
  10. 10Biometric information (Photograph, Thumb Impression and IRIS) of all candidates shall be captured at examination centres. Candidates must cooperate with security personnel for identity and security checks.
  11. 11The Provisional Answer Key for Stage-I shall be displayed on the official websites for 2-3 days, and candidates may challenge any answer key by paying Rs. 500/- per question challenged (refundable if challenge is accepted).
  12. 12No Score Card will be dispatched by post; candidates must download their Score Cards from the official websites after declaration of final result, as they will be available online for a limited period only.
  13. 13Mobile phones, Bluetooth devices, pagers, calculators, electronic watches with calculator facility or any other electronic gadget are strictly prohibited inside the examination premises. Possession of barred items will be treated as Unfair Means (UFM).
  14. 14Merely appearing and passing in the examination does not confer any right to the candidate for selection or appointment. Selection is subject to eligibility, rank in merit list, medical fitness and verification of original documents.
  15. 15Information uploaded on the official website shall not be provided under the RTI Act during the course of the recruitment process; factual information shall be provided only after declaration of the final result.
  16. 16All candidates already employed in Central/State Government or Public Undertakings must apply subject to production of NOC from their Cadre Controlling Authority as and when asked for.
  17. 17All disputes pertaining to the conduct of this examination shall fall within the jurisdiction of Allahabad only. The High Court reserves the right to alter the number of vacancies, modify the examination process or modify/alter the minimum cut-off marks without assigning any reason.
  18. 18Examination records will be retained for up to 90 days from the date of declaration of the final result. Canvassing in any form shall disqualify the candidature of the applicant.
  19. 19Candidates are bound by a Non-Disclosure Agreement upon registering. Sharing or reproducing any question paper content in part or whole through any medium is a punishable offence leading to cancellation of candidature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for Allahabad High Court RO/ARO/Computer Assistant Recruitment 2026?
A
The online application window closes on 21 June 2026. Fee payment is accepted up to 22 June 2026.
Q2 How many vacancies are available for RO, ARO and Computer Assistant?
A
Total vacancies announced: 543 (Review Officer 138, Assistant Review Officer 380, Computer Assistant 25). Numbers are indicative and may change.
Q3 Where can I apply and download the official notification?
A
Apply online at the Allahabad High Court portal or NTA recruitment site: https://allahabadhighcourt.in/ or https://exams.nta.nic.in/allhcre. Download the advertisement/notification from the official website.
Q4 What are the essential educational qualifications required?
A
A Bachelor’s Degree from a university established by law in India (or equivalent). Additionally, a recognised computer qualification: Diploma/Degree in Computer Science, NIELIT ‘O’ Level, or CCC certificate.
Q5 Is there a typing speed requirement?
A
Yes. Minimum English typing speed of 25 words per minute on computer is mandatory.
Q6 What is the age limit and are there relaxations?
A
Age as on 01-07-2026: RO & ARO — 21 to 35 years; Computer Assistant — 18 to 35 years. Age relaxations for SC/ST/OBC and other reserved categories of Uttar Pradesh apply as per rules under the 1976 Act.
Q7 What is the application fee for each category?
A
General/ OBC: ₹1,200 per post (bank charges extra). SC/ST/EWS of Uttar Pradesh: ₹1,000 per post (bank charges extra). Separate fee is required for each post if applying to more than one.
Q8 What is the pay scale for these posts?
A
Pay scales (7th CPC): Review Officer — Level 8 (₹47,600–₹1,51,100); Assistant Review Officer — Level 7 (₹44,900–₹1,42,400); Computer Assistant — Level 4 (₹25,500–₹81,100).
Q9 What is the selection process for recruitment?
A
Selection is through a recruitment examination/written test conducted as per Allahabad High Court rules. Exam details, date and admit card information will be notified online; final selection is based on performance in the exam and rules of the Court.
Q10 How do I apply and what documents are required?
A
Apply online between 01–21 June 2026 at the official portal. Pay fee by 22 June 2026. Keep scanned copies of educational certificates, computer qualification, identity proof, recent photograph, and any category (SC/ST/OBC/EWS) certificates ready. If applying for multiple posts, submit separate applications and fees.
