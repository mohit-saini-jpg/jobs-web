Selection Process

1Written/OMR Based Main Examination (100 Questions, 100 Marks, 120 Minutes) – with Negative Marking (1/4)
2Candidates who did not appear in UPSSSC PET-2025 or have not been issued a PET-2025 Score Card by the Commission.
3Candidates who scored zero, negative, or were absent in PET-2025.
4Candidates who do not possess the mandatory essential educational qualification (B.Com + ‘O’ Level Computer Diploma) as on 03-08-2026.
5Candidates not domiciled in UP who wish to claim reservation benefits.
6OBC candidates belonging to the Creamy Layer.
7EWS candidates whose EWS certificate has not been issued between 01-04-2026 and 03-08-2026 or is not based on FY 2025-26 income.
8Candidates who provide false, incomplete, or unverifiable information in the online application form.
9Candidates who are unable to produce valid documents at the time of Document Verification.
10Male candidates with more than one living wife, or female candidates who have married a man who already has a living wife — they are not eligible unless exemption has been granted by the Governor.
11Not logging in through PET-2025 Registration Number and OTP — this is mandatory; there is no separate new registration for this Main Exam application.
12Not selecting (checking) the specific post/department you wish to apply for in the application form — unselected posts will not be considered.
13Not giving the preference order of departments/posts in the application — UPSSSC will attempt to allot based on merit and preferences given.
14Not ensuring that your EWS certificate is valid as on the cut-off date and is on the correct prescribed format (UP Government format, not a general income certificate).
15Not completing fee reconciliation by 10-08-2026 even if application is submitted before 03-08-2026 — application will not be finally submitted without fee reconciliation.
16Not downloading and printing the application form after successful fee reconciliation — it is mandatory to keep a printout for Document Verification.
17Waiting until the last date to apply — UPSSSC advises candidates to apply well before the closing date to avoid server overload.
18Not checking that the PwBD disability type and sub-category are correctly mentioned in the application form as per the latest UP Government orders.

How to Apply

  1. 1PET-2025 Registration Number for login.
  2. 2Photograph and signature (as uploaded during PET-2025; cannot be changed — click “Continue” after viewing).
  3. 3Details of educational qualification (Board/Institution name, year of passing, roll number, certificate date, percentage/marks).
  4. 4Preference order of departments/posts being applied for.
  5. 5Category/reservation certificate details (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/PwBD/DFF/ESM) as applicable.
  6. 6Domicile/permanent address details (auto-populated from PET-2025 data).
  7. 7EWS certificate (issued between 01-04-2026 and 03-08-2026 in prescribed format), if applicable.
  8. 8Online applications are open from 14-07-2026 to 03-08-2026. Fee reconciliation and application amendment window closes on 10-08-2026.
  9. 9Only one application is to be submitted; if a candidate submits more than one application for the same post, only the last submitted application will be considered.
  10. 10The application form can be amended only for permitted fields (correspondence address, personal details like EWS/PwBD/DFF category, gender, outstanding sportsperson, marital status, mandatory/desirable qualification) up to 10-08-2026.
  11. 11After fee payment and reconciliation, the application is automatically and finally submitted — candidates should download and print it immediately.
  12. 12Candidates must indicate category/subcategory of PwBD disability in the application and update it as per the latest UP Government orders on Divyangjan empowerment.
  13. 13All information in the application is final — no corrections to date of birth, educational qualification percentage/marks, or caste will be entertained after submission.
  14. 14For candidates appearing in examinations at an allotted centre, change of centre will not be entertained under any circumstances.
  15. 15Canvassing in any form will lead to cancellation of candidature and debarment from all UPSSSC examinations.
  16. 16The Commission does not correspond personally with any candidate. All updates are published on upsssc.gov.in — candidates must regularly check the website.
  17. 17The UP Public Examinations (Prevention of Unfair Means) Act, 2024 provisions will be strictly applicable during the examination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 Is PET-2025 mandatory to apply for UPSSSC Auditor & Assistant Accountant Main Exam 2026?
A
Yes, it is absolutely mandatory. Only candidates who appeared in UPSSSC PET-2025 and have been issued a PET-2025 Score Card by the Commission are eligible to apply for this Main Examination. Candidates who did not appear in PET-2025 or scored zero/negative cannot apply.
Q2 What is the total number of vacancies and which posts are being filled?
A
A total of 1829 vacancies are being filled for the posts of Auditor (Lekha Pareekshak) and Assistant Accountant (Sahayak Lekhakaar) across 22 departments of the Government of Uttar Pradesh under UPSSSC Advertisement No. 14-Exam/2026.
Q3 What is the essential educational qualification for these posts?
A
For most posts, candidates must possess a Bachelor’s Degree in Commerce (B.Com) from a recognized University AND an ‘O’ Level Computer Diploma from a Government-recognized institution. For the Auditor post under Local Fund Accounts Examination, a Post Graduate Diploma in Accountancy is also acceptable. Exact qualifications vary by department as per the relevant service rules — candidates must check the notification carefully.
Q4 What is the last date to apply and what happens after 03-08-2026?
A
The last date to submit the online application form and pay the fee is 03-08-2026. After this date, no new applications or fee payments will be accepted. However, candidates who have already applied can still reconcile the fee and amend permitted fields in their application up to 10-08-2026. After 10-08-2026, no amendment or correction in the application form will be possible.
Q5 How will candidates be shortlisted for the Main Written Examination?
A
Shortlisting for the Main Examination will be done on the basis of the normalized score obtained by candidates in PET-2025. Candidates equal to 15 times the number of category-wise vacancies will be shortlisted, including all candidates who have scored up to the final cut-off normalized score (calculated up to two decimal places).
Q6 What is the exam pattern for the Main Examination?
A
The Main Examination is a single-shift Objective Type MCQ paper with 100 questions carrying 100 marks in 120 minutes (2 hours). It has three parts: Part 1 (65 marks) covers Accounting subjects including Audit, Double Entry, RTGS, e-Banking, Advance Accountancy, and Taxation; Part 2 (15 marks) covers Computer & IT concepts; Part 3 (20 marks) covers General Knowledge of Uttar Pradesh. There is negative marking of 1/4 (25%) for each wrong answer.
Q7 What is the application fee for this recruitment?
A
The application fee is Rs. 25/- (online processing charge only) for all categories — Unreserved, OBC, SC, and ST. There is no separate application fee. The Main Examination fee will be charged separately from shortlisted candidates before the Admit Card for the Main Exam is issued.
Q8 Can candidates choose which department/post to apply for?
A
Yes. Candidates must select their preferred department/post at the time of filling the online application. If a department or post is not selected/checked by the candidate, no claim for that post will be entertained. Candidates must also indicate their preference order of departments. UPSSSC will attempt to allot the post based on merit and candidate preference, but the final allotment decision rests with the Commission.
Q9 What are the EWS certificate requirements for this exam?
A
The EWS (Economically Weaker Section) certificate must have been issued between 01-04-2026 and 03-08-2026, must be based on FY 2025-26 income (annual family income below Rs. 8 lakhs from all sources), and must be valid for FY 2026-27. It must be on the prescribed format issued as per UP Government Office Memo No. 3/2019/4/1/2002/Ka-2/19TC.Si.-II, dated 14-03-2019. A general income certificate will not be accepted.
Q10 How will the login be done for the online application — is there a fresh registration?
A
No fresh registration is required. Candidates must log in using their PET-2025 Registration Number along with the OTP sent to the mobile number/email registered at the time of PET-2025. All personal details (name, date of birth, domicile, category, EWS/horizontal reservation details, photo, and signature) will be auto-populated from PET-2025 data and cannot be changed, except for the permitted fields mentioned in the notification.
