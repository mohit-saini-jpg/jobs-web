Selection Process

1Shortlisting based on qualifications and experience + Interview (Board of Directors reserves right to relax conditions)
2Step 1 – Application Screening: Applications are screened based on post-wise eligibility criteria (qualification, experience, age).
3Step 2 – Shortlisting: Candidates with higher qualifications than stipulated will be given preference. Computer literacy is mandatory for all posts.
4Step 3 – Interview: Shortlisted candidates will be called for an interview at the Head Office or a designated location. Date and venue will be communicated by the bank.
5Step 4 – Document Verification: Original documents will be verified at the time of interview or joining.
6Candidates who do not meet the minimum experience requirements for the post applied for - the bank explicitly requires verified years of relevant banking experience for all posts.
7Fresh graduates with no work experience - all posts require a minimum of 1 year (Graphic Designer) to 15 years (Chief Law Officer) of relevant experience.
8Candidates above the age limit specified for their post, unless the Board decides to grant relaxation.
9Candidates applying for legal posts (DGM Chief Law Officer / Branch Manager Legal Officer) who do not hold LLB/LLM qualifications - other qualifications are not acceptable for these posts.
10Candidates who are not computer literate - computer literacy is a mandatory requirement for all posts without exception.
11Candidates unwilling or unable to be posted at any of the bank's locations across Pune, Belgaum, Thane, or Chh. Sambhaji Nagar.
12Candidates who cannot submit a physical application by post to the Head Office within 10 days of the advertisement date - no online application mode is available.
13Candidates applying for Branch Manager (Board Secretary) who are not proficient in both English and Marathi correspondence.
14Candidates for Steno/Typist post who cannot type at 40 w.p.m. in English - this is a minimum threshold, not a preferred standard.
15Not mentioning the post applied for clearly on the application envelope - the notification explicitly states the post must be written on the envelope; unlabelled envelopes may be misdirected or rejected.
16Downloading and using an incorrect or outdated application form - the form must be obtained from the bank's official website www.punepeoples.bank.in only.
17Missing the 10-day deadline - with such a short window, candidates must act immediately after seeing the advertisement. Postal delays must be accounted for.
18Not attaching all necessary supporting documents (qualification certificates, experience certificates, age proof) with the application - incomplete applications may be rejected outright.
19Applying for a post with experience from a private sector company that is not a Co-operative or Nationalized Bank for senior posts - most senior roles specifically require banking experience in Co-operative or Nationalized Banks.
20Claiming CAIIB qualification without valid certification - CAIIB is a key differentiator for senior posts and will be verified.
21Applying for multiple posts in the same envelope - each post application should be treated separately with a clearly labelled envelope.
22Not verifying the communication address before sending - the application must be sent to: 477/478, 1st Floor, Market Yard, Gultekadi, Pune – 411037.
23Candidates for Branch Manager (Legal Officer) applying based only on academic law qualifications without actual practicing/banking experience - 10 years of practical legal experience is mandatory.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 What is the last date to apply for Pune People's Co-Op Bank Recruitment 2026?
A
The last date is within 10 days from the date of advertisement, i.e., on or before 19-06-2026. Applications must reach the Head Office in Pune by post within this period. Candidates are advised to dispatch their applications well in advance to avoid postal delays.
Q2 What is the salary/pay for these posts?
A
The notification does not mention specific salary details. Candidates are advised to contact the bank directly at 7066051999 / 7066050999 or visit www.punepeoples.bank.in for salary-related information. Pay is likely as per industry norms for Co-operative Banks and will be discussed at the time of interview.
Q3 Is experience in a private bank acceptable for senior management posts?
A
For posts like General Manager, Deputy General Manager, and Assistant General Manager, the notification specifically requires experience in a Co-operative or Nationalized Bank. Experience in purely private sector banks may not fulfill this criterion. For Branch Manager (Legal Officer, Estate Manager, Board Secretary), the experience requirement is more flexible and domain-specific.
Q4 Can I apply for more than one post?
A
The notification does not explicitly prohibit applying for more than one post. However, each application must be sent in a separate envelope with the post name clearly written on the envelope. Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria for each post you apply for.
Q5 Is CAIIB qualification mandatory or preferred?
A
CAIIB (Certified Associate of Indian Institute of Bankers) is listed as a qualification alongside MBA for senior posts (General Manager, DGM, AGM, Branch Manager). It is not explicitly stated as mandatory vs. preferred — candidates with MBA or CAIIB are both considered. However, having CAIIB will strengthen your application for banking-specific roles.
Q6 What is the selection process - is there a written test?
A
The notification does not mention a written test. Selection is based on shortlisting of applications on merit (qualifications and experience) followed by an Interview. The Board of Directors reserves the right to relax conditions for deserving candidates.
