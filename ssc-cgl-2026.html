Eligibility Criteria

Applicants must have a bachelor’s degree from a recognized university. Final year students can apply provisionally. For certain posts (like Assistant Audit Officer), specific subject requirements may apply.

Selection Process

  1. Tier‑I (Prelims): Computer-based test with 4 sections (General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, English Comprehension). 100 questions, 200 marks, 60 minutes.
  2. Tier‑II (Mains): Computer-based tests. Paper I (Quantitative Ability) and Paper II (English Language & Comprehension) are compulsory. Paper III (Statistics) for posts under Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation. Paper IV (General Studies – Finance & Economics) for AAO posts.
  3. Tier‑III: Descriptive paper (Pen & Paper) – Essay/Letter/Application writing in English/Hindi.
  4. Tier‑IV: Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test (wherever applicable).

Application Fee

₹100 for General/OBC candidates. Women, SC/ST, PwD and Ex-Servicemen are exempted from fee.

FAQs

Is there negative marking in SSC CGL?

Yes, there is a negative marking of 0.5 marks for each wrong answer in Tier‑I and Tier‑II exams.

Can final year students apply?

Yes, if they meet the educational qualification by the cut-off date specified in the notification.

What is the salary for SSC CGL posts?

Salary varies by post, level and department. Pay scale ranges from Level‑4 to Level‑8 of 7th CPC. For example, Assistant Audit Officer (Level‑8) has starting basic pay of around ₹47,600.
