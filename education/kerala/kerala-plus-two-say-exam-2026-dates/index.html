Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026 Dates

The Directorate of Higher Secondary Education (DHSE), Kerala has announced the Kerala Plus Two SAY (Save A Year) Exam 2026 dates for students who wish to clear failed subjects or improve their marks. The exams will be conducted in offline, pen-and-paper mode across designated exam centres in the state.

The SAY exams are scheduled between June 29 and July 3, 2026, giving students a quick second chance soon after the declaration of the regular Plus Two results. This helps candidates avoid losing an academic year and move ahead with higher studies.

Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam Date 2026 and Schedule

DHSE Kerala has fixed the Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026 dates from June 29 to July 3, 2026. These dates apply to both SAY (supplementary) and improvement candidates.

The detailed subject-wise timetable, including the exact dates for each paper (languages, Science, Commerce and Humanities subjects), will be issued as a separate notification. Typically, the board conducts exams in two sessions:

  • Forenoon session: around 9:30 am to 12:15 pm
  • Afternoon session: around 2:00 pm to 4:45 pm

Students should refer to the official timetable PDF once released and carefully note their subject dates and sessions.

What Is Kerala Plus Two SAY (Save A Year) Exam?

The Kerala Plus Two SAY (Save A Year) exam is a special opportunity for students who could not pass one or more subjects in the regular March Plus Two examination. Instead of waiting a full year, they can appear in the SAY exam soon after results.

The exam also serves improvement candidates who have already passed but want to enhance their scores in one subject. This dual purpose makes SAY an important second-chance and score-improvement mechanism under DHSE Kerala.

Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026 Eligibility Criteria

SAY (supplementary) candidates

Students are generally eligible for the Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026 if:

  • They appeared in the regular Plus Two (Class 12) board examination in March 2026, and
  • They failed to secure the minimum required grade (often D+ or equivalent pass mark) in one or more subjects.

Only those subjects in which the student has not qualified can be chosen for SAY. The exact number of subjects allowed and detailed conditions are clarified in the official DHSE notification.

Improvement candidates

Improvement candidates are students who have:

  • Qualified for higher studies in the March 2026 Plus Two exams, but
  • Wish to improve their marks in any one permitted subject.

Improvement candidates usually appear again in that specific subject in the SAY session, and as per board rules, the better of the two marks is carried forward. Students should verify the improvement rules, including any restrictions, from the official circular.

Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026 Application Process

Application dates and fees

DHSE Kerala generally opens the application window shortly after declaring the regular Plus Two results and publishing the SAY notification. During this window, eligible students can apply for:

  • SAY (supplementary) exams
  • Improvement exams
  • In many cases, related processes such as photocopy or scrutiny of answer scripts

The exact application dates, fee per subject, additional charges and any late fee will be mentioned in the official notification. Students must strictly adhere to these dates to avoid rejection of their applications.

How to apply for Kerala +2 SAY exam through school

Kerala Plus Two SAY exam applications are normally processed through the respective schools. A typical step-by-step process that you can include is:

  1. Contact your school office or class teacher after the SAY notification is published to know the application dates and instructions.
  2. Confirm the subjects you want to apply for - failed subjects for SAY or one subject for improvement.
  3. Fill in the SAY/improvement application form with correct personal details, register number and subject codes as per your March 2026 hall ticket.
  4. Pay the prescribed examination fee per subject and any additional fees specified by DHSE Kerala.
  5. Submit the filled form and fee proof to the school within the deadline; the school will forward applications to the board.
  6. Keep a photocopy or screenshot of the application and payment details for your records.

Kerala Plus Two Result 2026 and Revaluation Options

The regular Kerala Plus Two result 2026 is declared before the SAY notification so that students know whether they have qualified or need to reappear. After results, DHSE usually opens multiple options:

  • SAY exam for those who failed in one or more subjects
  • Improvement exam for those who passed but want better marks
  • Revaluation, photocopy and scrutiny (as per subjects and rules mentioned in the notification)

Students who are only a few marks short of the pass mark in a subject may consider revaluation or scrutiny, while those with significantly low scores often prefer appearing directly in the SAY exam. Schools generally guide students on what is more appropriate in each case.

Kerala Plus Two SAY Exam 2026: Exam Pattern and Passing Rules

The Kerala Plus Two SAY exam follows the same syllabus and pattern as the regular March board examination. This means:

  • The type of questions (objective, very short, short answer, long answer) remains similar.
  • The overall marks and duration for each paper match the board’s standard pattern.
  • Internal assessment or practical components (where applicable) generally carry forward as per DHSE guidelines.

To pass in a subject in the SAY exam, students must secure the minimum prescribed marks or grade, typically equivalent to the regular board pass criteria. Once they qualify in all required subjects through SAY, they are treated as having passed Plus Two in the relevant academic year.

Important Instructions for Kerala +2 SAY Exam Day

Students should treat the SAY exam with the same seriousness as the main board exam. Some essential exam-day instructions you can include are:

  • Reach the exam centre well before the reporting time to avoid last-minute stress.
  • Carry the SAY admit card, school ID card and permitted stationery items.
  • Read all instructions printed on the question paper and answer booklet carefully.
  • Use the cool-off time to understand the questions and plan your answers.
  • Follow the invigilator’s instructions and maintain discipline in the examination hall.
  • Avoid any unfair practices, as they can lead to cancellation of the exam and further action.
