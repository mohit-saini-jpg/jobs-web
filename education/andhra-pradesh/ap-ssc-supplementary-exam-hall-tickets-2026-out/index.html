3How to Download AP SSC Supplementary Hall Tickets 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphFollow these steps to download your AP 10th Class supply hall ticket 2026:
2listordered
  • Visit the official BSEAP website: bse.ap.gov.in and open the SSC section.
  • Click on the “SSC Advanced Supplementary Examinations 2026 - Hall Tickets” link or the direct supplementary hall ticket link shown on the homepage.
  • On the hall ticket selection page, choose the appropriate category (e.g., regular/private/OMR school type) if asked.
  • Enter the required details - usually roll number / previous hall ticket number and date of birth, and select district/school as instructed.
  • Click on “Submit / Download Hall Ticket” to view your AP SSC Supplementary Exam Hall Ticket 2026 on the screen.
  • Verify all printed information and then download and print 2-3 clear A4‑size copies of the hall ticket.
3paragraphIf the hall ticket is not displayed, recheck your roll number and date of birth, ensure your application was accepted by the school/board, and contact the school or board officials if the problem continues.
4Details Mentioned on AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphAfter downloading the hall ticket, students should carefully verify the following details:
2listunordered
  • Student’s name and photograph
  • Roll number and, where applicable, admission number
  • Exam name: SSC Advanced Supplementary Examinations 2026
  • Class: 10th / SSC
  • Subject‑wise exam dates, subject names and paper codes
  • Exam centre name, centre code and full address
  • District and school details
  • Reporting time, exam timings and important instructions
3paragraphIf any discrepancy is found in the hall ticket (name spelling, photograph, subjects or exam centre), students must immediately inform their school headmaster/principal or the BSEAP office for guidance before the exams start.
5Documents Required with AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphOn each exam day, students must carry:
2listunordered
  • A printed copy of the AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026 (clear A4‑size printout - colour or good‑quality black and white).
  • A valid ID proof, such as school ID card or any other photo ID accepted by the board (if mentioned in the instructions).
  • Any additional document instructed by the school or BSEAP (for example, special permission letters, if applicable).
3paragraphStudents should reach the exam centre well before the reporting time mentioned on the hall ticket to complete verification and seating formalities. Late entry after the scheduled time may not be allowed.
6Exam‑Day Instructions for AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphTo ensure smooth conduct of the AP SSC Supplementary Examinations 2026, students should follow these guidelines:
2listunordered
  • Reach the exam centre well before the reporting time mentioned on your AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026 so that verification, seating and attendance can be completed; late entry after the scheduled time or gate‑closing time may not be allowed.
  • Carry only essential items: your AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026 (clear A4‑size printout), a valid photo ID if instructed (such as school ID card or government‑issued ID) and permitted stationery such as blue/black pens, pencils, eraser and other items allowed by the board.
  • Do not carry mobile phones, smart watches, calculators (if not permitted), Bluetooth devices, notes, books or any other electronic gadgets or written material inside the examination hall; such items are strictly prohibited and may lead to cancellation of your paper and further disciplinary action.
  • Follow all instructions printed on the AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026 and those announced by the invigilators during the exam; indulging in any unfair means, talking in the exam hall or disobeying exam rules can result in cancellation of the examination and other penalties.
  • Keep your AP SSC Supplementary Hall Ticket 2026 safely even after the exam, as it may be required later for result verification, revaluation processes or school/board documentation.
