4JEPBN 2026 Eligibility Criteria
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphEligibility conditions for JEPBN 2026 cover citizenship, domicile, academic qualification, age and work experience, especially for West Bengal State Government employees. Candidates must satisfy both eligibility for application and eligibility for admission as specified by the Nursing Directorate, Department of Health and Family Welfare, Government of West Bengal.
2paragraphJEPBN 2026 Citizenship and Domicile
3listunordered
  • Applicant must be a citizen of India.
  • Applicant must be a domicile of West Bengal, except certain employees of the Government of West Bengal who have separate provisions.
  • Detailed domicile criteria include continuous residence of at least ten years or parents being permanent residents of West Bengal, supported by prescribed certificates (Proforma A1 or B) or specified documents.
4paragraphJEPBN 2026 Educational Qualification
5listunordered
  • Candidate must have passed the 10+2 level examination from any recognised board listed on the WBJEEB website.
  • Candidate must have passed the General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) course from an institute recognised by the Indian Nursing Council.
  • Candidate must obtain registration or RNRM certificate from the State Nursing Council of any State of India.
  • “Pass” implies clearing both theory and practical (where applicable) and completing all course requirements including internship before admission.
6paragraphJEPBN 2026 Age Limit and Work Experience
7listunordered
  • For West Bengal State Government employees: upper age limit is 53 years as on the last date of application.
  • For others including ESI and Central Government employees: no upper age limit.
  • West Bengal State Government nursing personnel must have at least 3 years of uninterrupted, regularised experience in Government service as on the last date of application.
  • Work experience is not essential for other candidates.
5Online Application Guidelines
Sr.TypeStyleItemsText
1listunordered
  • Use only www.wbjeeb.nic.in or www.wbjeeb.in to access the JEPBN 2026 application form.
  • Keep a valid mobile number and unique email ID because all future communication will be sent to those contacts.
  • Registration details like name, parents’ names, domicile and date of birth cannot be changed later, so they must exactly match official documents.
  • Do not share application number and password with anyone to avoid misuse.
2paragraphStep-by-Step Application Process for JEPBN 2026
3listordered
  • Registration Enter personal details such as name, father’s name, mother’s name, date of birth, gender, identification details, address, mobile number and email ID and create a password. After submission, an application number is generated and sent via SMS or email.
  • Fill Application Form Provide additional information including domicile, category, PwD status, religion, nationality and academic details.
  • Upload Images Upload recent colour photograph (10 to 200 KB) and signature (4 to 30 KB) in JPG/JPEG format in one step. If there is any discrepancy in images as communicated by SMS or email, correct it within one day or admit card will not be issued.
  • Pay Examination Fee Pay the examination fee of ₹3000 plus applicable bank service charges through Net Banking, Debit Card, Credit Card, UPI or QR Code. Fee once paid is not refundable under any circumstances, and candidates are advised not to wait till the last day to pay.
  • Download Confirmation Page After successful payment and completion of steps, download and safely keep the confirmation page; the application remains incomplete until this page is generated.
  • Application Correction Mostly all data except primary registration data can be corrected only during the notified correction period via login; WBJEEB will not entertain corrections after that.
6JEPBN 2026 Exam Pattern, Syllabus and Scoring
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphKnowing the exam pattern and syllabus of JEPBN 2026 helps candidates prepare in a focused and strategic way.
2paragraphJEPBN 2026 Exam Pattern
3listunordered
  • Mode: OMR-based Common Entrance Test.
  • Language: English only.
  • Total questions: 100 multiple-choice questions.
  • Duration: 90 minutes.
  • Structure: Single paper with two parts (Part A and Part B).
4paragraphJEPBN 2026 Syllabus - Part A and Part B
5paragraphThe JEPBN 2026 paper is based on the GNM syllabus and divided into two parts.
6listunordered
  • Part A (40 questions) covers: Anatomy, Physiology, Sociology, Psychology, Pharmacology, Microbiology, Nutrition, Pathology.
  • Part B (60 questions) covers: Foundation of Nursing, Medical Surgical Nursing, Pediatric Nursing, Psychiatric Nursing, Obstetrical Nursing, Community Health Nursing.
7paragraphJEPBN 2026 Scoring and Negative Marking
8listunordered
  • Each correct response carries 1 mark.
  • Each incorrect response carries -0.25 mark (25 percent negative).
  • Marking more than one option, even if one is correct, is treated as incorrect and attracts -0.25 mark.
  • No response attracts zero marks.
7JEPBN 2026 Reservation, Seat Matrix and Domicile Rules
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphWBJEEB follows reservation policies as per the Department of Health and Family Welfare and West Bengal University of Health Sciences for reserved category seats.
2paragraphJEPBN 2026 Reservation Policy
3listunordered
  • Reservations are available for SC, ST, OBC-Category-A (More Backwards), OBC-Category-B (Backwards), PwD and EWS categories as per notified Government orders.
  • Reserved seats are only for candidates domiciled in West Bengal, and valid certificates from competent authorities are mandatory.
  • OBC certificates issued before a certain date must be revalidated with specific classification of Category A or B.
4paragraphSeat Matrix for JEPBN 2026
5listunordered
  • Seat matrix for session 2026-27 will be declared separately by Department of Health and Family Welfare and WBUHS and published on the Board’s website before counselling.
  • Some seats in certain institutes or courses may not be offered through e-counselling and will not appear in the seat matrix.
6paragraphDomicile Requirements
7listunordered
  • Domicile of West Bengal is mandatory for admission in any category of Post Basic Nursing seat, except specific Government employees with TR.
  • Domicile is established either by continuous residence of ten years or by parents’ permanent residence with specified supporting documents or proforma certificates.

9JEPBN 2026 Admit Card, Exam Centre and Result
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphJEPBN 2026 Admit Card
2listunordered
  • Admit cards will be generated on the notified date for candidates to download and print from the WBJEEB website.
  • Candidates must carry a hard copy of the admit card, an identical colour photograph and a valid photo ID to the exam centre.
  • Admit cards must not be mutilated, distorted or soiled; such documents may not be accepted at the centre.
3paragraphJEPBN 2026 Exam Centre
4listunordered
  • JEPBN 2026 examination will be conducted only in the Kolkata-Salt Lake/ New Town zone.
  • Candidates must confirm the exact location and commuting options in advance to avoid inconvenience on exam day.
5paragraphJEPBN 2026 Answer Keys, Result and Rank Card
6listunordered
  • Model answer keys and OMR images will be uploaded for a brief period after the exam, and candidates can view and challenge them by paying ₹500 per question plus bank charges.
  • WBJEEB will publish final frozen answer keys, evaluate OMRs and declare results as rank cards containing scores and ranks, which candidates can download using their password.
  • Not all candidates may get a rank; WBJEEB can fix cut-off scores or ranks for counselling eligibility.
