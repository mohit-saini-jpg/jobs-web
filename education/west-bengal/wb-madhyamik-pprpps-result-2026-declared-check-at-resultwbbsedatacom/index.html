2What Is WB Madhyamik PPR/PPS Result 2026?
Sr.TypeTextHeadersRowsStyleItems
1paragraphThis section explains the meaning of PPR and PPS and why students apply for these services after the main Madhyamik result is published.
2paragraphThe Madhyamik PPR/PPS process is WBBSE's official post-result review and scrutiny mechanism. It gives students who are dissatisfied with their marks in the Madhyamik Pariksha (Class 10 Board Exam) an opportunity to have their answer scripts re-examined or re-checked. The two services differ in scope:
3table
  • Process
  • Full Form
  • What It Means
    • PPR
    • Post Publication Review
    • Full re-evaluation of the answer booklet by a senior examiner
    • PPS
    • Post Publication Scrutiny
    • Re-totalling and arithmetic verification of marks (not re-evaluation)
4listunordered
  • PPR is for students who believe their written answers were not fairly evaluated and want a senior examiner to re-read and re-mark their responses.
  • PPS is for students who suspect an error in mark totalling, missing mark entries, or calculation mistakes — the content is not re-evaluated, only the arithmetic is verified.

7WB Madhyamik 2026 - Passing Criteria
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphStudents checking their PPR/PPS result should be aware of the minimum passing marks to understand whether their revised score helps them clear the examination.
2paragraphFor the WBBSE Madhyamik Result 2026, students must secure a minimum of 35% marks (35 out of 100) in each subject individually. The overall minimum total to pass is 272 out of 800 marks. First Division requires 480 or more marks out of 800. For subjects with practicals, both theory and practical components must be cleared separately.
8RTI / Certified Copy of Answer Script 2026
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphStudents who want to go a step further and physically inspect their evaluated answer booklets can apply under the RTI (Right to Information) Act for a certified copy.
2paragraphIf students wish to see their actual answer scripts through RTI or Certified Copy, the deadline is July 23, 2026. This process remains offline. Students must submit an application on plain paper along with a ₹10 fee and a photocopy of their marksheet to the respective board office.
