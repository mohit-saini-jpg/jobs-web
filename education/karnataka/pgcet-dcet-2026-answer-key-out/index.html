3PGCET 2026 Answer Key: Release Details and Key Highlights
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe Karnataka PGCET 2026 answer key has been released in provisional form, allowing candidates to check the official correct options for every question in their respective paper. KEA issues separate answer keys for various PGCET papers such as MBA, MCA and M.Tech/ME/M.Arch, ensuring that candidates can verify the exact question booklet they received in the exam.
2paragraphThe provisional PGCET answer key is an important transparency measure, giving candidates a chance to identify suspected errors and submit objections if they find any discrepancy. After the objection window closes, KEA reviews all challenges with supporting documents, makes necessary corrections and publishes the final PGCET 2026 answer key, which then forms the basis for score computation and ranking.
3paragraphKarnataka PGCET 2026 exam overview
4paragraphKarnataka PGCET is a pen-and-paper based entrance exam conducted for admissions to postgraduate programmes in management, computer applications and engineering/architecture in colleges across Karnataka. The exam is divided into different papers and sections according to the course, with each paper carrying its own pattern and marking scheme notified by KEA.
5paragraphType of PGCET 2026 answer key (provisional and final)
6paragraphThere are two main stages of the PGCET answer key-provisional and final. The provisional answer key is the first version released for public scrutiny, while the final answer key is issued after evaluating objections and is considered binding for result preparation.
7paragraphImportant dates for PGCET 2026 answer key and result
8paragraphKEA publishes the schedule for answer key release, objection window, final answer key and result announcement through its official PGCET notifications. Candidates must regularly visit cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea to stay updated on these dates and avoid missing any important deadline related to objections or result checking.
4DCET 2026 Answer Key: Release Details and Key Highlights
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe Karnataka DCET 2026 answer key has also been released in provisional mode for all sets of the Diploma Common Entrance Test. Through this answer key, diploma candidates can tally the options they marked in the exam with the official correct answers and roughly estimate their DCET score even before the results are announced.
2paragraphJust like PGCET, DCET 2026 answer key is first provided as a provisional key, and KEA accepts objections for a limited period. After examining all valid challenges with documentary proof, KEA publishes the final DCET 2026 answer key, which is then used to calculate marks and prepare the DCET rank list.
3paragraphKarnataka DCET 2026 exam overview
4paragraphKarnataka DCET is conducted for diploma holders and eligible B.Sc graduates seeking lateral entry into the second year of Bachelor of Engineering (BE) and Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) courses in engineering colleges across the state. The test is typically held offline, and KEA provides multiple versions of the question paper, each supported by a corresponding answer key.
5paragraphDCET 2026 provisional and final answer key
6paragraphIn the provisional DCET answer key, KEA lists the correct option for every question in each set, allowing candidates to quickly cross-check their answers. Once objections are resolved, KEA publishes the final DCET 2026 answer key; this version is not open to further challenge and directly influences the scoring process.
7paragraphImportant dates for DCET 2026 answer key and result
8paragraphThe detailed date-wise schedule for DCET answer key release, objections and results is indicated through official KEA DCET notifications and calendar. Candidates must keep a close watch on the DCET section of the KEA website to know the last date for raising objections and the expected result declaration timeline.
5How to Download PGCET 2026 Answer Key from cetonline.karnataka.gov.in
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphStep-by-step process to login and download
2paragraphCandidates can follow the steps below to download the Karnataka PGCET 2026 answer key from the official KEA portal:
3listordered
  • Open your browser and go to the KEA website at cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea.
  • On the homepage, locate the “PGCET 2026” link or a notice titled “PGCET 2026 Provisional Answer Key” or “Answer Key / Objections”.
  • Click on the relevant answer key/objection link; this may redirect you to the KEA Answer Key/Objection portal.
  • Log in, if required, using your PGCET application number, candidate name, date of birth and other details as specified in the instructions.
  • Choose the correct course (MBA, MCA, M.Tech/ME/M.Arch) and select your question paper version.
  • View the PGCET 2026 answer key on your screen and download the PDF version for offline reference.
  • Save or print the answer key so you can carefully verify each response and calculate your estimated PGCET score.
4paragraphDetails mentioned in the Karnataka PGCET answer key 2026
5paragraphThe PGCET answer key generally includes the question number, four options and the correct option for every question in the paper. The document may also indicate the question paper code or version, helping candidates ensure they are checking the right set.

7How to Calculate Your PGCET, DCET 2026 Score Using Answer Key
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphMarking scheme for Karnataka PGCET 2026
2paragraphIn recent years, Karnataka PGCET has followed a pattern where each correct answer carries one mark, and there is typically no negative marking for incorrect responses. Candidates should confirm the exact marking scheme from the current year’s PGCET information brochure but can generally estimate scores quickly by counting correct answers.
3paragraphMarking scheme for Karnataka DCET 2026
4paragraphFor DCET, KEA generally allots a fixed number of marks for each correct answer and usually does not apply negative marking in many past cycles. However, candidates must check the DCET 2026 brochure or official notification for the precise marking pattern applicable this year.
5paragraphExample of calculating expected score
6paragraphSuppose a PGCET candidate attempts 120 questions and finds 90 answers correct and 30 incorrect based on the answer key; with 1 mark per correct response and no negative marking, the estimated score would be 90 marks. Similarly, a DCET candidate can compute their tentative score by multiplying the number of correct responses by the marks per question as specified by KEA.
8How to Raise Objections to PGCET, DCET 2026 Answer Key
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphKEA answer key objection link and login details
2paragraphKEA provides an online objection facility where candidates can challenge any question or answer they believe is incorrect in the provisional key. The link to this objection portal is usually provided along with the answer key notice on the KEA website and requires candidates to log in using their exam credentials, such as application number and date of birth.
3paragraphDocuments required to challenge answer key
4paragraphWhile submitting objections, candidates typically need to provide:
5listunordered
  • Exam name (PGCET or DCET), subject and question number
  • Question paper version code or set number
  • The answer they think is correct, with a clear explanation
  • Supporting reference such as a textbook, syllabus document or authoritative source.
6paragraphIncomplete challenges or objections without proper supporting material may be rejected by KEA during the scrutiny process.
7paragraphRules and guidelines for PGCET, DCET answer key objections
8paragraphObjections to PGCET and DCET answer keys can be submitted only within the officially specified dates; any complaint raised after the deadline is generally not entertained. Once KEA completes the evaluation of all valid objections, the final answer keys are uploaded, and no further changes or objections are permitted to those keys.
9PGCET, DCET 2026 Eligibility and Next Steps After Answer Key
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphBasic eligibility for Karnataka PGCET 2026 (MBA, MCA, M.Tech)
2paragraphThe basic eligibility criteria for PGCET differ for MBA, MCA and M.Tech/ME/M.Arch, but in general, candidates must hold a relevant bachelor’s degree from a recognized university with the minimum aggregate marks prescribed by KEA. For example, MBA applicants typically need a bachelor’s degree in any discipline, while M.Tech/ME/M.Arch aspirants must possess a BE/B.Tech or equivalent degree in a related branch as per course requirements.
3paragraphBasic eligibility for Karnataka DCET 2026 (lateral entry)
4paragraphKarnataka DCET is aimed at diploma holders and eligible B.Sc graduates who wish to join BE/B.Tech programmes through lateral entry into the second year. Candidates must have a diploma in engineering or an equivalent qualification from a recognized board, with a minimum aggregate percentage specified by KEA in the official information brochure.
5paragraphWhat happens after final answer key and result (rank, counselling)
6paragraphAfter publishing the final PGCET and DCET answer keys, KEA releases the results and rank lists for all candidates who appeared for the exams. Based on these ranks, category reservations and available seat matrix, candidates are invited to participate in KEA’s online counselling process, where they can register, fill options, lock choices and receive seat allotments for their preferred courses and colleges.
