3Courses Offered in NCERT RIE 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphNCERT is offering multiple teacher education programmes through RIE admissions 2026 for different qualification levels. The courses available are designed to prepare quality teachers for secondary and higher secondary schools.
2listunordered
  • Integrated M.Sc.Ed. (6-Year Programme) - For students who completed 12th with PCM and want to become higher secondary teachers in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
  • Bachelor of Education - B.Ed. (2-Year Programme) - For graduates wanting to teach at secondary level in Science & Mathematics or Language & Social Science
  • Integrated B.Ed.-M.Ed. (3-Year Programme) - A combined degree program for B.Ed. holders
  • Master of Education - M.Ed. (2-Year Programme) - Primarily designed for preparing teacher educators and education professionals
  • Master of Arts in Education Technology (2-Year Programme) - Specialized course in educational technology applications
3paragraphThese courses are available at various RIE campuses located in Ajmer, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Mysore, and Shillong.
4Eligibility Criteria for RIE CEE 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe eligibility requirements vary depending on which course you're applying for in NCERT RIE admission 2026:
2paragraphFor Integrated M.Sc.Ed. (6-Year):
3listunordered
  • 12th pass in Science with PCM stream
  • Minimum 50% marks in aggregate (45% for SC/ST/PwD candidates)
4paragraphFor B.Ed. (2-Year):
5listunordered
  • Bachelor's degree in Science/Arts/Commerce/Social Science/Humanities
  • At least 50% marks in graduation or post-graduation (45% for reserved categories)
6paragraphFor Integrated B.Ed.-M.Ed. (3-Year):
7listunordered
  • B.Ed. degree with minimum 50% marks
  • Relaxation of 5% for SC/ST/PwD candidates
8paragraphFor M.Ed. (2-Year):
9listunordered
  • B.Ed./B.A.B.Ed./B.Sc.Ed./B.El.Ed. or equivalent teaching degree
  • Minimum 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD)
10paragraphFor M.A. in Education Technology:
11listunordered
  • Bachelor's degree with relevant background
  • Minimum qualifying percentage as per NCERT norms
12paragraphCandidates appearing in their qualifying examination can also apply, but they must upload their final marks by the specified deadline.
5How to Apply for NCERT RIE Admission 2026
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe application process for RIE CEE 2026 is completely online and pretty straightforward. Follow these steps to complete your registration:
2listordered
  • Visit the official CEE portal at cee.ncert.gov.in
  • Click on the "NCERT RIE Admission 2026" or "Online Application" link (will be active from May 15)
  • Register yourself by providing basic details like name, email ID, and mobile number
  • Fill the application form with personal, educational, and other required information
  • Upload scanned copies of photograph, signature, and necessary documents
  • Select your preferred course and RIE centre
  • Pay the application fee through online mode (credit card/debit card/net banking/UPI)
  • Submit the form and take a printout for future reference
3paragraphMake sure all the details you enter are accurate because mistakes can lead to rejection of application. Keep your valid email ID and phone number handy as all communication will be sent through these.
6RIE CEE 2026 Exam Pattern
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe Common Entrance Examination for RIE admission 2026 follows a specific pattern that candidates should understand before appearing:
2listunordered
  • Exam Mode: Offline (pen and paper based)
  • Exam Date: June 28, 2026 (Sunday)
  • Duration: Specific timing will be mentioned on admit card
  • Question Type: Multiple choice questions (MCQs)
  • Subjects Covered: General Knowledge, Teaching Aptitude, Reasoning, English Language, and subject-specific questions depending on course
3paragraphThe syllabus includes topics from graduation level for most courses, while integrated programmes may have questions based on 12th standard subjects. Detailed syllabus and exam pattern will be available in the information brochure on the official website.

Candidates can raise objections against the answer key within the specified time frame, and NCERT will consider valid objections before declaring final results. Students are advised to keep checking the official website regularly for updates regarding answer key, results, and counselling schedule.
