Details

The TCS NQT eligibility criteria 2026 serve as the key requirements for candidates aiming to qualify through the Tata Consultancy Services National Qualifier Test (TCS NQT).

This test helps freshers and early-career professionals secure opportunities in IT and related roles at TCS and other participating companies.

TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test) is a widely recognized assessment by Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) that evaluates cognitive, technical, and domain skills to screen candidates for entry-level positions.

Educational Qualification
  • Candidates must be pursuing or have completed a full-time UG, PG, or Diploma program from a recognized AICTE/UGC-approved university or institute.
Eligible Degrees / Streams
  • Engineering streams (e.g., CSE, IT, ECE, Mechanical, Civil, etc.) via B.E./B.Tech/M.E./M.Tech.
  • Other programs like MCA, M.Sc (especially in CS/IT-related fields), and in some cases non-engineering degrees (B.Sc, BCA, etc.) for broader TCS iON NQT applicability.
  • All branches are generally eligible, but confirm per the specific hiring drive.
Minimum Percentage / CGPA Requirement
  • Candidates need a minimum of 60% aggregate or equivalent 6 CGPA across all academic levels: Class 10, Class 12, Diploma (if applicable), UG, and PG. This applies without rounding off, and performance must be consistent.
Year of Passing Eligibility
  • Pre-final and final year students.
  • Passed-out candidates from batches like 2024, 2025, and 2026 (and sometimes earlier, up to 2020–2026 in certain contexts).
  • For TCS-specific hiring, focus is often on 2024–2026 batches.
Age Limit
  • Candidates should generally be between 18 and 28 years old at the time of application. Always verify the exact range in the specific notification, as minor variations may apply.
Backlogs / Arrears Rules
  • A maximum of one active backlog is typically allowed at the time of appearing for the TCS NQT or selection process.
  • All backlogs must be cleared before joining TCS (if an offer is extended), or the offer may be withdrawn. Some drives specify no pending backlogs at selection.
Gap Criteria
  • The total academic gap across all education stages should not exceed two years (up to 24 months).
  • Any gaps must be justified with valid documentation (e.g., medical or other reasons). Extended education beyond standard duration is usually not permitted.
Eligible Batches (Freshers / Experienced)
  • Primarily targets pre-final/final year students (e.g., 2026 pass-outs) and recent graduates.
  • Professionals with up to 2 years of experience are eligible in many cycles.
Who Should Apply for TCS NQT

Engineering and non-engineering students/graduates targeting entry-level roles in IT services, consulting, digital, BFSI, and more at TCS or companies accepting NQT scores. It's ideal for freshers and those with minimal experience.

TCS NQT Selection Process

The process involves:

  • Online registration and TCS NQT (aptitude + domain tests).
  • Shortlisting based on scores for interviews (TCS or other companies).
  • TCS iON NQT scorecards remain valid for about two years, allowing applications to multiple opportunities.

Documents Required for TCS NQT Registration
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
  • Valid government-issued ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, etc.).
  • Academic marksheets/certificates (10th, 12th, Diploma/UG/PG).
  • Gap certificates and backlog status proof (if applicable).
Step-by-Step Process to Apply for TCS NQT
  • Visit the official TCS iON NQT page or the current notification page and select Apply Online.
  • Create/log in to your TCS iON profile, and enter personal, academic, and experience details accurately.
  • Select the NQT variant, exam date, and mode (remote or center-based).
  • Upload documents, review details, and submit; pay any applicable fee.
  • Note registration number, monitor emails/SMS, and download the admit card when available.
Important Instructions for Candidates
  • Verify you fulfill all TCS NQT eligibility criteria before registering; inconsistencies can lead to rejection later.
  • Ensure name, date of birth, and other details match across application, ID, and academics.
