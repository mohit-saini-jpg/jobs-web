6How to Download OJEE 2026 Admit Card
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphOnce admit cards are released, candidates will need to download them from the official website. A clear step-by-step guide helps users and also aligns well with AI Overviews.
2listordered
  • Visit the official OJEE website: ojee.nic.in (or odishajee.com).
  • On the homepage, click on the link titled “OJEE 2026 Admit Card” or similar.
  • You will be redirected to the candidate login page.
  • Enter your application number and password/date of birth.
  • Enter the security pin (captcha) as shown and click on “Login” or “Submit”.
  • Your OJEE 2026 admit card will be displayed on the screen.
  • Check your name, course, exam date, time, and exam centre address carefully.
  • Download the admit card in PDF format and take a clear printout.
  • Preserve multiple copies of the printed admit card till the end of the admission process.
3paragraphCandidates must carry the printed admit card plus a valid photo ID to the exam centre; otherwise, they may not be allowed to appear for the exam.
7OJEE 2026 Exam Pattern (Brief)
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphWhile detailed pattern varies course-wise, a concise overview is useful for aspirants landing on a date-focused query.
2listunordered
  • OJEE is generally conducted in computer-based test (CBT) mode for most courses.
  • The question paper consists mainly of objective-type multiple-choice questions (MCQs).
  • The number of questions, total marks, and duration differ depending on the course (for example, B.Pharm vs MBA vs MCA vs M.Tech).
  • Each correct answer usually carries a fixed number of marks, and there may be negative marking in some papers as per OJEE rules.
  • The syllabus for each paper is aligned with the relevant qualifying examination level - such as 10+2 for B.Pharm and B.Sc Nursing, and graduation-level subjects for MBA, MCA, M.Tech and M.Arch.
3paragraphCandidates should always refer to the official OJEE 2026 brochure for precise course-wise exam pattern, marking scheme, and detailed syllabus.
8OJEE 2026 Eligibility Criteria (Short Recap)
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphEligibility differs for each course; a high-level summary helps users decide quickly if they should apply.
2listunordered
  • B.Pharm and B.Sc Nursing: Candidates are generally required to have passed or be appearing in 10+2 or equivalent examination with relevant subjects (such as Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology) and minimum aggregate marks as prescribed by OJEE.
  • Lateral Entry to B.Tech/B.Pharm: Applicants usually need a diploma or equivalent qualification in the appropriate branch with a minimum percentage specified in the brochure.
  • MBA: Candidates should hold a bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognized university with at least the minimum qualifying marks mentioned by OJEE.
  • MCA: A bachelor’s degree with Mathematics at 10+2 or graduation level is typically required, along with minimum aggregate marks.
  • M.Tech/M.Arch: A relevant BE/B.Tech/B.Arch or equivalent degree with the required marks is usually mandatory.
3paragraphApplicants must carefully check the official OJEE 2026 notification for detailed, course-specific eligibility, reservation policies, age criteria (if any), and document requirements.

10OJEE 2026 - Important Instructions for Candidates
Sr.TypeStyleItems
1listunordered
  • Note that OJEE 2026 will be conducted on multiple days (4, 5, 7, 8, and 9 May). Your specific exam date and shift depend on your chosen course and will be printed on your admit card.
  • Complete your application form carefully and within the stipulated deadline; incomplete or late applications are usually rejected.

  • Visit your exam centre location in advance if possible, especially if it is in an unfamiliar area, to avoid last-minute confusion.
  • Reach the exam centre well before reporting time, carrying your printed admit card, valid photo ID, and any other documents/instructions mentioned on the admit card.
  • Follow all exam-day rules strictly, including guidelines on permitted and prohibited items, to avoid any disciplinary issues.
