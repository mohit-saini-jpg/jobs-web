Detailed Information

The Tripura Board of Secondary Education (TBSE) conducts the Madhyamik (Class 10) and Higher Secondary (Class 12) board examinations every year for students across Tripura. For both classes, the TBSE admit card is a mandatory document that allows students to enter the exam centre and appear for their papers. TBSE issues the admit cards in offline mode, and students must collect them from their respective schools before the examination.

Download TBSE Admit Card 2026 (Direct Link Section)

The TBSE admit card 2026 for both Madhyamik and Higher Secondary is made available on the official website, but only school representatives can log in and download them. Centre secretaries or school superintendents collect the admit cards from the board office or download them through the school login, then issue them to all registered students.

  • Official TBSE portal for admit card related notices: https://tbse.tripura.gov.in​
  • Students must visit their school to receive the printed admit card and verify all details carefully.

Official Website & Notification Section

All official notifications about TBSE Class 10 and Class 12 admit cards, exam dates, and guidelines are published on the Tripura Board of Secondary Education website. Updates are usually posted in the “Announcement” or “What’s New” section, where schools can access circulars and download links.

  • Official TBSE website: tbse.tripura.gov.in​

How to Download TBSE Class 10 & 12 Admit Card 2026 (School Login Steps)

Since regular students cannot download the admit card online, these steps are for school authorities.

  • Visit the official website of Tripura Board of Secondary Education at tbse.tripura.gov.in.
  • Scroll to the “What’s New” or “Announcement” section on the homepage.
  • Click on the activated link for “TBSE Madhyamik/HS Admit Card 2026”.
  • Log in using the school/centre credentials provided by the board.
  • Select the exam (Madhyamik or HS), class, and download admit cards for all registered students.
  • Print the admit cards, sign and stamp them if required, and distribute them to students before the exam dates.

For Students (Collection Process)

  • Visit your school on the date informed by the school administration.
  • Collect the original TBSE admit card 2026, check all details, and keep it safe till result declaration.

Details Mentioned on TBSE Admit Card 2026

The TBSE Madhyamik and HS admit cards carry important personal and exam-related details.

  • Student name
  • Roll number and registration number
  • Class (Madhyamik/HS) and stream (for Class 12)
  • School name and code
  • Exam centre name, code, and address
  • List of subjects and subject codes
  • Exam dates and timings (as per final routine)​
  • Candidate photograph and, where applicable, signature
  • Important exam day instructions and guidelines

Students must cross-check all entries and contact the school immediately if any mistake is found.

TBSE Exam Day Guidelines 2026

TBSE clearly mentions exam-day rules on the admit card, and students must follow them to avoid disqualification.

Mandatory to Carry

  • Original TBSE admit card 2026 (Class 10 or Class 12)
  • Valid school ID card or any identity proof, if asked by the centre​
  • Required stationery items such as pens, pencils, eraser, geometry box, as allowed.

Reporting and Conduct

  • Reach the exam centre at least 30 minutes to 1 hour before the reporting time mentioned on the admit card.
  • Sit in the allotted seat as per roll number and follow invigilator instructions throughout the exam.

Allowed Items

  • Transparent water bottle, basic stationery, and necessary medical items (if permitted by centre).

Prohibited Items

  • Mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators (unless specifically allowed), electronic gadgets, or any communication devices.
  • Any paper, chits, books or study material inside the exam hall.

What To Do If Admit Card Has Errors?

If any error is found in the TBSE Class 10 or Class 12 admit card, students should act quickly and use official channels only.

  • Immediately inform your class teacher or examination in-charge at school and show the incorrect admit card.
  • The school will contact TBSE/board office with supporting documents to correct the details as per school records and registration forms.
  • Students may be asked to submit a written application along with copies of previous school records (e.g., registration card, school ID).​
  • Collect the corrected admit card before the exam and ensure the previous incorrect copy is not used.
