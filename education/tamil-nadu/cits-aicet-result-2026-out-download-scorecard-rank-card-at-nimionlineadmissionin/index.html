7CITS 2026 Merit List - How It Is Prepared
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe CITS AICET 2026 merit list is compiled by DGT/NIMI based on candidate performance in the entrance test, reservation norms, and trade preferences. Here is how the ranking process works.
2listunordered
  • Merit list basis: AICET scores out of 100 marks.
  • Category-wise ranking: Separate merit lists are prepared for General, SC, ST, OBC, EWS, and PwD categories.
  • Tie-breaking: In case two candidates obtain identical scores, a tie-breaking method is applied as per the official prospectus.
  • Trade mapping: Candidates are ranked within the trade they applied for; an incorrect trade selection can result in rejection even after qualifying.
  • Cut-off marks: Cut-offs vary by trade, category, and institute, and are available alongside the result on the official portal.

  • Candidates must report to the allotted NSTI/IToT with original documents and pay admission fees to confirm their seat.

10About CITS (Craft Instructor Training Scheme) 2026-27
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphCITS is a one-year training programme run by the Directorate General of Training (DGT), Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship, Government of India. It prepares qualified candidates to become certified instructors at Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) and similar vocational training institutions across the country.
2listunordered
  • Training is conducted at National Skill Training Institutes (NSTIs) and Institutes of Training of Trainers (IToTs).
  • Successful candidates are awarded the National Craft Instructor Certificate (NCIC), a prestigious qualification recognized across India's vocational training ecosystem.
  • The programme covers both trade theory at the instructor level and modern pedagogical skills including lesson planning, evaluation techniques, and practical workshop management.
