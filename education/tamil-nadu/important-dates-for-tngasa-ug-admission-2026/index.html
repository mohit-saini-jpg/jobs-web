Details

TNGASA UG Admission 2026 has officially started. The Directorate of Collegiate Education, Tamil Nadu, has opened the online registration process for admission to various undergraduate courses in 181 Government Arts and Science Colleges across the state. Online applications commenced on 7 May 2026, and the last date to submit the TNGASA UG application form is 29 May 2026.

This is a golden opportunity for students looking for quality higher education in government institutions at affordable fees.

TNGASA UG Admission 2026: Eligibility Criteria
Educational QualificationMust have passed Higher Secondary (Class 12) or equivalent examination
Minimum MarksAs per course and category requirements
Age LimitUpper age limit is 21 years as on 01 July 2026 (relaxation applicable)
Nationality/DomicileTamil Nadu domicile preferred; open to others as per rules

Note: Relaxation in age and marks is provided for SC/SCA/ST, BC, MBC, DNC, and Differently Abled candidates as per Government norms.

TNGASA UG Application Fee 2026
SC / SCA / ST₹2/-
All Other Categories₹50/-

Fee is payable online. No refund will be issued after payment.

How to Apply for TNGASA UG Admission 2026
  1. Visit the official website https://www.tngasa.in/
  2. Click on Registration / Fees Payment
  3. Fill Personal, Academic, and Special Category details
  4. Pay the application fee online
  5. Complete Choice Filling (College & Course preferences)
  6. Upload required documents and photo
  7. Preview, submit, and download the application form
Documents Required for TNGASA UG 2026
  • Class 10 and Class 12 mark sheets
  • Transfer Certificate
  • Community Certificate (if applicable)
  • Income Certificate / EWS Certificate
  • Differently Abled Certificate (if applicable)
  • Passport-size photograph and signature
  • Nativity Certificate (if required)
TNGASA UG Counselling and Seat Allotment 2026

Admission is purely merit-based on Class 12 marks. After application, rank lists will be published. Eligible candidates will exercise web options for college and course preferences. Seat allotment will be done based on merit, reservation, and choices filled. Candidates must report to the allotted college with original documents for confirmation.
