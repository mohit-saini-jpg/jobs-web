TN SSLC 2026 Provisional Mark Sheet

The Directorate of Government Examinations, Tamil Nadu has released the TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet for all Class 10 students who appeared in the March/April 2026 public examinations. Students can now access their provisional marksheets online and use them immediately for admission to higher studies, counselling and documentation purposes.

This provisional marksheet is an important document because it serves as an official record of your subject-wise marks, total marks, grades and pass/fail status until the original mark sheet and certificate are distributed through schools. Students are strongly advised to download, save and print their provisional mark sheet as soon as possible to avoid last-minute issues during the admission process.

Official Websites to Download TN SSLC 10th Provisional Mark Sheet 2026

The TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet is available only through the official portals authorised by the Directorate of Government Examinations, Tamil Nadu. Students should avoid entering their registration number and date of birth on unverified websites or third-party links to protect their personal data.

You can download the provisional marksheet from the following official websites:

  • DGE Tamil Nadu official website: dge.tn.gov.in
  • TN Results portal: tnresults.nic.in
  • DigiLocker portal or app: digilocker.gov.in

If any of these websites is temporarily slow due to heavy traffic, students should wait for some time, refresh the page, or try again during non-peak hours.

How to Download TN SSLC 2026 Provisional Mark Sheet Online

Downloading the TN SSLC 2026 provisional marksheet is a simple process. Students can use either the TN Results portal, the DGE TN website, or DigiLocker. Below are the step-by-step instructions.

Steps to Download at tnresults.nic.in or dge.tn.gov.in

  1. Open any browser on your mobile, laptop or desktop and visit tnresults.nic.in or dge.tn.gov.in.
  2. On the homepage, look for the link that mentions “SSLC Examination 2026 - Provisional Mark Sheet”, “SSLC March/April 2026 Provisional Marks” or similar.
  3. Click on this link to open the mark sheet login page.
  4. Enter your SSLC registration number carefully in the first field.
  5. In the date of birth field, enter your date of birth exactly in the format mentioned on the page (for example, DD/MM/YYYY).
  6. After entering the details, click on the “Submit”, “Get Marks” or “View Mark Sheet” button.
  7. Your TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet will be displayed on the screen with your name, subjects, marks and result status.
  8. Check all details carefully, then click on the download or print icon and save the PDF on your device.
  9. Take one or two clear printouts on A4 paper for use in admissions and future reference.

If the page does not load or shows an error, double-check the registration number and date of birth. If the problem continues, wait and try again later, or contact your school for help.

How to Download TN SSLC 2026 Marksheet via DigiLocker

DigiLocker provides a secure, government-backed digital storage platform where students can access their marksheets anytime, even if they lose the printed copy.

Follow these steps to get your TN SSLC 2026 mark sheet through DigiLocker:

  1. Visit digilocker.gov.in or open the DigiLocker mobile app on your phone.
  2. Sign in using your registered mobile number or Aadhaar-linked account. If you are a new user, create an account by providing your mobile number and verifying it with OTP.
  3. After logging in, go to the “Issued Documents” section or search for “Tamil Nadu State Board” in the Education category.
  4. Select “Tamil Nadu SSLC 10th Result / Marksheet 2026” or the relevant SSLC 2026 service from the list.
  5. You may be asked to enter your registration number, date of birth and year of examination. Fill these details exactly as per your hall ticket.
  6. Once submitted, DigiLocker will fetch your TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet directly from the board’s database and store it in your account.
  7. You can download, view, or share this digital mark sheet whenever required.

Using DigiLocker helps you maintain a permanent digital record of your result, which is especially helpful for future verification and online admissions.

Details Mentioned on TN SSLC 2026 Provisional Mark Sheet

The TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet contains several important details that students must verify carefully. Any error in these details can cause issues during admission or document verification later.

Generally, the provisional mark sheet includes:

  • Student’s full name
  • Registration number and, in some cases, roll number
  • Date of birth
  • Name and code of the school (for regular students)
  • Name of examination: SSLC / Class 10
  • Academic year and month/year of examination
  • List of subjects with subject codes
  • Subject-wise marks (theory and practical, if applicable)
  • Total marks obtained
  • Grade or grade point, as per board’s grading system
  • Result status: Pass / Fail / Absent, etc.
  • Remarks, if any (for example, eligibility for compartment/supplementary exam)
  • Official seal and note stating that it is a provisional mark sheet

After downloading, students should match the spelling of their name and parents’ names (if printed), subject list, and marks with what they remember and with their hall ticket. In case of any discrepancy, they should immediately contact their school authorities or the board through the prescribed channels.

TN SSLC 2026 Answer Script Photocopy, Revaluation and Retotalling

Many students feel that their marks do not truly reflect their performance, especially in theory subjects. For such cases, the Directorate of Government Examinations allows students to apply for scanned copies of answer scripts, and later for revaluation or retotalling.

This process is time-bound and takes place in stages:

  • First, students apply for a scanned copy/photocopy of their answer script for the desired subjects.
  • After receiving and verifying the scanned copy, students can decide whether to apply for revaluation or retotalling.
TN SSLC 2026 Answer Script Photocopy Application: Key Points

The exact dates and timings are mentioned in the official DGE Tamil Nadu notification. However, the general pattern and rules are usually as follows:

  • Application is made through the student’s school for regular candidates, and through the exam centre or designated centres for private candidates.
  • The application window remains open only for a few days after the result and provisional mark sheet release.
  • Students must fill out a form mentioning the subject(s) for which they want the answer script photocopy.
  • A fixed fee per subject is to be paid at the time of application. This fee is non-refundable.

Only students who apply for and receive the scanned copies of their answer scripts are allowed to proceed further to revaluation or retotalling for those subjects. Therefore, students who are seriously doubtful about their marks should not miss this first step.

TN SSLC 2026 Revaluation / Retotalling Process

After the photocopy stage, DGE Tamil Nadu opens a separate window for revaluation and retotalling applications. The process typically involves:

  1. Checking the scanned answer script carefully to identify possible mistakes in evaluation, totaling, or unmarked answers.
  2. Deciding whether the perceived error is serious enough to justify applying for revaluation/retotalling.
  3. Submitting a revaluation or retotalling request either online or through your school, as specified by DGE.
  4. Paying the prescribed fee per subject for revaluation or retotalling.
  5. Keeping the acknowledgment receipt or application number safely for future tracking.

After the revaluation or retotalling process is completed, the board publishes the updated result. If the marks increase or decrease, the final marks after revaluation become binding and will be updated in the student’s record and final mark sheet.

TN SSLC 2026 Supplementary Exams (For Students Who Did Not Pass)

The Tamil Nadu board offers a second chance to students who fail in one or more subjects in the TN SSLC 2026 examination. These students can appear in the supplementary or compartment exams and attempt to clear the pending subjects within the same academic year.

Important points about supplementary exams generally include:

  • Eligibility: Students who are marked “Fail” or “Absent” in one or more subjects in the main TN SSLC 2026 exam.
  • Application: Done through schools or exam centres, usually soon after the main result announcement.
  • Subjects: Students can appear only in the subjects in which they have failed.
  • Dates: Conducted a few weeks or months after the main results; detailed time-table is shared by the DGE TN on its official website.

Students appearing for supplementary exams should carefully review their provisional mark sheet, identify weak areas, and follow a focused study plan so that they can clear all pending subjects in the next attempt.

Important Instructions for Students After Downloading TN SSLC 2026 Provisional Mark Sheet

Once you download your TN SSLC 2026 provisional mark sheet, keep the following points in mind:

  • Check every detail - name, registration number, date of birth, subject list and marks - for correctness.
  • Save a soft copy (PDF) of your mark sheet to your phone, email, or cloud storage so that you can access it anytime.
  • Take multiple clear printouts and keep them in a safe folder; you may need them for school admissions, counselling and scholarship forms.
  • If your marks are not as expected, discuss with your parents and teachers and decide quickly about applying for answer script photocopy and revaluation/retotalling within the official deadline.
  • Follow only official notices on dge.tn.gov.in and tnresults.nic.in or circulars from your school. Avoid believing rumours on social media regarding changes in marks, new rules or unofficial revaluation options.

Proper handling of your provisional mark sheet and timely decisions about revaluation or supplementary exams can significantly impact your academic journey.
