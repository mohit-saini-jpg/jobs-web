Queen Mary's College (Autonomous), Chennai (QMC Chennai) has opened the application process for 2026 admissions primarily through the centralized Tamil Nadu Government Arts and Science Colleges Admission (TNGASA) portal. Women candidates can apply online for various UG and PG courses based on merit and counselling.

Queen Mary's College Application Form 2026 Overview

Queen Mary's College (Autonomous), Chennai, affiliated to the University of Madras and accredited with NAAC 'A+' grade (62nd in NIRF 2025), offers admissions to UG (BA, B.Sc, B.Com, BCA) and PG courses. Admissions for most UG programs occur through the TNGASA portal, emphasizing merit from qualifying exams (HSC for UG) followed by counselling. Only women candidates are eligible.

  • UG Application: Apply via TNGASA
  • PG Application: Apply via TNGASAPG

Queen Mary's College Application Fee 2026

Application fees are typically handled through the centralized TNGASA portal. Specific fee details (including category-wise) are mentioned in the official prospectus on tngasa.in. No separate college fee is charged at the application stage for most UG programs.

How to Apply for Queen Mary's College Application Form 2026 (Step-by-Step)
  1. Visit the official TNGASA portal: www.tngasa.in.
  2. Register with valid details and fill the online application form.
  3. Select Queen Mary's College and preferred courses.
  4. Upload required documents and pay the application fee (if applicable).
  5. Submit and download the confirmation. Track merit lists and counselling schedules on the college website or TNGASA portal.
Queen Mary's College Admission Process & Selection Criteria

Admissions are merit-based on HSC marks (Part-III total out of 400 for UG) for most courses, followed by counselling. Rank lists are published on the college website. MCA requires TANCET scores. Special quotas exist for Physically Challenged, Sports, Ex-Servicemen, etc. Shift I and II admissions occur on the same day.
