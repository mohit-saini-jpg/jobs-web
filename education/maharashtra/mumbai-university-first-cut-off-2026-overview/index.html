How to Check Mumbai University First Cut Off 2026 Online

This section helps students who search with queries like “how to check MU first cut off”, “how to download Mumbai University merit list”, etc.

Step-by-step process to download MU 1st Merit List

  • Step 1: Open your browser and visit the official Mumbai University website https://www.mu.ac.in/ or the official MU UG admission portal, if provided.
  • Step 2: On the home page, find and click on the link for “UG Admissions 2026-27” or “First Merit List / First Cut Off 2026”.
  • Step 3: Select your stream and programme (for example, BA, BCom, BSc, BMS, BAF, BAMMC, etc.).
  • Step 4: Choose the name of your college from the list of participating colleges.
  • Step 5: Click on the link for the “First Merit List 2026” or “First Cut Off 2026” PDF for that college and course.
  • Step 6: The MU first merit list / cut off PDF will open on the screen. Check your name, application number, category and percentage.
  • Step 7: Download the PDF and save or print it for future reference.

If the link is not visible on the university portal, students should directly check their applied college’s official website for the latest merit list.

Courses and Colleges Covered Under MU First Cut Off 2026

The Mumbai University First Cut Off 2026 applies to a wide range of UG programmes across its affiliated colleges. Some of the major UG courses typically covered include:

  • Bachelor of Arts (BA)
  • Bachelor of Commerce (BCom)
  • Bachelor of Science (BSc)
  • Bachelor of Management Studies (BMS)
  • Bachelor of Accounting and Finance (BAF)
  • Bachelor of Arts in Multimedia and Mass Communication (BAMMC)
  • Other specialised undergraduate programmes, as notified

Top and popular MU-affiliated colleges generally publish separate course-wise and category-wise cut offs. These colleges often see higher first cut off percentages, especially for high-demand courses like BCom and BMS.

Eligibility Criteria for Mumbai University UG Admission 2026

Eligibility for MU UG admission is usually based on performance in the Class 12 or equivalent examination, along with course-specific requirements. While colleges may issue detailed criteria, the basic conditions include:

  • Candidates must have passed Class 12 (HSC) or an equivalent exam from a recognised board.
  • They must satisfy subject-wise and aggregate percentage requirements specified for the chosen course (for example, certain minimum marks in Commerce subjects for BCom, or in Science subjects for BSc).
  • Category-wise relaxations, reservations and quotas are applied as per government and university rules, where applicable.

Students should refer to the official prospectus or admission notification of their chosen college for precise course-wise eligibility details.

Documents Required After Selection in First Merit List

Once a candidate finds their name in the Mumbai University First Merit List 2026, they must prepare documents for verification and admission confirmation. Although the exact list may vary by college, the commonly required documents are:

  • Class 10 mark sheet and passing certificate
  • Class 12 mark sheet and passing certificate
  • Transfer Certificate (TC) / School Leaving Certificate
  • Migration Certificate (for candidates from other boards, if required)
  • Caste Certificate / Category Certificate (for reserved category students, if applicable)
  • Non-Creamy Layer Certificate (for applicable categories, if required)
  • Domicile Certificate (where required as per rules)
  • Recent passport-size photographs
  • Printout of the Mumbai University UG admission form
  • Printout or copy of the relevant merit list or cut off page showing the candidate’s name
  • Any additional documents specified by the college (such as gap certificate, undertakings, etc.)

Candidates should carry original documents along with photocopies as instructed by the college.

What Happens After Mumbai University First Cut Off 2026?

Getting listed in the first merit list is only the first step. Candidates must complete further admission formalities within the given time limit to secure their seat.

  • Confirm admission at the allotted college within the schedule mentioned in the notice.
  • Visit the college (or follow online instructions, if permitted) for document verification.
  • Pay the prescribed admission fee through the mode specified by the college (online or offline).
  • Keep the fee receipt and acknowledgement safe for future reference.
  • Track college notifications for orientation, timetable and further academic instructions.

If a candidate does not complete the process in time, their provisional offer may be cancelled and the seat may be allotted to another applicant in the second or third merit list.

Candidates whose names do not appear in the first list should wait for the second and third lists, or explore other colleges and courses where seats are still available.

Cut Off Trends for Mumbai University First Merit List

While exact percentages differ by college, course and category, some common patterns are seen every year:

  • Highly reputed colleges and courses often have very high first cut off percentages, especially for the open/general category.
  • Commerce and management programmes (such as BCom and BMS) in popular colleges usually see one of the highest first cut off levels.
  • Science courses with limited seats or specialised combinations may also display high merit list thresholds.
  • Mid-tier colleges and less competitive courses tend to have slightly lower cut offs, giving more flexibility to students with moderate scores.
  • Subsequent merit lists (second and third) sometimes show marginal relaxation in cut off percentages if seats remain vacant.

Students are advised to compare current cut offs with previous years’ trends for their target college and course to assess how competitive the admission is likely to be.

Mumbai University First Cut Off 2026 - Important Instructions for Students

To ensure a smooth admission process, candidates should keep the following points in mind:

  • Check only official university and college websites for cut off and merit list updates.
  • Note down merit list dates, document verification dates and fee payment deadlines carefully.
  • Keep multiple copies of all important documents ready to avoid last-minute issues.
  • Ensure that your contact details (mobile number and email ID) in the application form are correct and active so you do not miss any communication.
  • If you have applied to multiple colleges, check all relevant merit lists to maximise your chances of admission.
  • In case of any discrepancy in name, marks or category details, contact the college admission office immediately with supporting documents.
