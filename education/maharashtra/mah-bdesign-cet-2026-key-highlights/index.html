3What Is the MAH B.Design CET 2026 Tentative Score Card?
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe tentative score card is the first official release of a candidate's marks in the MAH B.Design CET 2026 examination. It is called "tentative" because the scores may be revised after the grievance and objection redressal process is completed. Once all grievances are resolved, the CET Cell releases the final score card, which is used as the basis for the Centralised Admission Process (CAP) and counselling for Bachelor of Design programmes across participating colleges in Maharashtra.
2paragraphThe MAH B.Design CET 2026 was conducted on April 5, 2026, at various examination centres within Maharashtra State, in offline mode.
4How to Download MAH B.Design CET 2026 Scorecard

2listordered

11Grievance Redressal Date
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe CET Cell will review and resolve submitted grievances by June 18, 2026. Candidates can track the status of their objections through the "Objection Tracking" feature available in their candidate login.
12About MAH B.Design CET 2026
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe MAH B.Design CET (Maharashtra Bachelor of Design Common Entrance Test) is conducted by the Maharashtra State Common Entrance Test (CET) Cell, Mumbai, on behalf of the state government. The examination serves as the gateway for admission to professional Bachelor of Design (B.Des) programmes offered by colleges and universities across Maharashtra for the academic year 2026-27.
2paragraphThe test was conducted in offline (pen-and-paper) mode on April 5, 2026, at multiple designated examination centres spread across Maharashtra State.
13What's Next After the MAH B.Design CET 2026 Scorecard?
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphAfter the grievance redressal process is completed on June 18, 2026, candidates can expect the following steps in the admission journey.
2listunordered
  • Final Score Card Release: The CET Cell will publish the final, confirmed score cards after resolving all grievances.
  • CAP Registration: Eligible candidates with valid scores will be required to register for the Centralised Admission Process (CAP).
  • Merit List / Rank List: A merit list will be prepared based on the final scores.
  • CAP Counselling Rounds: Seat allotment will take place through multiple CAP rounds, during which candidates can exercise their college and course preferences.
