MP DElEd Admit Card 2026 Out for 1st & 2nd Year

Madhya Pradesh Board of Secondary Education (MPBSE) has released the MP DElEd Admit Card 2026 for both 1st year and 2nd year Diploma in Elementary Education exams. The hall tickets are available online on the MPBSE MPOnline portal and can be downloaded separately for first‑year and second‑year students.

The MP DElEd 1st and 2nd year exams will be conducted in June 2026, and candidates must carry their admit card to the examination centre on all exam days. The direct links for “Print Admit Card for D.El.Ed 1st Year 2026” and “Print Admit Card for D.El.Ed 2nd Year 2026” have been activated on the official website.

How to Download MP DElEd Admit Card 2026 (1st & 2nd Year)

Follow these steps to download your MP DElEd 1st year or 2nd year admit card 2026:

  • Visit the official MPBSE MPOnline portal mpbse.mponline.gov.in.
  • On the homepage, look for the D.El.Ed forms / examination section and click on the link for “Print Admit Card for D.El.Ed 1st Year 2026” or “Print Admit Card for D.El.Ed 2nd Year 2026” as applicable.
  • You will be redirected to the admit card page (/AngularMpbse/#/deled1stadmitcard or /AngularMpbse/#/Deled2ndyearAdmitcard).
  • Enter your roll number / application number and the captcha code shown on the screen.
  • Click on Generate Admit Card / Submit. Your MP DElEd Admit Card 2026 will appear on the screen.
  • Verify all details such as name, exam year (1st/2nd), subjects and exam centre. Then download and print 2-3 clear A4‑size copies of the hall ticket.

If you face any issue in loading the page or generating the admit card, try again after some time or from a different browser, and contact your institute/MPBSE helpline if the problem continues.

Details Mentioned on MP DElEd Admit Card 2026

The MP DElEd 1st and 2nd year admit cards 2026 contain key candidate and exam information that must be checked carefully:

  • Candidate’s name and father’s name.
  • Roll number / application number.
  • Class / year: DElEd 1st Year or DElEd 2nd Year.
  • Exam session and year (June 2026).
  • Exam centre name, code and full address.
  • Subject names / paper codes and exam dates as per timetable.
  • Candidate’s photograph (if printed) and other basic details.
  • Important instructions for candidates appearing in the examination.

In case of any discrepancy in your name, year, subjects or exam centre details, you should immediately inform your college/institution or contact MPBSE/MPOnline support before the exam date.

Documents Required with MP DElEd Admit Card 2026

On each exam day of the MP DElEd 1st and 2nd year June 2026 exams, candidates must carry:

  • A printed copy of the MP DElEd Admit Card 2026 (A4‑size printout, preferably clear and not faded).
  • A valid photo identity proof such as Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN Card, Driving Licence, College ID or any other government‑issued ID, as accepted by the board/institution.

It is advisable to also keep a spare photocopy of the admit card and ID with you, and reach the centre with enough time for verification.

Exam‑Day Instructions for MP DElEd 1st & 2nd Year 2026

MPBSE and Rajya Shiksha Kendra issue detailed exam guidelines that candidates should follow during the MP DElEd June 2026 exams:

  • Reach the examination centre well before the reporting time mentioned on the admit card to complete frisking and seating; latecomers may not be allowed to enter the hall.
  • Carry your MP DElEd admit card and valid photo ID on every exam day; candidates without these documents will not be permitted to appear for the exam.
  • Bring only permitted items such as pens, pencils and basic stationery, as per board instructions; avoid carrying mobiles, smart watches, calculators (if not allowed), notes, books or any electronic devices into the exam hall.
  • Sit on the seat allotted to you and follow all instructions given by invigilators; any involvement in unfair means can lead to cancellation of your paper and further disciplinary action.
  • Keep your admit card safely even after the exams, as it may be required for future reference, internal verification or result checking.
