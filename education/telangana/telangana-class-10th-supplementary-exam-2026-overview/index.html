The Directorate of Government Examinations, Telangana conducts these exams in offline mode at designated centres across the state.

Who Is Eligible for Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exam 2026?

The Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exam 2026 is meant for students who need one more chance to clear their SSC subjects. Eligibility typically includes:

  • Students who failed in one or more subjects in the SSC Public Exams March 2026.
  • Earlier failed candidates (from recent years under the same pattern) who still have pending subjects and want to clear them.
  • Students who want to appear only for the specific subjects in which they have failed, not all papers.

Applications are generally not accepted directly from students on the board website; all eligible candidates must apply through their respective school headmaster or principal.

Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exam 2026 Application Process

The application and fee payment process for TS SSC Supplementary Exam 2026 is routed through schools to ensure accurate data and timely submission.

How to Apply for TS SSC Supplementary Exam 2026

Students can follow these steps to apply for the Telangana 10th supplementary exams:

  • Visit your school and contact the headmaster or exam in-charge soon after the SSC results are declared.
  • Inform them clearly about the subjects in which you want to appear for the supplementary exam.
  • Pay the prescribed exam fee to the school within the notified deadline (usually in cash or any accepted mode as per school instructions).
  • The school will enter your details and subjects online on the board portal and remit the consolidated fee through the integrated system.
  • Collect and keep a copy of the acknowledgement or receipt from the school as proof of your application.

Before the school finalises the entries, students should verify their name, subject codes, medium and other details to avoid errors on the hall ticket.

Exam Fee and Late Fee

Exact fee amounts are specified in the official notification every year, but the structure follows a subject-wise or group-wise pattern. Keep in mind the following points:

  • Students pay the exam fee to their school; the school then pays it online to the board.
  • A small late fee is usually allowed up to two days before the exam date of that subject, but this window is very short.
  • Paying on time ensures timely generation of hall tickets and avoids unnecessary penalties.

For the most accurate and updated fee details, students should check the official notification or consult their school.

Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Time Table 2026

The TS SSC Supplementary Exam 2026 time table covers all major subjects over multiple days between 5 June and 12 June 2026 in a single morning shift. The schedule is designed so that students get enough gaps between core papers like Mathematics, Science and Social Studies.

The detailed subject-wise time table is published in the official notification and circulated to all schools. Students should copy the dates and timings carefully into their study planner so that they reach the exam centre on the correct day and time.

TS SSC Recounting, Re-verification and Supplementary Exams

After the announcement of SSC March 2026 results, many students apply for recounting or re-verification of their answer scripts while also worrying about supplementary exam dates. The board usually allows students to apply for supplementary exams without waiting for recounting or re-verification results because the time gap between result and supplementary exams is very short.

If a student’s marks increase after recounting or re-verification and they pass a subject, they will no longer need to write that subject in the supplementary exam even if they have already paid the fee; however, students should follow the latest instructions issued by the board and their school regarding such cases. It is generally safer to apply for both processes within the given timelines if the student is unsure about their performance.

TS SSC Supplementary Exam 2026 Results and Marks Memo

The Telangana SSC Supplementary Exam 2026 results are usually released a few weeks after the completion of the exams. The board publishes the results online so that students across the state can access them easily.

Once results are declared:

  • Students can visit the official website and enter their hall ticket number and other required details to view their supplementary results.
  • After verification, students can download or obtain an updated marks memo (mark sheet) that reflects the new marks for subjects cleared in the supplementary exams.
  • This updated marks memo can be used to participate in Intermediate admissions and other Class 11 entry processes for the same academic year.

Clearing the supplementary examinations in 2026 allows students to continue their academic journey without losing an entire year.

Why Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exams 2026 Are Important

The Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exams 2026 are crucial because they act as a second chance for students who could not clear one or more subjects in the main SSC exams. Instead of waiting an entire year, students get an opportunity within weeks to correct their mistakes.

This system helps students:

  • Avoid academic gaps and directly join Intermediate (or other equivalent courses).
  • Improve their confidence and belief in their own abilities by overcoming previous failures.
  • Build stronger fundamentals in subjects they initially struggled with, which is especially important for higher studies.

With focused preparation and the right mindset, many students successfully clear these supplementary exams and move forward with a fresh start.

Preparation Tips for TS SSC Supplementary Exam 2026

Good preparation can significantly improve the chances of success in Telangana Class 10th Supplementary Exam 2026. Instead of re-reading everything, students should focus on their weak areas and exam strategy.

Here are some practical tips:

  • Start by analysing your March 2026 marks memo and note down the subjects and chapters in which you lost maximum marks.
  • Use your SSC textbooks, class notes and teacher guidance to revise only the syllabus that is relevant for the exam.
  • Practise previous years’ SSC question papers and model papers to become comfortable with the pattern and frequently repeated questions.
  • Prepare a simple daily schedule with fixed study slots for each subject and include a short revision session every night.
  • Avoid last‑minute cramming; instead, revise formulas, important points, maps, diagrams and key definitions regularly.
  • Take proper sleep, eat light food and stay hydrated so that you can concentrate during both preparation and exams.

Even if the time between result and exam is short, disciplined revision and targeted practice can make a big difference.
