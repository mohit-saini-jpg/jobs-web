What Is BITSAT Test City Allotment?

BITSAT test city allotment is the process in which BITS Pilani informs each registered candidate about the city where their BITSAT 2026 exam centre will be located. During the online application form, candidates were required to select three preferred test cities in order of priority, and the institute allots one of these cities based on availability and operational feasibility.

The test city allotment is only an intimation of the exam city and does not show the exact exam centre address, exam date or exam shift. Those details are finalised later through the slot booking process and are printed in the BITSAT 2026 admit card.

How to Check BITSAT 2026 Test City Allotment?

Step-by-Step Process to Check Allotted Test City

Candidates can check their BITSAT 2026 test city allotment in a few simple steps:

  • Visit the official BITS admission website: Click Here.
  • Click on the BITSAT 2026 - Test City Allotment / Candidate Login link on the homepage or BITSAT 2026 section.
  • Enter your BITSAT 2026 application number and password generated at the time of registration.
  • Complete any security code / captcha verification, if asked.
  • Click on the Login or Proceed button to access your dashboard.
  • On the dashboard, click on the option for Test City Allotment / City Intimation Slip.
  • Your allotted test city for BITSAT 2026 Session 1 will be displayed on the screen.
  • Download the intimation slip or take a clear screenshot and save it for future reference, especially for travel planning and slot booking.

Forgot Application Number or Password?

If a candidate has forgotten their BITSAT 2026 application number or password, they should:

  • Use the “Forgot Application Number/Password” option (or similar link) provided on the candidate login page.
  • Provide the registered email ID, mobile number and other details requested in the recovery form.
  • Check the registered email inbox/SMS for the recovered application number or password reset link and then log in again to view the city allotment.

Candidates must ensure that the email ID and mobile number used during registration remain active and accessible throughout the admission process.

BITSAT 2026 Exam Centres & Test City Allotment Rules

How Many BITSAT Exam Cities Are Available?

BITSAT 2026 is conducted in a large number of test cities across India, as mentioned in the official information brochure and on the BITSAT page of the BITS Pilani website. The list usually covers major cities and regional centres so that candidates from different parts of the country can select a convenient location.

While filling the online application form, candidates had to select three exam cities in order of preference. These preferences form the primary basis for the test city allotment, subject to seat availability in each centre.

How BITS Pilani Allots Your Test City

BITS Pilani allots the BITSAT 2026 test city to each candidate by considering multiple factors:

  • The three preferred cities chosen by the candidate in the application form.
  • The availability of seats in the test centres of those cities.
  • The total number of applicants for each city and operational constraints such as infrastructure and capacity.

In most cases, candidates are allotted one of their preferred cities, but this is not guaranteed, especially if seats in a particular city get filled or if there are logistical limitations. Once the city is allotted and displayed in the candidate login, changes are usually not permitted, except in very rare and specific situations as per BITS rules.

After City Allotment - What to Do Next?

Complete BITSAT 2026 Slot Booking

The next step after checking the BITSAT 2026 test city allotment is slot booking. In this process, candidates select:

  • Their exam date from the available days in the Session 1 window.
  • Their exam slot/shift (for example, forenoon or afternoon, depending on the schedule).

Key points for slot booking:

  • Slot booking is available only for a limited time as announced on the official website.
  • Allotment of preferred date and slot depends on real-time availability; early booking generally offers more options.
  • If a candidate misses the slot booking deadline, the exam conducting authorities may auto-assign a date and slot based on remaining availability.

Download BITSAT 2026 Admit Card

After slot booking closes, BITS Pilani releases the BITSAT 2026 admit card for Session 1 on the admission portal. Candidates must log in again using their application number and password to download the admit card.

The BITSAT 2026 admit card will contain:

  • Candidate’s name, photograph and signature.
  • Application number and basic details.
  • Exam date and time (slot) chosen or allotted.
  • Complete exam centre address within the allotted city.
  • Reporting time and gate closing time.
  • Important exam day instructions and guidelines.

Candidates should print the admit card on A4-size paper and check all details carefully. Any discrepancy should be reported to the helpdesk within the given time frame.

Important Instructions for BITSAT 2026 Exam Day

Candidates appearing for BITSAT 2026 must follow all exam day instructions mentioned on the admit card and on the official website:

  • Reach the exam centre well before the reporting time to complete security checks and biometric verification without rush.
  • Carry the printed admit card, a valid photo ID proof (such as Aadhaar card, Passport, Driving Licence, College ID as allowed) and any other documents mentioned in the instructions.
  • Do not carry prohibited items such as mobile phones, calculators, smartwatches, notes or electronic gadgets to the exam hall.
  • Follow all directions of the invigilators and maintain discipline inside the examination premises.

Reaching the city a day earlier (if it is far from your home town) can help avoid last-minute travel issues and ensure a stress-free exam attempt.
