3BITSAT 2026 Session 2 Eligibility Criteria

4How to Apply for BITSAT 2026 Session 2
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphCandidates who did not register during the initial BITSAT 2026 application window get a separate opportunity to apply exclusively for Session 2 during the dedicated Session 2 registration period. Those who have already selected “both sessions” while filling the original form do not need to submit a fresh application for Session 2.
2paragraphStep-by-step process to apply for BITSAT 2026 Session 2:
3listordered
  • Visit the official BITSAT 2026 admissions website (admissions.bits-pilani.ac.in) and click on the BITSAT link on the homepage.
  • Register as a new user by providing a valid email ID, mobile number and basic details to generate login credentials.
  • Log in to the application portal and fill in personal, academic and contact information accurately, matching your Class 12 records.
  • Choose your preferred exam cities and select the appropriate option for Session 2 only (or both sessions if the option is available and applicable).
  • Upload a recent passport-size photograph and signature in the specified format and size guidelines mentioned in the brochure.
  • Pay the prescribed application fee online through net banking, debit card or credit card, and submit the form before the last date.
4paragraphThe form should be reviewed carefully before final submission, as incorrect details can lead to issues during slot booking, hall ticket generation or admission. If any mistakes are noticed later, candidates can use the form correction window for Session 2 to rectify permitted fields within the given dates.
5BITSAT 2026 Session 2 Slot Booking Process

For Session 2, the slot booking window is open from 13 May 2026 to 15 May 2026, and candidates must complete this process within the specified period.
2paragraphHow to book BITSAT Session 2 slot:
3listordered
  • Go to the official BITSAT 2026 portal and log in with your application number and password.
  • Click on the “BITSAT 2026 Session 2 Slot Booking” link once it becomes active on the dashboard.
  • View the list of available dates, time slots and test centres for Session 2 and select the combination that best suits your schedule.
  • Confirm your choices and submit the slot booking form; once submitted, changes may not be allowed, so double-check before finalising.

6How to Download BITSAT 2026 Session 2 Hall Ticket
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe BITSAT 2026 Session 2 hall ticket (admit card) is issued online and can be downloaded from 20 May 2026 until the day of the exam. Only those candidates who have submitted the application form successfully and completed slot booking are eligible to download their Session 2 admit card.
2paragraphSteps to download BITSAT Session 2 admit card:
3listordered
  • Visit the official BITSAT 2026 admissions website and click on the “Download Hall Ticket” link for Session 2.
  • Log in using your application number and password or date of birth, as specified in the portal instructions.
  • After logging in, click on the hall ticket download link and verify details such as your name, photograph, test date, shift and test centre address.
  • Download the admit card in PDF format, print multiple copies and keep them safely for the exam day and future reference.
4paragraphThe hall ticket must be carried to the exam centre along with a valid photo ID proof, and these documents are verified at the entry gate before allowing candidates to proceed to the test lab. Any discrepancy in the hall ticket details should be reported to BITS Pilani authorities immediately for rectification before the exam.

9BITSAT 2026 Session 1 vs Session 2 - Which Attempt Should You Choose?
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphBITSAT allows candidates to appear in Session 1, Session 2 or both, and the best of the two scores is considered for admission to BITS Pilani and its other campuses. This structure gives students flexibility and an additional opportunity to improve their performance if they are not satisfied with their first attempt.
2paragraphSession 1 is usually held in April, while Session 2 takes place in May, giving candidates extra time to revise and analyse their earlier performance before attempting again. Choosing both sessions can be beneficial for those aiming for higher scores, but it also requires strategic planning of preparation, practice tests and travel arrangements.
