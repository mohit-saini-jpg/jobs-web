Why NEET UG 2026 Was Cancelled and Re Exam Announced

The original NEET UG 2026 exam held on 3 May was cancelled after reports emerged that a “guess paper” or leaked material closely matched a significant part of the actual question paper. Investigations by agencies such as the CBI and state-level Special Operations Groups indicated serious irregularities and possible involvement of multiple individuals in accessing and circulating the paper.

In response, the Government of India and NTA decided to cancel the exam nationwide and conduct a fresh test to protect the integrity of the medical entrance process. The re exam on 21 June 2026 is meant to give all candidates a level playing field and ensure that admissions are based on a fair and secure examination.

NEET 2026 Re Exam Date and Schedule

NEET 2026 Re Exam Date and Time

The NEET 2026 re exam will be held on 21 June 2026 (Sunday) across India, in line with the revised schedule announced by NTA. The exam will be conducted in offline, pen-and-paper mode similar to previous NEET cycles, and the detailed timing and reporting schedule will be specified in the admit card and official notice.

NTA has clarified that the syllabus, question paper pattern and marking scheme for the re exam will remain the same as the original NEET UG 2026 notification. This ensures that students can continue preparation based on the existing NEET framework without any academic disadvantage.

Important NEET 2026 Re Exam Events and Dates

Key revised milestones include:

  • NEET 2026 re exam date: 21 June 2026.
  • NEET 2026 re exam admit card: to be issued on or before 14 June 2026.
  • Advance city intimation slip: likely to be released before admit card, as per usual NTA practice.
  • Result and counselling: revised timelines will be notified after the re exam, with counselling schedules expected to shift accordingly.

Who Is Eligible for NEET 2026 Re Exam

All candidates who registered for NEET UG 2026 and were part of the original exam cycle are eligible to appear for the re exam. NTA has clearly stated that no fresh registration process will be opened; instead, the existing registration data, application forms and candidate profiles from the May 2026 exam will be used for the re exam.

There will be no additional examination fee for candidates; fees already paid for NEET UG 2026 will be refunded or adjusted as per NTA’s policy. Base eligibility criteria related to age, qualifying examinations and subject combinations remain the same as detailed in the original NEET 2026 information brochure.

NEET 2026 Re Exam Pattern, Mode and Changes

Exam Mode and Pattern

The NEET 2026 re exam will be conducted in pen-and-paper (offline) mode at designated test centres across India and, where applicable, abroad. The question paper pattern, including total questions, section-wise breakup, total marks and negative marking scheme, will follow the same structure as the original NEET UG 2026 notification.

Candidates should therefore continue to prepare based on the standard NEET exam format covering Physics, Chemistry and Biology (Botany and Zoology), with objective-type questions and the existing marking scheme.

Key Changes Announced for NEET 2026 Re Exam

Recent updates indicate that candidates appearing for the NEET 2026 re exam will get an additional 15 minutes during the test compared to the earlier schedule. The Education Minister has also mentioned that fresh admit cards will be issued and that students may get flexibility regarding exam centre selection, subject to details in the official notice.

NTA has signalled that enhanced security and monitoring measures will be implemented for the re exam, including tighter handling of question papers and stricter protocols at centres, to prevent any repeat of irregularities.

NEET 2026 Re Exam Admit Card: How to Download

NEET 2026 Re Exam Admit Card Release Date

The Education Minister has announced that NEET 2026 re exam admit cards will be released by 14 June 2026. NTA will issue fresh admit cards for the re exam, meaning the admit cards used for the 3 May exam will no longer be valid.

Step-by-Step Process to Download NEET Re Exam Admit Card

  • Visit the official NEET website: Click Here
  • Click on the link labelled “NEET UG 2026 - Re Exam Admit Card” (or similar).
  • Log in using your NEET application number, date of birth and the security pin displayed on the screen.
  • After logging in, click on “Download Admit Card” and review your personal details, exam centre, reporting time and instructions.
  • Download the admit card PDF and take at least two printouts on A4-size paper.
  • Check the exam-day guidelines printed on the admit card, including dress code and permitted items, and follow them strictly.

City Intimation, Exam Centre Change and Instructions

NTA typically publishes an advance exam city intimation slip before releasing the final admit card, allowing candidates to know their allotted exam city in advance. For NEET 2026 re exam, students should regularly check the official website for this slip, which helps in travel and accommodation planning.

Any provision to modify or confirm exam centres for the re exam will be detailed only through official notices; candidates should not rely on unofficial messages regarding centre changes. Once the admit card is issued, students must verify the centre address carefully and plan to arrive well before the reporting time to avoid last-minute issues.

NEET 2026 Re Exam Guidelines for Students

Documents to Carry on Re Exam Day

On the day of the NEET 2026 re exam, candidates should carry:

  • A printed copy of the NEET re exam admit card.
  • A valid photo identity proof such as Aadhaar card, PAN card, passport, voter ID or other documents mentioned by NTA.
  • Recent passport-size photographs as specified in the admit card/information bulletin.
  • Any additional document such as PwD certificate or category certificate if required and mentioned by NTA.

Exam-Day Instructions and Do’s and Don’ts

Students must follow reporting time, frisking and seating instructions mentioned on the admit card and in NTA guidelines. Items like mobile phones, smart watches, Bluetooth devices, notes and other electronic gadgets are typically prohibited inside the exam hall, while only permitted stationery and basic items are allowed.

Given the heightened scrutiny after the paper leak row, candidates should strictly avoid any unfair means or suspicious behaviour and cooperate fully with invigilators and centre staff. Adhering to rules will help ensure a smooth conduct of the re exam and protect the efforts of genuine aspirants.

What Happens to NEET 2026 Result and Counselling After Re Exam

Because the original exam has been cancelled, NEET 2026 results and all-India ranks will now be based only on the scores obtained in the re exam of 21 June 2026. NTA is expected to announce a revised result declaration date after successfully conducting the re exam.

Subsequently, the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) and state authorities will adjust their counselling schedules for MBBS, BDS and other courses in line with the updated NEET result timeline. Candidates are advised to regularly check official counselling portals and notices so they do not miss registration, choice filling or document verification deadlines once the new schedule is released.

Preparation Strategy for NEET 2026 Re Exam

With the re exam scheduled for 21 June 2026, students now have a limited but valuable window to revise concepts and address weak areas. Aspirants can use this time to take full-length mock tests in offline mode, simulate exam conditions and refine time management strategies.

It is also important to maintain consistency, sleep well, and avoid overconsuming rumours or unverified news about the paper leak and re exam, as this can increase stress. Focusing on NCERT-based revision, previous years’ questions and error analysis can significantly improve scores in the re exam.
