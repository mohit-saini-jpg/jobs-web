3Why NEET UG 2026 Was Cancelled and Re Exam Announced
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphThe original NEET UG 2026 exam held on 3 May was cancelled after reports emerged that a “guess paper” or leaked material closely matched a significant part of the actual question paper. Investigations by agencies such as the CBI and state-level Special Operations Groups indicated serious irregularities and possible involvement of multiple individuals in accessing and circulating the paper.
2paragraphIn response, the Government of India and NTA decided to cancel the exam nationwide and conduct a fresh test to protect the integrity of the medical entrance process. The re exam on 21 June 2026 is meant to give all candidates a level playing field and ensure that admissions are based on a fair and secure examination.

3paragraphNTA has clarified that the syllabus, question paper pattern and marking scheme for the re exam will remain the same as the original NEET UG 2026 notification. This ensures that students can continue preparation based on the existing NEET framework without any academic disadvantage.

5Who Is Eligible for NEET 2026 Re Exam
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphAll candidates who registered for NEET UG 2026 and were part of the original exam cycle are eligible to appear for the re exam. NTA has clearly stated that no fresh registration process will be opened; instead, the existing registration data, application forms and candidate profiles from the May 2026 exam will be used for the re exam.
2paragraphThere will be no additional examination fee for candidates; fees already paid for NEET UG 2026 will be refunded or adjusted as per NTA’s policy. Base eligibility criteria related to age, qualifying examinations and subject combinations remain the same as detailed in the original NEET 2026 information brochure.
6NEET 2026 Re Exam Pattern, Mode and Changes
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphExam Mode and Pattern
2paragraphThe NEET 2026 re exam will be conducted in pen-and-paper (offline) mode at designated test centres across India and, where applicable, abroad. The question paper pattern, including total questions, section-wise breakup, total marks and negative marking scheme, will follow the same structure as the original NEET UG 2026 notification.
3paragraphCandidates should therefore continue to prepare based on the standard NEET exam format covering Physics, Chemistry and Biology (Botany and Zoology), with objective-type questions and the existing marking scheme.

5paragraphRecent updates indicate that candidates appearing for the NEET 2026 re exam will get an additional 15 minutes during the test compared to the earlier schedule. The Education Minister has also mentioned that fresh admit cards will be issued and that students may get flexibility regarding exam centre selection, subject to details in the official notice.
6paragraphNTA has signalled that enhanced security and monitoring measures will be implemented for the re exam, including tighter handling of question papers and stricter protocols at centres, to prevent any repeat of irregularities.

8NEET 2026 Re Exam Admit Card: How to Download
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphNEET 2026 Re Exam Admit Card Release Date
2paragraphThe Education Minister has announced that NEET 2026 re exam admit cards will be released by 14 June 2026. NTA will issue fresh admit cards for the re exam, meaning the admit cards used for the 3 May exam will no longer be valid.
3paragraphStep-by-Step Process to Download NEET Re Exam Admit Card
4listordered
  • Visit the official NEET website: Click Here
  • Click on the link labelled “NEET UG 2026 - Re Exam Admit Card” (or similar).
  • Log in using your NEET application number, date of birth and the security pin displayed on the screen.
  • After logging in, click on “Download Admit Card” and review your personal details, exam centre, reporting time and instructions.
  • Download the admit card PDF and take at least two printouts on A4-size paper.
  • Check the exam-day guidelines printed on the admit card, including dress code and permitted items, and follow them strictly.

Sr.TypeText
1paragraphBecause the original exam has been cancelled, NEET 2026 results and all-India ranks will now be based only on the scores obtained in the re exam of 21 June 2026. NTA is expected to announce a revised result declaration date after successfully conducting the re exam.
