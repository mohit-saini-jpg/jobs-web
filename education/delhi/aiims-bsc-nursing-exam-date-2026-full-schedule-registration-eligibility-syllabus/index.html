AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 Eligibility Criteria

Before worrying about the AIIMS BSc Nursing exam date 2026, you need to be sure you actually satisfy the eligibility criteria for your category and course. While the official notice will give the final word, the general eligibility pattern for AIIMS BSc Nursing (Hons) usually looks like this.

Nationality & gender

  • Indian nationals are eligible to apply for AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026.
  • Some seats may also be available for OCI / foreign nationals as per AIIMS rules (check the latest prospectus).
  • Female candidates are primarily eligible for BSc Nursing (Hons), although AIIMS notifications sometimes specify institute‑wise rules, so check carefully.

Age limit

  • Minimum age is usually 17 years as on 31 December 2026.
  • There is generally no upper age limit for AIIMS BSc Nursing (Hons), but candidates should confirm this in the 2026 brochure once released.

Educational qualification

  • Class 12 or equivalent from a recognised board with Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English.
  • Minimum aggregate marks are typically around 55% for General / OBC and 50% for SC / ST categories (subject to official confirmation for 2026).

For BSc Nursing (Post-Basic), candidates usually need a valid 10+2 plus a registered Nursing qualification (GNM) along with registration as a nurse and midwife with the State Nursing Council.

AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 Application Process

AIIMS conducts the entire AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 application process in online mode via its PAAR portal, so there is no offline form or printed application. The process is transparent but slightly long, so doing it slowly on a laptop rather than rushing on a mobile is a good idea.

Step-by-step registration

  • Visit the official AIIMS exams website aiimsexams.ac.in and go to the BSc Nursing 2026 section.
  • Complete Basic Registration under the PAAR system by entering personal details and uploading your photo, signature and thumb impression in the correct format.
  • After acceptance of basic registration, proceed for Final Registration, where you will fill academic details, select exam city, and pay the application fee online.
  • Verify all details before submitting; mistakes in category, name or date of birth can cause issues later in counselling.

AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 Syllabus

Although the AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 syllabus is not very different from your Class 11 and 12 NCERT topics, the level of questions can be slightly tricky. Most of the paper focuses on Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and sometimes General Knowledge or English depending on the year’s pattern.

Subject-wise syllabus overview

  • Physics: Mechanics, kinematics, work, energy, power, heat and thermodynamics, waves, optics, electrostatics, current electricity, modern physics.
  • Chemistry: Atomic structure, chemical bonding, states of matter, thermodynamics, equilibrium, redox, solutions, organic chemistry basics, biomolecules.
  • Biology: Cell structure, cell division, genetics, evolution, human physiology, plant physiology, reproduction, ecology, human health and diseases.
  • General Knowledge / English (if applicable): Basic GK, current affairs related to health and science, comprehension, vocabulary and basic grammar.

AIIMS generally sticks close to the NCERT level for core topics, so finishing NCERTs properly is still one of the best strategies even in 2026.

AIIMS BSc Nursing 2026 Admit Card and Exam day guidelines

Around one week before the AIIMS BSc Nursing exam date 2026, AIIMS will release the admit card for all eligible candidates on its official website. Candidates have to download it themselves; AIIMS does not send any hard copy by post.

  • Log in using your registration ID and password and download the admit card in PDF form.
  • Check your name, photo, exam date, shift timing, and test centre address carefully.
  • Carry a printed copy of the admit card along with a valid photo ID like Aadhaar, PAN, driving licence or passport.
  • Reach the centre at least 60-90 minutes before reporting time to avoid last‑minute panic and document issues.

It’s also better not to carry unnecessary items like books, notes or electronic gadgets, since AIIMS centres are usually strict about barred items.

After the AIIMS BSc Nursing exam date 2026 is over, AIIMS declares the result in online mode only, usually within a few weeks.The result may be released as a scorecard and sometimes as a merit list with roll numbers and category‑wise cut‑off scores

Counselling and seat allotment

  • You will fill your choices of AIIMS institutes and courses in the given time window.
  • Seat allotment is done based on rank, category, preferences and seat availability.
  • Once a seat is allotted, you need to download the allotment letter and report to the respective AIIMS with original documents for verification.

AIIMS may also allow upgradation in further rounds, so if you are not happy with your first‑round seat, you can still opt for a better choice in the next round while following the official rules.

Preparation tips for AIIMS BSc Nursing exam date 2026

With the AIIMS BSc Nursing exam date 2026 already fixed in June, you actually know the exact number of months and days you have left to prepare. A slightly disciplined but flexible plan can easily push you above the cut‑off if you start on time.

  • Finish NCERT Class 11 and 12 for Physics, Chemistry and Biology thoroughly before jumping into any advanced books.
  • Practice AIIMS nursing previous year questions and mock tests to understand question framing and time pressure.

Also, take good care of your sleep in the last week before 27 June 2026, because two hours of focused mind in the exam hall will beat months of half‑sleepy study any day.
