5Provisional vs Final COMEDK 2026 Answer Key - What Changed?
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphCOMEDK first publishes a provisional answer key to allow candidates to verify their responses and report any discrepancies. For COMEDK 2026, the provisional answer key was released on 16 May 2026 along with the response sheets, and candidates were allowed to raise objections until 18 May 2026, 2 PM. Each objection required a fee payment of ₹500 per question, which was refunded if the challenge was found valid.
2paragraphAfter reviewing all the objections and supporting documents received from candidates, COMEDK finalised the corrected responses and released the final answer key on 23 May 2026. The final answer key is binding and is used to calculate scores and prepare the rank list, and there is no further provision to challenge it.
6How to Calculate Your COMEDK 2026 Score Using Final Answer Key
Sr.TypeTextStyleItems
1paragraphThe COMEDK UGET exam follows a straightforward marking scheme, making it easy to estimate your score once the final answer key and response sheet are available.
2listunordered
  • Each correct answer carries 1 mark.
  • There is no negative marking for incorrect or unattempted questions.
3paragraphTo calculate your approximate score:
4listordered
  • Download the COMEDK 2026 final answer key and your individual response sheet from comedk.org.
  • Match each question ID in your response sheet with the corresponding answer in the final key.
  • Count the total number of questions you have answered correctly.
  • Since there is no negative marking, your raw score is simply the total number of correct responses.
5paragraphThis raw score will then be used by COMEDK, along with the performance of all candidates, to derive your rank and merit position for admissions.

8COMEDK UGET 2026 Eligibility (Quick Reminder)
Sr.TypeText
1paragraphAlthough the final answer key stage comes after the exam, many students and parents landing on this page may want to re-check whether the candidate satisfied basic eligibility. COMEDK UGET 2026 is open to Indian nationals who have passed or are appearing in the 10+2 (or equivalent) examination with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects along with Chemistry / Biotechnology / Biology / Computer Science / Electronics as one of the optional subjects. For General category candidates, a minimum aggregate of 45% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics is required, while it is 40% for SC, ST and OBC candidates of Karnataka.
2paragraphCandidates are strongly advised to refer to the detailed COMEDK UGET 2026 information brochure on comedk.org for category-wise conditions, reservation rules and other academic eligibility requirements.
