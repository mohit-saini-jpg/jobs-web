What is CTI / CITS?

CITS full form and purpose

CITS stands for Craft Instructor Training Scheme. It is a specialised training programme for those who want to become instructors in ITIs and similar training institutions. CTI is commonly used in admission notices and candidate searches for this same admission process.

The scheme focuses on upgrading both technical knowledge and teaching skills. Trainees learn trade theory at instructor level, modern teaching methods, lesson planning, evaluation, and how to handle practical training in workshops.

Why CITS is important for ITI candidates

For ITI, Diploma and Degree holders, CITS is a strong pathway to build a career in teaching and training. Many state and central level recruitment rules either require or strongly prefer CITS‑qualified candidates for Instructor posts. Having CITS on your profile often makes you more competitive in ITI Instructor recruitments and related skill‑training jobs.

Step‑by‑step CTI / CITS online form process

  • Open the official portal Visit nimionlineadmission.in in your browser. On the homepage, look for “CITS Admission 2026-27” or “Apply Online” and click on it. Alternatively, go directly to cits2026.nimi.co.in/register.php if that link is active for the current session.
  • Register as a new candidate Click on “New Registration” and enter your basic details such as name, mobile number, email ID and password. Complete OTP verification if required and create your login account.
  • Login to your dashboard Use your registered email/mobile and password to log in. After login, you will see the CITS 2026 application form.
  • Fill personal details Enter your name, parents’ names, date of birth, gender, category (General / OBC / SC / ST / EWS), address and ID proof details exactly as per your official documents.
  • Enter educational details Fill in the details of your ITI / NTC / NAC / Diploma / Degree - trade or branch, year of passing, board or university, marks/percentage or grade, and certificate numbers.
  • Select CITS trade and exam city From the list, choose the CITS trade you want to apply for. Then select your preferred AICET exam city or centre from the available options.
  • Upload documents Upload scanned copies of your passport‑size photograph, signature and supporting documents (educational certificates, caste certificate, disability certificate, etc.) in the prescribed size and file format.
  • Pay application fee Proceed to the payment section and pay the required examination fee online. Once payment is successful, note down the transaction ID or download the payment receipt.
  • Submit and print confirmation Review all the details entered in the form carefully. After verifying, click on “Final Submit”. Download the filled application form and confirmation page and take a printout for future reference.

CTI / CITS Admission 2026 - Selection Process

The selection process usually follows these broad steps:

  • Online application Candidates submit their forms and pay the entrance exam fee within the specified date.
  • AICET (All India Common Entrance Test) All registered candidates appear for the CITS entrance exam at their allotted centre / city.
  • Merit list and result Based on AICET scores, a merit list is prepared. Cut‑off marks may vary by category and trade.
  • Counselling and choice filling Qualified candidates participate in online counselling, where they choose their preferred NSTI / IToT / other institutions and trades.
  • Seat allotment and reporting Seats are allotted as per score, category, preferences and availability. Candidates must report to the allotted institute with original documents and pay admission fees to confirm their seat.
