NTA GAT‑B and BET Admit Card 2026

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has released the GAT‑B and BET Admit Card 2026 for candidates appearing in the Graduate Aptitude Test‑Biotechnology (GAT‑B) and Biotechnology Eligibility Test (BET). Candidates who successfully applied can now download their hall tickets in online mode only; no hard copy will be sent by post or email.

The admit card carries details such as candidate name, roll number, exam date, shift time, reporting time, exam centre and exam‑day guidelines. Candidates must verify all details printed on the admit card and carry a printed copy along with a valid photo ID to the allotted exam centre.

How to Download NTA GAT‑B/BET Admit Card 2026
  1. Visit the official NTA exam portal (for GAT‑B/BET 2026).
  2. Click on the “GAT‑B/BET 2026 Admit Card” or “Download Hall Ticket” link on the homepage.
  3. Log in using your Application Number and Date of Birth (and security pin, if asked).
  4. Click on “Submit / Login” to view the admit card.
  5. Check all details and then download and print 2–3 clear A4‑size copies of the admit card.

If the photograph or signature on the admit card is unclear, candidates should carry additional passport‑size photographs (same as used in the application form) to the exam centre.

Details Mentioned on GAT‑B/BET Admit Card 2026
  • Candidate’s name, photograph and signature
  • Application number and roll number
  • Exam name: GAT‑B 2026 / BET 2026
  • Exam date and shift timing
  • Reporting time and gate‑closing time
  • Exam centre name, code and full address
  • Exam‑day instructions and items permitted/prohibited in the exam hall

Any discrepancy in the candidate’s particulars or exam details must be reported immediately to NTA through the official helpline before the exam date.

Documents Required with NTA GAT‑B and BET Admit Card 2026

On the examination day, candidates must carry:

  • A printed copy of the GAT‑B/BET Admit Card 2026 (clear A4‑size printout).
  • One original valid photo ID proof: Aadhaar Card / PAN Card / Passport / Voter ID / Driving Licence or any other government‑issued ID as specified in the information bulletin.
  • Additional documents if applicable (PwD certificate and scribe permission letter for candidates availing such facility).

Entry into the exam centre will not be permitted without the printed admit card and valid photo ID.
