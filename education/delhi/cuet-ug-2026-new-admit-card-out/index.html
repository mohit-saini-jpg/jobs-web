CUET UG 2026 New Admit Card Out

The National Testing Agency (NTA) has issued the new CUET UG 2026 admit card for candidates whose exam has been rescheduled. As per the latest NTA update, the updated CUET UG 2026 hall ticket has been released on 27 May 2026 for the postponed test now scheduled on 31 May 2026.

Candidates must download this revised admit card and carefully check the updated exam date, shift and examination city, as earlier admit cards for the postponed slot will not be valid for entry. The new hall ticket is available through the NTA examination services login link at examinationservices.nic.in/AdmitCardService/AcCuetUG/Login and via the CUET official site cuet.nta.nic.in.

How to Download CUET UG 2026 New Admit Card

Follow these steps to download the new CUET UG 2026 hall ticket for the rescheduled exam:

  • Go to the official link for CUET admit cards: examinationservices.nic.in/AdmitCardService/AcCuetUG/Login or visit cuet.nta.nic.in and click on “Admit Card for CUET (UG) - 2026”.
  • examinationservices.nic.in/AdmitCardService/AcCuetUG/Login or visit cuet.nta.nic.in and click on “Admit Card for CUET (UG) - 2026”.
  • On the candidate login page, enter your Application Number.
  • Enter your Password or Date of Birth in the specified format and type the Security Pin (captcha) displayed on the screen.
  • Click on Sign In / Login to open your dashboard.
  • Click on the link “CUET UG 2026 New Admit Card / Updated Admit Card” for your exam date.
  • Your CUET UG 2026 updated admit card will be displayed with revised exam date, shift and centre; download and print 2-3 A4‑size copies for use on exam day.

NTA advises students to carefully read the advisory and instructions given on the admit card and information bulletin before appearing for the CBT.

Details Mentioned on CUET UG 2026 New Admit Card

The CUET UG 2026 new admit card carries updated information about your rescheduled exam slot, including:

  • Candidate’s name and photograph.
  • CUET application number and roll number.
  • Exam name: Common University Entrance Test (CUET) UG 2026.
  • Revised exam date (e.g., 31 May 2026) and shift time (morning/afternoon/evening).
  • Examination city, centre code and full address.
  • Category, gender and PwBD status (if applicable).
  • Paper details: subjects/sections for which you will appear.
  • Important NTA instructions: reporting time, gate‑closing time, items allowed/prohibited, and dress‑code advisory.

If there is any discrepancy in your name, photograph, subjects or exam city, you must contact the NTA Helpdesk immediately via email or helpline numbers mentioned in the CUET information bulletin and admit card notice.

Documents Required for CUET UG 2026 Exam

On the day of the CUET UG 2026 exam, candidates must carry the following documents to the test centre:

  • A printed copy of the CUET UG 2026 new admit card (preferably colour, A4‑size, with all details clearly visible).
  • A valid original photo identity proof, such as Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, Voter ID, Driving Licence, or other government‑issued ID that matches the details on the admit card.
  • A recent passport‑size photograph (same as uploaded in the CUET application), to be pasted on the attendance sheet if required.

NTA also instructs candidates to bring only the items specifically allowed in the admit‑card instructions, such as a simple transparent ball‑point pen, transparent water bottle (if permitted) and PwBD certificates/supportive devices where applicable.

Exam‑Day Instructions for CUET UG 2026

NTA has issued detailed CUET UG 2026 exam‑day guidelines that candidates must follow:

  • Reporting time: Reach the exam centre at least 2 hours before the commencement of the exam to complete frisking and registration; no entry will be allowed after the gate‑closing time mentioned on your admit card.
  • Seating: A specific seat with your roll number will be allotted; you must sit only on your designated seat/terminal.
  • Prohibited items: Mobile phones, smart watches, electronic gadgets, calculators (unless allowed in advisory), log tables, handbags, notes, books, and food items are strictly prohibited inside the exam hall.
  • Dress code: Follow the simple dress code suggested by NTA (avoid metallic items, heavy jewellery, caps, scarves etc.) to ensure smooth frisking at the entrance.
  • Conduct: Follow all instructions displayed on the computer screen and given by invigilators; any involvement in unfair means can lead to cancellation of candidature and debarment from future NTA exams.
